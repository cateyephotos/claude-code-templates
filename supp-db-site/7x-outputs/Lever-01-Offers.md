# Lever 1: Your Offers — 7 Strategically Sequenced Offers

**Positioning:** Mass Appeal (low barrier, high volume)
**Tone:** Calm / authoritative, dry humor, no superfluous adjectives
**Constraint:** Pre-revenue, 1 hour/week, email-only channel, SEO-driven traffic
**Priority Revenue Target:** PDF guide sales

---

## OFFER #1 — RED (Not Interested / Unaware / Resistant)

### "Evidence Confidence Scorecard"

**Buyer Thinking:** "I don't need a supplement database — I just Google what I need or ask my doctor."

**Promise Options:**
1. "How Evidence-Backed Is Your Supplement Stack? Score it in 2 minutes."
2. "Rate Your Stack: Find out which of your supplements are backed by clinical trials — and which are marketing."
3. "The 2-Minute Supplement Evidence Check — most people score below 40%."

**Selected Promise:** Option 3 — triggers curiosity with a specific, slightly alarming benchmark.

**Value Proposition:** Most supplement users have never checked whether their stack has clinical evidence. This scorecard shows them — without judgment, without selling, without prescribing alternatives. The realization is the offer.

**Marketing Hooks:**
1. "You take 4 supplements. Do you know how many have Tier 1 evidence?"
2. "93% of supplement buyers have never checked the clinical trial data. Here's a 2-minute way to fix that."
3. "Your supplement stack has an evidence score. Want to see it?"

**Components:**
- Interactive quiz (8-10 questions about current supplements)
- Personalized evidence score (1-100)
- Brief breakdown: how many supplements fall into each evidence tier
- No fix, no pitch, no recommendations — just the score

**Pricing:** Free (diagnostic only)

**Urgency:** None. This is a recognition tool, not a sales moment.

**Risk Reversal:** N/A (free)

**Transition to Orange:** After scoring, display: "Curious why your score matters? See what the evidence tiers actually mean →"

## OFFER #2 — ORANGE A (Curious, Not Sure)

### "The Evidence Gap: Why Most Supplements Fail the Science Test"

**Buyer Thinking:** "Interesting that there's an evidence-rated database... but I can probably find this info free on Examine.com or WebMD."

**Promise Options:**
1. "Why 'Clinically Proven' Means Almost Nothing — and What to Look for Instead"
2. "The 4 Evidence Tiers That Separate Real Supplement Science from Marketing"
3. "What Your Supplement Label Doesn't Tell You About Clinical Evidence"

**Selected Promise:** Option 2 — educational, specific, introduces the framework.

**Value Proposition:** This short training reframes how the buyer evaluates supplements. It introduces the 4-tier evidence classification and shows them that "clinically proven" is a marketing phrase, not a scientific standard. Hope comes from realizing there IS a clear system for evaluation.

**Marketing Hooks:**
1. "The supplement industry uses 'clinically proven' the way fast food uses 'natural.' Here's the actual evidence scale."
2. "There's a reason your supplements feel inconsistent. It's not you — it's the evidence tier."
3. "'Clinically proven' can mean one mouse study. Here's how to read the real evidence."

**Components:**
- Email mini-series (3 emails over 5 days) OR single PDF guide
- Explains the 4-tier system with real examples (Tier 1 vs Tier 4 supplement comparison)
- Shows the top 5 most overhyped supplements vs top 5 most underrated
- Ends with: "Want to see how your specific health goal scores?"

**Pricing:** Free (email opt-in)

**Urgency:** None. Belief-building only.

**Risk Reversal:** N/A (free)

**Transition to Yellow:** After consuming, prompt: "Now that you know the tiers, want to see how supplements stack up for YOUR health goal? Run a free AI analysis →"

## SEQUENCING AUDIT

| # | Stage | Offer | Job | Leads To |
|---|-------|-------|-----|----------|
| 1 | Red | Evidence Confidence Scorecard | Self-recognition without pressure | Orange A or B |
| 2 | Orange A | Evidence Gap Training (email series) | Reframe: "clinically proven" is meaningless without tiers | Yellow A (AI) |
| 3 | Orange B | Top 20 Supplements Ranked (PDF) | Proof: show the data, build trust with skeptics | Yellow A or B |
| 4 | Yellow A | Free AI Stack Analysis | Quick win: personalized evidence report | Light Green (Pro) |
| 5 | Yellow B | Single PDF Evidence Guide ($9) | Tangible reference document, first paid "yes" | Light Green (Pro) |
| 6 | Light Green | Pro Monthly ($9.99/mo) | Full system: database + guides + AI + interactions | Dark Green (Annual) |
| 7 | Dark Green | Pro Annual ($79.99/yr) | Commitment + savings + future Stack Trial Tracker | Retention + advocacy |

**Sequencing Check:**
- Each offer has ONE job matching its readiness stage
- No premature selling (Red and Orange are 100% free)
- Yellow offers split: one product-led (AI trial), one asset-led (PDF sale — revenue priority)
- The $9 PDF guide at Yellow B is the **first revenue touchpoint** — aligned with priority
- Light Green → Dark Green transition is pure logic (same product, better price)
- Future Stack Trial Tracker app at Dark Green adds long-term retention and differentiation

**One Improvement:** Consider making the Orange B "Top 20 Ranked" PDF a $5-$7 micro-purchase instead of free. This would: (a) create an earlier revenue touchpoint, (b) qualify buyers before the $9 guide, and (c) fund content creation at 1 hour/week constraint. However, for mass appeal at pre-revenue stage, keeping it free maximizes email list growth. **Recommendation: Start free, convert to paid micro-offer once email list exceeds 500.**
