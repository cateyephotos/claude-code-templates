# Lever 3: Your Leads — Complete Lead Architecture

**Positioning:** Mass Appeal (low barrier, high volume)
**Tone:** Calm / authoritative, dry. No superfluous adjectives.
**Constraint:** Pre-revenue, 1 hour/week, email only, SEO-driven
**Revenue Priority:** PDF guide sales ($9 each)

---

## STEP 1 — STRATEGY LOCK

**Primary entry point:** Red — "The 2-Minute Supplement Evidence Check" (scorecard quiz)

**Deployment order:** One at a time. Red first.
- Red scorecard deploys immediately across all 93 monograph pages and evidence pages
- Orange/Yellow PDF deploys after Red is live and capturing emails (week 2-3)
- Green AI demo deploys last — it already exists as a product feature, just needs CTA framing (week 4)

**Goal:** Mass appeal. The Red scorecard is low-friction, curiosity-driven, and works for every audience segment — healthcare professionals checking patients' stacks, fitness enthusiasts validating their protocol, casual consumers wondering if their multivitamin is real.

**Rationale for Red-first:** The scorecard requires no content creation (it references existing database data). It produces the strongest emotional reaction (gap recognition). And it captures the widest audience — including people who would never click "Download our PDF."

## STEP 2 — LEAD MAGNETS

### 2A. RED: "The 2-Minute Supplement Evidence Check"

**Buyer Readiness Stage:** Red (Not interested / Unaware / Resistant)

**Emotional state entering:** Casual confidence. They take supplements. They assume those supplements work. They have never questioned the evidence behind their routine.

**Psychological job:** Create a gap between what they believe and what the data shows — without judgment, without selling, without telling them they're wrong. The scorecard does the talking. The number does the persuading.

#### Promise Options

1. "Find out how many of your supplements are backed by strong clinical evidence — in 2 minutes."
2. "You take supplements daily. Do you know which ones have Tier 1 evidence? Score your stack."
3. "Most supplement users score below 40% on clinical evidence. Where does your stack land?"

**Selected:** Option 1 — broadest appeal, lowest resistance, clear time commitment.

#### What They Get

**Format:** Interactive quiz/scorecard (8-10 questions)

- Input their current supplements (select from database of 93)
- Receive a personalized Evidence Confidence Score (0-100)
- See a breakdown: how many supplements fall into each of the 4 evidence tiers
- One-line verdict per supplement: "Strong evidence," "Moderate evidence," "Limited evidence," "Insufficient evidence"
- No recommendations. No fixes. No upsell during the quiz itself.

**What it helps them see:** The gap between what they spend money on and what clinical trials actually support. Most people discover 50-70% of their stack sits in Tier 3 or 4.

**What it intentionally does NOT do:**
- Does not recommend alternatives
- Does not explain the tier system in depth
- Does not pitch any product or service
- Does not tell them they're wrong — just shows them the score

#### Transition Logic

**Next stage:** Orange (Curious, wants to understand)

**Transition trigger:** After displaying the score, show: "Your stack scored [X]/100. Curious what the evidence tiers actually mean — and which 20 supplements score highest? Get the free ranking →"

**Why this feels natural:** They just saw a number. The number raised a question. The Orange offer answers that question. No leap required.

### 2B. ORANGE/YELLOW: "Top 20 Supplements Ranked by Clinical Trial Strength"

**Buyer Readiness Stage:** Orange (Curious, not sure) to Yellow (Problem-aware, mildly motivated)

**Emotional state entering:** Slightly unsettled. The scorecard showed them something they didn't expect. They want to understand the system — or they want to see proof that this ranking method is credible.

**Psychological job:** Build trust through transparency. Show the ranking methodology in action. Let them verify with real data. The PDF converts curiosity into belief: "This system is rigorous, and it works."

#### Promise Options

1. "20 popular supplements. Ranked by real clinical evidence. No marketing, no affiliates."
2. "See which supplements actually survive peer review — the Top 20, ranked by trial strength."
3. "The evidence report most supplement companies don't want you to read. 20 supplements. 4 tiers. Zero funding."

**Selected:** Option 1 — direct, concrete, and the "no marketing, no affiliates" line addresses the primary objection for this audience.

#### What They Get

**Format:** Downloadable PDF (12-15 pages)

- The 4-tier evidence classification system explained in plain language (1 page)
- 20 supplements ranked from strongest to weakest evidence
- Per supplement: evidence tier, number of qualifying clinical trials, one-line verdict, key citation
- 3 "surprise" rankings that contradict popular belief (e.g., a best-seller that scores Tier 4)
- Source methodology page: PubMed, Cochrane, ClinicalTrials.gov — zero industry funding
- Final page CTA: "Want to see how supplements rank for YOUR specific health goal? Run a free AI Stack Analysis →"

**What it helps them understand:**
- How the 4-tier system works with real examples
- Which popular supplements have strong evidence vs. which are coasting on marketing
- That SupplementDB's methodology is verifiable — every claim links to a DOI

**What it intentionally does NOT do:**
- Does not cover all 93 supplements (that's the database's job)
- Does not provide dosage protocols (that's the $9 guide's job)
- Does not personalize to their health goal (that's the AI Analyzer's job)

#### Transition Logic

**Next stage:** Yellow (Problem-aware) → Green (AI Stack Analysis or $9 PDF guide)

**Transition trigger:** Inside the PDF, final page: "These 20 are just the start. Run a free AI analysis to see which supplements score highest for your specific health goal → Or get the full evidence guide for [Sleep/Cognition/Anxiety] with dosage protocols for $9 →"

**Why this feels natural:** They've seen the ranking. They trust the methodology. Now they want it applied to their situation. The AI demo or domain-specific guide is the obvious next step.

### 2C. GREEN: "Free AI Stack Analysis"

**Buyer Readiness Stage:** Green (Decided the problem is real, looking for the right solution)

**Emotional state entering:** Motivated and specific. They have a health goal. They have supplements. They want to know if their stack is optimized — and they trust this system enough to try it.

**Psychological job:** Deliver immediate, personalized value. The AI analysis IS the product demo. When the output is good enough to act on, the buyer realizes: "If the free version does this, what does Pro give me?" The demo sells the subscription.

#### Promise Options

1. "Get a personalized evidence report for your supplement stack — free, in 5 minutes."
2. "Your health goal. Your supplements. The clinical evidence. One AI-generated report."
3. "See exactly which supplements in your stack have strong evidence for your goal — and which are dead weight."

**Selected:** Option 3 — most emotionally specific. "Dead weight" triggers loss aversion.

#### What They Get

**Format:** Product demo (existing AI Stack Analyzer, 3 free analyses/month)

- Select a health goal from 13 options
- Input current supplements
- Receive: overall stack score, per-supplement evidence strength, mechanism coverage analysis, gap identification, specific recommendations
- Output is detailed enough to act on — but limited to 3/month to create natural scarcity

**What it helps them decide:**
- Whether their current stack is evidence-backed for their specific goal
- Which supplements to keep, which to question, which gaps exist
- Whether the full system (Pro) is worth $9.99/month for ongoing access

**What it intentionally does NOT do:**
- Does not provide full dosage protocols (that's the $9 guide)
- Does not include interaction warnings in the free tier
- Does not allow more than 3 analyses/month (Pro gets 25)

#### Transition Logic

**Next stage:** Yellow B ($9 PDF guide) or Light Green (Pro subscription)

**Transition trigger:** After analysis results, display: "Want the full evidence guide for [detected health goal] with dosage protocols, interaction warnings, and all citations? Get the PDF for $9 →" and "Need unlimited analyses + all 8 health guides? Go Pro for $9.99/month →"

**Why this feels natural:** They just received a personalized report. The report showed them exactly what they're missing. The paid offers fill those gaps. No persuasion needed — the output did the work.

## STEP 7 — VISUAL BRIEF

**Theme:** Dark clinical journal. Think NEJM meets dark mode.

### Mood
- Clean, sparse, clinical
- Dark background (#0a0a0a or #111827) with high-contrast white text
- Evidence-tier green accents (#22c55e for Tier 1, graduated down to amber/red for lower tiers)
- No gradients, no shadows, no rounded-everything modern SaaS aesthetic
- Feels like reading a well-typeset research paper at 11pm

### Style
- Typography-first design. Large, clear headings in a sans-serif (Inter or similar)
- Monospace accents for data points (scores, tier numbers, citation counts)
- Minimal illustration — data IS the visual
- Evidence tier badges as the primary color system (green/yellow/orange/red pills or tags)
- White space used aggressively to signal confidence

### Text Hierarchy (Opt-in Page)
1. **Headline** — Large, bold, white on dark. One line.
2. **Subheadline** — Smaller, gray (#9ca3af), directly below. Clarifies the promise.
3. **Bullet benefits** — Left-aligned, clean, with subtle tier-color accents on key phrases
4. **Bio block** — Slightly recessed, smaller font, gray background card (#1f2937)
5. **CTA button** — Tier 1 green (#22c55e), white text, sharp corners, no animation

### Text Hierarchy (PDF Cover — Top 20 Ranking)
1. **Title** — "Top 20 Supplements Ranked by Clinical Trial Strength"
2. **Subtitle** — "Evidence tiers, citation counts, and one-line verdicts from 471+ peer-reviewed papers"
3. **Badge** — "SupplementDB | Zero Industry Funding"
4. **Visual element** — Minimal bar chart showing 4 evidence tiers, or a simple ranking number list

### Text Hierarchy (Scorecard Results Page)
1. **Score** — Massive number (72pt+), centered, color-coded by tier range
2. **Interpretation** — One sentence below the score
3. **Breakdown** — 4-tier bar or donut showing supplement distribution
4. **Next step CTAs** — Two options, clearly differentiated

### Usage
- **Opt-in page:** Dark background, green CTA, typography-driven
- **Social/Reddit:** Simple card — dark background, headline in white, green accent bar, SupplementDB logo. No stock photos.
- **PDF cover:** Same dark theme, clean type hierarchy, evidence tier color bar across bottom
- **Email:** Plain text preferred. If HTML, dark theme header only, rest is readable text.

## FINAL QUALITY CHECK

### Each lead magnet matches buyer readiness: PASS

| Lead Magnet | Readiness Stage | Match |
|-------------|----------------|-------|
| Evidence Scorecard (Red) | Unaware / Not interested | Correct — creates gap recognition without selling |
| Top 20 Ranking PDF (Orange/Yellow) | Curious / Wants to understand | Correct — builds belief through transparent data |
| Free AI Stack Analysis (Green) | Decided, looking for solution | Correct — personalized demo delivers immediate value |

### Each step creates momentum, not friction: PASS

- Red → Orange: "You saw a score. Now understand what it means." (Curiosity → Education)
- Orange → Yellow/Green: "You understand the system. Now see it applied to YOUR goal." (Education → Personalization)
- Green → Paid: "The free analysis showed you the gaps. The guide fills them." (Personalization → Purchase)
- No step asks the user to believe something before they've experienced it
- No step sells before the previous step has done its job

### The system moves people forward intentionally: PASS

- The scorecard is the widest funnel (works for all segments, no email required to start)
- The Top 20 PDF is the belief builder (email capture happens here)
- The AI analysis is the product demo (converts belief into "I need this")
- The $9 guide is the first purchase (low risk, high perceived value vs. consultation cost)
- Every transition is a logical next question, not a marketing pitch

### Flags and Fixes

**Flag 1:** The Red scorecard requires no email to start, but email capture should happen on the results page (before showing full tier breakdown). This means the scorecard has two conversion points: (a) the quiz itself (engagement), and (b) the results gate (email capture). If ungated results reduce email capture, consider gating the per-supplement breakdown while keeping the overall score ungated.

**Flag 2:** The thank-you page offers three next steps. For a 1-hour/week constraint, simplify: make Option 1 (Top 20 PDF) the primary/default CTA, and show Options 2 and 3 below the fold. Don't split attention until the system is generating consistent email signups.

**Flag 3:** Reddit and Quora (Tier 4 placements) are high-effort relative to returns at pre-revenue stage. Deprioritize until the on-site funnel is converting. Focus the 1 hour/week on: (a) deploying scorecard CTAs on monograph pages, (b) setting up the email capture flow, (c) writing the Top 20 PDF. Reddit/Quora can wait until month 2-3.

**Flag 4:** The Top 20 PDF is listed as free in this lead system but was flagged in Lever 1 as a potential $5-$7 micro-purchase once the email list exceeds 500. Ensure the opt-in page copy and infrastructure support a future price gate. Build the PDF with a quality level that justifies paid pricing from day one — don't create a "free-looking" asset.
