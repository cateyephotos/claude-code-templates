## STEP 2: CUSTOM TESTIMONIAL ASK MESSAGES

### Ask Script #1: After AI Analysis Win

**Trigger:** User runs AI Stack Analyzer, engages with results for 2+ minutes, or returns to run a second analysis within 7 days.

**Channel:** Email (automated, sent 48 hours after trigger)

**Subject line:** Quick question about your analysis

**Body:**

> Hey [first name],
>
> You ran a stack analysis for [goal] a couple days ago. I'm curious — did anything in the results surprise you?
>
> I'm building SupplementDB as a solo founder, and the most useful thing I can learn right now is whether the analysis actually changed how you think about your stack.
>
> If it did — even a small shift — I'd love to hear about it. A few sentences is plenty. If it didn't, that's useful too.
>
> Either way, no pitch here. Just trying to build something that's actually useful.
>
> — [Your name]

**Why this works:** Low pressure, no ask for "testimonial" (loaded word), frames feedback as helping the product. The question "did anything surprise you" is specific enough to generate usable content.

---

### Ask Script #2: After Guide Purchase + 7 Days

**Trigger:** User purchases a PDF Evidence Guide and 7 days have passed.

**Channel:** Email (manual or automated)

**Subject line:** Did the [supplement] guide change anything?

**Body:**

> Hey [first name],
>
> You picked up the [Supplement Name] Evidence Guide about a week ago. I'm wondering — did you end up adjusting your dosage, timing, or form based on what was in there?
>
> I track clinical evidence across 471+ papers for this database, and the thing I care most about is whether that evidence actually reaches people in a way that's useful. Not impressive. Useful.
>
> If the guide changed even one thing about how you take [supplement], I'd really like to hear what. A sentence or two is perfect. I may ask if I can share it (anonymously or with your name, your call) to help others who are on the fence.
>
> Thanks for being an early supporter.
>
> — [Your name]

**Why this works:** Anchors to a specific behavioral change ("did you adjust"), which produces testimonials with concrete details rather than vague praise.

### Ask Script #3: After Spontaneous Positive Feedback

**Trigger:** User sends unsolicited positive comment via email, support, or social.

**Channel:** Reply to their message within 24 hours.

**Body:**

> That genuinely made my day — thank you.
>
> Would you be open to me using what you said (or something close to it) on the site? I can keep it anonymous or use your first name — totally your call. I can also draft something based on your message and send it for your approval so you don't have to write anything new.
>
> Either way, I appreciate you taking the time to say that.

**Why this works:** Captures the feedback at peak enthusiasm. Offering to draft it removes friction entirely. Giving them control over attribution reduces hesitation.

## STEP 3: TANTALIZING TESTIMONIAL EXTRACTION TEMPLATE

### Structured Question Set (Adapted for SupplementDB)

Use these when a source agrees to give a testimonial. Send as a simple numbered list via email. Preface with: "Answer whichever ones feel relevant. Skip anything that doesn't apply. Even 2-3 answers gives me what I need."

**1. BEFORE (Pain/Confusion/Frustration)**
- Before you found SupplementDB, how were you deciding which supplements to take and at what dose?
- What was the most frustrating part of trying to research supplements on your own?

**2. DECISION HESITATION**
- When you first saw SupplementDB, was there anything that made you hesitant to use it (or pay for it)?
- What almost stopped you from [running the analysis / buying the guide / signing up for Pro]?

**3. DURING (What Felt Different)**
- What was the first thing you noticed that felt different from other supplement resources you've tried?
- Was there a specific moment where you thought "this is actually useful"?

**4. AFTER (Results/Relief/Confidence)**
- Has anything changed about your supplement routine since using SupplementDB?
- Do you feel more or less confident about your current stack now? Why?

**5. DIFFERENTIATION (Why This Over Alternatives)**
- Have you used other supplement research tools (Examine, WebMD, Reddit, etc.)? How does this compare?
- What does SupplementDB do that you haven't found anywhere else?

**6. RECOMMENDATION**
- If a friend asked you whether SupplementDB was worth using, what would you tell them?
- Who do you think would get the most out of this?

## STEP 6: DEPLOYMENT MAP

### Where Each Testimonial Type Goes

| Placement | Testimonial Type | Objection Neutralized | Buyer Stage |
|-----------|-----------------|----------------------|-------------|
| **Database homepage, above fold** | Micro-testimonial (evidence tiers quote) | "Is this legit?" | Awareness |
| **AI Analyzer results page** | Skeptic-to-Believer (#1) | "I can Google this" | Consideration |
| **Guide sales page, top** | Value Validator (#2) | "Not worth $9" | Decision |
| **Guide sales page, mid** | Beginner Win (#3) | "I don't know where to start" | Decision |
| **Pricing page** | Upgrade Justifier (#4) | "Why pay?" | Decision |
| **Pricing page** | Relatable Peer (#4 alt) | "This isn't for people like me" | Decision |
| **Post-purchase thank-you page** | Case Study snippet | "Did I make the right call?" | Post-purchase reinforcement |
| **Welcome email (Pro)** | Upgrade Justifier (#4) | Buyer's remorse prevention | Post-purchase reinforcement |
| **Guide cross-sell emails** | Screenshot-style (@sarah_wellness) | "Another guide? Really?" | Cross-sell |
| **Checkout page (inline)** | Micro-testimonial | Last-second hesitation | Conversion |

### Page Placement Architecture

**Top-of-page proof:** Micro-testimonial or screenshot-style — establishes that real people use this and find it useful. Low commitment, high scannability.

**Mid-page objection proof:** Skeptic-to-Believer or Value Validator — appears directly after the section that triggers the objection it neutralizes. Example: place Value Validator directly below guide pricing.

**Bottom-of-page decision proof:** Relatable Peer or Beginner Win — catches the reader who scrolled the whole page and is still undecided. This is the "people like me use this" moment.

**Post-purchase reinforcement proof:** Case Study or Upgrade Justifier — appears in thank-you page or welcome email. Reduces refund risk. Reinforces the decision. Opens the door to the next offer.

## STEP 7: TESTIMONIAL ENGINE MAINTENANCE

### Tagging System

Every testimonial stored in Notion or Convex gets tagged with:

1. **Objection tag:** `skeptic` | `price` | `beginner` | `alternatives` | `professional-use` | `time`
2. **Buyer type tag:** `beginner` | `optimizer` | `practitioner` | `researcher` | `casual`
3. **Outcome tag:** `saved-money` | `changed-stack` | `gained-confidence` | `saved-time` | `found-gap`
4. **Offer tag:** `free-db` | `ai-analyzer` | `guide` | `pro-monthly` | `pro-annual`
5. **Format tag:** `written` | `screenshot` | `case-study` | `micro` | `video`
6. **Status tag:** `draft` | `approved` | `deployed` | `retired`

### Rotation Strategy

- Rotate testimonials on sales pages every 60 days
- Never show the same testimonial on two adjacent pages in the same user flow
- Retire any testimonial older than 6 months unless it references a still-current feature
- A/B test pairs: swap one testimonial at a time and measure click-through on the CTA below it

### Collection Cadence

| Milestone | Action |
|-----------|--------|
| **5 active users** | Send Ask Script #1 to anyone who ran 2+ analyses. Goal: 1 testimonial. |
| **10 active users** | Send Ask Script #1 and #3 (if positive feedback received). Goal: 2-3 testimonials. Deploy first proof on homepage. |
| **25 active users** | Send structured question template to best 3 sources. Goal: 2 full testimonials + 1 case study. Deploy on guide sales page. |
| **First 5 guide sales** | Send Ask Script #2 at day 7. Goal: 1 value-validator testimonial. Deploy on guide page and checkout. |
| **50 active users** | Full testimonial audit. Ensure coverage across all 5 objection tags. Fill gaps with targeted asks. |
| **10 Pro subscribers** | Send Ask Script #1 adapted for Pro features. Goal: 2 upgrade-justifier testimonials. Deploy on pricing page. |
| **100 active users** | Formalize quarterly collection cycle. Rotate deployed testimonials. Begin A/B testing placement. |

### Freshness Checklist (Quarterly)

- [ ] Are all deployed testimonials still accurate to current product features?
- [ ] Do we have at least 1 testimonial per major objection?
- [ ] Do we have at least 1 testimonial per buyer type?
- [ ] Has any testimonial been live for 6+ months without rotation?
- [ ] Are there any new objections emerging from support emails that need proof coverage?
- [ ] Is the most recent testimonial less than 90 days old?

## INCENTIVE STRATEGY

### Primary Incentive: Emotional Recognition (No Discounts)

**"Evidence Pioneer" Recognition:**

Users who provide testimonials receive:
- A badge on their profile (if/when user profiles exist): "Evidence Pioneer — Early Supporter"
- First name listed on a "Built With Early Supporters" section of the About page
- Early access to new features (24-hour head start on new guides or analyzer updates)

This costs nothing, scales infinitely, and creates a sense of ownership that discounts never produce.

### Friction Reduction (The Real Incentive)

The actual conversion driver for testimonial collection is not the reward — it is the effort reduction:

1. **You draft, they approve.** After receiving any positive feedback, draft a 2-3 sentence testimonial based on their words. Send it with: "I drafted something based on what you said — feel free to edit, approve, or scrap it. No pressure either way."
2. **Numbered question list, not a form.** Send the structured questions as a plain-text numbered list in email. Not a Google Form. Not a Typeform. Just reply to the email.
3. **Explicit permission framing.** Always specify: anonymous or named, their choice. Always ask before publishing.
