### Key Evidence Quality Observations

1. **Mechanism evidence is disproportionately strong relative to clinical evidence:** The serotonin precursor mechanism and BBB penetration are biochemically well-established (Turner 2006, Bertoldi 2019 — both Moderate GRADE). The clinical evidence, however, is dominated by older small trials and narrative reviews (Low to Very Low GRADE).

2. **No study post-2012 with clinical benefit data:** The most recent clinical benefit paper is Hinz 2012 (a narrative review). All primary RCTs are from 1971–2004. The evidence base has not received new primary RCT data in over 20 years for the primary indications.

3. **The Cochrane finding (Shaw 2002, PMID 11869656) is critical but absent from the 16-citation file:** The Cochrane review on tryptophan/5-HTP for depression concluded "insufficient evidence to determine whether 5-HTP is more effective than placebo" — this finding drives the Tier 2 rather than Tier 1 assignment and is documented throughout this provenance record.

4. **Depression evidence is most studied but most contested:** Multiple older RCTs and reviews exist, but the Cochrane review's "insufficient evidence" conclusion means the depression indication has the paradox of being best-studied but least conclusively supported.

5. **Safety evidence is proportionally stronger than clinical benefit evidence:** The serotonin syndrome and EMS contamination safety signals are well-documented (Moderate GRADE). This creates an unusual profile: well-established mechanism + well-characterized risks + moderate clinical benefit evidence.
