# Screening Decisions: 5-HTP (ID: 34)
## Pipeline Run: 2026-03-06

### Inclusion Criteria
1. Human subjects (clinical trials, meta-analyses, systematic reviews, pharmacokinetic studies)
2. Published in peer-reviewed journal with confirmed DOI and PMID
3. Relevant to 5-HTP's claimed benefits (depression, sleep, migraine, fibromyalgia), mechanisms (serotonin precursor, BBB), or safety (serotonin syndrome, EMS contamination history)
4. Provides quantitative outcome data OR mechanistic evidence OR safety signal documentation
5. Study type: RCT, meta-analysis, systematic review, comprehensive clinical review, mechanistic review, safety assessment, pharmacokinetic study
6. Exception: Up to 2 foundational mechanistic reviews retained for mechanism section; mathematical models included if establishing rate-limiting enzyme context
7. **Critical inclusion requirement:** Safety signals (serotonin syndrome, EMS contamination history) are mandatory inclusions per Mode 2 protocol

### Exclusion Criteria
1. Retracted papers
2. Animal or preclinical studies
3. Non-supplementation populations (e.g., clinical populations with IV 5-HTP for metabolic disorders)
4. Non-English without English abstract
5. Conference abstracts only (no full publication)
6. Protocol/design papers without outcome data
7. Papers with unconfirmed or ambiguous PMID/DOI
8. Duplicate publications of the same dataset
9. Multi-component interventions where 5-HTP contribution cannot be isolated (except when it is the primary compound studied and comparison is with standard therapy)

### Key Screening Decisions and Notes

**Foundational 1971 Wyatt study (PMID 5556734) — inclusion rationale:**
The Wyatt 1971 study is the first controlled human investigation of 5-HTP's sleep and mood effects. Despite its age, it remains the historical anchor for sleep indications and is cited in all subsequent systematic reviews. Retained as historical clinical anchor.

**Mathematical model Best 2010 (PMID 20815946) — inclusion rationale:**
This is technically a computational/mathematical model rather than a clinical study. It is included in the mechanisms section because it provides quantitative validation of the rate-limiting enzyme bypass claim — the most fundamental mechanistic assertion about 5-HTP. No equivalent experimental human quantification exists.

**Angst 1977 "systematic review" (PMID 202213) — note on classification:**
The Angst 1977 paper is listed as a "systematic review" in the enhanced citations file, but by modern standards it is a narrative review of early clinical studies. It is included as the earliest synthesis of 5-HTP depression evidence and classified accordingly in the GRADE evaluation.

**No conflict of interest (COI) flagging required:**
5-HTP research is predominantly funded by academic and pharmaceutical institutions. Unlike chondroitin sulfate (Rottapharm issue) or some nutraceuticals, there is no single manufacturer dominating the research funding landscape. Most included studies are from academic European and US institutions with no supplement industry funding disclosed.

**Safety papers as mandatory inclusions:**
The EMS contamination history (Slutsker 1990) and serotonin syndrome risk (Ener 2003) are mandatory inclusions per Mode 2 protocol — counter-evidence and safety signals must be represented. These papers document real risks that distinguish 5-HTP from many other amino acid supplements.
