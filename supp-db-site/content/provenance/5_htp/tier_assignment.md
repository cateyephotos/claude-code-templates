### Tier 2 Rationale (Full)

5-HTP receives Tier 2 based on the combination of:

1. **Mechanistic certainty (Moderate GRADE):** The serotonin precursor mechanism, BBB penetration, and rate-limiting enzyme bypass are biochemically well-established and form a uniquely strong foundation relative to the clinical evidence. This distinguishes 5-HTP from many Tier 2 supplements where even the mechanism is contested.

2. **Multi-indication clinical breadth:** Unlike single-indication supplements with one or two trials, 5-HTP has primary clinical data across four separate indications (depression, sleep, migraine, fibromyalgia) from independent research groups across four decades. The consistent directional signal across independent investigations is meaningful.

3. **Cochrane "insufficient evidence" vs. "no evidence":** Shaw 2002 concluded insufficient evidence — not that evidence is negative or that effect is absent. The distinction matters: insufficient evidence reflects underpowered small trials, not null or contrary findings. The clinical signal exists but has not been properly tested at modern standards.

4. **Safety profile well-characterized:** The primary safety constraint (serotonin syndrome with concurrent serotonergic medications) is well-documented and actionable. Well-characterized risk is a positive indicator for Tier 2 assignment relative to supplements with unknown or poorly documented safety profiles.

5. **Disqualifiers for Tier 1:** No large modern RCT; Cochrane inconclusive; no regulatory approval for any indication; no approved drug equivalent status.

6. **Disqualifiers for Tier 3:** 50+ years of research; multiple independent groups; established mechanism; multiple systematic reviews; characterized safety profile.

### Mandatory Safety Disclosure (Mode 2 Protocol)

Per Mode 2 mandatory safety signal documentation requirements:

> **⚠ Serotonin Syndrome Risk:** 5-HTP should not be combined with SSRIs, SNRIs, MAO inhibitors, tramadol, or other serotonergic medications without medical supervision. Concurrent use creates risk of serotonin syndrome — a potentially serious and life-threatening condition. This is the primary clinical safety constraint for 5-HTP supplementation.

> **Manufacturing Quality Note:** Sourcing from pharmaceutical-grade, third-party-tested manufacturers is recommended. The 1989–1990 EMS contamination outbreak in tryptophan supplementation (Slutsker 1990) established manufacturing quality as a non-trivial concern for indole-precursor supplements.
