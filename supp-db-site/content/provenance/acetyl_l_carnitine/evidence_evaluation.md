5. **No ALC monotherapy data** — Cannot determine if ALC alone would have meaningful ED effects

## Evaluation Conclusion

Both Barbonetti 2024 and Niu 2025 provide Level 1 evidence (SR+NMA) with moderate confidence. They expand the ALCAR evidence base into two new clinical domains — erectile dysfunction and male fertility — providing the first systematic comparative efficacy data for ALC-containing interventions in these areas. However, both papers study ALCAR as part of combination therapies rather than in isolation, and the primary carnitine form analyzed is not always ALCAR specifically. Quality scores of 7/10 for both papers reflect strong NMA methodology tempered by moderate directness (ALC in combination, not isolated; population-specific findings).

The addition of these papers does not change the fundamental evidence profile of ALCAR: it remains a supplement with broad domain coverage but insufficient depth in any single domain for Tier 1 classification. The key gap — an ALCAR-specific meta-analysis with pooled cognitive effect estimates — remains unfilled.
