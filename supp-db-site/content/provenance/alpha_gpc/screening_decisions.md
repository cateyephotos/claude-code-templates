## Inclusion Criteria

Papers were included if they met ALL of the following:

1. **Population:** Humans (not animal, in vitro, or in silico)
2. **Intervention:** Alpha-GPC (choline alfoscerate / L-alpha-glycerylphosphorylcholine) as a standalone supplement or clearly isolated intervention arm. Not as a minor component of a multi-ingredient nootropic stack or combined pharmaceutical.
3. **Outcome:** Cognitive function, neuroprotection, acetylcholine-related endpoints, or other endpoints directly relevant to the supplement's primary benefit claims
4. **Design:** Systematic review, meta-analysis, network meta-analysis, umbrella review, or large RCT (n >= 100)
5. **Recency:** Published 2024 or later
6. **Novelty:** Not already cited in the existing enhanced citations file

## Included Papers (n = 2)

### 1. Sagaro & Amenta 2025 — Alpha-GPC vs Citicoline SR+MA

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **PMID** | 41426989 |
| **Title** | Systematic review and meta-analysis comparing choline alfoscerate and citicoline for cognitive decline |
| **Authors** | Sagaro GG, Amenta F |
| **Journal** | Frontiers in Neurology |
| **Year** | 2025 |
| **Design** | Systematic review with meta-analysis |
| **Scale** | 3 RCTs, n = 358 participants |
| **Registration** | PROSPERO CRD42024626782 |
| **Key Finding** | Alpha-GPC vs citicoline: SCAG (Sandoz Clinical Assessment-Geriatric) weighted mean difference (WMD) = -3.92, favoring Alpha-GPC over citicoline. Alpha-GPC showed superior cognitive outcomes in the comparative analysis. |
| **Decision** | **INCLUDED** |
| **Rationale** | First meta-analysis to include Alpha-GPC as a primary intervention. PROSPERO registered, demonstrating methodological rigor and pre-specified protocol. While the evidence base is small (3 RCTs, 358 participants), this represents a critical milestone for Alpha-GPC evidence — the transition from individual trial evidence to meta-analytic synthesis. The comparative design (Alpha-GPC vs citicoline) is particularly informative for the supplement database as both compounds compete in the choline donor market. The SCAG scale, while older, is a validated geriatric cognitive assessment instrument. |

### 2. Jeon et al. 2024 — Multicenter RCT in MCI

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **PMID** | 39300341 |
| **Title** | Efficacy and safety of choline alphoscerate in patients with mild cognitive impairment: A multicenter, randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled trial |
| **Authors** | Jeon S, et al. |
| **Journal** | BMC Geriatrics |
| **Year** | 2024 |
| **Design** | Multicenter, randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled trial |
| **Scale** | n = 100 (MCI patients), 12-week duration, 600 mg/day Alpha-GPC |
| **Key Finding** | ADAS-cog change from baseline: -2.34 points in Alpha-GPC group vs placebo (significant improvement). Safety profile favorable with no serious adverse events attributable to Alpha-GPC. |
| **Decision** | **INCLUDED** |
| **Rationale** | Highest-quality single RCT for Alpha-GPC to date. Meets multiple gold-standard design criteria: multicenter (reducing site-specific bias), randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled. The n = 100 sample size meets the inclusion threshold for large RCTs. The MCI population is clinically relevant as it represents the primary therapeutic target for cholinergic interventions. ADAS-cog is the gold-standard cognitive endpoint used in Alzheimer's disease drug trials (donepezil, rivastigmine), enabling direct comparison with pharmaceutical benchmarks. The 12-week duration provides adequate treatment exposure for cognitive outcomes. Good safety data (no serious AEs) strengthens the overall evidence profile. Published in BMC Geriatrics, a reputable peer-reviewed geriatrics journal. |

## Notes

- The 20.0% inclusion rate is higher than the Panax Ginseng update (14.3%), reflecting the smaller and more focused Alpha-GPC literature compared to the taxonomically complex Panax genus. With fewer total search results, the signal-to-noise ratio is higher because there are fewer species confusion, multi-herb formula, and wrong-route papers to exclude.
- The most significant finding from screening is that only ONE meta-analysis exists for Alpha-GPC (Sagaro 2025), and it contains only 3 RCTs. This is a critical evidence gap — the transition from individual trial evidence to meta-analytic synthesis is still in its very early stages for Alpha-GPC.
- The Jeon 2024 RCT was only captured through targeted retrieval (Search 5) because it uses the alternate spelling "alphoscerate" rather than "alfoscerate." This highlights the importance of nomenclature-aware search strategies for Alpha-GPC.
- The 3 narrative reviews excluded (Li 2025, Granata 2025, Che 2025) suggest increasing academic interest in Alpha-GPC, which may forecast more systematic reviews and meta-analyses in future update cycles.
- The comparative design of Sagaro 2025 (Alpha-GPC vs citicoline) is particularly valuable because it directly addresses the consumer decision between the two primary choline donor supplements, a common question in the nootropic community.
