4. **Multi-domain convergence:** Evidence now spans 7+ distinct outcome domains (anxiety, stress, depression, sleep, cortisol, testosterone, serotonin) with dedicated meta-analytic support for each. Very few dietary supplements achieve this breadth of meta-analytic coverage

### Research Maturity: Mature
**Justification:** Ashwagandha (Withania somnifera) has a substantial evidence base spanning multiple decades of traditional use and over 15 years of modern clinical research. The 2024-2026 evidence cycle demonstrates research maturity through three markers: (1) dose-response meta-analyses are being conducted, indicating the field has moved past the basic "does it work?" question to the more refined "how much is needed?" question; (2) hormonal endpoint meta-analyses bridge mechanism and clinical outcome; and (3) clinical population studies (mental disorder patients) extend findings beyond healthy volunteers. The availability of 7+ meta-analyses across distinct outcome domains, combined with dose-response data and mechanistic quantification, indicates a mature research program. Research maturity is rated as "Mature" reflecting the depth, breadth, and sophistication of the current evidence base.

### Evidence Gaps Identified
1. Long-term safety data (>12 months continuous use) remains limited -- most RCTs are 4-12 weeks in duration
2. Standardization of ashwagandha extracts varies across studies (KSM-66, Sensoril, generic root powder) -- meta-analyses pool different extract types which may introduce heterogeneity
3. The extremely large SMD values in Alsanie 2026 (5.68-6.87) warrant methodological investigation -- these values are atypical for supplement meta-analyses and may reflect scale-related inflation
4. Female-specific hormonal effects (e.g., DHEA-S, estrogen, progesterone) are underrepresented -- the testosterone data is male-specific
5. Interaction studies with psychiatric medications (SSRIs, benzodiazepines, sleep aids) are sparse -- important given the clinical population evidence from Marchi 2025
6. Pediatric and adolescent evidence is absent -- all meta-analyses include adults only
7. Thyroid function effects require dedicated meta-analysis -- individual case reports suggest possible thyroid stimulation, but systematic data is lacking
