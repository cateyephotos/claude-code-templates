## Score Calibration Notes

- **Score 55** positions astaxanthin at the upper-middle range of Tier 3 (typical range: 45–65). This reflects:
  - Genuine mechanistic understanding established (Park 2010 RCT mechanism data, Pashkow 2008 mitochondrial mechanism, Nakagawa 2011 membrane data)
  - Consistent directional signal across multiple benefit domains (antioxidant, exercise, skin, cardiovascular) from small but well-designed RCTs
  - Adequate safety profile from two safety RCTs
  - Sound formulation PK guidance
- **Primary evidence gap**: No RCT with N≥100; no Cochrane review; no single landmark trial; this is the defining characteristic separating Tier 3 from Tier 2
- **Primary strength**: Mechanistic understanding is unusually well-developed for a Tier 3 supplement — astaxanthin's transmembrane orientation mechanism is clearly characterized; this is why mechanisticBasis receives "Established" label even at Tier 3
- **Comparison to pipeline peers (IDs 41–46)**:
  - Inositol (ID 41, score 68): Meta-analyses for PCOS — Tier 2 appropriate
  - Selenium (ID 42, score 67): SELECT trial + meta-analyses — Tier 2 appropriate
  - Choline (ID 43, score 66): Single RCT — Tier 2 appropriate
  - Alpha-Lipoic Acid (ID 44, score 68): ALADIN/SYDNEY + meta-analyses — Tier 2 appropriate
  - Lutein (ID 45, score 70): AREDS2 (N=4,203) — Tier 2 upper end
  - **Astaxanthin (ID 46, score 55)**: Multiple small RCTs (max N=49), no landmark trial — Tier 3 appropriate
- Score 55 is justified by the consistent multi-domain evidence pattern: antioxidant (Park 2010), exercise (Earnest 2011), skin (Tominaga 2012), cardiovascular (Fassett 2011 review), cognitive fatigue (Satoh 2009) — breadth compensates for depth within Tier 3
