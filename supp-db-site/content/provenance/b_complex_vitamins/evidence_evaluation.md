#### Study: long_2013_stress — "Effects of Vitamin and Mineral Supplementation on Stress"
- **Study type:** Meta-analysis (8 RCTs)
- **PMID:** 23362497 | **DOI:** 10.1097/PSY.0b013e31827d5fbd
- **GRADE domains:**
  - Risk of bias: Some concerns — heterogeneity across included RCTs; different product formulations
  - Inconsistency: Not serious — I² reported and acceptable for meta-analysis
  - Indirectness: Not serious — healthy/nonclinical adults studied
  - Imprecision: Not serious — pooled effects with CIs reported
  - Publication bias: Suspected — small study effects possible; Egger test not reported
- **Overall GRADE certainty:** Moderate
- **Assigned evidence level:** Level 1
- **Actual findings (verified):** Stress SMD = 0.35 (p = 0.001); Anxiety SMD = 0.32 (p < 0.001); Depression NOT significant (p = 0.089)
- **Previous entry error:** Citation was under wrong PMID (23362501 = Shankar 2013 "Social isolation") and claimed cognitive/energy benefits not reported in this meta-analysis
- **Quality score contribution:** 7/10 — meta-analysis is strong design but heterogeneous; only 8 RCTs

#### Study: wang_2022_cognitive — "Folic acid and vitamin B12 treatment for MCI and dementia"
- **Study type:** Meta-analysis (84 studies)
- **PMID:** 35273283 | **DOI:** 10.1017/S1041610221002362
- **GRADE domains:**
  - Risk of bias: Some concerns — variable quality across 84 included studies; heterogeneity in populations
  - Inconsistency: Some concerns — I² not specified, large number of studies with variable designs
  - Indirectness: Not serious — targets MCI/elderly population
  - Imprecision: Not serious — massive sample (58,676 participants)
  - Publication bias: Likely — large positive meta-analysis in this literature
- **Overall GRADE certainty:** Moderate
- **Assigned evidence level:** Level 1
- **Quality score contribution:** 8/10 — largest meta-analysis available; massive sample despite heterogeneity concerns

#### Study: clarke_2010_homocysteine — "Homocysteine-Lowering Trials for Prevention of Cardiovascular Events"
- **Study type:** Meta-analysis (8 RCTs, n=37,485)
- **PMID:** 20937919 | **DOI:** 10.1016/S0140-6736(10)60505-2
- **GRADE domains:**
  - Risk of bias: Low — large, well-powered trials; Lancet quality threshold
  - Inconsistency: Not serious — consistent null finding across 8 large trials
  - Indirectness: Not serious — high cardiovascular risk populations
  - Imprecision: Not serious — very large sample, tight CIs
  - Publication bias: Unlikely — null results publication in Lancet
- **Overall GRADE certainty:** High
- **Assigned evidence level:** Level 1
- **Actual findings (verified):** NO significant reduction in major vascular events (RR=1.01, 0.97–1.05); NO significant stroke reduction (RR=0.96, 0.87–1.06); NO significant coronary events (RR=1.03, 0.97–1.10)
- **Previous entry error:** File fabricated stroke RR=0.88 (95% CI: 0.82–0.94) — not what the paper reports
- **Quality score contribution:** 9/10 — exemplary large meta-analysis with null results accurately representing the evidence

#### Study: markun_2021_b12_systematic — "Supplementation with Vitamin B12 Does Not Affect Cognitive Function"
- **Study type:** Systematic review (16 RCTs)
- **PMID:** 33809274 | **DOI:** 10.3389/fnut.2021.618766
- **GRADE domains:**
  - Risk of bias: Low to Moderate — PRISMA-compliant systematic review; variable quality across included RCTs
  - Inconsistency: Not serious — consistent null finding
  - Indirectness: Not serious — directly examines cognitive outcomes
  - Imprecision: Not serious — 6,276 participants total; adequate power
  - Publication bias: Minimal — null result publication
- **Overall GRADE certainty:** Moderate
- **Assigned evidence level:** Level 1
- **Actual findings (verified):** "No evidence for an effect of B12 alone or B complex supplementation on any subdomain of cognitive function"; "No overall effect on depression"
- **Previous entry error:** File claimed 43 studies/4,062 participants (actual: 16 RCTs/6,276 participants) and fabricated cognitive SMD=0.20 and depression SMD=−0.15
- **Quality score contribution:** 7/10 — solid systematic review; null results important for accurate evidence portrait

#### Study: young_2019_psychological — "Investigating the Effects of a Multinutrient Supplement on Cognition"
- **Study type:** RCT (crossover, double-blind)
- **PMID:** 31527485 | **DOI:** 10.1017/S1368980019002258
- **GRADE domains:**
  - Risk of bias: Low — randomized crossover with washout period; pre-registered
  - Inconsistency: Partially serious — crossover may introduce carryover effects
  - Indirectness: Not serious — healthy adults
  - Imprecision: Some concerns — small-medium sample
  - Publication bias: Undetected
- **Overall GRADE certainty:** Moderate
- **Assigned evidence level:** Level 2
- **Actual findings (verified):** Stress SMD=0.23 (p=0.03, significant); Anxiety SMD=0.03 (p=0.71, NOT significant); Depression SMD=0.15 (p=0.07, NOT significant)
- **Previous entry error:** File claimed anxiety and depression were both significant (p<0.05) when only stress reached significance
- **Quality score contribution:** 6/10 — crossover design is strength; small sample is limitation
