### Research Maturity: Mature
**Justification:** B vitamins have been studied since the 1920s (discovery era). The clinical supplementation evidence dates to the 1980s. There are now 100+ clinical trials across all B vitamin types. The null results in cognitive and cardiovascular domains reflect a mature field that has been adequately powered to detect effects, and the lack of significant effects in those domains should be considered "settled" rather than "emerging." The positive stress effect is the most recent consistent positive finding.

---

### Evidence Gaps Identified
1. **Dose-response for stress outcomes:** Long 2013 pooled diverse formulations; optimal B vitamin ratio and dose for stress reduction is unknown
2. **Cognitive benefit in non-deficient populations:** Most positive cognitive studies are in deficient or elderly populations; healthy adult data is sparse
3. **Long-term cardiovascular outcomes:** Clarke 2010 showed null acute cardiovascular effects; long-term stroke prevention data (>5 years) is limited
4. **Individual B vitamin attribution:** B-complex studies rarely isolate which B vitamin drives observed effects
5. **Active vs. inactive forms:** Most studies do not distinguish methylated (5-MTHF, methylcobalamin) from synthetic (folic acid, cyanocobalamin) forms
