# Screening Decisions: Bacopa monnieri (ID 1) - Mode 2 Evidence Update

## Screening Metadata
- **Date**: 2026-03-05
- **Total Papers Screened**: 7
- **Included**: 3
- **Excluded**: 4

## INCLUDED Papers

### 1. Tiemtad et al. 2026
- **PMID**: 41678913
- **Title**: "Bacopa monnieri versus Ginkgo biloba for enhancing cognitive function: a network meta-analysis of randomized controlled trials"
- **Journal**: Phytomedicine, 153, 157915
- **DOI**: 10.1016/j.phymed.2026.157915
- **Study Type**: Systematic review + Network meta-analysis
- **Scale**: 29 RCTs, n=2107
- **Inclusion Rationale**: Largest Bacopa-specific meta-analysis to date with dose-response data.

### 2. Feng et al. 2026
- **PMID**: 41640686
- **Title**: "Effects of plant substances on cognitive function in the elderly: a network meta-analysis"
- **Journal**: Frontiers in Pharmacology, 16, 1672171
- **DOI**: 10.3389/fphar.2025.1672171
- **Study Type**: Network meta-analysis
- **Scale**: 25 studies, n=1861, 10 plant substances
- **Inclusion Rationale**: Provides SUCRA ranking comparison across multiple nootropics in elderly.

### 3. Lopresti & Smith 2025
- **PMID**: 41091332
- **Title**: "The Effects of a Bacopa monnieri Extract (Bacumen) on Cognition, Stress, and Fatigue in Healthy Adults"
- **Journal**: Clinical Drug Investigation, 45(12), 967-982
- **DOI**: 10.1007/s40261-025-01492-1
- **Study Type**: RCT (double-blind, placebo-controlled)
- **Scale**: n=101, 12 weeks, 300mg/day
- **Inclusion Rationale**: INCLUDED despite NEGATIVE primary cognitive outcomes (verbal learning p=0.391, attention p=0.713, working memory p=0.610) -- adds nuance. POSITIVE secondary outcomes: stress reactivity (p=0.03), anti-fatigue effects.

### 1. Verma et al. 2025
