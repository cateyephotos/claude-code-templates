## Key Themes

### 1. Dose-Response is the Big Finding
Tiemtad 2026 NMA reveals >=600mg/day significantly superior for working memory (SMD=2.03, SUCRA 100%). Traditional 300mg recommendation may need revisiting.

### 2. Lopresti 2025 Negative Primary is Consistent, Not Contradictory
The negative primary cognitive outcomes at 300mg/day are CONSISTENT with the dose-response finding from Tiemtad 2026. This is not a contradiction but a confirmation that dose matters.

### 3. Bacopa Ranks #1 Among Plant Nootropics for Elderly Cognition
Feng 2026 independently confirms Bacopa as the top-ranked plant substance for executive function (SUCRA 91.3%) and language (SUCRA 93%) among 10 plant substances in elderly populations.

### 4. Evidence Base Has Matured Substantially
The evidence base has grown from 6-RCT meta-analysis (Pase 2012) to 29-RCT network meta-analysis (Tiemtad 2026). This represents a significant maturation of the Bacopa literature.

### 5. Remaining Gaps
- No stress/anxiety meta-analysis exists for Bacopa.
- No long-term safety meta-analysis available.
- Dose optimization remains unclear (is >=600mg/day optimal, or is there a ceiling?).

---

## Clinical Implications

May need to recommend 600mg/day for cognitive benefits while maintaining 300mg for stress/adaptogenic effects. This creates a dual-dosing consideration that should be reflected in the supplement database.
