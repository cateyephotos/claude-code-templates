## Inclusion Criteria

1. Human subjects (for clinical evidence sections; animal/in vitro accepted for mechanisms)
2. Published in peer-reviewed journal
3. Available DOI (REQUIRED for all included papers)
4. Relevant to berberine's claimed benefits (blood sugar, metabolic health, cardiovascular), mechanisms, safety, or dosage
5. Systematic reviews and meta-analyses prioritized over individual RCTs
6. PMID verified against PubMed API

## Exclusion Criteria

1. Non-English publication without English abstract
2. Retracted papers
3. Conference abstracts without full publication
4. Duplicate publications of the same dataset
5. Studies with critical methodological flaws
6. Narrative reviews without systematic methodology (for benefits/safety sections)
7. Studies focused exclusively on berberine derivatives or formulations not reflecting standard supplementation

## Screening Notes

Berberine is one of the most extensively studied plant-derived supplements, with over 50 RCTs captured across the included meta-analyses. The screening strategy prioritized the highest levels of evidence (systematic reviews and meta-analyses) published in 2023-2025, which aggregate individual trial data. This approach provides more robust effect estimates than citing individual RCTs.

Three of the safety citations share DOIs with benefit citations because the meta-analyses included safety endpoints alongside efficacy outcomes. This is appropriate and avoids duplicating papers unnecessarily.

The "Shi 2025 umbrella review" mentioned in prior pipeline notes was not found in PubMed. The Nazari 2023 umbrella meta-analysis (PMID 38016844) provides equivalent umbrella-level evidence synthesis.

All 9 included PMIDs were verified against the PubMed API on 2026-03-05. Previously existing citations in the file contained fabricated PMIDs (sequential patterns like 28789012, 29890123, etc.) that were entirely replaced.
