## Decision Tree Walkthrough

### Step 1: Does the supplement have human clinical data?

### Step 2: How many randomized controlled trials (RCTs)?
- **Answer:** 50+ RCTs identified (captured within meta-analyses)

### Step 3: Any RCT with sample size n > 100?

- **Largest RCT:** Multiple RCTs with n>100 within the meta-analyses; Wang 2024 MA includes cumulative 4,150 participants

### Step 4: Does a systematic review or meta-analysis exist?
- **Answer:** Yes — 5 systematic reviews/meta-analyses + 1 umbrella meta-analysis
- **Reviews identified:**
  - Wang et al. 2024 (50 RCTs, T2DM)
  - Nie et al. 2024 (10 RCTs, NAFLD)
  - Elahi Vahed et al. 2025 (23 RCTs, Obesity)
  - Liu et al. 2025 (MetS, placebo-controlled)

### Step 5: Are findings consistent across reviews?
- **Answer:** Yes
- **Consistency assessment:** All meta-analyses show consistent glucose-lowering, lipid-improving, and weight-reducing effects. Effect sizes are moderate and statistically significant across all reviews. Safety profile consistently reported as favorable.

Findings are remarkably consistent across independent research groups, multiple health domains, and different meta-analytic approaches.

## Evidence Gaps Identified

1. **Long-term safety data:** Most RCTs are 1-3 months; no MA specifically addresses safety beyond 6 months of continuous use
2. **Population diversity:** Majority of RCTs conducted in Asian populations; effects in diverse ethnic groups need more study
3. **Bioavailability optimization:** Despite known poor oral bioavailability, few MAs address different formulations (e.g., berberine HCl vs. phytosome)
4. **HDL-C and blood pressure:** Meta-analyses consistently show no significant effect on HDL-C or systolic/diastolic blood pressure
