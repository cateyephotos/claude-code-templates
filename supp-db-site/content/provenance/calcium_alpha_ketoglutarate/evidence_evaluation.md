# Evidence Evaluation: Calcium Alpha-Ketoglutarate (Ca-AKG)
## Pipeline Run: 2026-04-19

### Evaluation Framework
- **Primary:** GRADE certainty-of-evidence framework (High / Moderate / Low / Very Low)
- **Bias assessment:** Cochrane Risk of Bias 2.0 concepts adapted for non-RCT designs
- **Evidence-level mapping:** Oxford Centre for Evidence-Based Medicine levels 1–5

### Per-Study Evaluation

#### Demidenko 2021 — PMID 34847066
- **Design:** Retrospective paired-sample analysis of TruAge DNA-methylation tests (customer cohort).
- **Risk of bias:** High — retrospective, self-selected users, no control group, no blinding, regression to the mean not addressed.
- **GRADE certainty:** Low → Very Low for biological-age effect attribution.
- **Evidence level:** Level 3.
- **Key limitation:** Co-formulation with vitamins A and D prevents attribution solely to Ca-AKG.

#### Asadi Shahmirzadi 2020 — PMID 32877690
- **Design:** Longitudinal lifespan + healthspan study in C57BL/6 mice (Ca-AKG vs control chow).
- **Risk of bias:** Low within preclinical design; pre-registered frailty scoring, survival analysis, cytokine measurements.
- **GRADE (preclinical):** High (for mouse); not directly applicable to human outcomes.
- **Evidence level:** Level 4 (animal).

#### Chin 2014 — PMID 24828042
- **Design:** Target identification via affinity pulldown → biochemical validation → C. elegans lifespan assays.
- **Risk of bias:** Low — multi-modality mechanistic work with independent replication.
- **GRADE (mechanism):** High.
- **Evidence level:** Level 4 (invertebrate + cellular).

#### Wang 2020 — PMID 33154378
- **Design:** Aged mouse AKG supplementation + MSC differentiation + ChIP-seq / RNA-seq.
- **Risk of bias:** Low within preclinical design.
- **GRADE (mechanism):** High.
- **Evidence level:** Level 4.

#### Liu 2023 — PMID 36924682
- **Design:** Human cartilage AKG quantification + mouse OA model intervention.
- **Risk of bias:** Low within preclinical/translational design.
- **GRADE:** Moderate (mechanism); preliminary translational.
- **Evidence level:** Level 4.

#### Gyanwali 2022 — PMID 34952764
- **Design:** Narrative review (not systematic).
- **Risk of bias:** Moderate — narrative synthesis without formal PRISMA protocol.
- **GRADE:** Not formally applicable (review).
- **Evidence level:** Level 5.

#### Lim 2025 — PMID 40819772
- **Design:** Feasibility/recruitment evaluation for the ABLE RCT.
- **Risk of bias:** N/A — feasibility report, not efficacy data.
- **GRADE:** Protocol-level only.
- **Evidence level:** Level 2 (prospective RCT, methods-only).

### Aggregate Quality Metrics
- **Average per-study quality:** 7/10 (weighted toward mechanism papers).
- **Evidence-level distribution:** L2 ×1, L3 ×1, L4 ×4, L5 ×1.
- **Replication:** Lifespan signal replicated worms → mice by independent groups. Mechanism (ATP synthase/TOR, histone demethylation, mitophagy) each supported by separate high-impact papers.
- **Publication bias:** Not formally assessed (too few human studies to funnel-plot).
- **Funding heterogeneity:** Mix of NIH-extramural, non-US government, and industry (Ponce de Leon Health sponsored Rejuvant-related work) — noted as a potential source of enthusiasm bias in the biological-age signal.
- **Geographic diversity:** USA, Singapore, China — good geographic representation for mechanism; human data is primarily Singapore (Lim) and US-consumer cohort (Demidenko).
