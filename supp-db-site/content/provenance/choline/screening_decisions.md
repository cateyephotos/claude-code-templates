## Domain Assignment Review

### Mechanisms Domain (4 citations)

| Citation | Assignment Decision | Rationale |
|----------|-------------------|-----------|
| Zeisel & da Costa 2009 (PMID 19906248) | ✅ RETAINED — mechanisms | Foundational review of choline's biochemical roles: methyl donor for SAM/DNA methylation, precursor for acetylcholine neurotransmitter synthesis, building block for phosphatidylcholine membrane phospholipid. Exemplary mechanisms citation; high citation count in field. |
| Blusztajn, Slack & Mellott 2017 (PMID 28608931) | ✅ RETAINED — mechanisms | Comprehensive coverage of choline mechanisms in brain-specific context: acetylcholine synthesis, sphingomyelin production, epigenetic regulation via histone methylation. Extends Zeisel 2009 into neurodevelopmental mechanisms specifically. |
| Li & Vance 2008 (PMID 18005667) | ✅ RETAINED — mechanisms | Molecular-level pathway paper on CDP-choline (Kennedy pathway) for phosphatidylcholine biosynthesis. Bridges general biochemistry to membrane integrity and cellular signaling mechanisms. Appropriate molecular basis citation. |
| Shaw et al. 2009 (PMID 19454612) | ✅ RETAINED — mechanisms | Epidemiological study of choline/betaine in pregnancy on neural tube defect outcomes. Although primarily benefits research, its inclusion in mechanisms domain is justified by the methylation-dependent mechanism underlying neural tube defect risk — directly demonstrates that the methylation pathway operates physiologically. |

**Mechanisms domain decision:** All 4 retained. Domain is appropriately populated with a combination of mechanistic reviews and pathway-level papers.

### Deficiency Domain (2 citations)

| Citation | Assignment Decision | Rationale |
|----------|-------------------|-----------|
| Buchman et al. 1995 (PMID 7590654) | ✅ RETAINED — deficiency | Classic clinical demonstration of choline deficiency → hepatic steatosis (fatty liver) in parenteral nutrition patients, reversed by choline supplementation. Direct deficiency causation study; Hepatology journal; appropriate landmark citation for deficiency domain. |
| Wallace et al. 2018 (PMID 30248994) | ✅ RETAINED — deficiency | Nutrients systematic review on prevalence of choline deficiency across US population with emphasis on pregnancy; >90% of pregnant women inadequate; critical public health deficiency documentation. |

**Deficiency domain decision:** Both retained. Domain accurately characterizes clinical consequences (Buchman 1995) and population-level inadequacy (Wallace 2018). Note: Wallace et al. 2018 (PMID 30248994) is distinct from Wallace & Fulgoni 2017 (PMID 28449127) — different authors list, different year, different focus, same first author. No duplicate issue.

## Tier Screening Decision

**Screened for Tier 1:** Does the evidence base include a large-N (≥200 participants) RCT with High GRADE Cochrane or equivalent systematic review for a primary benefit domain?

- Caudill et al. 2018 is an RCT but has modest N; no GRADE assessment to High level

- Caudill et al. 2018 (RCT; maternal supplementation)
- IOM 1998 Adequate Intake established (authoritative dietary reference)
- Zeisel 2009 + Blusztajn 2017 provide robust mechanistic basis
- Safety well-established through Conlay 1992 + Taylor 2017
- **Tier 2 CONFIRMED**
