# Screening Decisions: Chondroitin Sulfate (ID: 32)
## Pipeline Run: 2026-03-06

### Inclusion Criteria
1. Human subjects (clinical trials, meta-analyses, systematic reviews, guidelines)
2. Published in peer-reviewed journal with confirmed DOI and PMID
3. Relevant to chondroitin sulfate's claimed benefits, mechanisms, or safety
4. Provides quantitative outcome data (effect sizes, WMD, SMD, WOMAC scores, JSN measurements)
5. Study type: RCT, meta-analysis, systematic review, IPD meta-analysis, NMA, GRADE review, or clinical guideline
6. Exception: Up to 2 in vitro mechanistic papers retained for mechanism section if providing key biological plausibility data
7. **Critical inclusion requirement:** Null findings (GAIT trial, Rojas-Briones 2017 GRADE) are mandatory inclusions; prior version omission was a pipeline violation

### Exclusion Criteria
1. Retracted papers (regardless of whether retraction was flagged in abstract)
2. Animal or veterinary studies
3. Non-OA/non-joint populations (e.g., IBD studies using chondroitin as mucosal agent)
4. Non-English without English abstract
5. Conference abstracts only (no full publication)
6. Protocol/design papers without outcome data
7. Papers with unconfirmed or ambiguous PMID/DOI
8. Duplicate publications of the same dataset

### Conflict of Interest Flagging

**PMID 30535141 (Gregori 2018, JAMA NMA)**: Co-authors Giacovelli G and Minto C affiliated with Rottapharm Biotech (manufacturer of pharmaceutical-grade chondroitin sulfate Condrosulf). Paper was included but all findings are reported with explicit COI flagging. This is the same COI previously documented in the glucosamine literature for the same research group, applied to chondroitin sulfate here. NMA favors pharmaceutical-grade CS which is the Rottapharm product.

**Pharmaceutical vs. nutraceutical grade as primary interpretive axis:** The CS evidence base uniquely requires stratification by formulation quality. Unlike glucosamine (where funding source is the primary confounder), for CS the pharmaceutical vs. nutraceutical grade distinction is documented as the strongest predictor of trial outcome. Rojas-Briones 2017 (GRADE review) formally documents very-low certainty for nutraceutical-grade CS while acknowledging pharmaceutical-grade CS has more defensible evidence. This distinction is maintained throughout all screening and synthesis decisions.

**GAIT trial mandatory inclusion (correcting prior version violation):** The prior database entry (32_chondroitin_sulfate_enhanced.js, 2025-01-25) omitted PMID 16495392 (GAIT trial). This was a critical pipeline violation. The GAIT trial (n=1,583) is the largest single RCT for the nutraceutical-grade CS + glucosamine combination and produced a null primary outcome for CS. Its inclusion as KEY COUNTER-EVIDENCE is mandatory per Mode 2 evidence update protocol.
