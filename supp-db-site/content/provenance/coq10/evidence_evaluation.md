# CoQ10 (ID: 18) — Evidence Evaluation
**Date:** 2026-03-05

## Paper 1: Talebi et al. 2024 (PMID: 38479900)
- **GRADE Assessment:** MODERATE-HIGH quality
- **Evidence Level:** Level 1 (SR+dose-response MA)
- **Risk of Bias:** Low — comprehensive GRADE assessment included
- **Precision:** Good — 28 RCTs, n=830, significant p-values for primary outcomes
- **Consistency:** High — consistent CK, LDH, MDA reductions across studies
- **Directness:** High — directly studies CoQ10 for exercise recovery
- **Dose-Response:** Confirmed — per 100mg/day: CK -23.07, LDH -27.21, Mb -7.09, MDA -0.17
- **Quality Score:** 8/10
- **Limitation:** Most studies in Asian populations; generalizability caution

## Paper 2: Daei et al. 2024 (PMID: 38630421)
- **GRADE Assessment:** MODERATE quality
- **Evidence Level:** Level 1 (SR+MA with meta-regression)
- **Risk of Bias:** Low to moderate
- **Precision:** Moderate — 12 studies, n=489
- **Consistency:** Good — FMD consistently improved
- **Directness:** High — directly measures endothelial function
- **Dose-Response:** Confirmed — meta-regression slope 0.01, p=0.006
- **Quality Score:** 7/10
- **Limitation:** Moderate heterogeneity; variable populations

## Paper 3: Patiño-Cardona et al. 2024 (PMID: 39728267)
- **GRADE Assessment:** HIGH quality (umbrella review = highest evidence level)
- **Evidence Level:** Level 1 (umbrella review of meta-analyses)
- **Risk of Bias:** Low — systematic methodology across multiple MAs
- **Precision:** Excellent — synthesizes multiple MAs with thousands of participants
- **Consistency:** High for glycemic outcomes; moderate for lipid outcomes
- **Directness:** High — directly evaluates CoQ10 metabolic effects
- **Quality Score:** 9/10
- **Limitation:** Lipid results inconsistent across source MAs
