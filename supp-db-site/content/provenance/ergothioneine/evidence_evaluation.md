# Evidence Evaluation: Ergothioneine
## Pipeline Run: 2026-04-19

### Evaluation Framework
- **Primary:** Modified GRADE (Grading of Recommendations Assessment, Development and Evaluation) — applied informally
- **Bias assessment:** For the single completed RCT (Yau 2024), Cochrane Risk of Bias 2.0 considerations (sample size, blinding, allocation concealment)
- **Quality scoring:** Pipeline composite (RCTs + meta-analyses + sample sizes + replication + consistency + recency)

### Per-Study Evaluation

#### Smith 2020 — Heart, n=3236, 21.4yr cohort (PMID 31672783)
- **Study type:** Prospective population-based cohort with LC-MS metabolomics
- **GRADE domains:**
  - Risk of bias: Low (registry-grade outcomes, multivariable Cox adjustment, Bonferroni-corrected metabolite selection)
  - Inconsistency: Not assessable (single cohort)
  - Indirectness: Not serious (population-based adults, hard mortality and CVD endpoints)
  - Imprecision: Not serious (n=3236, 843 deaths, 603 incident CVD events — well-powered)
  - Publication bias: Not assessable
- **Overall GRADE certainty:** **Moderate** (observational; cannot establish causality)
- **Assigned evidence level:** **Level 3** (cohort)
- **Quality score contribution:** Anchors Tier 3 — single biggest human dataset

#### Yau 2024 — J Alzheimer's Dis pilot RCT, n=19, 12 months (PMID 39544014)
- **Study type:** Double-blind, placebo-controlled pilot RCT (NCT03641404)
- **GRADE domains:**
  - Risk of bias: Some concerns (single-centre, n=19 is very small, intermittent dosing schedule)
  - Imprecision: Serious (sample size limits efficacy inference)
  - Safety reporting: Strong (monthly hematology, renal, hepatic markers)
- **Overall GRADE certainty:** **Low for efficacy / Moderate for safety**
- **Assigned evidence level:** **Level 2** (RCT)
- **Quality score contribution:** Establishes the supplement as having ANY completed human RCT, qualifying it for Tier 3 rather than Tier 4

#### Kondoh 2022 — FEBS Letters, frailty/cognition (PMID 35090053)
- **Study type:** Cross-sectional / observational human metabolomics
- **GRADE certainty:** **Low** (cross-sectional, no causal inference)
- **Evidence level:** **Level 3**
- **Role:** Corroborates Smith 2020 association via independent cohort; ties decline specifically to cognition (not muscle) in elderly

#### Sprenger 2025 — Cell Metabolism MPST mechanism (PMID 39965563)
- **Study type:** Mechanistic — proteome-wide thermal stability + in vivo mouse exercise
- **GRADE certainty:** **High for mechanism** (multiple converging methods)
- **Evidence level:** **Level 4** (preclinical)
- **Role:** First physiologically relevant direct molecular target of ergothioneine identified — high-impact mechanism paper

#### Gründemann 2022 — FEBS Letters ETT inventory (PMID 34958679)
- **Study type:** Authoritative narrative review
- **Evidence level:** **Level 5**
- **Role:** Definitive synthesis of OCTN1/SLC22A4 substrate specificity and tissue distribution; cited universally

#### Borodina 2020 — Nutrition Research Reviews (PMID 32051057)
- **Study type:** Authoritative narrative review (Kell, Halliwell co-authored)
- **Evidence level:** **Level 5**
- **Role:** Foundational biology / nutraceutical review; safety overview

#### Tian 2023 — British Journal of Nutrition Horizons review (PMID 38018890)
- **Study type:** Horizons review
- **Evidence level:** **Level 5**
- **Role:** Most recent authoritative ageing-and-cardiometabolic synthesis; integrates Smith 2020 cohort

#### Ames 2018 — PNAS longevity-vitamin classification (PMID 30322941)
- **Study type:** Conceptual / framework review
- **Evidence level:** **Level 5**
- **Role:** Names ergothioneine as a putative "longevity vitamin" — high prestige, frequently cited as motivation for human research

#### Liu 2023 — Molecules antioxidant biochemistry (PMID 36838636)
- **Study type:** Mechanistic review (skin focus)
- **Evidence level:** **Level 5**
- **Role:** Synthesises in vitro free-radical scavenging data; supports OCTN1 mitochondrial/nuclear localisation claim

#### Katsube 2024 — GeroScience mouse lifespan (PMID 38446314)
- **Study type:** Preclinical lifespan + healthspan study (mouse + C. elegans)
- **GRADE certainty:** **Moderate for preclinical lifespan extension**
- **Evidence level:** **Level 4**
- **Role:** First mammalian lifespan study; provides preclinical foundation for human longevity-RCT motivation

#### Tian 2021 — ErgMS protocol (PMID 34715934)
- **Study type:** RCT protocol publication
- **Evidence level:** **Level 2**
- **Role:** Establishes 5 vs 30 mg/day human dosing framework

#### Cheah 2023 — Antioxidants doxorubicin (PMID 36829879)
- **Study type:** Preclinical in vivo (mouse)
- **Evidence level:** **Level 4**
- **Role:** Identifies oncology supportive-care indication

### Aggregate Quality Metrics
- **Total citations:** 12 unique papers (13 unique PMID/DOI verifier slots)
- **Evidence-level distribution:** Level 2: 2, Level 3: 2, Level 4: 4, Level 5: 4
- **Replication assessment:** Independent groups in Singapore (Halliwell/Cheah), Sweden (Smith/Melander), Japan (Kondoh, Katsube), UK (Tian/Moore), USA (Sprenger/Spiegelman) — strong cross-lab consilience
- **Publication-bias assessment:** Not formally assessed; field is small and concentrated in a handful of leading labs which is a noted limitation
- **Funding-source diversity:** Mix of academic and translational research funding (Sprenger 2025 has Aevum Therapeutics-related disclosures; most others publicly funded)
- **Geographic diversity:** Sweden, Singapore, UK, Japan, USA — adequate
- **Temporal recency:** 2/12 papers from 2024–2025; 6/12 from 2022–2025 — actively expanding evidence base
