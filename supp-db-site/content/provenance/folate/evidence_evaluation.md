#### Study 2: durga_2007_cognitive — Durga et al. 2007 Lancet RCT
- **PMID:** 17240287 | **DOI:** 10.1016/S0140-6736(07)60109-3
- **Study type:** Randomized controlled trial (double-blind, placebo-controlled)
- **Journal:** The Lancet (impact factor >200)
- **Assigned section:** benefits (cognitive function)
- **GRADE domains:**
  - Risk of bias: Low — double-blind, placebo-controlled, adequate allocation concealment; published in Lancet with rigorous peer review; pre-specified cognitive composite outcomes; intent-to-treat analysis
  - Inconsistency: Not serious — consistent findings across cognitive domains measured; supported by subsequent meta-analyses (Wang 2022)
  - Indirectness: Not serious — general population (50–70-year-olds with elevated homocysteine); directly applicable to supplement use context
  - Imprecision: Not serious — n=818; 3-year duration; adequate statistical power; effect sizes reported with 95% CI
  - Publication bias: Undetected — large trial; Lancet publication; pre-registered
- **Overall GRADE certainty:** High
- **Assigned evidence level:** Level 1
- **Quality score contribution:** 10/10
- **Notes:** Definitive RCT for cognitive function in older adults. 3-year follow-up (800 mcg folic acid/day) — the longest duration cognitive folate RCT. Cohen's d effect sizes significant across memory, information processing, and sensorimotor speed domains.

#### Study 6: rossignol_2021_folate_receptor — Rossignol & Frye 2021 J Pers Med
- **PMID:** 34834493 | **DOI:** 10.3390/jpm11121310
- **Study type:** Systematic review + meta-analysis
- **Journal:** Journal of Personalized Medicine (open-access; MDPI)
- **Assigned section:** mechanisms, benefits (autism/CFD), dosage
- **GRADE domains:**
  - Risk of bias: Low-Moderate — systematic review methodology; open-access MDPI journal has lower impact factor than specialty journals; included studies are predominantly case series and small open-label trials in ASD population
  - Inconsistency: Some concerns — ASD is a heterogeneous condition; FRα autoantibody prevalence varies by study; folinic acid response rates vary by ASD subpopulation
  - Indirectness: Some concerns — cerebral folate deficiency (CFD) is a specific mechanistic subpopulation; results not generalizable to all supplement users
  - Imprecision: Some concerns — ASD-CFD subgroup is rare; individual studies have small n; meta-analytic pooling limited by trial heterogeneity
  - Publication bias: Suspected — rare condition; positive case reports more likely published
- **Overall GRADE certainty:** Low-Moderate
- **Assigned evidence level:** Level 1
- **Quality score contribution:** 7/10
- **Notes:** Critical for the FRα autoantibody mechanism and cerebral folate deficiency pathway. The 71% ASD vs 4% control prevalence difference for FRα autoantibodies is a clinically important finding. Evidence quality is limited by small trial sizes but mechanistic novelty is high.

### Replication Assessment
- **NTD prevention**: Replicated across decades of independent trials; meta-analyzed multiple times; USPSTF Grade A = highest replication standard
- **Cognitive function (aging)**: Durga 2007 RCT confirmed by Wang 2022 meta-analysis; moderate replication
- **Depression (adjunctive)**: Maruf 2021 meta-analysis pools multiple independent RCTs; moderate replication
- **CVD/homocysteine**: Homocysteine lowering well-replicated; hard cardiovascular endpoints inconsistent across B-vitamin trials (folate-specific contribution unclear)
- **Fertility/PCOS**: Cochrane review pools available RCTs; moderate replication within the review
- **ASD/cerebral folate deficiency**: Emerging evidence; multiple small studies meta-analyzed; limited by rare condition and small trial sizes

### Publication Bias Assessment
- **Formal funnel plot:** Not applicable to narrative reviews; meta-analyses likely addressed in their own supplementary materials
- **Risk assessment:** Moderate risk for depression and ASD literature (smaller trials in positive direction more likely published); Low risk for NTD prevention (massive literature with regulatory backing); Low risk for PK/mechanistic literature
