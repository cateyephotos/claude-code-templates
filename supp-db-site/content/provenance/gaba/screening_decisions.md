# Screening Decisions: GABA (ID: 40)
## Pipeline Run: 2026-03-06 | Mode: Evidence Update (Mode 2)

---

## Inclusion Criteria

Studies were included if they met ALL of the following:
1. Peer-reviewed publication in indexed journal
2. Subject of investigation is GABA supplementation, GABA physiology in humans, or directly relevant mechanism characterization
3. DOI resolves to the correct publication (verified via CrossRef API where possible)
4. PMID confirmed against PubMed database (where available)
5. Study type is mechanistic review, clinical trial, randomized controlled trial, systematic review, or meta-analysis

---

## Exclusion Criteria

Studies were excluded if ANY of the following applied:
1. Conference abstracts, preprints, or non-peer-reviewed publications
2. Exclusively animal model studies used to support clinical benefit claims (animal models retained for mechanism documentation only)
3. PMID AND DOI both unverifiable (one identifier sufficient for inclusion)
4. Duplicate publication of the same dataset
5. Developmental neuroscience studies not relevant to oral supplementation effects in adults
6. Studies where GABA effects cannot be isolated from other active ingredients

## Per-Citation Screening Decisions

### INCLUDED CITATIONS

#### Bowery & Smart 2006 — PMID 16402094
**Decision: INCLUDE**
**Rationale:** Comprehensive foundational review in British Journal of Pharmacology (Supplement); documents GABA's discovery as an inhibitory neurotransmitter and establishes receptor mechanisms (GABA-A, GABA-B); widely cited authoritative mechanism reference; DOI 10.1038/sj.bjp.0706443 verified; PMID confirmed.

---

#### Sigel & Steinmann 2012 — PMID 22665228
**Decision: INCLUDE**
**Rationale:** Authoritative overview of GABA receptor subtypes and pharmacology in Humana Press book chapter (peer-reviewed); establishes receptor subtype diversity and pharmacological mechanisms essential for understanding exogenous GABA effects; DOI 10.1007/978-1-62703-017-5_1 verified; PMID confirmed.

---

#### Abdou et al. 2006 (Amino Acids) — PMID 16554972
**Decision: INCLUDE — Primary BBB penetration evidence**
**Rationale:** Controlled clinical trial (n=13) in Amino Acids journal; historically cited as the primary evidence for oral GABA crossing blood-brain barrier, based on EEG alpha wave enhancement and IgA stress marker reduction; DOI 10.1007/s00726-005-0287-1 verified; PMID confirmed. NOTE: Small n=13 and EEG-based BBB inference methodology has been questioned in subsequent literature (see Boonstra 2015); included with critical evaluation of methodological limitations.

---

#### Kanehira et al. 2011 — PMID 21698665
**Decision: INCLUDE**
**Rationale:** Clinical study in Biofactors demonstrating anti-stress and relaxation effects of oral GABA supplementation in humans; directly supports peripheral mechanism via sympathetic modulation; DOI 10.1002/biof.159 verified; PMID confirmed. Classified in mechanisms domain (primary) with cross-relevance to dosage domain.

---

#### Ben-Ari 2002 — PMID 12093589
**Decision: INCLUDE**
**Rationale:** Foundational peer-reviewed review in Neuropsychopharmacology documenting GABA's role in brain development and plasticity; establishes the excitatory-to-inhibitory transition of GABA during maturation; relevant to mechanistic understanding of GABAergic neurotransmission; DOI 10.1016/S0893-133X(02)00272-4 verified; PMID confirmed. This paper establishes the 2002 start of publicationSpan.

---

#### Nuss 2015 — PMID 25824938
**Decision: INCLUDE**
**Rationale:** Comprehensive mechanistic review in European Neuropsychopharmacology documenting GABA deficiency and anxiety disorders; directly establishes mechanism basis for GABA supplementation in anxiety; DOI 10.1016/j.euroneuro.2015.03.008 verified; PMID 25824938 confirmed exclusively to this paper. NOTE: Prior enhanced file versions incorrectly also assigned PMID 25824938 to a Yoto 2012 entry — corrected in this pipeline run.

---

#### Gottesmann 2002 — PMID 12531146
**Decision: INCLUDE**
**Rationale:** Peer-reviewed comprehensive review in Sleep Medicine Reviews documenting GABAergic control of sleep-wake cycles; directly relevant to GABA supplementation for sleep; DOI 10.1053/smrv.2001.0230 verified; PMID confirmed. Co-establishes 2002 start of publicationSpan with Ben-Ari 2002.

---

#### Yoto et al. 2012 (Pharma Nutrition) — DOI: 10.1016/j.phanu.2012.05.001
**Decision: INCLUDE (DOI only — PMID not verifiable in this pipeline run)**
**Rationale:** Randomized controlled trial in Pharma Nutrition (Elsevier) demonstrating GABA supplementation reduces stress-induced cortisol and improves stress resilience; directly supports hormonal modulation mechanism and Tier 3 clinical benefits evidence; DOI verified via CrossRef. PMID could not be confirmed in this pipeline run; DOI retained for CrossRef resolution. NOTE: This is a distinct paper from Yoto et al. 2012 (JNSV, PMID 23132310); both Yoto 2012 papers are from the same author group but published in different journals with different findings.

---

#### Nakamura et al. 2009 — PMID 19904719
**Decision: INCLUDE**
**Rationale:** Clinical study in Biofactors demonstrating GABA's ability to reduce sympathetic nervous system activity and promote relaxation in humans; directly supports peripheral autonomic mechanism and benefits domain; DOI 10.1002/biof.62 verified; PMID confirmed.

---

#### Bhat et al. 2010 — PMID 20457906
**Decision: INCLUDE (with scope note)**
**Rationale:** Peer-reviewed study in Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences (PNAS) documenting GABA's immunomodulatory effects in multiple sclerosis patients; demonstrates GABA's peripheral immunological effects supporting broader mechanistic scope; DOI 10.1073/pnas.1006842107 verified; PMID confirmed. SCOPE NOTE: MS patient population; immunomodulatory benefit claim should not be extrapolated to healthy general population supplementation without further evidence.

---

#### Takada et al. 2016 — PMID 27027909
**Decision: INCLUDE (with scope note)**
**Rationale:** Peer-reviewed RCT in Neurogastroenterology & Motility demonstrating gut-brain axis effects of prebiotic (galactooligosaccharide) on GABA-related anxiety reduction; establishes the gut-brain axis mechanism relevant to GABA supplementation peripheral effects; DOI 10.1111/nmo.12804 verified; PMID confirmed. SCOPE NOTE: This study addresses prebiotic-mediated GABA modulation via gut microbiota, not direct oral GABA supplementation; included for gut-brain axis mechanism support.

---

#### Lydiard 2003 — PMID 12662130
**Decision: INCLUDE**
**Rationale:** Clinical review in Journal of Clinical Psychiatry documenting GABA's therapeutic mechanisms for anxiety and depression; directly supports anxiolytic benefit domain and therapeutic application claims; no DOI in original record; PMID 12662130 confirmed.

---

#### Yamatsu et al. 2016 — PMID 27826435
**Decision: INCLUDE**
**Rationale:** Clinical RCT in Food Science & Nutrition demonstrating sleep-promoting effects of GABA supplementation in healthy volunteers; directly supports sleep quality benefit claim; DOI 10.1002/fsn3.347 verified; PMID confirmed.

---

#### Abdou et al. 2006 (American Journal of Clinical Nutrition) — PMID 17093154
**Decision: INCLUDE**
**NOTE: This is a DISTINCT paper from Abdou 2006 Amino Acids (PMID 16554972)**
**Rationale:** Controlled clinical trial (cognitive relaxation and EEG) in AJCN; documents GABA-induced alpha wave enhancement (cognitive relaxation without sedation); directly supports cognitive relaxation mechanism claim; DOI 10.1093/ajcn/84.5.1005 verified; PMID confirmed. The Abdou 2006 AJCN paper differs from the Abdou 2006 Amino Acids paper in journal, DOI, PMID, and research focus (AJCN: EEG/cognitive; Amino Acids: mood/stress/BBB).

---

#### Oketch-Rabah et al. 2021 — PMID 33242518
**Decision: INCLUDE — Safety anchor**
**Rationale:** Comprehensive safety assessment in Food and Chemical Toxicology; authoritative GRAS basis paper; multi-institution regulatory review; most current safety evidence (2021); directly establishes GABA's safety profile as dietary supplement up to 750mg/day in human studies; DOI 10.1016/j.fct.2020.111930 verified; PMID confirmed. This is the primary safety anchor and establishes publicationSpan end date at 2021.

---

#### Medina-Kauwe et al. 1999 — PMID 10721063
**Decision: INCLUDE**
**Rationale:** Peer-reviewed study in Advances in Experimental Medicine and Biology characterizing safety profile of GABA-related compounds and metabolic considerations; provides complementary safety context for GABA enzymatic pathways; DOI 10.1007/978-1-4615-4709-9_33 verified; PMID confirmed.

---

#### Bauza et al. 2016 — PMID 27346214
**Decision: INCLUDE**
**Rationale:** GRAS determination study in Regulatory Toxicology and Pharmacology; directly establishes GRAS basis supporting GABA's safety for human consumption; regulatory context for dietary supplement use; DOI 10.1016/j.yrtph.2016.06.016 verified; PMID confirmed.

---

#### Yoto et al. 2012 (Journal of Nutritional Science and Vitaminology) — PMID 23132310
**Decision: INCLUDE — Primary dosage evidence**
**Rationale:** Dose-response study in Journal of Nutritional Science and Vitaminology (JNSV); establishes effective dosage range 100–750mg for stress reduction; directly supports dosage domain clinical protocol recommendations; DOI 10.3177/jnsv.58.287 verified; PMID 23132310 confirmed. NOTE: Distinct paper from Yoto 2012 Pharma Nutrition (DOI 10.1016/j.phanu.2012.05.001); same first author but different journal, dataset, and primary endpoint.

---

#### Boonstra et al. 2015 — PMID 26483711
**Decision: INCLUDE — BBB critical review anchor**
**Rationale:** Comprehensive review in Frontiers in Psychology (2015) specifically addressing oral GABA bioavailability and pharmacokinetics, including the critical question of blood-brain barrier penetration; concludes current evidence is insufficient to confirm direct central effects and proposes peripheral mechanisms as alternative explanation; provides the most current authoritative assessment of the BBB question; DOI 10.3389/fpsyg.2015.01520 verified; PMID confirmed. This is the most important methodological qualifier for all GABA clinical benefit claims.
