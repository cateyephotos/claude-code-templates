## Score Calibration Notes

- **Score 65** positions ginger in the upper-middle range of Tier 2 (55–75). This reflects:
  - Two Tier 2-qualifying meta-analyses as primary benefit anchors (NVP N=1,278; OA N=593)
  - Well-established dual COX/LOX inhibition mechanism supported by multiple specialized reviews
  - Pregnancy-specific safety evidence for the primary NVP indication
  - Regulatory-grade dosage standardization (Commission E)
  - Good recency (most recent citation 2018 vs. 14-year gap for astaxanthin)

- **Primary evidence strength**: The two large pooled meta-analyses (Viljoen 2014 NVP and Bartels 2015 OA) provide solid Tier 2 anchors that lift ginger above a typical Tier 3 single-RCT evidence base

- **Why score 65 and not higher (70+):**
  - No Cochrane review (Tier 1 requirement)
  - No single landmark RCT with N≥200 (all individual RCTs small: N≤120)
  - GRADE MODERATE only for best meta-analyses; no GRADE HIGH evidence
  - 8-year recency gap (most recent citation 2018)
  - Clinical effect sizes are modest (SMD ~0.28–0.29 for primary meta-analyses)

- **Comparison to pipeline peers (IDs 41–47)**:
  - Inositol (ID 41, score 68): Meta-analyses for PCOS, N>500 pooled
  - Selenium (ID 42, score 67): SELECT trial (N=35,533) + meta-analyses
  - Choline (ID 43, score 66): Adequate single RCT + guideline support
  - Alpha-Lipoic Acid (ID 44, score 68): ALADIN/SYDNEY trials + meta-analyses
  - Lutein (ID 45, score 70): AREDS2 (N=4,203, Cochrane-equivalent)
  - Astaxanthin (ID 46, score 55): Multiple small RCTs (max N=49), no meta-analysis — Tier 3
  - **Ginger (ID 47, score 65)**: Two Tier 2-qualifying meta-analyses (NVP + OA), Commission E — Tier 2 appropriate

Score 65 is calibrated to reflect that ginger has stronger pooled evidence than a typical mid-Tier 2 supplement (via two separate N≥500 pooled meta-analyses) but lacks the single dominant landmark trial or Cochrane review that distinguishes the 68–72 range.
