## Paper 1: Wieland et al. 2026 — Cochrane Systematic Review

### Identification

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **PMID** | 41641880 |
| **DOI** | Pending (Cochrane Database Syst Rev) |
| **Title** | Ginkgo biloba for cognitive impairment and dementia |
| **Authors** | Wieland LS, et al. |
| **Journal** | Cochrane Database of Systematic Reviews |
| **Year** | 2026 |
| **Quality Score** | **9/10** |

### Study Design and Scale

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Design** | Cochrane systematic review |
| **Methodology** | GRADE certainty assessment |
| **Studies Included** | 82 RCTs |
| **Total Participants** | n = 10,613 |
| **Registration** | Cochrane Protocol |
| **Follow-up Range** | Variable (weeks to years) |

### Key Findings

| Population | Finding | Certainty (GRADE) |
|---|---|---|
| Dementia (all-cause) | Small to moderate cognitive benefits | Low |
| Mild Cognitive Impairment (MCI) | Probably no effect | Moderate |
| Healthy older adults | Insufficient evidence | Very low |
| Safety (all populations) | Adverse events equivalent to placebo | Moderate |

### PICO Assessment

- **Population:** Adults with dementia, MCI, or cognitive complaints. Multiple etiologies included (Alzheimer's, vascular, mixed).
- **Intervention:** Ginkgo biloba extract (predominantly EGb 761), various doses (80-240 mg/day). Oral supplementation.
- **Comparator:** Placebo in most trials. Some active comparator arms.
- **Outcomes:** Global cognitive function (primary), specific cognitive domains, activities of daily living, behavioral symptoms, quality of life, adverse events.

### Strengths

1. Cochrane review — the gold standard for systematic evidence synthesis
2. Largest GB evidence synthesis ever conducted (82 RCTs, n = 10,613)
3. GRADE certainty assessments provide actionable confidence levels
4. Updates the foundational Birks & Grimley Evans 2009 review after 17 years
5. Comprehensive safety analysis with definitive AE equivalence to placebo
6. Clear population-specific conclusions (dementia vs. MCI distinction)

### Weaknesses

1. Positive finding (dementia benefits) rated at **low certainty** — limits clinical confidence
2. Null finding (MCI) rated at **moderate certainty** — stronger evidence for no effect than for effect
3. Heterogeneity in GB preparations across the 82 RCTs (not all used EGb 761)
4. Many included RCTs are older trials with methodological limitations
5. Healthy adult evidence classified as "very low" certainty — cannot draw conclusions for the supplement consumer population

### Impact on Evidence File

- **Citation category:** Benefits — Cognitive function
- **Citation ID:** To be assigned (e.g., `ginkgo_ben_005`)
- **Evidence weight:** High — anchors the entire evidence profile
- **Updates:** Supersedes older SR citations in the file

### Study Design and Scale

| **Total Participants** | n = 2,107 |

### Key Findings

| Cognitive Domain | Best Intervention | GB 240mg Ranking | SUCRA |
|---|---|---|---|
| Working memory | High-dose Bacopa | Inferior | Bacopa SUCRA 100% |
| Executive function | GB 240mg | Moderate efficacy | — |
| Overall cognitive composite | High-dose Bacopa | Second tier | — |

### PICO Assessment

- **Population:** Adults with cognitive complaints or mild impairment (mixed populations across included RCTs).
- **Intervention:** Ginkgo biloba extract at 120mg and 240mg daily doses; Bacopa monnieri at high and low doses.
- **Comparator:** Placebo (indirect comparisons via network).
- **Outcomes:** Working memory, executive function, cognitive composite scores.

### Strengths

1. First head-to-head NMA comparing two major herbal nootropics
2. PROSPERO registered — methodological transparency
3. SUCRA rankings enable direct, quantitative supplement comparison
4. Dose-response data for GB (120mg vs. 240mg)
5. Bayesian framework provides probabilistic evidence interpretation

### Weaknesses

1. Relatively small total sample (n = 2,107) compared to GB's broader evidence base
2. Heterogeneity in GB preparations (not all EGb 761)
3. Indirect comparisons (Bacopa vs. GB via shared placebo arms, not direct head-to-head trials)
4. Population heterogeneity across included RCTs
5. Limited to two herbal nootropics — broader competitive landscape not captured

### Impact on Evidence File

- **Citation category:** Benefits — Cognitive function (comparative)
- **Citation ID:** To be assigned (e.g., `ginkgo_ben_006`)
- **Evidence weight:** Moderate — important for competitive context
- **Updates:** Adds new dose-response and comparative efficacy data

## Paper 3: Masserini et al. 2025 — VCI Meta-Analysis

### Identification

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|
| **PMID** | 41198594 |
| **DOI** | Pending |
| **Title** | Therapeutic strategies for vascular cognitive impairment: A comprehensive meta-analysis |
| **Authors** | Masserini F, et al. |
| **Journal** | Ageing Research Reviews |
| **Year** | 2025 |
| **Quality Score** | **7/10** |

### Study Design and Scale

| Parameter | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Design** | Comprehensive meta-analysis |
| **Methodology** | Effect size calculations (Cohen's d), 95% CIs |
| **Studies Included** | 173 trials |
| **Total Participants** | n = 22,347 |
| **Registration** | Not reported |
| **Scope** | All therapeutic strategies for VCI |

### Key Findings

| Outcome | GB Effect Size | 95% CI | Rank |
|---|---|---|---|
| VCI cognitive function | d = 0.83 | 0.00 — 1.67 | **#1 among all interventions** |
| VCI functional improvement | d = 0.50 | Not reported | Top tier |

### PICO Assessment

- **Population:** Adults with vascular cognitive impairment (VCI) across multiple etiologies (post-stroke, small vessel disease, mixed).
- **Intervention:** Ginkgo biloba extract (standalone). Also compared against pharmacological interventions, other supplements, cognitive training, exercise.
- **Comparator:** Placebo or standard care.
- **Outcomes:** Cognitive function composite scores, functional status, activities of daily living.

### Strengths

1. Massive scale — 173 trials and 22,347 participants across all VCI interventions
2. Establishes a clinically important niche for GB (VCI population)
3. #1 ranking among all interventions for VCI cognition is a significant finding
4. Effect size (d = 0.83) is clinically meaningful (large effect)
5. Also shows functional improvement (d = 0.50), supporting real-world relevance

### Weaknesses

1. **Very wide confidence interval** (0.00-1.67) includes the null effect — statistical significance is borderline
2. No registration reported — transparency concern
3. VCI-specific finding may not generalize to other dementia etiologies or healthy populations
4. GB studies within this MA may overlap with those in Wieland 2026 Cochrane
5. Effect size calculation methods not fully described
6. Potential for publication bias in the VCI GB literature

### Impact on Evidence File

- **Citation category:** Benefits — Vascular cognitive impairment (new niche)
- **Citation ID:** To be assigned (e.g., `ginkgo_ben_007`)
- **Evidence weight:** Moderate — important niche finding but limited by wide CI
- **Updates:** Establishes new VCI-specific evidence category

### Key Findings

| Finding Area | Result |
|---|---|
| Direction of evidence | Most SRs (majority of 16) favor EGb 761 for cognition |
| Behavioral symptoms | Favorable evidence from multiple SRs |
| SR methodological quality | AMSTAR 2 reveals generally **poor** quality across the GB SR literature |
| Specific EGb 761 dose | Most evidence supports 240mg/day |

### PICO Assessment

- **Population:** Adults with dementia or cognitive impairment (as defined by the 16 included SRs).
- **Intervention:** EGb 761 specifically (the standardized ginkgo extract by Schwabe Pharmaceuticals, 240mg/day in most trials).
- **Comparator:** As defined by each included SR (predominantly placebo).
- **Outcomes:** Cognitive function, behavioral and psychological symptoms of dementia (BPSD), safety.

### Strengths

1. Umbrella-level evidence — sits atop the evidence hierarchy
2. PROSPERO registered — methodological transparency
3. AMSTAR 2 quality assessment — provides critical meta-level quality insights
4. Specific to EGb 761 — the most clinically validated GB formulation
5. Rigorous screening (16 from 126 SRs) with clear inclusion criteria
6. Identifies a quality ceiling for the GB evidence base

### Weaknesses

1. AMSTAR 2 reveals **poor SR quality** — undermines confidence in the favorable conclusions
2. EGb 761-specific focus means findings may not apply to other GB extracts
3. 16 SRs is a relatively small sample for an umbrella review
4. Potential conflict of interest (some authors may have industry connections to Schwabe Pharmaceuticals — not explicitly assessed)
5. Does not provide pooled effect estimates (qualitative synthesis only)
6. Limited to cognition and behavior — does not cover other GB indications

### Impact on Evidence File

- **Citation category:** Benefits — Cognitive function (meta-evidence)
- **Citation ID:** To be assigned (e.g., `ginkgo_ben_008`)
- **Evidence weight:** Moderate — valuable meta-level perspective but tempered by poor SR quality finding
- **Updates:** Adds umbrella-level evidence context and quality calibration

## Cross-Paper Synthesis

### Evidence Convergence

| Theme | Papers Supporting | Confidence |
|---|---|---|
| GB benefits in dementia | Wieland 2026, Pfuhlmann 2025 | Moderate (low certainty per GRADE) |
| GB no benefit in MCI | Wieland 2026 | Moderate (moderate certainty per GRADE) |
| GB #1 for VCI | Masserini 2025 | Low (wide CI) |
| Bacopa outperforms GB (working memory) | Tiemtad 2026 | Moderate |
| GB safety = placebo | Wieland 2026 | Moderate-High |
| Poor underlying SR quality | Pfuhlmann 2025 | High (AMSTAR 2) |

### Evidence Gaps Identified

1. No new RCTs in healthy adults (the primary supplement consumer population)
2. No dose-response MA specific to EGb 761 vs. non-standardized extracts
3. No long-term prevention trials (> 5 years)
4. No evidence synthesis for GB + peripheral circulation endpoints
5. Tinnitus evidence not captured in this cognitive-focused update
