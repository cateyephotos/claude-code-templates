## Inclusion Criteria

Papers were included if they met ALL of the following:

1. **Population:** Humans (not animal or in vitro)
2. **Intervention:** Ginkgo biloba extract as a standalone supplement (not as a minor component of a multi-herb formula)
3. **Outcome:** Cognitive function, dementia, or directly related neurological endpoints
4. **Design:** Systematic review, meta-analysis, network meta-analysis, umbrella review, or large RCT (n > 100)
5. **Recency:** Published 2024 or later
6. **Novelty:** Not already cited in the existing enhanced citations file

## Notes

- The high exclusion rate (82.6%) is driven primarily by the large number of Chinese medicine combination formula studies indexed under "Ginkgo biloba" in PubMed. These studies use GB as one ingredient among many and do not provide evidence for GB as a standalone supplement.
- The zero results from PubMed Search 2 (cognitive RCTs) suggest that the GB clinical trial evidence base has reached maturity, with new evidence coming primarily through evidence syntheses rather than novel primary trials.
- All 4 included papers represent the highest tiers of evidence hierarchy: 1 Cochrane SR, 1 NMA, 1 large-scale MA, and 1 umbrella review.
