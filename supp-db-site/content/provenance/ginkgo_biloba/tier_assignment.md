## Tier Decision: Tier 2

### Decision Rationale

Ginkgo biloba is assigned **Tier 2** based on the following assessment:

#### Arguments FOR Tier 2

1. **Cochrane 2026 with 82 RCTs and GRADE** — The presence of a current Cochrane review with GRADE certainty assessments is one of the strongest possible evidence indicators. This alone would typically support Tier 2.

2. **Most extensive herbal nootropic evidence base** — With 82 RCTs in the Cochrane review alone, GB has more clinical trial data than any other herbal nootropic supplement. This breadth of evidence supports a higher tier assignment.

3. **Multiple evidence synthesis types** — The evidence base includes a Cochrane SR, network meta-analysis, large-scale meta-analysis, and an umbrella review. This multi-layered evidence structure is characteristic of Tier 2 supplements.

4. **Consistent benefits in dementia population** — Across multiple evidence syntheses, GB shows reproducible benefits for cognitive function in dementia populations.

5. **Strong VCI clinical niche** — Masserini 2025 ranks GB #1 among all interventions for VCI cognition (d = 0.83). This identifies a specific clinical population where GB has the strongest evidence.

6. **supplements.js already assigned Tier 2** — The primary data source (supplements.js) had already assigned Tier 2 based on the pre-existing evidence. The new evidence supports maintaining this assignment.

7. **Definitive safety profile** — Cochrane confirms AEs equivalent to placebo, which is the highest level of safety evidence attainable.

#### Arguments AGAINST Tier 1 Upgrade

1. **MCI "probably no effect" at moderate certainty** — The moderate-certainty null finding for MCI is actually stronger evidence than the low-certainty positive finding for dementia. This means GB does NOT benefit the population most likely to use it as a supplement (people with early cognitive decline).

2. **Dementia benefits at low certainty only** — GRADE low certainty means the true effect may be substantially different from the estimated effect. This level of certainty does not support Tier 1.

3. **VCI effect has very wide CI (0.00-1.67)** — The confidence interval for the #1 VCI ranking includes zero (no effect). This borderline statistical significance limits the strength of the VCI niche claim.

4. **AMSTAR 2 reveals poor underlying SR quality** — Pfuhlmann 2025 found that most systematic reviews of GB have poor methodological quality per AMSTAR 2 criteria. This undermines confidence in the overall evidence base.

5. **Mixed certainty across populations** — The evidence does not consistently support benefits. Dementia (low certainty positive), MCI (moderate certainty null), healthy adults (very low certainty) — this inconsistency is incompatible with Tier 1.

6. **Bacopa outperforms GB for working memory** — Tiemtad 2026 shows high-dose Bacopa achieves SUCRA 100% for working memory, outperforming all GB doses. GB is not the best-in-class herbal nootropic for the most consumer-relevant cognitive domain.

7. **No evidence in healthy adults** — The primary supplement consumer population (healthy adults seeking cognitive enhancement) has "very low" certainty evidence. Tier 1 would require strong evidence in this population.
