# Screening Decisions: Glucosamine (ID: 28)
## Pipeline Run: 2026-03-06

### Inclusion Criteria
1. Human subjects (clinical trials, meta-analyses, systematic reviews, guidelines)
2. Published in peer-reviewed journal with confirmed DOI and PMID
3. Relevant to glucosamine's claimed benefits, mechanisms, or safety
4. Provides quantitative outcome data (effect sizes, WMD, SMD, WOMAC scores)
5. Study type: RCT, meta-analysis, systematic review, IPD meta-analysis, NMA, or clinical guideline
6. Exception: Up to 2 in vitro mechanistic papers retained for mechanism section if providing key biological plausibility data

### Exclusion Criteria
1. Retracted papers (regardless of whether retraction was flagged in abstract)
2. Animal or veterinary studies
3. Non-English without English abstract
4. Conference abstracts only (no full publication)
5. Protocol/design papers without outcome data
6. Papers with unconfirmed or ambiguous PMID/DOI
7. Duplicate publications of the same dataset

### Conflict of Interest Flagging

**PMID 30575881 (Gregori 2018, JAMA NMA)**: Co-authors Giacovelli G and Minto C are affiliated with Rottapharm Biotech (manufacturer of patented crystalline glucosamine sulfate Dona/Viartril-S). Paper was included but all findings are reported with explicit COI flagging in the citation findings field. This COI is the most prominent industry conflict in the glucosamine literature and is central to the evidence interpretation.

**Funding bias as primary moderator**: Knapik 2018 (PMID 30566740) documents that funding source is the strongest predictor of trial outcome. This is explicitly documented in the tier assignment and synthesis notes as the central interpretive issue for the glucosamine evidence base.
