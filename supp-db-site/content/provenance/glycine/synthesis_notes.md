# Research Synthesis: Glycine
## Pipeline Run: 2026-04-19

### Synthesis Methodology
- Narrative synthesis with quantitative effect-size extraction where reported (Cohen's d, percent change vs placebo)
- Each `findings` field grounded in PubMed-served abstract text retrieved on 2026-04-19
- GlyNAC trials clearly partitioned: cited only for **glycine-specific mechanistic inference** (substrate-level GSH restoration) and as supportive aging-hallmark evidence — not as primary glycine-only efficacy

### Mechanism Synthesis

#### Glutathione synthesis substrate (rate-limiting)
- **Sources:** Sekhar 2021 J Nutr review (PMID 34587244); Kumar 2021 pilot RCT (PMID 33783984)
- **Convergent evidence:** Multiple Sekhar lab papers consistently identify cellular glycine + cysteine deficiency as rate-limiting for de novo GSH synthesis in older adults
- **Strength assignment:** Strong (mechanism); evidence transferred to glycine via the GlyNAC platform

#### NMDA co-agonist activity
- **Sources:** File 1999 Bioglycin RCT (PMID 10587285)
- **Strength assignment:** Strong (well-established receptor pharmacology); single human cognition RCT supports the functional translation

#### Collagen synthesis substrate
- **Sources:** Dunstan 2017 (PMID 28330481) — quantifies urinary glycine loss and benefit of AA supplementation
- **Convergent evidence:** Glycine is 33% of collagen by mass (Gly-X-Y triplet); endogenous synthesis is generally insufficient for collagen-turnover demand in adults

### Benefit Synthesis

#### Sleep Quality
- **Sources:** Bannai 2012 J Pharmacol Sci review (PMID 22293292); Bannai 2012 Front Neurol RCT (PMID 22529837); Uneyama 2017 review (PMID 27872968)
- **Effect sizes:** 3 g pre-bed glycine reduced VAS daytime fatigue and improved psychomotor vigilance in 25%-sleep-restricted volunteers; subjective sleep quality improved in insomnia-tendency subjects across the Bannai program
- **Mechanism:** Core body temperature reduction via cutaneous vasodilation + SCN AVP/VIP modulation
- **Confidence in claim:** Moderate (small replicated trials from one group)

#### Memory Enhancement
- **Sources:** File 1999 (PMID 10587285)
- **Effect sizes:** Significant improvement in episodic memory retrieval in young (mean 20.7 yr) and middle-aged men (mean 58.9 yr); significant sustained-attention improvement in middle-aged
- **Confidence in claim:** Preliminary (single trial)

#### Combination supplement sleep
- **Sources:** Langan-Evans 2023 MSSE (PMID 36094342)
- **Effect sizes:** -24 ± 25 min sleep onset latency (p=0.002); +22 ± 32 min total sleep time (p=0.01); +2.4 ± 3.9% sleep efficiency (p=0.03)
- **Confidence in claim:** Preliminary for glycine specifically (combination supplement)

#### Longevity (via GlyNAC precursor strategy)
- **Sources:** Kumar 2023 J Gerontol A RCT (PMID 35975308); Kumar 2021 pilot (PMID 33783984); Kumar 2022 mouse lifespan (PMID 35268089); Kumar 2020 HIV (PMID 33007928); Kumar 2023 mouse brain (PMID 37237908)
- **Effect sizes:** 16-week GlyNAC vs alanine placebo in older adults: corrected GSH deficiency, lowered oxidative stress, improved mitochondrial fuel oxidation, reduced inflammation, improved gait speed, muscle strength and 6-minute walk; +24% mouse lifespan
- **Caveat:** GlyNAC is the fixed glycine + NAC combination; glycine-only causality cannot be isolated from these trials. Citations are positioned as substrate-level mechanistic inference with explicit caveat language in each evidence card.
- **Confidence in claim:** Moderate for the GlyNAC strategy; lower confidence specifically for pure glycine

### Safety Synthesis
- **Sources:** Bannai 2012 review (PMID 22293292); Kumar 2023 GlyNAC RCT (PMID 35975308) safety arm
- **Risk-level determination:** Low — decades of safe dietary and pharmacological use; no clinically significant adverse events at trial-validated doses (3 g pre-bed; ~7 g/day in GlyNAC); high doses (>45 g/day) can cause GI upset

### Dosage Synthesis
- **Sleep:** 3 g pre-bed (Bannai canonical) — most validated single-supplement dose
- **Glutathione/longevity (precursor strategy):** 0.1 g/kg/day glycine + 0.1 g/kg/day NAC in the GlyNAC trials (~7 g/day glycine for a 70 kg adult)
- **Collagen-adjunct:** 2–10 g/day with vitamin C (clinically reasonable but not formally trial-validated)

### Cross-Study Patterns
- **Consistent findings:** Sleep effect at 3 g pre-bed; aging-hallmark improvements via the GlyNAC precursor strategy; glycine biochemistry across multiple roles
- **Contradictory findings:** None identified; GlyNAC results reproduce across Sekhar lab studies
- **Dose-response:** Not formally characterised in pure-glycine trials
- **Population-specific effects:** Stronger evidence in older adults (cognitive decline; aging hallmarks) and sleep-restricted volunteers; younger healthy adults show smaller effect sizes

### Limitations of This Synthesis
1. Pure-glycine RCT base is small (2 trials); cognition trial is 26 years old
2. Sleep RCTs use subjective measures rather than polysomnography
3. GlyNAC evidence cannot isolate glycine-only contribution
4. Single-group dominance in the GlyNAC literature (Sekhar lab)
