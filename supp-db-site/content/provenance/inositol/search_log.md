## Exclusion Summary

### Major Exclusion Categories
1. **Animal-only studies without human translation** (~340 hits excluded): Excluded all rodent/in vitro studies unless specifically included for mechanistic context (Vucenik 1995 animal study retained in benefits[] with note)
2. **Case reports and small n<5 series** (~89 hits excluded): Insufficient for clinical evidence; no systematic methodology
3. **Non-peer-reviewed sources** (~120 hits excluded): Conference abstracts, grey literature, manufacturer reports
4. **Duplicate/superseded publications** (~45 hits excluded): Earlier publications superseded by included meta-analyses
5. **Off-topic (food composition studies, phytic acid hydrolysis, industrial inositol)** (~90 hits excluded): Not relevant to supplementation evidence
6. **Non-English publications without available translation** (~31 hits excluded): Cannot assess quality

### Key Inclusion Decisions
- Meta-analyses given priority over individual RCTs (Unfer 2017, Zeng 2018, Taylor 2004, Brown 2018)
- Foundational mechanism papers retained despite age (Berridge 1989, Majerus 1986) — landmark citations defining the field
- Vucenik 1995 retained in benefits[] with explicit animal-model note — mechanistically relevant but does not support human clinical claims
- Clements 1980 retained with year-uncertainty note — only available pharmacokinetics citation; DOI resolves to 1990 publication despite year field

## Literature Landscape Assessment

### Research Maturity
Mature research field with publication history spanning 1986–2019 in the included citations. Core signaling mechanisms established in 1986–1989 (Majerus/Berridge era). Clinical research accelerated 2000–2019 with multiple meta-analyses. No major negative trials or replication failures identified.

### Tier 2 Evidence Basis
Inositol meets Tier 2 criteria based on four published meta-analyses across distinct benefit domains:
1. PCOS management: Unfer 2017 (ERMPS, n>400 pooled) + Zeng & Yang 2018 (Endocrine Connections)
2. Gestational diabetes: Brown 2018 Cochrane

This distinguishes Inositol from supplements with only individual RCTs (Tier 3) and warrants Tier 2 assignment.
