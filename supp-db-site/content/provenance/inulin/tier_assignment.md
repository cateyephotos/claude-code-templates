## Why Tier 2 (Not Tier 1)

**Tier 1 requires:** Multiple systematic reviews/meta-analyses + large RCTs (n>200) + consistent replication with large effect sizes (e.g., Cohen's d > 0.8 across primary outcomes).

**Inulin did not meet Tier 1 because:**

1. **Modest effect sizes for key outcomes:**
   - Weight: -0.97 kg (32 RCTs) — clinically meaningful but small absolute effect
   - LDL: -0.14 mmol/L (38 RCTs) — statistically significant but modest
   - Fasting glucose in T2DM: -0.60 mmol/L — moderate, but largely in diabetic populations only

2. **GRADE certainty is low-to-moderate for most outcomes:**
   - Significant heterogeneity (I² = 60–80% for most outcomes)
   - Multiple meta-analyses note high risk of bias in industry-funded studies
   - Optimal dose and form (FOS vs long-chain inulin) not yet standardized

3. **No dominant large single RCT:**
   - No RCT with n>500 for a single primary outcome exists
   - Tier 1 supplements (e.g., omega-3, vitamin D, creatine) have multiple n>1000 trials

4. **Cognitive/neurological effects emerging but not established:**
   - Medawar 2024 (PMID 37793780) brain/mood RCT is a single study; insufficient for Tier 1 claim

**Why Not Tier 3:**
- Five meta-analyses with ≥30 RCTs each clearly surpass Tier 3 thresholds
- Multiple independent research groups replicate core effects
- Dose-response established across outcomes
- Safety profile well-characterized
