# Evidence Evaluation: Iron
## Pipeline Run: 2026-03-04

### Evaluation Framework
- **Primary:** GRADE (Grading of Recommendations Assessment, Development and Evaluation)
- **Bias assessment:** AMSTAR-2 for systematic reviews, Cochrane Risk of Bias 2.0 for included RCTs
- **Quality scoring:** Modified composite scoring

### Per-Study Evaluation

#### Study: caballero_apaza_2026_umbrella — "Effect of iron supplements on cognitive development in children: an umbrella review"
- **Study type:** Umbrella review (17 systematic reviews)
- **GRADE domains:**
  - Risk of bias: Low — AMSTAR-2 and ROBIS applied to included SRs; 3 reviews rated high confidence
  - Inconsistency: Some concerns — benefits in anemic children consistent, but non-deficient populations show negligible effects
  - Indirectness: Not serious — directly addresses iron supplementation and cognitive development
  - Imprecision: Not serious — large evidence base across 17 SRs
  - Publication bias: Low concern — comprehensive search with grey literature
- **Overall GRADE certainty:** Moderate (downgraded for inconsistency across deficient vs non-deficient)
- **Assigned evidence level:** Level 1 (umbrella review = highest evidence)
- **Quality score contribution:** 9/10

#### Study: fiani_2025_nonanemic — "Psychiatric and cognitive outcomes of iron supplementation in non-anemic individuals"
- **Study type:** Systematic review & meta-analysis (18 studies, 12 RCTs, n=1408)
- **GRADE domains:**
  - Risk of bias: Some concerns — mixed RCT and pre-post designs pooled separately
  - Inconsistency: Not serious — consistent improvement across domains in RCTs
  - Indirectness: Not serious — directly addresses non-anemic iron-deficient populations
  - Imprecision: Some concerns — moderate sample sizes per individual outcome
  - Publication bias: Low concern — systematic search of PubMed and Scopus
- **Overall GRADE certainty:** Moderate
- **Assigned evidence level:** Level 1 (meta-analysis of RCTs)
- **Quality score contribution:** 9/10

#### Study: pengelly_2024_athletes — "Iron deficiency and sports performance in female athletes"
- **Study type:** Systematic review (23 studies, 669 athletes)
- **GRADE domains:**
  - Risk of bias: Some concerns — moderate quality per Downs and Black (77%), small group sizes in most studies (n≤20)
  - Inconsistency: Not serious — consistent direction of effect for endurance
  - Indirectness: Not serious — directly addresses athletes with iron deficiency
  - Imprecision: Serious — many studies underpowered (group sizes ≤20)
  - Publication bias: Some concerns — limited to English-language databases
- **Overall GRADE certainty:** Low-Moderate (downgraded for imprecision and small samples)
- **Assigned evidence level:** Level 2 (systematic review without formal meta-analysis)
- **Quality score contribution:** 7/10

#### Study: dabir_2025_leukemia — "Iron supplementation during pregnancy and childhood leukemia risk"
- **Study type:** Meta-analysis (9 case-control studies, n=4,281)
- **GRADE domains:**
  - Risk of bias: Some concerns — case-control design inherent limitations
  - Inconsistency: Some concerns — I²=63.2% (moderate heterogeneity)
  - Indirectness: Not serious — directly addresses safety concern
  - Imprecision: Not serious — adequate sample with narrow CI
  - Publication bias: Assessed via Egger's and Begg's tests
- **Overall GRADE certainty:** Moderate
- **Assigned evidence level:** Level 2 (meta-analysis of observational studies)
- **Quality score contribution:** 8/10

#### Study: xue_2025_perioperative — "IV iron for perioperative anemia: network meta-analysis"
- **Study type:** Network meta-analysis (34 RCTs, n=4,688)
- **GRADE domains:**
  - Risk of bias: Low — all included studies are RCTs
  - Inconsistency: Not serious — no significant inconsistencies between direct and indirect evidence
  - Indirectness: Some concerns — IV iron is specialized clinical context, not general supplementation
  - Imprecision: Not serious — large total sample
  - Publication bias: Low concern — comprehensive search strategy
- **Overall GRADE certainty:** Moderate (downgraded for indirectness — IV iron vs oral)
- **Assigned evidence level:** Level 1 (network meta-analysis of RCTs)
- **Quality score contribution:** 8/10

### Aggregate Quality Metrics
- **Average study quality:** 8.2/10
- **Evidence level distribution:** Level 1: 3, Level 2: 2
- **Replication assessment:** Cognitive benefits replicated across umbrella review (17 SRs) and independent MA (Fiani 2025); endurance benefits consistent across 23 studies
- **Publication bias assessment:** Low concern across most studies; formal assessment conducted in meta-analyses
- **Funding source analysis:** Mix of independent academic research (majority) and institutional funding
- **Geographic diversity:** Peru, USA, Lebanon, Canada, Australia, Iran, China represented
