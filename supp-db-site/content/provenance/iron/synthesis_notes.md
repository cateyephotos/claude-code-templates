# Research Synthesis: Iron
## Pipeline Run: 2026-03-04

### Synthesis Methodology
- **Approach:** Narrative synthesis with quantitative effect size extraction from meta-analyses
- **Effect size metric:** Cohen's d (standardized mean difference), odds ratios (OR), and performance improvements (%) as reported in source meta-analyses
- **Confidence assessment:** GRADE certainty of evidence (applied per-study in evidence_evaluation.md)

### Benefit Synthesis

#### Benefit: Cognitive Development — "Iron supplementation improves cognitive performance in iron-deficient children"
- **Sources:** caballero_apaza_2026_umbrella (new), larson_2017_cognition (existing)
- **Effect sizes reported:**
  - Caballero-Apaza 2026 (umbrella review, 17 SRs): Small but meaningful improvements in intelligence, memory, and attention in anemic children
  - Larson 2017 (existing MA): Cognitive improvement in school-age children with iron supplementation
- **Pooled estimate:** The umbrella review provides the highest level of evidence confirmation. Benefits are targeted — anemic/deficient children show clear cognitive improvements; universal supplementation in non-deficient children NOT supported.
- **Replication status:** Well-replicated across 17 systematic reviews in umbrella
- **Clinical significance threshold:** Meaningful cognitive gains in deficient children per AMSTAR-2 high-confidence reviews
- **Confidence in claim:** High for deficient children, Low for non-deficient children
- **Key synthesis decision:** Maintained cognitive benefit claim but strengthened with umbrella-level evidence. Added nuance that benefits are deficiency-dependent in children.

#### Benefit: Psychiatric/Cognitive in Non-Anemic — "Iron supplementation improves psychiatric and cognitive outcomes in non-anemic iron-deficient individuals" (NEW DOMAIN)
- **Sources:** fiani_2025_nonanemic (new — this is a NEW benefit domain)
- **Effect sizes reported:**
  - Fiani 2025 (12 RCTs, n=1408): anxiety d=0.34, fatigue d=0.34, physical well-being d=0.42, cognitive intelligence d=0.46, short-term memory d=0.53
  - Pre-post studies: depression d=0.93, fatigue d=1.01, psychiatric symptoms d=1.13
- **Pooled estimate:** Consistent small-to-moderate effects across multiple psychiatric and cognitive domains in RCTs. Pre-post studies show larger effects but with inherent methodological limitations.
- **Replication status:** 12 RCTs from independent research groups
- **Clinical significance threshold:** d≥0.3 considered clinically meaningful for psychiatric/cognitive outcomes
- **Confidence in claim:** Moderate-High (large MA with consistent effects, though supplementation effects absent when excluding ID participants)
- **Key synthesis decision:** Added as entirely new benefit domain. This is a GAME-CHANGER — demonstrates benefits BEFORE anemia develops. Benefits are still deficiency-dependent (absent without ID) but extend the target population beyond anemia to iron-deficient non-anemic (IDNA).

#### Benefit: Sports Performance — "Iron supplementation improves endurance performance in iron-deficient female athletes"
- **Sources:** pengelly_2024_athletes (new), existing exercise-related evidence
- **Effect sizes reported:**
  - Pengelly 2024 (23 studies, 669 athletes): endurance performance improved 2-20% with 100mg/day for up to 56 days; maximal aerobic capacity improved 6-15%
  - Iron deficiency prevalence: up to 60% of female athletes
- **Pooled estimate:** Consistent endurance benefits across 23 studies in high-level female athletes. Magnitude of effect (2-20%) is practically significant for competitive sport.
- **Replication status:** Replicated across 23 studies, 16 sports
- **Clinical significance threshold:** 2% endurance improvement considered meaningful for competitive athletes
- **Confidence in claim:** Moderate (systematic review without formal meta-analytic pooling, small group sizes)
- **Key synthesis decision:** Strengthens exercise performance domain substantially. Added female athletes as priority population.

### Safety Synthesis

#### Safety Aspect: Pregnancy iron and childhood leukemia risk
- **Sources:** dabir_2025_leukemia (new)
- **Adverse event assessment:** No significant association between pregnancy iron supplementation and childhood leukemia (OR=1.01, 95% CI 0.84-1.21). No AML risk (OR=1.01) or ALL risk (OR=1.00).
- **Risk level determination:** Low — comprehensive meta-analysis of 9 studies (n=4,281) provides reassurance
- **Key synthesis decision:** Added as critical safety evidence. Addresses a common patient/clinician concern about pregnancy supplementation safety. This null finding STRENGTHENS the safety profile.

#### Safety Aspect: Perioperative IV iron safety
- **Sources:** xue_2025_perioperative (new)
- **Assessment:** All IV iron preparations demonstrated acceptable safety profiles in perioperative setting (34 RCTs, n=4,688). No significant differences in adverse events between preparations.
- **Risk level determination:** Low for IV iron in clinical settings under supervision

### Dosage Synthesis

#### Dosage: Athletic performance dose
- **Sources:** pengelly_2024_athletes (new)
- **Optimal dosage determination:** ~100mg/day of elemental iron for up to 56 days via oral supplementation for endurance benefits in iron-deficient female athletes
- **Duration evidence:** Endurance benefits within 56 days oral; maximal aerobic capacity improvements with 36-126 days at 16-100mg/day
- **Key synthesis decision:** Added athlete-specific dosage guidance complementing existing general and pregnancy dosage.

### Cross-Study Patterns
- **Consistent findings across all studies:**
  - Iron supplementation benefits are deficiency-dependent across ALL domains (cognitive, psychiatric, athletic)
  - Larger effect sizes in more severely deficient individuals
  - Benefits extend beyond anemia to iron-deficient non-anemic (IDNA) populations
  - Safety well-established at recommended doses including during pregnancy
- **Contradictory findings:**
  - Universal cognitive supplementation in non-deficient children shows negligible effects (Caballero-Apaza 2026) vs targeted benefits in deficient children
- **Dose-response relationship:** Not formally established for cognitive outcomes; athletic performance shows dose-dependent improvement
- **Population-specific effects:** Female athletes, menstruating women, children, and pregnant women are priority populations. Non-anemic iron-deficient individuals now recognized as benefiting from supplementation.

### Limitations of This Synthesis
1. Umbrella review (Caballero-Apaza 2026) confirms existing evidence rather than adding novel data
2. Fiani 2025 non-anemic MA includes both RCTs and pre-post studies (pooled separately)
3. Pengelly 2024 sports review lacks formal meta-analytic pooling and has small group sizes
4. Dabir 2025 pregnancy safety study based on case-control designs with moderate heterogeneity (I²=63.2%)
5. Xue 2025 perioperative NMA is IV iron — may not generalize to oral supplementation context
