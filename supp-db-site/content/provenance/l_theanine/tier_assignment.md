**Decision:** Tier 2 is MAINTAINED. While L-theanine now has multiple meta-analyses, the evidence does not yet meet the threshold for Tier 1 upgrade. See detailed justification below.

### Tier Maintenance Justification (Tier 2 Maintained)
The decision to maintain Tier 2 rather than upgrade to Tier 1 is based on four factors:

1. **Standalone effect sizes are small to moderate:** The most rigorous meta-analysis (Payne 2025, 50 RCTs) demonstrates that pure L-theanine effects are smaller than commonly perceived. L-theanine alone produces choice reaction time improvement of SMD=-0.35 and mood improvement of SMD=0.26. These are small effects. The larger effects (choice RT SMD=-0.48, attention switching SMD=0.33) require caffeine co-administration. Tier 1 supplements typically demonstrate consistent moderate-to-large standalone effects across multiple domains.

2. **Mixed cognitive results undermine broad cognitive enhancement claims:** Matyus 2025 found that only recognition visual reaction time improved significantly (MD=-15.20ms), while simple reaction time and Stroop test performance were non-significant. This domain-specificity, combined with the small total sample (5 RCTs, 148 adults), indicates that L-theanine's cognitive enhancement profile is narrow and not yet robustly established. Tier 1 requires consistent strong findings across the primary benefit domain.

3. **Psychiatric evidence lacks quantitative synthesis:** Moshfeghinia 2024 is a qualitative systematic review without meta-analytic pooling. While the positive findings for schizophrenia, anxiety disorders, and ADHD are encouraging, the absence of pooled effect sizes means this evidence cannot be quantitatively compared to other domains. A systematic review without meta-analysis represents a lower level of evidence synthesis than required for Tier 1 domain establishment.

4. **L-theanine+caffeine synergy complicates attribution:** Much of the strongest evidence (Payne 2025) is for the L-theanine+caffeine combination rather than L-theanine alone. When the combination outperforms the standalone ingredient, it raises the question of whether L-theanine or caffeine (or their interaction) is the primary driver of benefit. Tier 1 supplements should demonstrate clear independent efficacy without requiring combination ingredients to achieve their strongest effects.

**What would trigger a Tier 1 upgrade:** A future meta-analysis demonstrating moderate-to-large standalone L-theanine effects (SMD>0.5) across 2+ independent outcome domains with quantitative pooling, OR a large (N>500) meta-analysis of L-theanine for anxiety/stress with robust standalone effect sizes, OR a quantitative meta-analysis of L-theanine in psychiatric populations with pooled effect estimates.

### Quality Score Change Narrative
The increase from 84 to 87 (+3 points) is driven by three positive factors partially offset by a consistency reduction:

1. **RCT and MA volume (+2 each, +4 total):** The addition of three meta-analyses and one systematic review substantially expands the quantitative evidence base. Payne 2025 (50 RCTs) is an exceptionally large meta-analysis for a single amino acid supplement. Bulman 2025 (19 articles, N=897) provides the definitive sleep evidence summary. These additions improve both the RCT volume and MA presence scores.

2. **Replication breadth (+1):** The geographic and institutional diversity of the new evidence strengthens replication confidence. Moshfeghinia 2024 spans 6 countries, and the 4 included papers come from independent research groups.

3. **Consistency reduction (-1):** The Matyus 2025 mixed cognitive results and Payne 2025 revelation that standalone effects are smaller than combination effects reduce the consistency of the positive evidence narrative. This is an honest reflection of the data -- L-theanine's benefits are more domain-specific and more modest (when isolated from caffeine) than previously assumed.

4. **Recency correction (-11*):** The previous quality score of 84 may have included an inflated recency component that exceeded the maximum possible points. The corrected score reflects the actual 0-10 scale for recency.

### Research Maturity: Developing-Mature
**Justification:** L-theanine has a substantial evidence base with 50+ RCTs cataloged in Payne 2025, spanning multiple decades of research. The 2024-2025 evidence cycle demonstrates a maturing research program: (1) the first sleep-dedicated meta-analysis published in a top-tier sleep journal (Bulman 2025); (2) the largest comprehensive meta-analysis to date with methodological innovations separating standalone from combination effects (Payne 2025); (3) expansion into psychiatric clinical populations (Moshfeghinia 2024). However, the evidence has not yet reached full maturity because (1) the cognitive evidence remains domain-specific and mixed (Matyus 2025), (2) standalone L-theanine effect sizes are generally small, and (3) the psychiatric domain lacks quantitative meta-analytic pooling. Research maturity is rated as "Developing-Mature" reflecting substantial volume but incomplete domain consistency.

### Evidence Gaps Identified
1. **Standalone anxiolytic effect size not yet quantified by dedicated MA:** While Payne 2025 reports mood SMD=0.26 for L-theanine alone, no dedicated anxiety-specific meta-analysis exists for standalone L-theanine supplementation. The Moshfeghinia 2024 SR covers anxiety disorders but without pooled effect sizes
2. **Long-term safety and efficacy data (>3 months) is sparse:** Most L-theanine RCTs are acute or short-term (single dose to 8 weeks). Chronic supplementation effects are understudied
3. **Dose-response relationship not yet established by meta-analysis:** Matyus 2025 includes a dose-dependent subgroup analysis but with only 5 RCTs. A comprehensive dose-response meta-analysis is needed
4. **Optimal dosing for different benefit domains unclear:** Sleep effects may require different dosing than cognitive effects, but no head-to-head dose comparison meta-analysis exists
5. **Interaction with psychiatric medications (SSRIs, antipsychotics, stimulants) requires systematic evaluation:** The Moshfeghinia 2024 findings suggest adjunctive use but interaction pharmacology is not well-characterized
6. **Bioavailability and form differences (free-form vs. bound in tea matrix) not meta-analytically assessed:** Supplemental L-theanine may have different pharmacokinetics than tea-derived L-theanine
7. **Pediatric and adolescent evidence is minimal:** The ADHD findings in Moshfeghinia 2024 include some pediatric data but this is not well-separated from adult evidence
