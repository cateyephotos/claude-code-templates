### Per-Study GRADE Evaluation

#### 1. Fernandez-Sanchez 2024 — Endurance Meta-Analysis
**PMID:** 38345351 | **DOI:** 10.1080/02640414.2024.2309434
**Journal:** Journal of Sports Sciences | **Study Type:** Systematic Review + Meta-Analysis (8 RCTs)
**Outcome domain:** Endurance exercise performance

| GRADE Domain | Rating | Justification |
|---|---|---|
| Risk of Bias | Serious | Underlying RCTs have small N (n=8–40); blinding quality variable; high risk in several constituent trials |
| Inconsistency | Not serious | Findings consistently null across all subgroups; ES = -0.05 (95% CI: -0.34 to 0.24) with homogeneous null |
| Indirectness | Not serious | Directly addresses endurance performance with consistent populations (physically active adults) |
| Imprecision | Very serious | Total N = 187 across 8 RCTs; 95% CI crosses zero; wide interval; seriously underpowered |
| Publication Bias | Undetected | Funnel plot not reported due to n<10 studies; likely exists given heterogeneous funding |

**GRADE Certainty for Endurance Outcomes: Very Low** (confirmed by authors; two serious downgrade factors)
**Quality Score: 7/10** — High methodological quality within meta-analysis conduct; score reflects execution quality, not certainty of findings

### Key Evidence Quality Observations

1. **Dominant GRADE certainty is "Low":** Most cognitive-stress RCTs earn Low GRADE certainty due to imprecision (n<50 in every individual RCT). No single RCT has n>100.

2. **Only meta-analysis has GRADE very-low — for the null finding:** The one formal GRADE assessment (Fernandez 2024) evaluated endurance and rated it Very Low. The cognitive-stress evidence, while suggestive, has not undergone formal GRADE pooling.

3. **All RCTs are acute-protocol dominant:** With the exception of Deijen 1999 (1-week), every RCT uses single acute doses. Chronic supplementation benefits have no controlled RCT evidence beyond 1 week.

4. **Harris 2024 is the largest RCT (n=40) — and it uses a combination supplement:** The inability to isolate tyrosine's contribution is a limitation for all combination studies.

5. **Two studies (Banderet 1989, Mahoney 2007) use n=8:** These studies have historical and mechanistic value only. They are insufficient for standalone efficacy claims but remain consistently replicated directionally in subsequent work.
