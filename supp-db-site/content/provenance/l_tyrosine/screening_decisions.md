# Screening Decisions: L-Tyrosine (ID: 33)
## Pipeline Run: 2026-03-06

### Inclusion Criteria
1. Human subjects (clinical trials, meta-analyses, systematic reviews, military/occupational studies)
2. Published in peer-reviewed journal with confirmed DOI and PMID
3. Relevant to L-tyrosine's claimed benefits (cognitive, stress, physical performance), mechanisms, or safety
4. Provides quantitative outcome data (effect sizes, cognitive task performance, stress biomarkers)
5. Study type: RCT, meta-analysis, systematic review, rapid evidence assessment, or comprehensive review
6. Exception: Up to 1 mechanistic review paper retained for mechanism section if providing key biological plausibility data
7. **Critical inclusion requirement:** Null findings (Fernandez 2024 endurance meta-analysis with GRADE very-low) are mandatory inclusions per Mode 2 protocol

### Exclusion Criteria
1. Retracted papers
2. Animal or preclinical studies
3. Non-supplementation populations (e.g., phenylketonuria patients requiring medical tyrosine management)
4. Non-English without English abstract
5. Conference abstracts only (no full publication)
6. Protocol/design papers without outcome data
7. Papers with unconfirmed or ambiguous PMID/DOI
8. Duplicate publications of the same dataset

### Key Screening Decisions and Notes

**Fernandez 2024 endurance meta-analysis as mandatory counter-evidence:**
The prior database version (2025-08-20) included this paper but without adequate weighting as counter-evidence. The meta-analysis (8 RCTs, n=187) explicitly assigned GRADE "very low" certainty to endurance outcomes and found null effect (ES = -0.05). This is the most rigorous pooling of endurance evidence and directly refutes physical performance claims. It is retained as KEY COUNTER-EVIDENCE.

**Small sample sizes throughout:**
The majority of included RCTs are n=8–40. This is not a screening violation — these are the actual trials in the L-tyrosine literature. The small N is a legitimate evidence quality limitation documented in the evidence evaluation. There are no large (n>100) pre-registered RCTs for L-tyrosine.

**Cognitive-stress vs. physical performance stratification:**
The L-tyrosine evidence base requires stratification by outcome domain: cognitive performance under stress (consistent positive signal) vs. physical/endurance performance (null signal). This is documented throughout the screening and synthesis.

**Banderet 1989 (PMID 2736402) inclusion rationale:**
This 1989 study (n=8, military cadets, cold/altitude stress) is seminal and specifically cited in all subsequent reviews as the foundational environmental stress evidence. Despite small N and age, it is retained as the historical mechanistic anchor.

**No conflict of interest (COI) flagging required:**
Unlike the chondroitin sulfate evidence base (Rottapharm co-author issue), the L-tyrosine literature does not have a dominant manufacturer-funded research program. Most funding comes from military research agencies (US Army Research Institute of Environmental Medicine), NIH, or academic institutions.
