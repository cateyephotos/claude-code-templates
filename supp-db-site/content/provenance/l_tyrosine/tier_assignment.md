### Decision Tree — Tier 2 vs. Tier 3 Discrimination

**Why NOT Tier 2:**
- Tier 2 requires: substantial research base + multiple RCTs with at least some n>100 + meta-analysis with consistent findings for same intervention
- No individual L-tyrosine RCT has n>100. This is the definitive Tier 2 threshold failure.
- The Fernandez 2024 meta-analysis (the only formal GRADE-assessed meta-analysis) rated evidence as GRADE "very low" for its evaluated outcome (endurance). Tier 2 requires at minimum "Low" certainty in aggregate.
- Cognitive-stress benefits, while directionally consistent, have not been formally pooled in a meta-analysis with GRADE assessment. They derive from reviews and small RCTs — insufficient for Tier 2.
- All individual cognitive RCTs are n=8–40. Even combined across studies, the N for any specific outcome is well below Tier 2 thresholds.
- Effect sizes for cognitive stress (d=0.3–0.7) are in range but unvalidated by large-N trials.

**Why NOT Tier 1:**
- Tier 1 requires: large consistent effects across independent large-N reviews and pre-registered RCTs
- No pre-registered large RCTs exist
- No high-certainty (Moderate or High GRADE) evidence for any L-tyrosine outcome
- Null for physical performance (the most studied domain in systematic meta-analysis) further prevents Tier 1

**Tier 3 is justified because:**
- Multiple independent research groups (US Army, Dutch labs, academic medical centers) produce directionally consistent cognitive-stress results
- A plausible, pharmacologically coherent mechanism (catecholamine depletion reversal) explains the findings
- Safety is well-established at studied doses
- The evidence base, while limited, is genuine human clinical data — not exclusively preclinical
- Tier 3 appropriately signals "developing evidence with conditional efficacy" which is an accurate characterization

### Research Maturity: Developing

**Justification:** The L-tyrosine evidence base has not progressed through the foundational → development → current/mature phases that characterize Tier 1–2 supplements:

- **Foundational period (1989–1999):** Banderet 1989 and Deijen 1999 established the environmental stress paradigm with military populations. These studies remain the most-cited references despite n=8 and n=21 respectively — indicating the field has not generated larger replacement trials.
- **Development period (2000–2015):** Lieberman 2015 and Nobre 2015 synthesized the accumulated small RCTs; Colzato lab expanded from military to cognitive laboratory outcomes. No large trials were conducted. The field remained dependent on small N studies throughout this period.
- **Current period (2015–2024):** Jongkees 2020 refined the baseline-dependency model; Fernandez 2024 provided the first formal GRADE meta-analysis (null for endurance); Harris 2024 is the most recent RCT. The 2024 evidence has clarified the endurance null and refined the stress-cognitive claim — but no large pre-registered trials were initiated.

"Developing" reflects that the central mechanistic hypothesis is established and directionally consistent, but the evidence base lacks the large-scale clinical trials needed to progress to "Mature." The field appears stable at small-N without evidence of large trial planning.

---

### Evidence Gaps Identified

1. **Absence of any large (n>100) pre-registered RCT:** This is the primary gap. The entire positive cognitive-stress evidence base rests on RCTs with n=8–40. A single pre-registered, NIH- or European-funded trial with n>200, measuring cognitive performance under standardized stress, would transform the evidence tier.

2. **No formal GRADE meta-analysis for cognitive-stress outcomes:** The only GRADE-rated meta-analysis (Fernandez 2024) evaluated endurance and found null. The cognitive-stress domain — where benefits are most consistent — has never undergone formal systematic meta-analytic pooling with GRADE certainty assessment.

3. **Chronic supplementation trials (>4 weeks):** Deijen 1999 (1 week) is the only chronic study. Whether cognitive-stress benefits persist, diminish (tolerance), or enhance with continued supplementation is unknown.

4. **Female participants:** The preponderance of evidence comes from male military cadets and male laboratory subjects. Female hormonal cycles affect dopaminergic function; tyrosine response in female athletes is not characterized.

5. **Biomarker-validated responder identification:** The Jongkees 2020 baseline-dependency finding suggests that pre-selection by dopaminergic function could substantially increase observed effect sizes. No RCT has used pharmacological biomarkers to pre-select likely responders.

6. **Isolated tyrosine vs. combination supplements:** Harris 2024 (the most recent and largest RCT) used L-theanine + L-tyrosine. A direct comparison of tyrosine alone vs. combination vs. placebo in the same trial would clarify the contribution of each component.

7. **Head-to-head comparison with caffeine or other cognitive supplements under stress:** L-tyrosine is frequently combined with or compared against caffeine anecdotally. A direct RCT comparison would clarify relative efficacy and combination strategies.

### Tier Assignment Final Determination

**Assigned Tier: 3 (maintained from prior entry)**

**Rationale summary:**
- Tier 2 prevented by: absence of any n>100 RCT (definitive threshold failure); GRADE very-low for the only formally assessed outcome (endurance); cognitive-stress benefits lack formal meta-analytic GRADE certification; all individual RCTs severely underpowered (n=8–40)
- Tier 1 prevented by: all of the above, plus null physical performance evidence
- Tier 3 is correct: Genuine human clinical evidence exists; consistent directional signal from multiple independent groups; mechanistically coherent; safety well-established; evidence is real but insufficient in scale for higher tier assignment
- **The supplement's efficacy is stress-conditional, not absent:** Tier 3 should not be interpreted as "doesn't work" — it means "works under specific conditions (acute stress/catecholamine depletion) with limited-scale supporting evidence"
