## Rationale

### Why NOT Tier 1

Tier 1 requires the strongest evidence base characterized by multiple meta-analyses, Cochrane reviews, or large-scale RCTs with definitive pooled estimates. Lion's Mane does not meet these criteria:

1. **No meta-analysis with pooled effect estimates exists.** The Menon 2025 SR provides a combined weighted mean but does not compute formal meta-analytic pooled estimates with confidence intervals, forest plots, or heterogeneity statistics.
2. **All RCTs are small (n<=41).** No large-scale RCT (n>100) has been conducted for H. erinaceus in any indication.
3. **No Cochrane review** exists or is registered for H. erinaceus supplementation.
4. **Tier 1 threshold** requires multiple independent meta-analyses or at minimum one Cochrane review confirming efficacy. Neither condition is met.

### Why NOT Tier 3

Tier 3 represents preliminary or emerging evidence with limited human clinical data. Lion's Mane has progressed beyond this threshold:

1. **4+ RCTs with consistent positive findings** across multiple independent research groups demonstrate reproducibility.
2. **1 PRISMA SR with PROSPERO registration** (Menon 2025, CRD42024571250) provides systematic evidence synthesis meeting international reporting standards.
3. **Strong preclinical evidence** supporting well-characterized mechanisms (NGF stimulation, BBB penetration, erinacine bioavailability at 24.39%) corroborate human findings.
4. **Multiple domains of benefit** (cognition, mood, gut health, neuroprotection) observed across studies, indicating broad therapeutic potential beyond a single endpoint.

### Tier 2 Justification Summary

Tier 2 represents moderate evidence with multiple RCTs showing consistent positive results and at least one systematic review, but lacking the definitive meta-analytic synthesis or large-scale confirmatory trials required for Tier 1. This accurately reflects the Lion's Mane evidence base.

## Path to Tier 1

The following developments would support a future upgrade to Tier 1:

1. **Publication of a formal meta-analysis** pooling the existing 5+ RCTs with forest plots, I-squared heterogeneity, and pooled effect estimates with 95% CIs
2. **A large-scale RCT** (n>100) confirming cognitive benefits in a well-defined population (MCI or healthy aging)
3. **A Cochrane review** or equivalent high-quality independent synthesis
4. **Long-term outcome data** (6+ months) demonstrating sustained benefits
