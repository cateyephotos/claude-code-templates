## Score Calibration Notes

- **Score 70** is appropriate and reflects AREDS2 quality uplift within Tier 2. This is the highest score in the current pipeline (IDs 41–45 range 66–70), appropriate given AREDS2 is the strongest single trial across all processed supplements
- **Primary strength:** AREDS2 (N=4,203, JAMA) in the benefits domain is extraordinary — this alone drives benefits domain raw score to 78, the highest in the pipeline. This is the definitive quality anchor
- **Primary drag:** Safety domain (66) reflects absence of a large dedicated safety RCT (equivalent to SYDNEY 2 in the ALA pipeline); mechanisms domain (66) driven by Sindhu 2010 (lower journal tier) and 1993 foundational paper
- **Mechanisms domain note:** Bone 1993 gets score 7 despite age because it is foundational field-establishing work; penalizing it would misrepresent the quality of the mechanistic evidence
- **Dosage domain:** Trieschmann 2007 (dose-response RCT, IOVS) is a high-quality dosage anchor; Bone 2003 PK study provides complementary pharmacokinetic context; Bowen 2002 provides formulation bioavailability guidance
- **Comparison to peers (IDs 41–45):**
  - Inositol (ID 41, score 68): Meta-analyses for PCOS/metabolic outcomes; similar profile
  - Selenium (ID 42, score 67): 2 meta-analyses, dosage domain has weaknesses
  - Choline (ID 43, score 66): Single RCT for cognitive benefits, no meta-analysis
  - Alpha-Lipoic Acid (ID 44, score 68): 3 meta-analyses but mechanisms are older reviews
  - **Lutein (ID 45, score 70)**: AREDS2 (N=4,203) is the differentiating factor — no other supplement in this pipeline has an anchor trial of this scale and journal quality
