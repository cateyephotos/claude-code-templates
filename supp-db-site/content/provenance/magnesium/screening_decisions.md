# Screening Decisions: Magnesium
## Pipeline Run: 2026-03-04

### Inclusion Criteria
1. Human subjects (clinical trials, observational studies, or meta-analyses thereof)
2. Published in peer-reviewed journal (2025 or later)
3. Available DOI and PMID
4. Relevant to magnesium's claimed benefits, mechanisms, or safety
5. Meta-analysis, systematic review, or large RCT preferred
6. Adds NEW information beyond existing 16 database citations
7. Oral supplementation route (excludes IV magnesium or pharmaceutical magnesium sulfate)
8. Magnesium-specific effects must be isolable (excludes multi-mineral or multi-supplement protocols)

### Exclusion Criteria
1. Conference abstracts only (no full publication)
2. Duplicate publications of same dataset
3. Narrative reviews without novel meta-analytic data or quantitative synthesis
4. Multi-supplement or multi-mineral protocols where magnesium effects cannot be isolated
5. Animal or in-vitro studies without human data
6. Clinical perioperative or emergency medicine settings (not relevant to supplement use)
7. Populations with specific rare diseases not generalizable to supplement consumers
8. Studies where magnesium showed no significant effects as a secondary endpoint
9. Observational studies without supplementation intervention
10. Comment/letter format without original data

### Screening Notes
The screening process was notable for a high exclusion rate due to multi-supplement protocols. Five of the 11 excluded papers involved magnesium combined with other minerals (PCOS multi-mineral), vitamins (Mg + Vitamin D, Ca/Mg/VitD), or supplements (soccer network MA), making it impossible to attribute observed effects to magnesium alone. This reflects a common challenge in magnesium research: because magnesium deficiency often co-occurs with other nutrient deficiencies, researchers frequently study multi-supplement interventions. For the provenance database, only studies where magnesium effects can be isolated are included to maintain attribution integrity.

Two papers were excluded for perioperative clinical contexts (dental surgery pain, cardiac surgery POAF) where magnesium is used as a pharmaceutical agent rather than a dietary supplement. These clinical applications, while valid, are outside the scope of the supplement database.
