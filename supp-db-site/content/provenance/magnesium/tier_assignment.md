### Tier Upgrade Justification (2 -> 1)
The previous Tier 2 assignment was based on a citation count of 16 and quality score of 78. The upgrade is driven by:

1. **Definitive cardiovascular evidence:** Argeros 2025 (38 RCTs, n=2709) published in Hypertension provides the most comprehensive blood pressure meta-analysis for any mineral supplement. SBP -2.81 mmHg and DBP -2.05 mmHg are clinically meaningful at the population level. Subgroup analyses showing SBP -7.68 mmHg in hypertensive patients on medication demonstrate clinically significant adjunctive benefit.

2. **New metabolic health domain:** Two independent meta-analyses (Maqrashi 2025 for T2DM with 23 RCTs; Basit 2026 for prediabetes with 5 RCTs) establish magnesium's role in glycemic control across the diabetes continuum. The FBG reduction (WMD -0.58 mmol/L in T2DM) and OGTT improvement (MD -0.99 mmol/L in prediabetes) provide consistent evidence across disease stages.

3. **Mechanistic depth:** Cepeda 2025 (28 studies) establishes the anti-inflammatory mechanism via CRP reduction, providing a biological bridge between the observed cardiovascular and metabolic benefits. CRP is elevated in both hypertension and metabolic syndrome, and magnesium's ability to reduce CRP connects these benefit domains mechanistically.

4. **Independent replication of inflammation finding:** Wang 2025 (8 RCTs, n=444) independently confirms CRP reduction in metabolic syndrome (SMD -0.327), replicating Cepeda 2025's finding in a different population with additional subgroup insights (sex differences, optimal duration).

5. **Multi-domain convergence:** Evidence now spans blood pressure (38 RCTs), sleep quality (pre-existing), glycemic control in T2DM (23 RCTs), glycemic control in prediabetes (5 RCTs), metabolic syndrome inflammation (8 RCTs), and anti-inflammatory mechanism (28 studies). This breadth of evidence across multiple physiological systems justifies the highest tier classification.

### Quality Score Change Narrative
The increase from 78 to 90 (+12 points) is driven by five factors:

1. **RCT volume (+4):** The Argeros 2025 blood pressure meta-analysis synthesizes 38 RCTs (n=2709), and the Maqrashi 2025 T2DM meta-analysis pools 23 RCTs (n=1345). Together, these add over 60 RCTs to the synthesized evidence base, substantially increasing the volume and quality of available evidence. Both meta-analyses use standard meta-analytic methodology with appropriate subgroup analyses and heterogeneity assessments.

2. **Meta-analysis completeness (+4):** Prior to this update, the magnesium database had meta-analytic support for blood pressure and sleep quality but lacked dedicated meta-analyses for metabolic health and inflammation. The five new papers close these gaps: every major claimed benefit (blood pressure, sleep, glycemic control) and mechanism (anti-inflammatory) now has at least one dedicated meta-analysis. The meta-analysis coverage score reaches maximum (20/20) reflecting comprehensive synthetic evidence across all domains.

3. **Sample sizes (+2):** The blood pressure meta-analysis includes 2709 participants across 38 RCTs, making it the largest pooled sample for any magnesium supplementation outcome. The T2DM meta-analysis adds 1345 participants. These large pooled samples reduce imprecision and increase confidence in the effect estimates.

4. **Replication (+1):** The CRP anti-inflammatory finding was independently confirmed by two separate meta-analyses from different research groups (Cepeda 2025 from Colombia, Wang 2025 from China), demonstrating cross-cultural replication. The five new papers originate from diverse geographic regions (US, Middle East, Pakistan, Colombia, China), expanding international representation.

5. **Consistency (+1):** Blood pressure effects are consistent in direction and magnitude across all 38 RCTs in the Argeros meta-analysis. Glycemic control improvements are consistent across both T2DM and prediabetes populations, suggesting a mechanistic effect on insulin sensitivity that spans the diabetes continuum. The only inconsistency is the inconclusive oxidative stress marker findings in Cepeda 2025, which do not undermine the established benefit domains.

### Research Maturity: Mature (upgraded from Moderate)
**Justification:** Magnesium is one of the most extensively studied dietary minerals, with research spanning over 40 years across cardiovascular, metabolic, neurological, and musculoskeletal domains. The 2025-2026 evidence cycle added 5 meta-analyses covering blood pressure (38 RCTs), T2DM glycemic control (23 RCTs), prediabetes glycemic control (5 RCTs), oxidative stress/inflammation (28 studies), and metabolic syndrome inflammation (8 RCTs). The breadth of evidence across multiple physiological systems, combined with the availability of subgroup analyses (age, sex, disease status, duration, dose) and mechanistic data (CRP reduction pathway), indicates a research base that is both deep and actively synthesized. The upgrade to "Mature" reflects the exceptional breadth of evidence domains and the availability of clinical subgroup data for personalized supplementation guidance.

### Evidence Gaps Identified
1. **Sleep quality meta-analyses:** No new sleep-specific magnesium meta-analyses were identified since 2025. The sleep domain relies on pre-existing evidence and may require dedicated search in the next update cycle.
2. **Mental health (depression/anxiety):** No qualifying meta-analyses for magnesium and depression or anxiety were identified. Individual studies suggest potential benefits but systematic synthesis is lacking.
3. **Form-specific comparisons:** Limited evidence comparing magnesium glycinate, citrate, oxide, threonate, and other forms at the meta-analytic level. Wang 2025 noted tablet/capsule superiority but did not compare specific magnesium salts.
4. **Long-term cardiovascular outcomes:** Blood pressure reduction is well-documented, but long-term outcomes (stroke, MI, cardiovascular mortality) have not been synthesized in a magnesium-specific meta-analysis.
5. **Dose-response analysis:** While subgroup analyses suggest dose-dependent effects, no dedicated dose-response meta-analysis exists for magnesium supplementation across benefit domains.
6. **Muscle function and exercise performance:** Pre-existing evidence exists but no new meta-analyses were identified in this update cycle.
