## Individual Paper Assessments

### Paper 1: Tang et al. 2025 (PMID: 40662882)

**Citation:** Tang et al. (2025). Melatonin for ICU patients: Systematic review and meta-analysis. *Critical Care Medicine*.

| GRADE Domain            | Rating      | Notes                                                    |
|-------------------------|-------------|----------------------------------------------------------|
| Risk of Bias            | Low         | GRADE-assessed by authors; 32 RCTs with adequate randomization |
| Inconsistency           | Low         | Consistent direction of effects across outcomes          |
| Indirectness            | Low         | Directly measures melatonin supplementation in target population |
| Imprecision             | Low         | Large sample (n=3,895), narrow confidence intervals      |
| Publication Bias        | Low         | Comprehensive search, funnel plot analysis reported      |
| **Overall GRADE**       | **High**    | Largest melatonin MA to date                             |

**Key Effect Sizes:**
- Delirium incidence: RR = 0.72 (28% relative risk reduction)
- ICU length of stay: MD = -0.57 days (clinically modest but statistically significant)
- Sleep quality: SMD = 0.54 (moderate effect)

**Strengths:**
- Largest melatonin meta-analysis published (32 RCTs)
- GRADE framework applied by original authors
- Multiple clinically relevant outcomes assessed
- Critical care setting adds a new clinical domain

**Limitations:**
- ICU population may not generalize to community settings
- Heterogeneity in melatonin dosing across included studies
- ICU LOS reduction is modest (0.57 days)

**Evidence Contribution Score:** 9/10

### Paper 2: Terao & Kodama 2024 (PMID: 38461503)

**Citation:** Terao & Kodama (2024). Network meta-analysis of interventions for Alzheimer's disease. *Journal of Alzheimer's Disease*.

| GRADE Domain            | Rating      | Notes                                                    |
|-------------------------|-------------|----------------------------------------------------------|
| Risk of Bias            | Moderate    | NMA relies on indirect comparisons; variable trial quality |
| Inconsistency           | Moderate    | Some heterogeneity across AD intervention trials         |
| Indirectness            | Moderate    | NMA compares across different intervention classes       |
| Imprecision             | Low         | Large pooled sample (n=4,599)                            |
| Publication Bias        | Moderate    | Potential bias toward positive pharmaceutical trials     |
| **Overall GRADE**       | **Moderate**| NMA design introduces inherent limitations              |

**Key Findings:**
- Melatonin ranked superior to anti-amyloid antibodies on certain outcome measures
- Supports neuroprotective role of melatonin in AD

**Strengths:**
- Network meta-analysis enables cross-class comparisons
- Large sample size across included studies
- Published in a domain-specific high-impact journal
- Provides comparative effectiveness data against pharmaceuticals

**Limitations:**
- Indirect comparisons carry more uncertainty than direct head-to-head trials
- Melatonin dose and duration varied across included RCTs
- Outcome measures may not capture all clinically relevant AD domains
- NMA transitivity assumption may not fully hold across diverse interventions

**Evidence Contribution Score:** 7/10

### Paper 3: Yousef et al. 2025 (PMID: 40588412)

**Citation:** Yousef et al. (2025). Melatonin for sleep disturbances in Parkinson's disease: Meta-analysis. *Journal of Sleep Research*.

| GRADE Domain            | Rating      | Notes                                                    |
|-------------------------|-------------|----------------------------------------------------------|
| Risk of Bias            | Moderate    | Variable quality across 8 included studies               |
| Inconsistency           | Low         | Consistent benefit direction across studies              |
| Indirectness            | Low         | Directly assesses melatonin for PD sleep                 |
| Imprecision             | Moderate    | Smaller sample (n=409); wider confidence intervals       |
| Publication Bias        | Moderate    | Limited number of studies may mask publication bias      |
| **Overall GRADE**       | **Moderate**| Clinically meaningful but moderate certainty             |

**Key Effect Sizes:**
- PSQI total score: MD = -1.75 (clinically meaningful; MCID for PSQI is typically 1.5-3.0)
- Sleep onset latency: MD = -9.74 minutes (practically significant)

**Strengths:**
- Addresses a specific clinical population with high unmet need
- PSQI improvement exceeds minimal clinically important difference
- Sleep onset latency reduction is practically meaningful for patients
- Parkinson's disease sleep disturbances are poorly served by existing interventions

**Limitations:**
- Relatively small total sample size (n=409)
- Only 8 studies included, limiting statistical power for subgroup analyses
- PD-specific population limits generalizability
- Potential confounding from concurrent PD medications

**Evidence Contribution Score:** 6/10
