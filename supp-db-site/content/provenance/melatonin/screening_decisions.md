## Inclusion Criteria

1. Systematic review (SR), meta-analysis (MA), or network meta-analysis (NMA) published 2024-2026
2. Melatonin supplementation as a primary intervention (not minor component)
3. Human subjects
4. Clinically relevant outcomes with quantitative effect sizes reported
5. Not already present in the existing enhanced citations file
6. Minimum study pool of 5+ RCTs or substantial sample size (n > 300)

## Exclusion Criteria

1. Melatonin is a minor or incidental component of the intervention
2. Melatonin receptor agonists (ramelteon, tasimelteon) rather than exogenous melatonin
3. Already captured in existing citation database
4. Underpowered analyses (fewer than 5 studies or n < 300)
5. Overlapping population/analysis with a larger included study
6. Published before 2024

## Included Papers (n = 3)

### 1. Tang et al. 2025
- **PMID:** 40662882
- **Title:** Melatonin for ICU patients: Systematic review and meta-analysis
- **Journal:** Critical Care Medicine
- **Study Design:** Systematic review + meta-analysis
- **Studies Included:** 32 RCTs
- **Total Participants:** n = 3,895
- **Key Findings:**
  - Delirium incidence: RR = 0.72 (significant reduction)
  - ICU length of stay: MD = -0.57 days
  - Sleep quality: SMD = 0.54 (moderate improvement)
- **Quality Assessment:** GRADE-assessed
- **Inclusion Rationale:** Largest melatonin meta-analysis to date (32 RCTs), GRADE-assessed, adds ICU/critical care domain to evidence base. Provides strong evidence for sleep quality and delirium prevention in hospitalized populations.

### 2. Terao & Kodama 2024
- **PMID:** 38461503
- **Title:** Network meta-analysis of interventions for Alzheimer's disease
- **Journal:** Journal of Alzheimer's Disease
- **Study Design:** Network meta-analysis
- **Studies Included:** 10 RCTs
- **Total Participants:** n = 4,599
- **Key Findings:**
  - Melatonin ranked superior to anti-amyloid antibodies for certain outcomes
- **Inclusion Rationale:** NMA design allows indirect comparisons across AD interventions. Finding that melatonin ranks favorably against pharmaceutical interventions is clinically significant. Adds neurodegenerative disease domain.

### 3. Yousef et al. 2025
- **PMID:** 40588412
- **Title:** Melatonin for sleep disturbances in Parkinson's disease: Meta-analysis
- **Journal:** Journal of Sleep Research
- **Study Design:** Meta-analysis
- **Studies Included:** 8 studies
- **Total Participants:** n = 409
- **Key Findings:**
  - PSQI improvement: MD = -1.75 (clinically meaningful)
  - Sleep onset latency: MD = -9.74 minutes
- **Inclusion Rationale:** Addresses melatonin in Parkinson's disease-specific sleep disturbances, a population with high unmet need. Adds PD-specific evidence to the neurology domain.
