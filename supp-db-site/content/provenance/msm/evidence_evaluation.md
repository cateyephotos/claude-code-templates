### Aggregate Quality Metrics

- **Average study quality:** 5.1/10 (range 3–9)
- **Evidence level distribution:**
  - Level 1 (NMA/SR): 2 papers (Crawford 2019, Chen 2025)
  - Level 2 (RCT): 5 papers (Debbi 2011, Toguchi 2023, Kim 2006, Usha 2004, Withee 2017)
  - Level 3 (review): 1 paper (Butawan 2017)
- **Replication assessment:** Clinical signal replicated across OA RCTs (Debbi 2011, Toguchi 2023, Kim 2006, Usha 2004). However, the GRADE synthesis by independent evaluators (Crawford 2019, Chen 2025) consistently assigns Low certainty and recommends against or finds insufficient evidence.
- **Publication bias assessment:** Small positive RCT pool is vulnerable to publication bias. Most trials are underpowered. The GRADE SR (Crawford 2019) and NMA (Chen 2025) — both independent — conclude against MSM.
- **Funding source analysis:** 2 independent government-funded (Crawford 2019 — US military; Chen 2025 — no industry affiliation documented). 5 trials with unclear/uncharacterized funding.
- **Geographic diversity:** USA (Crawford, Withee, Kim), Israel (Debbi), Japan (Toguchi), India (Usha), China (Chen NMA)

---

### Summary Judgment

The MSM evidence base is **small and internally inconsistent**. Four OA RCTs with positive results are individually underpowered (n<100 in 3 of 4) and do not meet MCID thresholds. Two independent synthesis sources — one government GRADE systematic review (Crawford 2019) and one 2025 NMA (Chen 2025) — both assign Low certainty to MSM. The overall pattern mirrors glucosamine: positive signals in small trials, null/against finding from independent synthesis. Tier 3 is appropriate.

**Evidence Level Designation: Tier 3** — Preliminary clinical signal with contested independent synthesis.
