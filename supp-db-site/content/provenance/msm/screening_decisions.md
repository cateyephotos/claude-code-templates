# Screening Decisions: MSM (Methylsulfonylmethane, ID: 29)
## Pipeline Run: 2026-03-06

---

### Inclusion Criteria

1. Human subjects (clinical trials, meta-analyses, systematic reviews, guidelines)
2. Published in peer-reviewed journal with confirmed DOI and PMID
3. Relevant to MSM's claimed benefits (joint pain, anti-inflammatory, exercise recovery), mechanisms, or safety
4. Provides quantitative outcome data (effect sizes, SMD, WOMAC scores, AE rates) OR is a systematic review/guideline documenting evidence synthesis
5. Study type: RCT, meta-analysis, NMA, systematic review, review with safety data, or in vitro mechanistic (max 2)
6. Exception: Up to 2 in vitro mechanistic papers retained for mechanism section if providing key biological plausibility data

### Exclusion Criteria

1. Retracted papers (regardless of content)
2. Animal or veterinary studies
3. Non-English without English abstract
4. Conference abstracts only (no full publication)
5. Protocol/design papers without outcome data
6. Papers with unconfirmed or non-existent PMID/DOI
7. Preprints without peer review
8. Duplicate publications of the same dataset

### Conflict of Interest Flagging

**No systematic industry COI identified** comparable to the glucosamine Rottapharm situation. The two OA RCTs with positive results (Debbi 2011, Toguchi 2023) do not have documented industry co-author affiliations in PubMed metadata. However, funding source for these trials is not clearly characterized as independent. The key independent evidence (Crawford 2019, US military GRADE SR; Chen 2025 NMA) both conclude against or assign Low certainty to MSM — mirroring the funding bias pattern documented for glucosamine.

**Fabrication documentation:**
The prior version of `29_enhanced.js` contained a citation attributed to "Kim 2006" with PMID 21708034. This PMID belongs unambiguously to Debbi EM et al. 2011 "Efficacy of methylsulfonylmethane supplementation on osteoarthritis of the knee." This represents a citation fabrication error (incorrect PMID assigned to a non-existent paper). The pipeline excludes the fabricated "Kim 2006" citation. The correct Kim LS et al. paper (PMID 23836598) is retained with its proper attribution.
