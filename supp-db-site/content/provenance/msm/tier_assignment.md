5. **Are findings consistent across reviews?** → **NO** — 4 small RCTs show positive effects; 1 independent GRADE SR (Crawford 2019) recommends AGAINST; 1 NMA (Chen 2025) assigns GRADE Low

**Decision: Tier 3** — Has some RCTs but none exceeds n>100; independent synthesis recommends against or assigns Low certainty. Cannot achieve Tier 2 (would require ≥1 RCT n>100 AND consistent independent meta-analytic support).

### Decision Tree — Tier 2 vs Tier 3 Discrimination

**Why NOT Tier 2:**
- Tier 2 requires multiple RCTs with moderate samples (typically n>100 each) and at least one supportive systematic review
- No MSM RCT exceeds n=88 (Toguchi 2023); the largest RCT is below the n>100 threshold
- Crawford 2019 (the highest-quality independent SR) recommends AGAINST MSM — not supportive
- Chen 2025 NMA assigns GRADE Low certainty — consistent with the against/insufficient finding
- The evidence base does not support "at least one systematic review with positive findings from independent sources"

**Why NOT Tier 4:**
- Tier 4 would imply mostly animal/in vitro or traditional use with no RCTs
- 4 positive OA RCTs (even if small) establish that MSM has been tested in humans
- Mechanisms (NF-κB inhibition, sulfur donation) are biochemically established in vitro
- Safety data is reasonably well characterized (Butawan 2017 review, GRAS status)

**Tier 3 is justified because:**
- Multiple RCTs exist (5 total) — more than Tier 4 threshold
- All RCTs are small (n<100) and individually underpowered — below Tier 2 threshold
- Independent synthesis (Crawford 2019, Chen 2025) recommends against or assigns Low certainty — inconsistent with Tier 2
- Mechanism evidence establishes biological plausibility (Tier 3 characteristic)

### Research Maturity: Emerging

**Justification:** MSM has been clinically tested since 2004 but remains in an "Emerging" state due to consistently small trial sizes, absence of large independent RCTs, and contradictory independent synthesis. Unlike glucosamine (which is "Developing" — with 30+ RCTs and multiple NMAs), MSM has only 5 clinical trials and 2 independent syntheses. The field has not invested in powered independent trials to confirm or refute the preliminary signal. "Emerging" reflects real human clinical data (distinguishing from "Theoretical") with insufficient scale for definitive conclusions.

---

### Evidence Gaps Identified

1. **No n>100 independent RCT**: The fundamental evidence gap. A well-powered (n>200) blinded RCT with pre-specified MCID thresholds and independent funding is required to confirm or refute the OA pain signal.

2. **No funding-stratified meta-analysis**: Unlike glucosamine (Knapik 2018), no published analysis stratifies MSM results by funding source. This is needed to test whether the positive RCT signal reflects industry bias.

3. **MCID threshold application**: Crawford 2019 applied GRADE but did not publish pooled effect sizes with MCID comparisons. Chen 2025 NMA assigned Low certainty without detailed MCID analysis. A Liu-2017-style MCID analysis for MSM is absent.

4. **Form and bioavailability**: OptiMSM (distillation-purified) vs. non-branded MSM — no head-to-head comparison. Bioavailability data is limited to single-dose PK studies.

5. **Exercise recovery evidence base**: Only 1 RCT (Withee 2017) tests the exercise recovery claim. This is insufficient for even a Tier 3 characterization with confidence.

6. **Mechanism-to-outcome pathway**: In vitro NF-κB inhibition is established; whether oral MSM achieves sufficient plasma concentrations for in vivo NF-κB inhibition is unknown. No biomarker-based clinical mechanistic study exists.
