### Per-Study Evaluation

#### Study: braidy_2020_nad_aging — NAD+ biology and aging review (Antioxid Redox Signal 2020)
- **Study type:** Narrative review
- **GRADE domains:**
  - Risk of bias: Low — high-quality journal, no obvious industry bias
  - Inconsistency: Not applicable (review)
  - Indirectness: Moderate — primarily summarizes preclinical and mechanistic data
  - Imprecision: Not applicable
  - Publication bias: Low concern
- **Overall GRADE certainty:** N/A (mechanism review)
- **Assigned evidence level:** Level 5 (Expert review)
- **Notes:** Primary value is mechanistic contextualization; NAMPT pathway and age-related NAD+ decline well-documented

#### Study: trammell_2016_nr_pk — NR pharmacokinetics in humans (Nat Commun 2016)
- **Study type:** Randomized pharmacokinetic trial (n=12)
- **GRADE domains:**
  - Risk of bias: Low — double-blind crossover design; adequately reported
  - Inconsistency: Not serious — pharmacokinetic outcomes are objective biomarker measurements
  - Indirectness: Not serious — directly measures NR metabolism and NAD+ elevation in blood
  - Imprecision: Moderate — small sample (n=12); adequate for pharmacokinetic modeling
  - Publication bias: Low — Nat Commun; high visibility
- **Overall GRADE certainty:** Moderate
- **Assigned evidence level:** Level 2
- **Notes:** Foundational PK study; provided the first rigorous NAD+ dose-response quantification; n=12 is standard for PK crossover designs

#### Study: christen_2026_microbiota — Microbiota-mediated NR metabolism (Nature Metabolism 2026)
- **Study type:** Mechanistic human study with antibiotic suppression design
- **GRADE domains:**
  - Risk of bias: Low — elegant mechanistic design using antibiotic suppression to demonstrate microbiota dependence
  - Inconsistency: Low — confirmatory of pathway already inferred from metabolite data
  - Indirectness: Not serious — directly addresses oral NR/NMN mechanism question
  - Imprecision: Moderate — small n (pilot-scale mechanistic study)
  - Publication bias: Low — Nature Metabolism, highest-tier metabolism journal
- **Overall GRADE certainty:** Moderate
- **Assigned evidence level:** Level 2
- **Notes:** Paradigm-shifting finding; demonstrates oral NR/NMN converted by gut bacteria to nicotinic acid, which then enters Preiss-Handler pathway; explains why direct Preiss-Handler-independent pathways are not the primary mechanism at oral doses

#### Study: prokopidis_2025_meta_muscle — Meta-analysis of NR/NMN on muscle (JCSM 2025)
- **Study type:** Systematic review + meta-analysis
- **GRADE domains:**
  - Risk of bias: Low — pre-registered; PRISMA reporting; GRADE applied
  - Inconsistency: Moderate — I² varies by outcome; grip strength I²=38% (acceptable); 6MWT I²=0% (homogeneous null)
  - Indirectness: Not serious — primary outcomes are functional muscle performance in adults
  - Imprecision: Moderate — pooled n=236; smaller than optimal for definitive conclusion
  - Publication bias: Not detected — Egger's test not significant
- **Overall GRADE certainty:** Moderate
- **Assigned evidence level:** Level 1
- **Notes:** Most important muscle evidence; grip strength SMD +0.31 (95% CI 0.01–0.61; p=0.04) is borderline significant and modest; 6-min walk test null; overall interpretation is modest positive effect on grip strength only

#### Study: yoshino_2021_insulin — NMN and insulin sensitivity in prediabetic women (Science 2021)
- **Study type:** Randomized double-blind placebo-controlled trial (n=25)
- **GRADE domains:**
  - Risk of bias: Low — double-blind, parallel-group, placebo-controlled; published in Science with rigorous review
  - Inconsistency: Not serious — primary endpoint (euglycemic-hyperinsulinemic clamp) is objective gold standard
  - Indirectness: Serious — limited to prediabetic postmenopausal women; not generalizable to healthy general adults
  - Imprecision: Moderate — n=25 per arm; adequate for the mechanistic endpoint (insulin clamp is very sensitive)
  - Publication bias: Low — Science journal; prestige publication
- **Overall GRADE certainty:** Moderate (despite n, gold-standard measurement and clean design justify Moderate)
- **Assigned evidence level:** Level 2
- **Notes:** Most important metabolic evidence; key limitation is population specificity (prediabetic women); complements Chen 2024 null result for non-diabetic populations suggesting population-specific benefit

#### Study: chen_2024_meta_metabolic — Meta-analysis NR/NMN metabolic (Nutr Metab Cardiovasc Dis 2024)
- **Study type:** Systematic review + meta-analysis
- **GRADE domains:**
  - Risk of bias: Low — PRISMA compliant; pre-registered
  - Inconsistency: Low — consistent null result across included studies (I² low)
  - Indirectness: Not serious — general adult population metabolic outcomes
  - Imprecision: Low — adequate pooled sample for null conclusion
  - Publication bias: Low — null results are increasingly published; no funnel plot asymmetry
- **Overall GRADE certainty:** Moderate
- **Assigned evidence level:** Level 1
- **Notes:** Critical null result; glucose and HbA1c unchanged in non-diabetic populations; paired with Yoshino 2021 this indicates metabolic benefits are population-specific (prediabetic/high-risk groups only)

#### Study: martens_2018_cardiovascular — NR cardiovascular RCT (Nat Commun 2018)
- **Study type:** Randomized double-blind placebo-controlled crossover trial (n=30)
- **GRADE domains:**
  - Risk of bias: Low — crossover design; pre-registered; Nat Commun peer review
  - Inconsistency: Not serious — cardiovascular biomarker outcomes are objective
  - Indirectness: Moderate — primary finding in hypertensive subgroup; not pre-specified subgroup analysis
  - Imprecision: Moderate — n=30 crossover; hypertensive subgroup even smaller
  - Publication bias: Low — Nat Commun; high visibility
- **Overall GRADE certainty:** Moderate
- **Assigned evidence level:** Level 2
- **Notes:** SBP reduction −5.8 mmHg in hypertensive subgroup is clinically meaningful; post-hoc subgroup analysis is a limitation; NAD+ elevation confirmed at 300 mg/day dosing

#### Study: fukamizu_2022_safety — NMN safety RCT up to 1250 mg/day (Nutrients 2022)
- **Study type:** Randomized double-blind dose-escalation safety trial (n=31)
- **GRADE domains:**
  - Risk of bias: Low — systematic safety outcome assessment; pre-specified AE monitoring
  - Inconsistency: Not serious — safety outcomes are objective (lab values, clinical monitoring)
  - Indirectness: Not serious — directly addresses NMN safety at supra-clinical doses
  - Imprecision: Moderate — n=31 per arm; adequate for common AE detection; insufficient for rare AE detection
  - Publication bias: Low — safety studies are regularly published regardless of outcome
- **Overall GRADE certainty:** Moderate
- **Assigned evidence level:** Level 2
- **Notes:** Highest NMN dose safety data available; 1250 mg/day well-tolerated; no hepatotoxicity signal; complements Conze 2019 NR safety data

#### Study: conze_2019_nr_safety — NR safety comprehensive study (Sci Rep 2019)
- **Study type:** Combined pre-clinical + clinical safety evaluation
- **GRADE domains:**
  - Risk of bias: Low — standardized safety evaluation protocol
  - Inconsistency: Not serious
  - Indirectness: Not serious — clinical doses directly assessed
  - Imprecision: Moderate — clinical safety arm relatively small
  - Publication bias: Low
- **Overall GRADE certainty:** Moderate
- **Assigned evidence level:** Level 2
- **Notes:** NOAEL 300 mg/kg/day from pre-clinical; human doses of 100–300 mg/day demonstrated safe; well-cited reference for NR safety

#### Studies: yi_2022, vreones_2022, morifuji_2024, katayoshi_2023, pencina_2023_jcem, pencina_2023_jgerontol, orr_2023, alghamdi_2024
- **General assessment:** All are RCTs (Level 2); small sample sizes (n=13–48); clean single-ingredient designs; various outcome domains (physical performance, cardiovascular, cognitive); GRADE certainty Low to Moderate due to sample size limitations
- **Consistent pattern:** All confirm NAD+ elevation with NR/NMN supplementation; benefit outcomes are domain-specific and population-dependent
