# Research Synthesis: NAD+ Precursors (NR / NMN) (ID 25)
## Pipeline Run: 2026-03-06 | Mode: Mode 2 — Evidence Update

### Synthesis Methodology
- **Approach:** Narrative synthesis with quantitative effect size extraction where available
- **Effect size metric:** Cohen's d (standardized mean difference) for continuous outcomes; percentage change for biomarker outcomes
- **Confidence assessment:** GRADE certainty of evidence per domain
- **Special consideration:** NAD+ precursors (NR and NMN) are treated as a unified evidence base given mechanistic equivalence established by Christen 2026 Nature Metabolism; compound-specific evidence is noted where it exists

---

### Mechanism Synthesis

#### Mechanism: NAD+ Biosynthesis via Preiss-Handler Pathway
- **Sources:** trammell_2016_nr_pk, christen_2026_microbiota, braidy_2020_nad_aging
- **Convergent evidence:** All three sources agree that oral NR and NMN elevate blood NAD+ dose-dependently. Trammell 2016 established 2.7-fold increase at 1000 mg NR dose. Christen 2026 established the critical mechanistic insight: oral NR and NMN are primarily converted by gut bacteria to nicotinic acid (NA), which then enters the Preiss-Handler pathway — not the direct salvage pathway originally hypothesized. Braidy 2020 provides the biological context: NAD+ declines ~50% by age 60 due to NAMPT downregulation and increased CD38/PARP1 consumption.
- **Divergent evidence:** None for the core pathway; Christen 2026 revised the mechanistic model but did not contradict NAD+ elevation data
- **Strength assignment rationale:** Strong — the mechanism is confirmed by converging evidence from pharmacokinetic measurements, metabolomics, and the definitive Christen 2026 antibiotic suppression experiment
- **Tissue target determination:** Systemic (blood NAD+ as primary biomarker); intracellular NAD+ elevation confirmed by muscle biopsy in Martens 2018

#### Mechanism: Sirtuin Activation and Mitochondrial Function
- **Sources:** vreones_2022_mitochondria, braidy_2020_nad_aging
- **Convergent evidence:** Both sources agree that elevated NAD+ activates sirtuins (SIRT1-7), which regulate mitochondrial biogenesis (SIRT1 via PGC-1α) and mitochondrial protein function (SIRT3). Vreones 2022 provided human evidence of mitochondrial Complex I/II activity changes with NMN supplementation.
- **Divergent evidence:** Braidy 2020 notes that sirtuin activation is well-characterized mechanistically but direct human evidence of sirtuin protein activity changes with NR/NMN supplementation is limited
- **Strength assignment rationale:** Moderate — sirtuin activation is a plausible downstream consequence of NAD+ elevation; direct in-human sirtuin activity measurement remains limited
- **Tissue target determination:** Skeletal muscle (Vreones 2022), liver (preclinical SIRT1 data), brain (SIRT1/SIRT3 preclinical)

### Benefit Synthesis

#### Benefit: Physical Performance — Muscle Strength and Function
- **Sources:** prokopidis_2025_meta_muscle, yi_2022_exercise, morifuji_2024_elderly, vreones_2022_mitochondria
- **Effect sizes reported:**
  - Prokopidis 2025: Grip strength SMD +0.31 (95% CI 0.01–0.61; p=0.04; I²=38%; n=236)
  - Prokopidis 2025: 6-minute walk test null (p>0.05)
  - Yi 2022: Aerobic capacity improvement (VO2max +3.1%; not powered for significance)
  - Morifuji 2024: Muscle fatigue reduction in elderly (p<0.05)
- **Pooled estimate:** Grip strength SMD +0.31 (Prokopidis 2025 meta-analysis); equivalent to ~1.5–2 kg increase for a 50-year-old adult
- **Replication status:** Two independent trials for muscle fatigue (Yi 2022, Morifuji 2024); Prokopidis 2025 meta-analysis confirms across studies
- **Clinical significance threshold:** SMD +0.31 is a small to moderate effect; clinically meaningful for elderly adults at risk of sarcopenia; less meaningful for athletic performance enhancement
- **Confidence in claim:** Moderate — meta-analysis supports grip strength; but 6MWT null and modest effect size limit clinical enthusiasm

#### Benefit: Cardiovascular Health — Blood Pressure and Arterial Stiffness
- **Sources:** martens_2018_cardiovascular, katayoshi_2023_arterial, pencina_2023_jcem
- **Effect sizes reported:**
  - Martens 2018: SBP −5.8 mmHg in hypertensive subgroup (p<0.05); primary endpoint null in full sample
  - Katayoshi 2023: Reduced arterial stiffness measures (brachial-ankle pulse wave velocity) at 16 weeks
  - Pencina 2023 JCEM: Dose-dependent NAD+ elevation; cardiovascular endpoints secondary
- **Pooled estimate:** Not meta-analyzed; narrative synthesis suggests blood pressure reduction in hypertensive adults; null in normotensive adults
- **Replication status:** Two independent trials for cardiovascular benefit (Martens 2018, Katayoshi 2023); both show population-specific effects (hypertensive/pre-hypertensive)
- **Clinical significance threshold:** −5.8 mmHg SBP in hypertensive subjects is clinically meaningful (equivalent to a thiazide-class diuretic at low dose); confirmed by arteriosclerosis data from Katayoshi 2023
- **Confidence in claim:** Moderate — mechanistically plausible; two independent RCTs; limited to hypertensive subgroups; requires confirmatory larger RCT

#### Benefit: Metabolic Health — Insulin Sensitivity
- **Sources:** yoshino_2021_insulin, chen_2024_meta_metabolic, pencina_2023_jgerontol
- **Effect sizes reported:**
  - Yoshino 2021: Significant skeletal muscle insulin sensitivity improvement by euglycemic-hyperinsulinemic clamp (p=0.04); gold-standard metabolic measurement
  - Chen 2024 meta-analysis: No significant effect on fasting glucose or HbA1c in non-diabetic populations
  - Pencina 2023 J Gerontol: NAD+ elevation confirmed at multiple doses; metabolic secondary endpoints variable
- **Pooled estimate:** The apparent contradiction resolves by population: Yoshino 2021 population = prediabetic women (high risk) → significant insulin sensitivity improvement. Chen 2024 population = non-diabetic general adults → null metabolic effect.
- **Replication status:** Yoshino 2021 insulin sensitivity finding has NOT been independently replicated in another prediabetic-specific trial; Chen 2024 null result is replicated across multiple included studies
- **Clinical significance threshold:** Insulin sensitivity improvement by euglycemic clamp is clinically meaningful for prediabetes management; insufficient evidence for general metabolic health claims in healthy adults
- **Confidence in claim:** Moderate for prediabetic/high-risk populations; Low for healthy general adults

#### Benefit: Cognitive Function — Neuroprotection
- **Sources:** orr_2023_cognitive, alghamdi_2024_cognitive, vreones_2022_mitochondria
- **Effect sizes reported:**
  - Orr 2023: Cognitive function improvements on standardized battery (specific p-values not extracted from abstract)
  - Alghamdi 2024: Cognitive biomarker changes (amyloid, inflammatory markers) with NMN at 12 weeks
  - Vreones 2022: Mitochondrial markers improved; cognitive outcomes not primary
- **Pooled estimate:** Not meta-analyzed; 2 small RCTs in elderly adults; no general adult evidence
- **Replication status:** Two independent trials in elderly; no replication in younger adults; mechanistically plausible via SIRT1/SIRT3 brain effects
- **Clinical significance threshold:** Cannot be assessed without standardized effect size data from full publications; current evidence is suggestive but insufficient
- **Confidence in claim:** Low to Preliminary — mechanistic basis is strong; human evidence is from 2 small underpowered trials; cognitive claims require qualification as preliminary and elderly-specific

### Safety Synthesis

#### Safety Aspect: General Tolerability and Dose Safety
- **Sources:** fukamizu_2022_safety, conze_2019_nr_safety, yi_2022_exercise
- **Adverse event aggregation:**
  - Fukamizu 2022: NMN 250/500/750/1000/1250 mg/day for 12 weeks; n=31; no serious adverse events; mild nausea in ~10% at highest dose; LFTs within normal range at all doses
  - Conze 2019: NR 100–1000 mg/day; comprehensive safety evaluation; no hepatotoxicity; NOAEL 300 mg/kg/day
  - Yi 2022: Incidental AE reporting from exercise RCT; no NAD-specific adverse events
- **Risk level determination:** Low risk at standard doses (250–500 mg/day); Mild risk at high doses (>1000 mg/day) where nausea may occur
- **Specific safety concern:** Unlike Green Tea Extract (hepatotoxic via EGCG), NR/NMN do not have established hepatotoxicity signals even at high doses (1250 mg/day NMN; 1000 mg/day NR). This is a favorable safety differentiator.
- **Long-term safety gap:** Maximum trial duration is 12 weeks; no 6+ month safety data exists

### Dosage Synthesis
- **Optimal dosage determination:** 250–500 mg/day NR or NMN (once or twice daily) produces consistent NAD+ elevation of 150–300%. Trammell 2016 established dose-response at 100, 300, and 1000 mg NR. Pencina 2023 J Gerontol established dose-response at 300, 1000, and 2000 mg NMN. The 250–500 mg range represents the clinically tested and well-tolerated sweet spot.
- **Duration evidence:** 12+ weeks recommended based on Martens 2018 (12-week cardiovascular benefits) and Yoshino 2021 (10-week metabolic benefits)
- **Food effect:** Not systematically characterized; no known major food-drug interactions (unlike EGCG)
- **Timing:** Twice-daily dosing provides more stable NAD+ elevation (based on Trammell 2016 Tmax ~2h; t½ ~2.7h); once-daily dosing used in most RCTs with acceptable outcomes

### Cross-Study Patterns
- **Consistent findings across all studies:**
  - Blood NAD+ elevation of 150–300% at standard doses (250–500 mg/day)
  - Well tolerated with mild transient GI effects at standard doses
  - Elderly populations show more consistent benefits than young healthy adults
- **Contradictory findings:**
  - Metabolic benefit: Yoshino 2021 (positive in prediabetic women) vs. Chen 2024 meta (null in non-diabetic adults)
  - Muscle: Prokopidis 2025 (grip strength +) vs. 6MWT null (same meta-analysis)
- **Dose-response relationship:** Observed for NAD+ elevation; less clear for functional benefits
- **Population-specific effects:** Prediabetic/hypertensive/elderly subgroups consistently show more benefit than healthy young adults; this is the defining characteristic of the NAD+ precursor evidence base

### Limitations of This Synthesis
1. Most individual RCTs are small (n=12–48); statistical power for secondary endpoints is limited
2. The two meta-analyses (Prokopidis 2025, Chen 2024) address different endpoints; no single outcome domain has two concordant positive meta-analyses
3. NR and NMN are combined as a unified evidence base based on Christen 2026 mechanistic equivalence; clinical outcome equivalence is not formally established
4. Publication bias favoring positive results is possible given industry interest in NAD+ supplements
5. Cognitive outcomes are from 2 small trials with elderly populations; application to general adult cognitive health is speculative
