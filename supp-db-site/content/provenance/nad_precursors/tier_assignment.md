**Step 3: Any RCT with n > 100?**
→ **NO** — The largest individual RCT is Prokopidis 2025 meta-analysis (pooled n=236), but no single primary RCT exceeds n=100. Individual RCTs range from n=12 (Trammell 2016 PK) to n=48 (Yi 2022). Yoshino 2021 Science is n=25 per arm. This is a critical limiting factor.
- The Prokopidis 2025 pooled meta-analysis functions as the equivalent of a moderately-powered trial for muscle endpoints.

**Step 4: Systematic review or meta-analysis exists?**
→ **YES** — Two meta-analyses from independent research groups:
- Prokopidis et al 2025 JCSM (muscle function; n=236 pooled)
- Chen et al 2024 Nutr Metab Cardiovasc Dis (metabolic outcomes; glucose/HbA1c)

**Step 5: Are findings consistent across reviews?**
→ **PARTIALLY** — The two meta-analyses address different outcome domains and have contradictory implications:
- Prokopidis 2025: Modest positive effect on grip strength (SMD +0.31; p=0.04); null for 6MWT
- Chen 2024: Null result for metabolic endpoints in non-diabetic populations
- Individual RCTs: Yoshino 2021 shows significant insulin sensitivity improvement, but only in prediabetic women (different population from Chen 2024's general adult meta-analysis)
- NAD+ elevation itself IS consistent across all RCTs at standard doses (150-300% increase)
- Benefit outcomes are population-specific: prediabetic/hypertensive subgroups show more benefit than healthy general adults

**Decision:** **TIER 2** — Two independent meta-analyses exist with partially consistent findings across different outcome domains. Multiple RCTs across 10+ independent research groups. However, three factors prevent Tier 1:
1. No single general-population RCT with n>200 for any benefit domain (Tier 1 threshold)
2. The two meta-analyses address different endpoints; no single outcome domain has two concordant meta-analyses
3. Population-specific benefit pattern means claims require qualification by study population
- Previous assignment of Tier 3 was a significant underestimate given the two available meta-analyses (Prokopidis 2025, Chen 2024) and the landmark Science trial (Yoshino 2021); Tier 2 is the correct classification.

### Research Maturity: Developing

**Justification:** NAD+ precursor research has a growing but still developing clinical evidence base:
1. No large single-compound RCT (n>200) for any benefit domain in a general adult population
2. Benefit pattern is population-specific (prediabetic, elderly, hypertensive subgroups); general healthy adult effects are modest or absent
3. Christen 2026 fundamentally revised the mechanistic understanding of how oral NR/NMN work (microbiota pathway) — this level of foundational revision in 2026 indicates the field is still developing
4. Cognitive and cardiovascular evidence is from small RCTs (n=22–40); both domains require confirmatory larger trials
5. Long-term safety data (>12 months) is absent; only 12-week maximum duration trials

Developing is distinct from Emerging (which would indicate <3 RCTs; no meta-analyses) and Mature (which would require consistent large-effect findings across multiple large RCTs in general populations).

### Evidence Gaps Identified

1. **No large general-population RCT**: The most important gap is the absence of a definitive pre-registered RCT (n>200) testing NR or NMN for metabolic, physical performance, or cardiovascular outcomes in healthy general adults. The current evidence base is heavily skewed toward specific subpopulations.

2. **NR vs. NMN clinical outcome equivalence**: Christen 2026 established mechanistic equivalence at the pathway level (both converted to NA→NAD+), but no head-to-head RCT comparing NR and NMN outcomes directly exists. Clinical outcome equivalence is assumed but not proven.

3. **Long-term safety registry**: Maximum trial duration is 12 weeks (Fukamizu 2022, Martens 2018). Long-term safety at 6–12+ months of supplementation has not been characterized in prospective trials.

4. **Cognitive benefit in non-elderly adults**: Both cognitive RCTs used elderly subjects (Orr 2023 n=22; Alghamdi 2024 n=28). NAD+ supplementation for cognitive health in mid-life adults (40–60 years) is uninvestigated.

5. **Responder subgroup identification**: Some RCTs show individual variation in NAD+ elevation response. Genetic predictors (NAMPT polymorphisms, NNMT activity) that might identify super-responders or non-responders have not been investigated in human trials.

6. **Oral bioavailability form comparison**: Standard capsule vs. liposomal NMN vs. sublingual NR vs. food-matrix-stabilized forms have not been compared in adequately powered human bioavailability trials. Commercial claims for superior bioavailability are unsupported.

### Confidence in Tier 2 Assignment: High

**Rationale**: Two independent meta-analyses across distinct outcome domains definitively place NAD+ precursors above Tier 3. The absence of large general-population RCTs and the population-specific benefit pattern clearly preclude Tier 1. The Tier 2 assignment is well-supported and would require either a large confirmatory RCT in a general population (upward to Tier 1) or failure to replicate meta-analysis positive findings (downward to Tier 3) to change.
