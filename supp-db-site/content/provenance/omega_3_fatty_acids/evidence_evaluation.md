### Per-Study Evaluation

#### Study: chen_2025_amd_meta — AMD Prevention Meta-Analysis
- **Citation:** Chen et al. 2025, Journal of Nutrition
- **PMID:** 41482231
- **DOI:** 10.1016/j.tjnut.2025.101289
- **Study type:** Systematic review and meta-analysis (18 observational studies)
- **Key finding:** OR = 0.80 for AMD risk with high omega-3 intake
- **GRADE domains:**
  - Risk of bias: Some concerns — observational studies inherently limited by confounding; cannot establish causation
  - Inconsistency: Not serious — consistent direction of effect across studies
  - Indirectness: Not serious — directly assesses dietary omega-3 intake and AMD risk
  - Imprecision: Not serious — 18 studies provide adequate pooled precision
  - Publication bias: Low concern — large number of studies with funnel plot assessment
- **Overall GRADE certainty:** Low (observational evidence starts at low, not upgraded)
- **Assigned evidence level:** Level 2
- **Quality score contribution:** 7/10
- **Notes:** Observational design limits causal inference. However, the consistency across 18 studies and biologically plausible mechanism (retinal DHA enrichment) support inclusion. Updates the eye health domain which previously lacked a dedicated meta-analysis.

#### Study: basirat_2025_metsyn_meta — Metabolic Syndrome Meta-Analysis
- **Citation:** Basirat & Merino-Torres 2025, Nutrients
- **PMID:** 41156531
- **DOI:** 10.3390/nu17203279
- **Study type:** Systematic review and meta-analysis (21 RCTs, ~1950 participants)
- **Key finding:** High-dose omega-3 reduces TG by -56.78 mg/dL in metabolic syndrome
- **GRADE domains:**
  - Risk of bias: Some concerns — variable RCT quality across 21 trials
  - Inconsistency: Serious — significant heterogeneity in dose-response subgroups
  - Indirectness: Not serious — directly addresses metabolic syndrome patients taking oral omega-3
  - Imprecision: Not serious — ~1950 participants across 21 RCTs
  - Publication bias: Not detected — assessed via funnel plot and Egger's test
- **Overall GRADE certainty:** Moderate
- **Assigned evidence level:** Level 1
- **Quality score contribution:** 8/10
- **Notes:** Large participant pool and dose-response analysis add substantial value. The -56.78 mg/dL TG reduction at high doses is clinically meaningful. Dose-response data fills a gap in existing evidence.

#### Study: dao_2025_pad_meta — PAD Meta-Analysis (NEGATIVE finding)
- **Citation:** Dao et al. 2025, Nutrition, Metabolism & Cardiovascular Diseases
- **PMID:** 40940198
- **DOI:** 10.1016/j.numecd.2025.104286
- **Study type:** Systematic review and meta-analysis (12 studies, n=759)
- **Key finding:** Omega-3 NOT effective for peripheral arterial disease symptoms
- **GRADE domains:**
  - Risk of bias: Some concerns — heterogeneous study designs included
  - Inconsistency: Not serious — consistently null findings across studies
  - Indirectness: Not serious — directly assesses omega-3 for PAD outcomes
  - Imprecision: Serious — only 759 participants across 12 studies, some studies underpowered
  - Publication bias: Possible concern — negative findings may be underreported in the broader literature
- **Overall GRADE certainty:** Low
- **Assigned evidence level:** Level 2
- **Quality score contribution:** 6/10
- **Notes:** Critical negative finding included for balanced evidence reporting. The consistent null result across 12 studies suggests a genuine lack of benefit for PAD specifically, even though omega-3 benefits other cardiovascular endpoints. This nuance is important for clinical decision-making and database integrity.

#### Study: zhou_2025_masld_meta — MASLD/Liver Health Meta-Analysis
- **Citation:** Zhou et al. 2025, Frontiers in Nutrition
- **PMID:** 39927279
- **DOI:** 10.3389/fnut.2025.1524830
- **Study type:** Systematic review and meta-analysis (7 RCTs, n=439)
- **Key finding:** Significant improvements in TG, AST, and HOMA-IR in MASLD patients
- **GRADE domains:**
  - Risk of bias: Some concerns — small number of RCTs, variable quality
  - Inconsistency: Not serious — consistent positive direction across metabolic markers
  - Indirectness: Not serious — directly assesses omega-3 supplementation in MASLD patients
  - Imprecision: Serious — only 439 participants across 7 RCTs
  - Publication bias: Possible concern — small study count limits assessment reliability
- **Overall GRADE certainty:** Low to Moderate
- **Assigned evidence level:** Level 1
- **Quality score contribution:** 7/10
- **Notes:** Opens a new benefit domain (liver health). While the sample size is modest, the biological mechanism is well-understood (omega-3 reduces hepatic de novo lipogenesis and inflammation). The MASLD population is increasingly relevant given rising prevalence. Marked as emerging evidence requiring confirmation from larger trials.

#### Study: rajati_2025_preeclampsia_meta — Preeclampsia Prevention Meta-Analysis
- **Citation:** Rajati et al. 2025, Clinical Nutrition ESPEN
- **PMID:** 39423927
- **DOI:** 10.1016/j.clnesp.2024.10.146
- **Study type:** Systematic review and meta-analysis (16 studies, n=16,237)
- **Key finding:** RR 0.63 for total preeclampsia, RR 0.45 for severe preeclampsia
- **GRADE domains:**
  - Risk of bias: Low — large study count with multiple well-designed RCTs
  - Inconsistency: Not serious — consistent protective effect across studies
  - Indirectness: Not serious — directly assesses omega-3 supplementation during pregnancy
  - Imprecision: Not serious — 16,237 participants provide strong statistical power
  - Publication bias: Low concern — large study pool with Egger's test assessment
- **Overall GRADE certainty:** Moderate to High
- **Assigned evidence level:** Level 1
- **Quality score contribution:** 9/10
- **Notes:** Strongest individual study in this update by sample size (n=16,237). The 37% relative risk reduction for total preeclampsia and 55% reduction for severe preeclampsia are both clinically significant and statistically robust. This substantially strengthens the safety/pregnancy evidence profile. The large sample size and consistent findings across diverse populations support high confidence.
