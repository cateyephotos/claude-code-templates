## Inclusion Criteria

Papers were included if they met ALL of the following:

1. **Population:** Humans (not animal or in vitro)
2. **Intervention:** Panax ginseng (P. ginseng C.A. Meyer) as a standalone supplement or clearly isolated intervention arm (not as a minor component of a multi-herb formula)
3. **Species:** P. ginseng specifically, or Panax species meta-analysis where P. ginseng is the dominant species analyzed. Excludes P. quinquefolius (American ginseng), P. notoginseng (Sanqi/Tienchi ginseng) unless pooled with P. ginseng.
4. **Outcome:** Cognitive function, cardiovascular risk markers, or other endpoints directly relevant to the supplement's primary benefit claims
5. **Design:** Systematic review, meta-analysis, network meta-analysis, umbrella review, or large RCT (n > 100)
6. **Recency:** Published 2024 or later
7. **Novelty:** Not already cited in the existing enhanced citations file

### 1. Zeng et al. 2024 — Cognitive Function SR+MA

| Field | Detail |

| **PMID** | 39474788 |
| **DOI** | 10.1002/ptr.8359 |
| **Title** | Systematic review and meta-analysis of ginseng effects on cognitive function |
| **Journal** | Phytotherapy Research |
| **Year** | 2024 |

| **Scale** | 15 RCTs, n = 671 |
| **Registration** | PROSPERO CRD42024514231 |
| **Key Finding** | Memory: SMD = 0.19 (95% CI 0.02-0.36, significant). High-dose: SMD = 0.33 (CI 0.04-0.61). No effect on overall cognition (p = 0.86), attention (p = 0.54), or executive function (p = 0.79). |
| **Decision** | **INCLUDED** |
| **Rationale** | First comprehensive ginseng-specific cognitive MA with domain-specific subgroup analysis. PROSPERO registered. Critical for establishing the evidence boundary for cognitive claims. |

### 2. Kim et al. 2025 — MMSE/ADAS-Cog SR+MA

| Field | Detail |
|---|---|

| **Title** | Effects of ginseng on MMSE and ADAS-Cog scores: A systematic review and meta-analysis |

| **Scale** | 8 RCTs |
| **Key Finding** | MMSE: MD = 0.68 (95% CI 0.03-1.32, p = 0.04, I-squared = 66%). ADAS-Cog: MD = -1.10 (95% CI -1.82 to -0.38, p = 0.003, I-squared = 0%). |

## Notes

- The high exclusion rate (85.7%) is driven primarily by species confusion in the Panax genus. Five of the 18 excluded papers involved P. notoginseng or P. quinquefolius rather than P. ginseng, reflecting the taxonomic ambiguity in the ginseng literature. This is a known issue in ginseng evidence synthesis — researchers must carefully distinguish between the three major commercial Panax species.
- The two superseded CVD papers (PMIDs 38877862, 39387709) were absorbed by the larger Jafari 2025 dose-response MA, which provides more comprehensive coverage of the same endpoints with greater statistical power.
- The absence of any large standalone RCTs (n > 100) in the Consensus search suggests that the ginseng clinical trial landscape is generating evidence primarily through meta-analytic synthesis of existing smaller trials rather than through new large-scale primary research.
- All 3 included papers provide quantitative effect estimates with confidence intervals, enabling precise claim calibration for the supplement database.
