## Search Limitations

1. **Species ambiguity:** The term "ginseng" in PubMed captures multiple Panax species (P. ginseng, P. quinquefolius, P. notoginseng). Species-level filtering was performed manually at the screening stage, not at the search stage, to avoid missing relevant cross-species meta-analyses.
2. **Language bias:** Searches were conducted in English only. A substantial proportion of Panax ginseng clinical trial literature is published in Chinese, Korean, and Japanese journals. English-abstract screening was performed for non-English publications.
3. **Route of administration bias:** PubMed indexes both oral supplementation studies and injectable ginseng preparations (particularly P. notoginseng saponin injections used in Chinese hospitals). These were excluded at screening but may have inflated the initial search results.
4. **Traditional medicine overlap:** Multiple search results involved ginseng as a component of traditional Chinese medicine (TCM) or Korean medicine multi-herb formulas. These were excluded as they do not provide evidence for standalone P. ginseng supplementation.
5. **Database coverage:** bioRxiv/medRxiv were not searched. Preprint activity for ginseng clinical evidence is minimal given the maturity of the field.
6. **Grey literature:** Conference abstracts, regulatory submissions (e.g., Korean FDA), and manufacturer-sponsored reports (e.g., Korea Ginseng Corporation) were not systematically searched.
