## Rationale for Tier 2 Assignment

### 1. Multiple RCTs with Consistent Positive Findings

| Study | Design | N | Duration | Population | Key Finding |
|-------|--------|---|----------|------------|-------------|
| Duan 2025 | RCT | 190 | 12 months | Elderly MCI | Memory improvement |
| Doma 2023 | RCT | 138 | — | Healthy adults | Working memory improvement |
| Kato-Kataoka 2010 | RCT | 78 | — | Elderly | Dose-dependent verbal recall improvement |
| Hirayama 2014 | RCT | 36 | — | Children with ADHD | ADHD symptom improvement |
| Baumeister 2008 | RCT | — | — | Adults | Stress/cognitive effects |

Five RCTs across four distinct populations (elderly MCI, healthy adults, children with ADHD, stressed adults) with consistent positive cognitive and stress-modulation findings. Total combined sample exceeds 440 participants. This exceeds Tier 3 criteria ("limited human studies").

### 2. Network Meta-Analysis Evidence (New)

Zhou et al. 2024 provides the first NMA systematically comparing PS against other interventions:

| Outcome | PS SUCRA Ranking | Position |
|---------|-----------------|----------|
| ADHD-RS Parent Attention | 0.39 | #1 of 12 drugs |
| ADHD-RS Parent Total | 0.34 | #1 of 12 drugs |
| CTRS Hyperactivity (PS+omega-3) | 0.26 | Top-ranked combination |

This PROSPERO-registered NMA (48 RCTs, n=3650) represents Level 1 evidence and strengthens the ADHD evidence domain from a single small RCT (Hirayama n=36) to NMA-supported positioning.

### 3. Strong Mechanistic Evidence

Multiple review papers confirm PS mechanisms:

- **Kim 2014:** Membrane function and neurotransmitter support
- **Ma 2022:** Anti-inflammatory neuroprotection mechanisms
- **Glade 2015:** Comprehensive PS mechanism review

Mechanistic evidence is well-established and consistent with observed clinical effects.

### 4. Well-Established Safety

| Study | N | Duration | Safety Finding |
|-------|---|----------|----------------|
| Vakhapova 2011 | 157 | 15 weeks | Excellent safety profile |
| Hellhammer 2014 | 75 | — | Safe + cortisol modulation |

No serious adverse events reported across multiple RCTs. Safety profile well-characterized.

### 5. Previous Tier 3 Designation Was Too Conservative

The enhanced citations file previously assigned Tier 3, which is appropriate for supplements with "limited human studies" or "preliminary evidence." With five RCTs (n=36 to n=190) across four populations, plus NMA support, the evidence base clearly exceeds Tier 3 thresholds.

### 6. Consistency with supplements.js

The main `supplements.js` file already had Tier 2 assigned for Phosphatidylserine (ID 12). The enhanced file's Tier 3 was inconsistent. This update reconciles both to Tier 2, which is justified by the evidence evaluation.

## Factors Preventing Tier 1

Despite the upgrade, Tier 1 was not assigned due to:

1. **No PS-specific meta-analysis with pooled cognitive effect estimates** — The Zhou 2024 NMA includes PS as one of 12 interventions but does not provide PS-specific pooled standardized mean differences
2. **No Cochrane review** for any PS indication
3. **Moderate individual RCT sizes** — Largest PS-specific RCT is n=190; no multi-center large-scale trials (n>500)
4. **ADHD evidence relies on single small RCT** (Hirayama n=36) supplemented by NMA ranking; dedicated PS-ADHD trials with larger samples needed
5. **No formal dose-response meta-analysis** — Dose-response relationship not characterized in meta-analytic framework
6. **Limited long-term data** — Only Duan 2025 provides 12-month outcomes
