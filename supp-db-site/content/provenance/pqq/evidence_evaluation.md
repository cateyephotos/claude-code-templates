### Evaluation Framework

- **Primary:** GRADE (Grading of Recommendations Assessment, Development and Evaluation)

#### Study 1: Tamakoshi 2023 — `tamakoshi_2023_memory`
**Full citation:** Tamakoshi M, Suzuki T, Nishihara E, Hayashi T, Matsunaga K, Taki K, Nakano M. "Effect of pyrroloquinoline quinone disodium salt supplementation on cognitive function in healthy adults: a randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled parallel group comparison study." *Food & Function* 14(3), 2023.

- **Study type:** Randomized controlled trial (parallel group)
- **Design details:** Double-blind, placebo-controlled; n=45 (PQQ 20mg/day n=22; placebo n=23); 12 weeks; healthy Japanese adults 40–65 years
- **Primary outcome:** Composite cognitive score (Cognitrax computerized battery — verbal memory, executive function, processing speed)

**Cochrane Risk of Bias 2.0 Assessment:**
| Domain | Rating | Justification |
|--------|--------|---------------|
| Randomization process | Low | Appropriate computer-generated randomization; allocation concealment reported |
| Deviations from intended intervention | Low | Double-blind maintained; no protocol deviations reported |
| Missing outcome data | Low | No notable dropout; intention-to-treat analysis used |
| Measurement of outcome | Some concerns | Cognitrax is validated; however cognitive assessments have practice effects (test-retest bias possible despite 12-week gap) |
| Selection of reported results | Some concerns | BioPQQ™-affiliated authors; possible selective reporting of cognitive subdomain results |

**Overall GRADE Certainty:** Low (downgraded 2 levels: small sample size → imprecision; funding bias → publication bias risk)
**Assigned Evidence Level:** Level 2 (RCT)
**Quality Score:** 6/10

**GRADE Domain Assessment:**
- Risk of bias: Some concerns (funding: Mitsubishi Gas Chemical is acknowledged; 4/5 authors are MGC employees or have MGC affiliations)
- Inconsistency: Not serious (consistent with Shiojima 2021 direction)
- Indirectness: Not serious (Japanese adults 40–65; generalizable to similar demographics)
- Imprecision: Serious (n=45 total; most cognitive subdomain analyses are exploratory)
- Publication bias: Suspected (industry-affiliated trial of proprietary ingredient)

#### Study 2: Shiojima 2021 — `shiojima_2021_cognitive`
**Full citation:** Shiojima Y, Takahashi M, Takahashi R, Moriyama H, Ito H, Tezuka M, Watanabe Y, Miyake M, Nakano M, Uebaba K, Ishibashi Y. "Effect of Pyrroloquinoline Quinone Disodium Salt (BioPQQ) Intake on the Cognitive Functions of Healthy Volunteers: A Randomized, Double-Blind, Placebo-Controlled, Parallel Group Comparison Study." *Journal of the American College of Nutrition* 40(5), 2021.

- **Study type:** Randomized controlled trial (parallel group)
- **Design details:** Double-blind, placebo-controlled; n=58 (PQQ 20mg/day n=29; placebo n=29); 12 weeks; healthy Japanese adults 40–65 years; included NIRS cerebral blood flow measurement
- **Primary outcome:** Composite cognitive score (multiple validated tests) + secondary outcome of prefrontal cortex blood oxygenation by fNIRS

**Cochrane Risk of Bias 2.0 Assessment:**
| Domain | Rating | Justification |
|--------|--------|---------------|
| Randomization process | Low | Appropriate allocation; concealment reported |
| Deviations from intended intervention | Low | Double-blind maintained |
| Missing outcome data | Low | Full dataset used; no notable dropout |
| Measurement of outcome | Some concerns | Multiple cognitive test battery creates multiple comparison issue; correction method not specified |
| Selection of reported results | Some concerns | Industry-funded (Mitsubishi Gas Chemical); 3/11 authors are MGC employees |

**Overall GRADE Certainty:** Low (downgraded 2 levels: imprecision at n=58; publication bias risk from industry funding)
**Assigned Evidence Level:** Level 2 (RCT)
**Quality Score:** 7/10 (highest-n RCT in PQQ evidence base; cerebral blood flow NIRS is objective measure)

**GRADE Domain Assessment:**
- Risk of bias: Some concerns (partial industry authorship)
- Inconsistency: Not serious (consistent with Tamakoshi 2023, Nakano 2016)
- Indirectness: Not serious (similar population to other included studies)
- Imprecision: Serious (n=58; cognitive subdomain analyses underpowered)
- Publication bias: Suspected (industry-affiliated)

#### Study 3: Hwang 2020 — `hwang_2020_pgc1a`
**Full citation:** Hwang PS, Machek SB, Cardaci TD, Wilburn DT, Kim CS, Surholt R, Willoughby DS. "Pyrroloquinoline Quinone (PQQ) Supplementation Improves Indicators of Mitochondrial Biogenesis and Antioxidant Activity in the Skeletal Muscle of Active Young Adult Males." *Journal of the American College of Nutrition* 39(1), 2020.

- **Study type:** Randomized controlled trial (parallel group)
- **Design details:** Double-blind, placebo-controlled; n=17 (PQQ 20mg/day n=9; placebo n=8); 6 weeks; healthy young adult males 22±3 years; exercise training continued as normal
- **Primary outcome:** Skeletal muscle biopsy markers — PGC-1α, Nrf2, TFAM, mitochondrial copy number

**Cochrane Risk of Bias 2.0 Assessment:**
| Domain | Rating | Justification |
|--------|--------|---------------|
| Randomization process | Low | Appropriate; allocation described |
| Deviations from intended intervention | Low | Double-blind maintained; BioPQQ™ supplied by MGC (acknowledged as gift) |
| Missing outcome data | Low | Complete data for all participants |
| Measurement of outcome | Low | Skeletal muscle biopsy with gene expression analysis is objective |
| Selection of reported results | Low | Independent research group (Baylor University, no MGC authorship) |

**Overall GRADE Certainty:** Moderate (downgraded 1 level: very small sample n=17 — imprecision)
**Assigned Evidence Level:** Level 2 (RCT)
**Quality Score:** 7/10 (highest methodological quality in PQQ evidence base — objective biopsy measure, independent research group)

**GRADE Domain Assessment:**
- Risk of bias: Low (independent academic group; BioPQQ™ was a gift but not industry authorship)
- Inconsistency: Not applicable (only study measuring PGC-1α in humans)
- Indirectness: Some (young males only; not generalizable to aging population where mitochondrial benefits are most relevant)
- Imprecision: Very serious (n=17; most underpowered study in set)
- Publication bias: Unlikely (independent research group with declared gift of supplement only)

#### Study 4: Nakano 2016 — `nakano_2016_pfc`
**Full citation:** Nakano M, Yamamoto T, Okumura H, Tsuda A, Ito S. "Effects of Oral Supplementation with Pyrroloquinoline Quinone on Stress, Fatigue, and Sleep." In: *Pyrroloquinoline Quinone (PQQ): Promising Agent for Brain Health.* Springer, 2016. doi: 10.1007/978-3-319-38810-6_29

- **Study type:** Randomized controlled trial (parallel group)
- **Design details:** Double-blind, placebo-controlled; n=41 (PQQ 20mg/day n=21; placebo n=20); 8 weeks; middle-aged Japanese adults 40–65 years; tNIRS assessment of prefrontal cortex blood oxygenation
- **Primary outcome:** Prefrontal cortex oxygenated hemoglobin concentration by tNIRS during cognitive task

**Cochrane Risk of Bias 2.0 Assessment:**
| Domain | Rating | Justification |
|--------|--------|---------------|
| Randomization process | Low | Randomization described; allocation concealment not fully detailed |
| Deviations from intended intervention | Low | Double-blind maintained |
| Missing outcome data | Low | Complete dataset used |
| Measurement of outcome | Low | tNIRS is an objective physiological measure |
| Selection of reported results | Some concerns | Published as book chapter (Springer edited volume) by MGC-affiliated authors |

**Overall GRADE Certainty:** Low (downgraded 2 levels: imprecision at n=41; publication bias — book chapter format limits peer review rigor vs. peer-reviewed journal)
**Assigned Evidence Level:** Level 2 (RCT)
**Quality Score:** 6/10

**GRADE Domain Assessment:**
- Risk of bias: Some concerns (publication format; MGC-affiliated authorship)
- Inconsistency: Not serious (consistent with Shiojima 2021 tNIRS/fNIRS direction)
- Indirectness: Not serious (target population — middle-aged adults — most relevant for cognitive decline prevention)
- Imprecision: Serious (n=41 for objective physiological endpoint; borderline adequate)
- Publication bias: Suspected (Springer edited volume by MGC-affiliated authors)

#### Study 5: Itoh 2016 — `itoh_2016_stroop`
**Full citation:** Itoh Y, Hine K, Miwa H, Tsuji T, Igata M, Tsuda T, Kato M, Mio K. "Effect of the Antioxidant Supplement Pyrroloquinoline Quinone Disodium Salt (BioPQQ) on Cognitive Functions." In: *Biological and Pharmaceutical Bulletin.* Springer, 2016. doi: 10.1007/978-1-4939-3023-4_40

- **Study type:** Randomized controlled trial (crossover)
- **Design details:** Double-blind, placebo-controlled crossover; n=20; BioPQQ™ 20mg/day; 12 weeks per arm with washout; young healthy adults
- **Primary outcome:** Stroop Color-Word interference test performance (executive function / cognitive flexibility)

**Cochrane Risk of Bias 2.0 Assessment:**
| Domain | Rating | Justification |
|--------|--------|---------------|
| Randomization process | Low | Crossover design with appropriate washout |
| Deviations from intended intervention | Low | Double-blind maintained |
| Missing outcome data | Low | Complete data; crossover design mitigates dropout impact |
| Measurement of outcome | Low | Stroop test is a validated, standardized cognitive assessment |
| Selection of reported results | Some concerns | Published as book chapter; MGC-affiliated authors |

**Overall GRADE Certainty:** Low (downgraded 2 levels: smallest study n=20; publication format concerns)
**Assigned Evidence Level:** Level 2 (RCT)
**Quality Score:** 5/10 (n=20 is severely underpowered; book chapter publication; crossover design partially mitigates sample size concern)

**GRADE Domain Assessment:**
- Risk of bias: Some concerns (publication format; potential industry affiliation)
- Inconsistency: Not applicable (single study for Stroop outcome)
- Indirectness: Some (young adults; Stroop effect is most relevant in aging population)
- Imprecision: Very serious (n=20; this is a severe limitation)
- Publication bias: Suspected (industry-adjacent publication)

#### Study 6: Nakano 2015 — `nakano_2015_ldl`
**Full citation:** Nakano M, Ubukata K, Yamamoto T, Yamaguchi H. "Effect of Pyrroloquinoline Quinone (PQQ) on Mental Status of Middle-Aged and Elderly Persons." *Journal of Nutritional Science and Vitaminology* 61(3), 2015.

- **Study type:** Randomized controlled trial (parallel group)
- **Design details:** Double-blind, placebo-controlled; n=29 (PQQ 20mg/day n=15; placebo n=14); 8 weeks; Japanese adults; LDL cholesterol measured as secondary outcome; primary was cognitive/mental status
- **Primary outcome (for this database entry):** LDL cholesterol change (secondary outcome of the trial)

**Cochrane Risk of Bias 2.0 Assessment:**
| Domain | Rating | Justification |
|--------|--------|---------------|
| Randomization process | Low | Appropriate |
| Deviations from intended intervention | Low | Double-blind maintained |
| Missing outcome data | Low | Full dataset |
| Measurement of outcome | Low | Serum LDL is a standard clinical chemistry measurement |
| Selection of reported results | High | The LDL result used in this database is a secondary outcome of a trial primarily designed for cognitive endpoints; positive LDL effect was only significant in a post-hoc high-LDL subgroup analysis |

**Overall GRADE Certainty:** Very Low (downgraded 3 levels: post-hoc subgroup analysis; small n=29; industry-affiliated)
**Assigned Evidence Level:** Level 2 (RCT) — but treated as exploratory/hypothesis-generating for the cardiovascular benefit claim
**Quality Score:** 4/10 (secondary outcome of underpowered trial; post-hoc subgroup)

**GRADE Domain Assessment:**
- Risk of bias: High for LDL outcome (post-hoc subgroup analysis; LDL was not pre-specified primary endpoint)
- Inconsistency: Not applicable (single study for this outcome)
- Indirectness: Not serious for cardiovascular endpoint
- Imprecision: Very serious (n=29 total; high-LDL subgroup is ~15 participants)
- Publication bias: Suspected (industry-affiliated)

- **GRADE Certainty contribution:** None for benefit claims (narrative reviews do not contribute to GRADE evidence quality for benefit claims in this database)

**Quality Score:** 5/10 (high-quality narrative review from peer-reviewed journal with good coverage; but non-systematic limits use for benefit grading)
**Assigned Evidence Level:** Level 3 (high-quality review; not meta-analytic)

### Aggregate Quality Metrics

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| Average study quality (RCTs only) | 5.8/10 |
| Evidence level distribution | Level 1: 0 | Level 2: 6 (RCTs) | Level 3: 1 (review) |
| Total RCT sample size | 210 (n=45+58+17+41+20+29) |
| Largest single RCT | n=58 (Shiojima 2021) |
| Independent research groups | 2 (Hwang 2020 = Baylor University; all others = MGC-affiliated) |

**Replication assessment:** All 5 RCTs show directional improvement in cognitive or mechanistic outcomes. However, 4 of 5 RCTs are by MGC-affiliated research groups using MGC proprietary product. Only Hwang 2020 (Baylor University) constitutes fully independent replication.

**Publication bias assessment:** High suspicion of publication bias. All human trials use proprietary BioPQQ™. Mitsubishi Gas Chemical funded or supplied PQQ in all 5 RCTs. Academic-independent trials may exist with null results that were not published.

**Funding source analysis:**
- Industry-funded or ingredient-supplied: 5/5 RCTs (BioPQQ™ supplied by MGC; 4/5 have MGC employee authorship)
- Government or independent funding: 0 RCTs (1 partial: Hwang 2020, BioPQQ™ gift only, all funding from Baylor University)
- Geographic distribution: Japan (4/5 RCTs), USA (1 — Hwang 2020)

**Critical limitation:** The PQQ human evidence base is entirely dependent on Mitsubishi Gas Chemical's proprietary BioPQQ™ form and primarily on MGC-affiliated authors. This represents a significant structural bias in the evidence base that cannot be corrected without independent academic trials.
