### Inclusion Criteria

1. Human subjects (animal/in vitro eligible for mechanisms section only with explicit labeling)
2. Reported on a single-ingredient PQQ intervention (multi-ingredient formulations excluded)
3. Published in peer-reviewed journal OR is a high-quality systematic review
4. Available DOI confirmed via CrossRef
5. Relevant to PQQ's claimed benefits (cognitive, mitochondrial, cardiovascular), mechanisms, or safety
6. PQQ intervention used a standardized form (BioPQQ™ or pharmaceutical-grade PQQ disodium salt)

---

### Exclusion Criteria

1. Multi-ingredient formulations where PQQ effect cannot be isolated
2. Conference abstracts only (no full peer-reviewed publication)
3. Duplicate publications of the same dataset (oldest record excluded)
4. Non-English without English abstract
5. Retracted papers
6. Studies on organisms other than humans (excluded from benefits/safety; eligible for mechanisms if no human equivalent exists)

### Inclusion Notes

**Note on Yan 2024 (narrative review):** This is a narrative review, not an RCT or systematic review. It is included in the mechanisms and safety sections because: (1) it provides the most comprehensive and current synthesis of PQQ's redox cofactor and antioxidant mechanisms; (2) it explicitly covers human safety pharmacology data. It is explicitly labeled as `"studyType": "Narrative review"` and `"evidenceLevel": "Level 3"` in the enhanced citations file to prevent tier inflation.

**Note on Nakano 2016 (book chapter):** This study is published as a chapter in a peer-reviewed scientific edited volume (Advances in Nutrition and Cancer, Springer). The DOI (10.1007/978-3-319-38810-6_29) resolves to the Springer publication. The chapter reports original RCT data (tNIRS cerebral blood flow). Inclusion is appropriate. Evidence level is assigned Level 2 (RCT).

**Note on Itoh 2016 (book chapter):** Similar publication format — chapter in peer-reviewed volume (Springer, DOI 10.1007/978-1-4939-3023-4_40) reporting original RCT data (Stroop test). Evidence level Level 2 (RCT). Smallest study in the included set (n=20).

---

### Note on BioPQQ™ Form Specificity

All 5 included human RCTs used BioPQQ™ (Mitsubishi Gas Chemical Company, Tokyo, Japan). This is the only commercially available fermentation-derived PQQ disodium salt form with peer-reviewed human clinical data. Evidence from this set is form-specific: extrapolation to other PQQ sources, forms, or doses is not directly supported.
