#### Study: serban_2016_blood_pressure — "Effect of quercetin on blood pressure: A systematic review and meta-analysis"
- **Study type:** Meta-analysis (7 RCTs, n=587) (Journal of Hypertension, IF ~4.0)
- **PMID:** 27405810 | **DOI:** 10.1097/HJH.0000000000001041
- **Year:** 2016
- **GRADE domains:**
  - Risk of bias: Some concerns — small number of RCTs (7); some heterogeneity in doses and patient populations
  - Inconsistency: Not serious — consistent BP-reducing direction across studies
  - Indirectness: Not serious — cardiovascular risk population; BP is primary clinical endpoint
  - Imprecision: Not serious — weighted mean differences with CI reported; n=587
  - Publication bias: Possible but undetectable in small meta-analysis
- **Overall GRADE certainty:** Moderate
- **Assigned evidence level:** Level 1
- **Key findings (verified):** SBP WMD = -3.04 mmHg (95% CI: -5.26 to -0.83, p = 0.007); DBP WMD = -2.63 mmHg (95% CI: -4.38 to -0.88, p = 0.003)
- **Clinical significance:** 3 mmHg SBP reduction is small but clinically meaningful at population level (translates to ~5% reduction in cardiovascular events per epidemiological data)
- **Quality score contribution:** 7/10 — consistent meta-analysis; limited by small RCT count and study age (2016)

#### Study: hickson_2019_senolytic — "Senolytics decrease senescent cells in humans: A pilot clinical trial"
- **Study type:** RCT (Phase 1 pilot, n=9) (EBioMedicine, IF ~8.1)
- **PMID:** 31542391 | **DOI:** 10.1016/j.ebiom.2019.08.069
- **Year:** 2019
- **GRADE domains:**
  - Risk of bias: High — no placebo control; unblinded; n=9 (very small); IPF population (generalizability limited)
  - Inconsistency: Not serious — single study; no comparison available
  - Indirectness: Serious — IPF patients with systemic senescent cell burden; unclear if results generalize to healthy aging
  - Imprecision: Very serious — n=9 provides insufficient statistical power for definitive conclusions
  - Publication bias: Likely — first-of-kind positive pilot, high journal impact
- **Overall GRADE certainty:** Very Low for general senolytic claim (pilot only)
- **Assigned evidence level:** Level 2 (first-in-human RCT, but Phase 1 without placebo)
- **Key findings (verified):** D+Q (dasatinib 100mg + quercetin 1250mg, intermittent) reduced adipose senescent cells; SASP markers (IL-1α, IL-6, MMP-9, MMP-12) reduced; physical function improved. Note: quercetin was combined with dasatinib; isolated quercetin effect unclear
- **Quality score contribution:** 5/10 — landmark study for senolytic concept; severely underpowered; combination therapy confounds quercetin-specific effect
