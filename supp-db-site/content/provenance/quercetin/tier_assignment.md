**Decision: Tier 2** — Multiple meta-analyses exist across 4 distinct outcome domains. However, findings are inconsistent across domains: strong positive for exercise recovery and blood pressure, mixed for inflammation (population-dependent), and pilot-only for senolytics. The inconsistency across domains prevents elevation to Tier 1, which requires consistent meta-analytic findings. Tier 2 reflects the honest evidence state: well-studied with some strong outcomes, but not uniformly replicable across all claimed benefits.

### Research Maturity: Developing
**Justification:** Quercetin has been studied in cell and animal models since the 1980s; human clinical trials have been conducted since the 2000s with meta-analyses emerging from 2010 onward. The evidence base is substantial but not yet mature — key gaps remain in optimal dose-form (bioavailability), long-term safety (>1 year continuous use), and the senolytic mechanism in humans specifically (Hickson 2019 needs Phase 2 replication with placebo). The field is actively evolving, with 2023–2024 reviews updating mechanistic understanding. Designation: Developing (progressing toward Mature).

---

### Evidence Gaps Identified
1. **Senolytic Phase 2 RCT:** Hickson 2019 (n=9, no placebo) must be replicated in larger, placebo-controlled design before the senolytic claim can be considered more than preliminary
2. **Bioavailability optimization:** Most clinical trials use standard quercetin powder; enhanced-bioavailability forms (Quercetin Phytosome, EMIQ) are not adequately represented in the meta-analytic literature
3. **Long-term safety:** Andres 2018 notes insufficient data for continuous use >12 months; no chronic toxicity data in humans
4. **Dose-response for anti-inflammatory effects:** Tabrizi 2019 shows MetS-specific effects; dose threshold for general population benefits unclear
5. **Head-to-head comparison with standard anti-inflammatory treatments:** No direct comparator RCTs available
6. **Cognitive outcomes:** No high-quality RCTs on cognitive/neuroprotective endpoints despite preclinical data support
