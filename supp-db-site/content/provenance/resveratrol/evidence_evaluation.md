# Evidence Evaluation: Resveratrol (ID: 27)
## Pipeline Run: 2026-03-06
## Framework: GRADE (Grading of Recommendations Assessment, Development and Evaluation)

### Evaluation Framework
- **Primary:** GRADE
- **Bias assessment:** Cochrane Risk of Bias 2.0 for RCTs; AMSTAR-2 for systematic reviews/meta-analyses
- **Quality scoring:** Custom composite (0-100 total, see tier_assignment.md)
- **Evidence Level Mapping:**
  - Level 1 = GRADE High (meta-analysis of RCTs, consistent, low bias)
  - Level 2 = GRADE Moderate (meta-analysis with some concerns, or single large RCT)
  - Level 3 = GRADE Low (small RCT, indirect evidence)
  - Level 4 = GRADE Very Low (single observational, cross-sectional)
  - Level 5 = GRADE Very Low (animal/in vitro only)

### Per-Study Evaluation

#### Study 1: molani_gol_2024_umbrella — Umbrella Meta-Analysis of Resveratrol RCTs
**Citation:** Molani-Gol R, Pourghassem Gargari B (2024). Eur J Nutr. DOI: 10.1007/s00394-024-03335-9. PMID: 38374352
**Study type:** Umbrella meta-analysis (19 meta-analyses, 81 RCTs, n=4088)

- **Risk of bias:** Low — formal umbrella review methodology, AMSTAR-2 applied to constituent meta-analyses
- **Inconsistency:** Not serious — heterogeneity addressed across constituent meta-analyses; findings consistent for inflammation/anthropometrics domains
- **Indirectness:** Not serious — human subjects, direct resveratrol supplementation
- **Imprecision:** Not serious — large aggregate sample (n=4088 across 81 RCTs)
- **Publication bias:** Suspected — supplement research inherently subject to positive result bias; umbrella review mitigates via aggregation
- **Overall GRADE certainty:** Moderate (upgraded from Low due to large N and umbrella design)
- **Assigned evidence level:** Level 1
- **Quality score contribution:** 10/10

### Replication Assessment
Anti-inflammatory (CRP/hs-CRP) effects replicated across multiple independent labs and populations (Gorabi 2021 [35 RCTs], Hosseini 2020 [T2D], Teimouri 2022 [CVD], Molani-Gol 2024 [umbrella]). Body composition replicated in obesity (Mousavi 2018) and T2D (Lahouti 2025). SIRT1 null replicated across 11 independent RCTs (Mansouri 2025). Cognitive/CBF effects replicated across 3 independent RCTs (Kennedy 2010, Wightman 2015, Thaung Zaw 2021).

### Publication Bias Assessment
No funnel plot meta-analysis performed for this pipeline run. Publication bias suspected across domains typical of dietary supplement research. Null findings (Mansouri 2025 SIRT1, Li 2021 bone, Rodrigues Uggioni 2024 lipids) are included, which partially offsets positive publication bias concern.

### Funding Source Analysis
From available metadata: Mix of government-funded (academic institutions), investigator-initiated, and some industry-adjacent funding. No studies with explicit conflict of interest disqualifying from inclusion.

### Geographic Diversity
Studies from multiple countries: Iran (multiple), Australia (Thaung Zaw, Kennedy, Wong), UK (Wightman), South Korea, Italy, Saudi Arabia, and others. Good geographic diversity reduces population-specific bias.
