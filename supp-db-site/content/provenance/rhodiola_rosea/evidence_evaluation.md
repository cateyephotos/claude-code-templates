# Evidence Evaluation: Rhodiola rosea
## Pipeline Run: 2026-03-05

### Evaluation Framework
- **Primary:** GRADE (Grading of Recommendations Assessment, Development and Evaluation)
- **Quality scoring:** Custom composite (see tier_assignment.md)

### Per-Study Evaluation

#### Study: wang_2025_endurance_performance -- Effect of Rhodiola rosea Supplementation on Endurance Performance
- **Citation:** Wang et al. 2025, Frontiers in Nutrition
- **PMID:** 41080184
- **DOI:** 10.3389/fnut.2025.1553747
- **Study type:** Systematic review + meta-analysis (26 RCTs, 668 participants)
- **Key finding:** Significant effects across multiple exercise performance domains: VO2 ES=0.32 (p<0.01), time to exhaustion (TTE) ES=0.38, time trial performance (TTP) ES=-0.40. Antioxidant capacity: total antioxidant capacity (TAC) ES=0.59, superoxide dismutase (SOD) ES=1.16. Muscle damage markers: creatine kinase (CK) ES=-0.84, lactate ES=-0.87. Dose-response analysis shows >600mg/day yields greater VO2 improvement.
- **GRADE domains:**
  - Risk of bias: Low -- 26 RCTs provide a robust evidence base. Frontiers in Nutrition is a well-established open-access journal with peer review. The meta-analysis includes comprehensive subgroup and dose-response analyses. The large number of included studies (26) dilutes the influence of any individual low-quality trial
  - Inconsistency: Not serious -- consistent direction of effect across all performance domains (VO2, TTE, TTP). Antioxidant and muscle damage outcomes further support the directional consistency. The dose-response analysis (>600mg/day threshold) adds internal consistency
  - Indirectness: Not serious -- directly assesses oral Rhodiola rosea supplementation for exercise/endurance performance outcomes in human subjects. Performance metrics (VO2, TTE, TTP) are well-validated and clinically relevant endpoints
  - Imprecision: Some concerns -- while 26 RCTs is a substantial number, 668 total participants means individual study sample sizes average ~25-26. Small individual study sizes may widen confidence intervals at the pooled level. However, the p<0.01 for VO2 suggests adequate statistical power for the primary outcome
  - Publication bias: Possible concern -- exercise performance supplement studies are susceptible to small study effects and selective outcome reporting. The relatively modest effect sizes (0.32-0.40 for performance metrics) are reassuring compared to inflated values sometimes seen in supplement meta-analyses
- **Overall GRADE certainty:** High
- **Assigned evidence level:** Level 1
- **Quality score contribution:** 9/10
- **Notes:** This is the largest and most comprehensive meta-analysis of Rhodiola rosea for exercise performance to date. The 26-RCT scope, quantitative pooling across multiple endpoints, and dose-response analysis represent a significant advancement over prior narrative reviews and smaller meta-analyses. The effect sizes are modest but clinically relevant: ES=0.32 for VO2 translates to a meaningful improvement in aerobic capacity, while the larger effect sizes for antioxidant markers (SOD ES=1.16) and muscle damage reduction (CK ES=-0.84, lactate ES=-0.87) suggest Rhodiola's primary exercise benefit may be through enhanced recovery rather than acute performance enhancement. The dose-response finding (>600mg/day for VO2 improvement) is particularly valuable for dosing guidance. This study elevates the Physical Performance domain from Moderate to Strong evidence.

#### Study: urata_2025_milder_depression -- Efficacy of Pharmacological Interventions in Milder Depression
- **Citation:** Urata Y et al. 2025, Neuropsychopharmacology Reports
- **PMID:** 40014460
- **DOI:** 10.1002/npr2.12529
- **Study type:** Network meta-analysis (8 RCTs, 1049 participants)
- **Key finding:** R. rosea demonstrated significant improvements in depressive symptoms vs placebo in the network comparison. Multiple supplements and pharmacological agents were compared using NMA methodology.
- **GRADE domains:**
  - Risk of bias: Some concerns -- while the NMA methodology is rigorous, R. rosea is one of several interventions compared, not the sole focus. The 8 RCTs and 1049 participants include data across multiple interventions, so the R. rosea-specific sample is a subset. The indirect comparisons inherent in NMA introduce additional uncertainty compared to direct pairwise meta-analysis
  - Inconsistency: Not serious -- the finding of significant depression improvement is consistent with prior individual RCTs of R. rosea for mood (e.g., Darbinyan 2007, Mao 2015). The direction of effect is consistent with R. rosea's known monoaminergic activity
  - Indirectness: Some concerns -- the NMA addresses "milder depression" broadly, and R. rosea's effect must be extracted from the network. The population (milder depression) is relevant to supplement use but the NMA design means R. rosea evidence is embedded within a broader analysis framework
  - Imprecision: Some concerns -- the R. rosea-specific sample within the 1049-participant NMA is smaller than the total pool. Confidence intervals for individual comparisons in NMA are typically wider than in pairwise meta-analyses
  - Publication bias: Possible concern -- NMA methodology partially mitigates publication bias through network consistency checks, but the relatively small number of R. rosea-specific trials limits bias assessment
- **Overall GRADE certainty:** Moderate-High
- **Assigned evidence level:** Level 1
- **Quality score contribution:** 7/10
- **Notes:** This NMA provides the first meta-analytic-level evidence for R. rosea in depression. While R. rosea is not the primary focus of the analysis (it is one of several supplements and pharmacological agents compared), the NMA methodology allows indirect comparison across interventions and the finding of significant improvement vs placebo strengthens the depression evidence domain. Published in Neuropsychopharmacology Reports, a peer-reviewed journal in the psychopharmacology field. The NMA design is both a strength (enables relative efficacy comparisons across interventions) and a limitation (R. rosea-specific evidence is diluted within the broader network). The 7/10 quality score reflects the high methodological quality of NMA but discounts for R. rosea not being the primary intervention under investigation.

#### Study: schwarz_2024_salidroside_exercise -- Salidroside and Exercise Performance
- **Citation:** Schwarz NA et al. 2024, Journal of the International Society of Sports Nutrition 21(1)
- **PMID:** 39601362
- **DOI:** 10.1080/15502783.2024.2436988
- **Study type:** Randomized controlled trial (n=50, double-blind, placebo-controlled)
- **Key finding:** Pure biosynthetic salidroside at 60mg/day enhanced O2 utilization during high-intensity interval training (HIIT, p<0.01) and reduced muscle damage markers. Results support salidroside as the key active compound mediating R. rosea's exercise performance benefits.
- **GRADE domains:**
  - Risk of bias: Low -- double-blind, placebo-controlled RCT design with adequate sample size (n=50). Published in Journal of the International Society of Sports Nutrition (JISSN), a respected sports nutrition journal. The use of pure biosynthetic salidroside eliminates confounding from other R. rosea constituents, providing clean mechanistic data
  - Inconsistency: Not applicable -- single RCT, not a pooled analysis. However, the direction and magnitude of effects are consistent with Wang 2025 meta-analysis findings for O2 utilization and muscle damage markers
  - Indirectness: Significant concerns -- the study uses pure biosynthetic salidroside at 60mg/day, which is 10-30x higher than the typical salidroside content in standard R. rosea extracts (standardized R. rosea extracts typically contain 1-3% salidroside, yielding 2-6mg salidroside per 200mg dose). This supraphysiological salidroside dose limits direct extrapolation to R. rosea whole extract supplementation. However, the study provides valuable mechanistic evidence identifying salidroside as the responsible bioactive compound
  - Imprecision: Not serious -- n=50 is adequate for an RCT of this design, and the p<0.01 for O2 utilization indicates adequate statistical power for the primary outcome
  - Publication bias: Low concern -- single study publication; the JISSN publication venue and the use of novel biosynthetic salidroside reduce likelihood that this is a selective report from a larger unpublished trial program
- **Overall GRADE certainty:** Moderate
- **Assigned evidence level:** Level 2
- **Quality score contribution:** 7/10
- **Notes:** This is a well-designed double-blind RCT that provides critical mechanistic evidence for R. rosea's exercise performance effects. By isolating salidroside (the primary bioactive glycoside in R. rosea), the study demonstrates that this specific compound is responsible for enhanced O2 utilization and reduced muscle damage during HIIT. However, the dose (60mg/day pure salidroside) is substantially higher than what is delivered by typical R. rosea supplements. Standard R. rosea extracts (200-600mg/day, standardized to 1-3% salidroside) deliver approximately 2-18mg salidroside per day. The 60mg dose used in this study represents a 3-30x amplification depending on the R. rosea product used. This limits direct extrapolation but strengthens the mechanistic case: if salidroside is the active compound, and it works at high doses, the question becomes whether lower salidroside doses (as delivered by whole R. rosea extracts) achieve sufficient bioactive concentrations. The Wang 2025 meta-analysis (which evaluates whole R. rosea extract) confirms that exercise performance benefits exist at standard doses, suggesting that even lower salidroside delivery is pharmacologically active. The 7/10 quality score reflects the excellent RCT design but discounts for the dose indirectness limitation.

### Aggregate Quality Metrics
- **Average study quality:** 7.7/10
- **Evidence level distribution:** Level 1: 2 papers (Wang 2025, Urata 2025), Level 2: 1 paper (Schwarz 2024)
- **Replication assessment:** Moderate -- three studies from different research groups converge on R. rosea's exercise performance and mood benefits, but the evidence base is smaller than for heavily researched adaptogens like ashwagandha
- **Geographic diversity:** China (Wang), Japan (Urata), USA (Schwarz)
- **Positive/negative balance:** All 3 studies report positive findings for R. rosea. No negative or null meta-analyses were identified in the 2024-2026 literature. The uniformly positive profile is consistent with prior literature but the small number of included studies (3) limits the ability to assess reporting balance
- **Effect size calibration note:** Exercise performance effect sizes from Wang 2025 (ES=0.32-0.40 for performance metrics) are modest and clinically plausible. The larger effect sizes for antioxidant markers (SOD ES=1.16) and muscle damage reduction (CK ES=-0.84, lactate ES=-0.87) suggest R. rosea's primary exercise benefit pathway may be through antioxidant-mediated recovery rather than acute performance enhancement. Depression effects from Urata 2025 NMA are significant but not individually quantified with R. rosea-specific effect sizes due to the NMA design
