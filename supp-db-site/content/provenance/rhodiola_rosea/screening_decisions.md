# Screening Decisions: Rhodiola rosea
## Pipeline Run: 2026-03-05

### Inclusion Criteria
1. Human subjects (clinical trials, observational studies, or meta-analyses thereof)
2. Published in peer-reviewed journal (2024 or later)
3. Available DOI and PMID
4. Relevant to Rhodiola rosea's claimed benefits, mechanisms, or safety
5. Meta-analysis, systematic review, or large RCT preferred
6. Adds NEW information beyond existing database citations
7. Oral supplementation route (excludes topical, IV, injectable, or non-standardized preparations)
8. Rhodiola rosea as primary intervention (excludes multi-herb TCM formulations, different Rhodiola species)

### Exclusion Criteria
1. Conference abstracts only (no full publication)
2. Duplicate publications of same dataset or preprint-final pairs
3. Narrative reviews without novel meta-analytic data or quantitative synthesis
4. Rhodiola rosea as peripheral/minor component in multi-ingredient formulations or broad supplement network meta-analyses
5. Animal or in-vitro studies without human data
6. Studies using Rhodiola species other than R. rosea (e.g., R. crenulata, R. imbricata)
7. Studies using non-oral routes of administration (injectable preparations)
8. Traditional Chinese medicine compound formulations where R. rosea effects cannot be isolated
9. Comment/letter/editorial format without original data

| 1 | wang_2025_endurance_performance | 41080184 | 10.3389/fnut.2025.1553747 | Systematic review + meta-analysis (26 RCTs, 668 participants) | NEW | Largest Rhodiola rosea exercise performance meta-analysis to date. Quantifies effects across VO2 (ES=0.32, p<0.01), time to exhaustion (TTE ES=0.38), time trial performance (TTP ES=-0.40). Also captures antioxidant capacity (TAC ES=0.59, SOD ES=1.16) and muscle damage markers (CK ES=-0.84, lactate ES=-0.87). Dose-response analysis identifies >600mg/day threshold for greater VO2 improvement. Provides the first comprehensive meta-analytic quantification of R. rosea's physical performance effects | benefits (physical performance, endurance, antioxidant, recovery) |
| 2 | urata_2025_milder_depression | 40014460 | 10.1002/npr2.12529 | Network meta-analysis (8 RCTs, 1049 participants) | NEW | Multi-supplement NMA evaluating pharmacological interventions for milder depression. R. rosea demonstrated significant improvements in depressive symptoms vs placebo. While R. rosea is one of several supplements compared (not the sole focus), the NMA design provides indirect comparison data and the finding strengthens R. rosea's depression evidence domain | benefits (depression, mood) |
| 3 | schwarz_2024_salidroside_exercise | 39601362 | 10.1080/15502783.2024.2436988 | Randomized controlled trial (n=50, double-blind) | NEW | Well-designed double-blind RCT examining pure biosynthetic salidroside (60mg/day) on exercise performance. Enhanced O2 utilization during high-intensity interval training (HIIT, p<0.01) and reduced muscle damage markers. Provides mechanistic evidence identifying salidroside as the key active compound responsible for R. rosea's exercise performance benefits. Supports the salidroside mechanism of action | benefits (exercise performance), mechanisms (salidroside) |
