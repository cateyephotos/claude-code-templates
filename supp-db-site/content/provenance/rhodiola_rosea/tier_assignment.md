**Decision:** Tier 2 is maintained. While the evidence has been strengthened (particularly in the Physical Performance domain with Wang 2025's 26-RCT meta-analysis), Rhodiola rosea does not yet meet Tier 1 criteria because: (1) stress and anxiety -- the primary consumer use case for R. rosea -- lack a dedicated meta-analysis in the 2024-2026 literature; (2) the depression evidence comes from a multi-supplement NMA where R. rosea is not the primary focus; and (3) the salidroside RCT uses isolated compound at supraphysiological doses, limiting direct applicability. The quality score increases modestly from 87 to 89 (+2), reflecting genuine evidence advancement without a tier-level shift.

### Tier Maintenance Justification (Tier 2 Maintained)
The decision to maintain Tier 2 (rather than upgrade to Tier 1) is based on four factors:

1. **Physical Performance domain upgraded but is only one domain:** Wang 2025 (26 RCTs, 668 participants) provides Strong evidence for exercise performance, upgrading this domain from Moderate. However, exercise performance is not R. rosea's primary consumer use case -- most consumers take R. rosea for stress, fatigue, and cognitive function. A Tier 1 upgrade requires strong evidence in the primary use domain, not just a secondary domain.

2. **No dedicated stress/anxiety meta-analysis exists:** R. rosea's most common consumer application is stress management and anti-fatigue. Despite being the primary marketed benefit, no dedicated R. rosea stress or anxiety meta-analysis was published in the 2024-2026 window. Prior meta-analyses (e.g., Ishaque 2012) are aging and predate current evidence standards. Until a modern, dedicated stress/anxiety meta-analysis is published, the primary use case evidence remains at the individual RCT level.

3. **Depression NMA is multi-supplement, not R. rosea-specific:** Urata 2025 is a valuable contribution but R. rosea is one of several supplements compared in the network. The R. rosea-specific evidence cannot be fully isolated from the NMA design. A Tier 1 upgrade for depression would require a dedicated R. rosea depression meta-analysis with pairwise comparisons.

4. **Salidroside RCT uses isolated compound at supraphysiological dose:** Schwarz 2024 uses pure biosynthetic salidroside at 60mg/day, which is 10-30x higher than salidroside content in typical R. rosea extracts. While mechanistically valuable, this dose indirectness limits the study's contribution to R. rosea whole-extract evidence assessment.

### Quality Score Change Narrative
The increase from 87 to 89 (+2 points) is driven by two factors:

1. **RCT volume and meta-analysis expansion (+2 each):** Wang 2025 provides the first comprehensive exercise performance meta-analysis for R. rosea (26 RCTs, 668 participants). This is the largest R. rosea meta-analysis to date and covers performance, antioxidant, and muscle damage endpoints with dose-response analysis. Urata 2025 adds NMA-level depression evidence. Combined, these studies substantially expand the meta-analytic foundation.

2. **Sample size improvement (+1):** The pooled participant counts (668 in Wang, 1049 in Urata NMA) increase the total evidence volume. The Schwarz 2024 RCT (n=50) adds a well-powered individual trial.

3. **No change in replication or consistency (0 each):** While three new studies add diversity, the total included count is modest. The consistency score is maintained because the primary use case (stress/anxiety) lacks new meta-analytic confirmation.

### Research Maturity: Developing-Moderate
**Justification:** Rhodiola rosea has a moderate evidence base with growing sophistication. The 2024-2025 evidence cycle shows signs of maturation: the first comprehensive exercise performance meta-analysis (26 RCTs, Wang 2025) and mechanism-specific RCT (salidroside isolation, Schwarz 2024) represent qualitative advances in research sophistication. However, key gaps prevent a "Mature" rating: (1) no dedicated stress/anxiety meta-analysis exists despite this being R. rosea's primary consumer application; (2) the depression evidence comes from a multi-supplement NMA rather than a dedicated R. rosea analysis; (3) cognitive function evidence has not been updated since pre-2024 RCTs; and (4) the overall evidence volume (26 RCTs pooled in the largest MA) is smaller than for more heavily researched supplements. Research maturity is rated as "Developing-Moderate" reflecting a supplement with genuine evidence that is advancing but has not yet achieved comprehensive domain coverage.

### Evidence Gaps Identified
1. **No dedicated R. rosea stress/anxiety meta-analysis (CRITICAL GAP):** Stress management is R. rosea's primary consumer use case, yet no dedicated meta-analysis has been published quantifying this effect. Prior individual RCTs (Darbinyan 2007, Olsson 2009, Edwards 2012) support the claim, but meta-analytic synthesis is needed for Tier 1 consideration
2. **No R. rosea-specific depression meta-analysis:** Urata 2025 NMA includes R. rosea but as one of several agents. A dedicated pairwise meta-analysis of R. rosea for depression would substantially strengthen this domain
3. **Cognitive function evidence aging:** No new cognitive function RCTs or meta-analyses identified in 2024-2026. The cognitive benefit claim relies on older studies (Spasov 2000, Shevtsov 2003, Darbinyan 2000) that predate current evidence standards
4. **Dose-response data limited to exercise performance:** Wang 2025 provides dose-response for VO2 (>600mg/day threshold), but no dose-response analysis exists for stress, fatigue, or cognitive outcomes
5. **Salidroside-to-whole-extract bridging studies needed:** Schwarz 2024 demonstrates salidroside's mechanism at 60mg/day, but studies bridging this to typical R. rosea extract salidroside content (2-18mg/day) are lacking. The dose gap (3-30x) leaves uncertainty about whether whole extracts deliver sufficient salidroside for the observed effects
