## BENEFITS DOMAIN — Individual Citation Scores

### ben_001 — Toulis KA et al.: European Journal of Endocrinology — Hashimoto's meta-analysis
**Score: 7/10**
- **Study design:** Systematic review and meta-analysis
- **Sample size:** 16 studies, 2,019 participants
- **Strengths:** Large participant pool for meta-analysis; multiple independent studies pooled; statistically significant TPO antibody reduction; GRADE Moderate; EJE is top-tier endocrinology journal
- **Limitations:** Primary RCTs included are mostly small (individual study n<200); GRADE not High due to sample size limitations; heterogeneity across included studies
- **Key finding:** Selenium supplementation (typically 200 µg/day) significantly reduces thyroid peroxidase (TPO) antibody levels in autoimmune thyroiditis

### ben_002 — Wichman J et al.: Thyroid — Hashimoto's meta-analysis (independent replication)
**Score: 7/10**
- **Study design:** Systematic review and meta-analysis
- **Sample size:** 4 RCTs, 463 participants (smaller than Toulis 2010 but independent)
- **Strengths:** Independent meta-analysis corroborating Toulis 2010 in same clinical domain; dose-specific findings (200 µg/day); both TPO and thyroglobulin antibody reductions; Thyroid is a top-tier specialty journal
- **Limitations:** Smaller participant count than Toulis 2010; overlap in included RCTs possible; also appears as duplicate in dosage domain
- **Key finding:** Confirms 200 µg/day selenium reduces TPO and TgAb in Hashimoto's patients; corroborates Toulis 2010

### ben_003 — Bleys J et al.: Atherosclerosis — cardiovascular/diabetes meta-analysis
**Score: 6/10**
- **Study design:** Meta-analysis
- **Sample size:** 25 trials, 219,439 participants (very large N)
- **Strengths:** Very large pooled sample; cardiovascular domain is important; Atherosclerosis is a well-regarded journal
- **Limitations:** 2008 (older, pre-SELECT era); finding is mixed/null for cardiovascular outcomes — selenium does not definitively improve cardiovascular risk in selenium-replete populations; this citation represents a domain where evidence is not strongly positive
- **Key finding:** Meta-analysis shows selenium status associated with cardiovascular markers but supplementation effect in general population unclear

### ben_004 — Vinceti M et al.: Cochrane — cancer prevention review
**Score: 8/10**
- **Study design:** Cochrane systematic review (gold standard methodology)
- **Sample size:** 55 studies included
- **Strengths:** Cochrane gold-standard methodology; comprehensive coverage; high methodological rigor; 2014 (relatively recent at time of publication)
- **Limitations:** Primary finding is null for cancer prevention in general population (not positive); GRADE Moderate; finding depends heavily on baseline selenium status (selenium-deficient populations may benefit; replete populations do not)
- **Key finding:** No significant reduction in cancer risk in selenium-replete populations; some benefit in deficient populations; SELECT trial included with definitive null result

### ben_005 — Klein EA et al.: SELECT trial — prostate cancer (null RCT)
**Score: 7/10**
- **Study design:** Large randomized controlled trial (SELECT)
- **Sample size:** 35,533 men, multi-year follow-up
- **Strengths:** Largest selenium RCT ever conducted; definitive null result for prostate cancer in selenium-replete men; high methodological quality; landmark study
- **Limitations:** Null result; year field "2011" may refer to follow-up publication (original SELECT 2009 JAMA paper is the more commonly cited version); PMID 15514263 warrants verification
- **Key finding:** Selenium (200 µg/day) does not reduce prostate cancer risk in men with normal selenium levels; seminal null finding for cancer prevention domain

### ben_006 — Cardoso BR et al.: Experimental Gerontology — cognitive function review
**Score: 5/10**
- **Study design:** Systematic review
- **Sample size:** Multiple observational and intervention studies pooled
- **Strengths:** Emerging domain with biological plausibility; systematic methodology; growing area of selenium research
- **Limitations:** Year mismatch (file: 2015, actual PMID: 2020); Experimental Gerontology is a moderate-impact journal; cognitive domain for selenium is emerging and less established than thyroid domain; mixed results across included studies
- **Key finding:** Selenium deficiency associated with cognitive decline; supplementation evidence is mixed; emerging domain requiring more large-N RCTs

## SAFETY DOMAIN — Individual Citation Scores

### safe_001 — MacFarquhar JK et al.: Journal of Nutrition — toxicity/selenosis
**Score: 7/10**
- **Study design:** Clinical toxicology assessment / case series analysis
- **Strengths:** Documents real-world selenosis cases; establishes symptom patterns (hair loss, nail changes, fatigue, neurological); provides basis for UL; J Nutr is top nutritional sciences journal

- Score 80–90: Tier 1 supplements (large-N RCTs, High GRADE Cochrane reviews)

- Score 50–59: Tier 3 supplements (single RCTs or observational only)
