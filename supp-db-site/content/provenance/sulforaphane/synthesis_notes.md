# Research Synthesis: Sulforaphane
## Pipeline Run: 2026-04-19

### Synthesis Methodology
- Narrative synthesis with quantitative effect-size extraction from RCTs (Cohen's d, percent change vs placebo, hazard ratios where reported)
- GRADE-aligned confidence per evidence_evaluation.md
- Each `findings` field grounded in PubMed-served abstract text retrieved on 2026-04-19

### Mechanism Synthesis

#### Nrf2/Keap1 pathway activation
- **Sources:** Clifford 2021 PROSPERO SR (PMID 33515348); Egner 2014 Qidong RCT human downstream evidence (PMID 24913818)
- **Convergent evidence:** Sulforaphane is among the most-studied phytochemicals in human Nrf2 trials and is the canonical small-molecule Nrf2 activator across in vitro, animal, and human studies
- **Strength assignment:** Strong

#### Phase II detoxification enzyme induction
- **Sources:** Egner 2014 Qidong RCT (PMID 24913818) — direct human demonstration of GST induction via mercapturic acid excretion
- **Strength assignment:** Strong (single large RCT with rigorous LC-MS/MS biomarker quantification)

#### Glucoraphanin-myrosinase activation and bioavailability
- **Sources:** Cramer 2012 (PMID 21910945); Sivapalan 2018 (PMID 29266773)
- **Convergent evidence:** Both crossover trials independently demonstrate that myrosinase-active formulations deliver 3- to 5-fold higher plasma SFN than myrosinase-inactive products
- **Strength assignment:** Strong (mechanistic certainty); essential consumer guidance

### Benefit Synthesis

#### Neuroprotection / ASD
- **Sources:** Singh 2014 PNAS (PMID 25313065); Zimmerman 2021 Mol Autism (PMID 34034808); Magner 2023 Nutrients (PMID 36771424)
- **Convergent and divergent evidence:** Strong positive signal in Singh 2014 young men (ABC -34%, p<0.001); attenuated in Zimmerman 2021 children (primary OACIS NS, secondary ABC d=-0.96 significant); null in Magner 2023 youngest cohort (3–7 years)
- **Pooled estimate:** Not pooled (heterogeneous outcome scales and age groups). Best summary: large effect in adolescent/young-adult moderate-to-severe ASD; smaller, age-attenuated effects in pediatric populations
- **Confidence in claim:** Moderate (mature evidence body but heterogeneous results)

#### Phase II detoxification of environmental pollutants
- **Sources:** Egner 2014 Qidong (PMID 24913818)
- **Effect sizes:** +61% benzene mercapturic acid (p≤0.01); +23% acrolein (p≤0.01); crotonaldehyde NS
- **Confidence in claim:** High (large RCT, biomarker grade)

#### Lung cancer chemoprevention
- **Sources:** Yuan 2025 Cancer Prev Res Phase II (PMID 40041932)
- **Effect sizes:** Bronchial Ki-67 -20% vs +65% placebo (p=0.014); high-density Ki-67 -44% vs +71% (p=0.004); dose-bioavailability correlation (p-trend=0.019)
- **Confidence in claim:** Moderate (single Phase II, surrogate endpoint, biologically plausible)

#### Insulin resistance / NAFLD
- **Sources:** Tian 2024 Food Funct (PMID 39045769)
- **Effect sizes:** Significant GLP-1 elevation correlated with HOMA-IR reduction (n=36 RCT subset)
- **Confidence in claim:** Low–Moderate (single RCT subset, mechanistic story strong)

#### Renoprotection (preclinical)
- **Sources:** Monteiro 2023 SR+MA (PMID 37023957) — 25 preclinical studies
- **Pooled SMDs:** creatinine clearance +1.88 (p<0.0001); plasma creatinine -1.24; urea -3.22; proteinuria -2.20; fibrosis -3.08; glomerulosclerosis -2.24 (all p<0.0001)
- **Confidence in claim:** Moderate for preclinical; not yet translated to human

### Safety Synthesis
- **Sources:** Shapiro 2006 Phase I (PMID 16965241); Chartoumpekis 2019 thyroid 12-week RCT (PMID 30735751); safety data embedded in all RCTs above
- **Adverse event aggregation:** No serious adverse events attributable to sulforaphane reported across the RCT record. Pediatric ASD trial (Zimmerman 2021) reported rare insomnia, irritability and taste/smell intolerance; none serious
- **Risk-level determination:** Low; explicitly addresses the goitrogenic theoretical concern (Chartoumpekis 2019 negative thyroid impact at 12 weeks)

### Dosage Synthesis
- **Range:** 40–150 μmol/day in published RCTs; 42 mg/day broccoli-seed tablet equivalent in Tian 2024
- **Bioavailability caveat:** 3- to 5-fold range in plasma SFN depending on myrosinase activity in the consumed product (Cramer 2012, Sivapalan 2018)
- **Duration evidence:** 12 weeks for metabolic/detoxification; 18–36 weeks for behavioural; 12 months for histopathological endpoints

### Cross-Study Patterns
- **Consistent findings:** Nrf2 activation across in vitro, animal and human; tolerability; bioavailability dependence on myrosinase
- **Contradictory findings:** ASD pediatric vs adolescent/young-adult age-stratified response
- **Dose-response:** Yuan 2025 demonstrated bioavailability-dependent Ki-67 reduction (p-trend=0.019) — first explicit human dose-response signal
- **Population-specific effects:** Stronger in oxidatively-stressed populations (smokers, NAFLD, ASD with redox dysfunction)

### Limitations of This Synthesis
1. No completed human meta-analysis of sulforaphane supplementation in a single indication
2. ASD evidence age-stratification incompletely resolved
3. Lung-cancer chemoprevention requires longer-term hard-endpoint trials
4. Many trials use heterogeneous SFN sources (sprout extracts, beverages, capsules) with different myrosinase status
