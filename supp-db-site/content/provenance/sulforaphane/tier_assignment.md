### Evidence Strength Assignments

| Domain | Rating | Justification |
|--------|--------|---------------|
| Mechanisms | **Strong** | Canonical Nrf2/Keap1 activator with PROSPERO SR (Clifford 2021); demonstrable Phase II enzyme induction in 291-person Qidong RCT; well-characterised glucoraphanin-myrosinase activation chemistry |
| Clinical Benefits | **Moderate** | Large positive effects in young men with ASD (Singh 2014); positive lung-cancer biomarker reduction (Yuan 2025); positive insulin-resistance signals (Tian 2024); pediatric ASD inconsistent |
| Safety | **Well-established** | Phase I (Shapiro 2006), 12-week thyroid-safety RCT (Chartoumpekis 2019), and 12-month exposure data (Yuan 2025) — no clinically significant adverse events |
| Dosage | **Partially established** | Wide RCT dose range (40 μmol/day to 150 μmol/day; up to 42 mg/day from broccoli-seed tablets); strong bioavailability dependence on myrosinase activity is the key product-selection caveat |

### Research Maturity: **Developing**

**Justification:** Active and broad clinical trial pipeline with 7+ completed RCTs and several recent additions (2024–2025), but no large definitive efficacy meta-analysis in any single human indication. The mechanism (Nrf2/Keap1) is mature; the clinical translational programme is developing.

### Evidence Gaps Identified
1. No completed human supplementation MA pooling effect sizes in a single indication
2. ASD evidence age-stratification not yet resolved (positive in young men, mixed in pediatrics)
3. Lung-cancer chemoprevention requires longer-term hard-endpoint trials beyond Ki-67 surrogate
4. Cardiovascular endpoints (BP, lipids, mortality) need adequately powered RCTs
5. Long-term safety data beyond 12 months is limited
