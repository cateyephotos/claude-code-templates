### Key Evidence Quality Observations

1. **Mechanisms have the strongest evidence base:** Four mechanism citations averaging 7.0/10 with Moderate GRADE provide a well-established biochemical foundation. Taurine's osmolyte, calcium regulation, and GABA modulation functions are among the best-documented in the amino acid supplement literature.

2. **Waldron 2018 is the clinical evidence anchor:** The only meta-analysis in the clinical benefit portfolio; Hedges' g=0.40 is a moderate effect that is statistically significant but below the threshold for Tier 2 elevation.

3. **Cardiovascular evidence quality is lower than mechanisms quality:** The two cardiovascular citations (Militante 2002, Zhang 2004) are older reviews (2002, 2004) with Low GRADE. The well-documented cardiovascular mechanism (nitric oxide, calcium regulation) is better supported than the clinical cardiovascular benefit evidence.

4. **Safety evidence is strong:** Shao & Hathcock 2008 (8/10, Moderate GRADE) provides authoritative GRAS basis. The safety profile is among the best-documented in the supplement database, consistent with taurine's status as the most abundant free amino acid in mammalian tissues.

5. **Neurological evidence is the most recent but most indirect:** Kim 2023 is the latest citation; mechanisms are compelling but large human RCTs for cognitive/neurological benefits have not yet been completed.
