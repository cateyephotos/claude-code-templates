### Key Evidence Quality Observations

1. **The null finding (Chhatre 2014) is among the highest-quality evidence:** The meta-analysis finding no significant testosterone effects in healthy men scores 8/10 and is Moderate GRADE. This inverts the typical supplement evidence base where positive effects are best documented — for Tribulus, the null finding has better evidence than the positive findings.

2. **Safety evidence is disproportionately strong relative to clinical benefit evidence:** Three safety studies average 7/10 and Moderate GRADE. The benefit studies average ~5.5/10 and Low–Very Low GRADE (excluding the null-finding meta-analysis).

3. **Sexual function is the strongest positive indication:** Kamenov 2017 (systematic review, 7/10 quality) provides the best positive clinical evidence, but the effects are modest and more pronounced in women than men.

4. **Metabolic and urinary claims rest entirely on animal/preclinical data:** Li 2012 (animal, 2/10) and Al-Ali 2003 (preclinical, 3/10) are the only evidence for metabolic and urinary indications. Human clinical validation is entirely absent for these claims.

5. **Year metadata errors in enhanced file (corrected):** Three citations had incorrect year fields (2020→2005, 2019→2003, 2018→2004) based on conflicting DOI year identifiers and PMID range analysis. These were corrected in the pipeline update.
