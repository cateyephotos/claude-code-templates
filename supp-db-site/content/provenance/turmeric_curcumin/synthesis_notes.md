# Research Synthesis: Turmeric/Curcumin
## Pipeline Run: 2026-03-04

### Synthesis Methodology
- **Approach:** Narrative synthesis incorporating new meta-analytic evidence
- **Effect size metric:** Standardized Mean Difference (SMD / Cohen's d)
- **Confidence assessment:** GRADE certainty of evidence

### Key Evidence Updates

#### Update 1: Cognitive Function — Now Strong Evidence
- **Sources:** wang_2025_cognitive_meta, yu_2025_cognitive_aging, feng_2026_plant_network_meta
- **Convergent evidence:** All three new meta-analyses confirm curcumin improves aspects of cognitive function
  - Wang 2025: Global cognitive function SMD = 0.82 (p = 0.010) across 9 RCTs
  - Yu 2025: Working memory SMD = 1.01, processing speed SMD = 0.37
  - Feng 2026: Curcumin ranks 2nd (SUCRA 89.3%) for executive function among plant substances
- **Divergent evidence:** Yu 2025 found overall global cognitive outcomes non-significant (SMD = 0.14), while Wang 2025 found them significant (SMD = 0.82). Discrepancy may be due to different study inclusion criteria and cognitive outcome measures
- **Dose-response:** Wang 2025 identifies curvilinear dose-response with optimal dose at 0.8g/day
- **Duration threshold:** Benefits significant only with >=24 weeks supplementation
- **Population modifier:** Effects stronger in adults >=60 years and Asian populations

#### Update 2: Depression/Mood — Reinforced by Chronic Disease Data
- **Sources:** Existing ng_2017_depression_meta, new yuan_2025 (Consensus, not yet indexed in PubMed)
- **Note:** Yuan et al. 2025 meta-analysis of 15 RCTs (n=1123) in chronic disease populations showed depression SMD = -0.65 (p = 0.01) and anxiety SMD = -0.22 (p = 0.01). This was found via Consensus API but PMID not yet available — tracked for future addition when indexed.

#### Update 3: Anti-inflammatory — Extended to Rheumatoid Arthritis
- **Sources:** fan_2026_rheumatoid_meta, xu_2025_umbrella_review
- **New clinical domain:** RA meta-analysis (6 RCTs, 244 patients) shows large effects across DAS-28, CRP, ESR, joint counts
- **Umbrella review confirmation:** Xu 2025 reviewed 25 meta-analyses and confirms broad anti-inflammatory benefits
- **Limitation:** RA evidence certainty is very low to low per GRADE; need larger trials

#### Update 4: Metabolic Health — New Clinical Domain
- **Sources:** bahari_2026_lipid_meta
- **New domain added:** Lipid profile benefits in prediabetes/diabetes (27 RCTs)
- **Effect sizes:** TG -13.73 mg/dL, TC -5.24, LDL -5.72, HDL +2.14
- **Clinical significance threshold:** Effects are statistically significant but clinically modest
- **Confidence:** Low per GRADE, high heterogeneity

### Limitations of This Synthesis
1. Some new meta-analyses have high heterogeneity (I-squared > 80%)
2. GRADE certainty is low to moderate for newer clinical domains
3. Yuan 2025 depression/anxiety study not yet findable in PubMed (Consensus only) — excluded from enhanced citations file for now
4. RA meta-analysis has small total sample (244 participants)
5. Curcumin formulation differences across studies make direct comparison difficult
