## Screening Decision Rules Applied

1. **Primary focus rule:** VitC must be the primary intervention or a clearly separable component of the study. Studies where VitC is one of many micronutrients assessed without separate analysis are excluded.
2. **Population generalizability:** Studies in highly specialized populations (neonatal, hemodialysis, ICU, surgical) are excluded unless findings have clear implications for general supplementation.
3. **Clinical outcomes rule:** Studies focused on bioavailability, pharmacokinetics, or genetic variation without clinical endpoints are excluded.
4. **Oral supplementation rule:** Studies using IV or parenteral VitC administration are excluded (different pharmacokinetics and clinical context).
5. **Conflict handling:** When two MAs on the same topic reach opposite conclusions, the study with the positive signal is retained but the conflict is documented and scored accordingly. Both papers are referenced in synthesis notes.
6. **Minimum VitC contribution:** In combination studies (C+E, HAT therapy), VitC must have separable effect estimates or be the dominant component. If not, the study is excluded.
