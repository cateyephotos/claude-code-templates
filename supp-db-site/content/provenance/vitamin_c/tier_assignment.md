## Research Maturity Assessment

| Indicator | Status | Details |
|-----------|--------|---------|
| **Years of research** | Mature | VitC research spans 90+ years (since Szent-Gyorgyi 1932) |
| **Total RCTs in literature** | Very high (500+) | Among the most-studied supplements globally |
| **Meta-analysis saturation** | High | Umbrella review stage reached for glycemic outcomes |
| **Mechanism understood** | Well-characterized | Electron donor, collagen synthesis, iron absorption, immune cell function, antioxidant recycling |
| **Dose-response characterized** | Partially | Some evidence for >500mg/day for antioxidant effects; >30 days for glycemic; dietary levels for cancer prevention |
| **Safety profile** | Well-established | Upper limit 2,000mg/day; GI distress at high doses; oxalate kidney stone risk in susceptible individuals |
| **Regulatory status** | Established essential nutrient | RDA established globally; GRAS status; approved health claims in many jurisdictions |

**Maturity verdict:** Vitamin C is one of the most mature supplement evidence bases. The evidence is in a refinement/confirmation phase rather than discovery phase. New research is clarifying dose-response, population-specific effects, and mechanistic pathways rather than establishing novel benefit domains.

## Evidence Gaps Identified

| Gap | Priority | Description |
|-----|----------|-------------|
| **Immune function (non-COVID)** | High | COVID-19 evidence is mixed and disease-specific. Need MAs of VitC in common cold, influenza, and general immune function in non-pandemic settings to support or refute immune benefit claims. |
| **Cancer prevention (RCT data)** | High | Current cancer evidence is entirely observational. Large-scale cancer prevention RCTs with VitC as primary intervention would strengthen the cancer domain substantially. |
| **Cardiovascular hard endpoints** | Medium | Current CV evidence uses surrogate markers (SBP, HDL). MAs of VitC on MACE, stroke, MI, or cardiovascular mortality would be valuable. |
| **VitC monotherapy vs. combination** | Medium | Multiple studies use C+E combinations. MAs directly comparing VitC alone vs. VitC+E would clarify independent VitC contribution. |
| **Dose-response optimization** | Medium | Optimal supplementation dose unclear across domains. Cancer evidence is dietary; glycemic evidence is supplemental. Dose-response MAs needed. |
| **Cognitive function** | Low | Limited MA-level evidence for VitC and cognitive outcomes. Emerging area that could become a new benefit domain. |
| **Long-term safety at high doses** | Low | Most RCTs are short-term. Long-term safety data (>1 year) at supplemental doses (500-2000mg/day) would strengthen safety profile. |
