# Screening Decisions: Vitamin D3 (Cholecalciferol)
## Pipeline Run: 2026-03-04

### Inclusion Criteria
1. Human subjects (clinical trials, observational studies, or meta-analyses thereof)
2. Published in peer-reviewed journal (2025 or later)
3. Available DOI and PMID
4. Relevant to vitamin D3 supplementation's claimed benefits, mechanisms, or safety in the general adult population
5. Meta-analysis, systematic review, or large RCT preferred
6. Adds NEW information beyond existing 23 database citations
7. Oral supplementation route (excludes intramuscular or UV-based vitamin D interventions)
8. General adult population focus (excludes pregnancy-specific, neonatal-specific, or surgical-setting-only studies)

### Exclusion Criteria
1. Conference abstracts only (no full publication)
2. Duplicate publications of same dataset
3. Narrative reviews without novel meta-analytic data or quantitative synthesis
4. Condition-specific studies too niche for a general supplementation database (e.g., BPPV, post-stroke rehabilitation)
5. Multi-supplement protocols where vitamin D effects cannot be isolated
6. Geographically restricted studies that limit generalizability
7. Studies published before the previous update cutoff date (pre-2025)
8. Negative or null findings in very niche conditions that do not inform the core evidence profile
9. Pregnancy/neonatal-specific outcomes (these belong in a separate maternal health profile)
10. Surgical or critical care settings that do not apply to general supplementation decisions

### Screening Notes
The vitamin D literature is one of the most active in all of nutritional science, generating a very high volume of meta-analyses across extremely diverse conditions. The screening process required particular discipline in excluding niche conditions and specialized clinical settings that, while scientifically valid, do not contribute to the core supplementation evidence profile for the general adult population. The 5 included papers were selected because they either (a) update established evidence domains with important new nuance (Jolliffe ARI, El Kababi SLE), (b) open genuinely new evidence domains with sufficient meta-analytic rigor (Martinekova liver, Bruna-Mejias glycemic control), or (c) provide critical dosage guidance via novel methodology (Zhang network meta-analysis). The exclusion of 15 papers should not be interpreted as a commentary on their scientific quality -- many are rigorous meta-analyses -- but rather reflects the scope boundaries of a general supplementation database.
