# Research Synthesis: Vitamin D3
## Pipeline Run: 2026-03-04

### Synthesis Methodology
- **Approach:** Narrative synthesis with quantitative effect size extraction from meta-analyses
- **Effect size metric:** Odds ratios (OR), mean differences (MD), weighted mean differences (WMD), standardized mean differences (SMD) as reported in source meta-analyses
- **Confidence assessment:** GRADE certainty of evidence (applied per-study in evidence_evaluation.md)

### Benefit Synthesis

#### Benefit: Immune Function — "Reduces acute respiratory infection risk in vitamin D-deficient populations"
- **Sources:** jolliffe_2025_ari_meta_update (new), autier_2017_immune (existing), martineau_2017_respiratory (existing)
- **Effect sizes reported:**
  - Jolliffe 2025 (46 RCTs, n=64,573): Overall OR 0.93 (95% CI 0.86-1.01) — NOT significant universally
  - Jolliffe 2025 subgroup (<25 nmol/L): OR 0.58 (significant) — STRONG deficiency-specific benefit
  - Martineau 2017 (original landmark MA): OR 0.88 overall, OR 0.30 in severely deficient
- **Pooled estimate:** The updated Jolliffe 2025 IPD meta-analysis supersedes the Martineau 2017 analysis with substantially larger sample (64,573 vs ~11,000). Universal ARI protection is no longer statistically significant. Protection confirmed specifically in vitamin D-deficient individuals (<25 nmol/L, OR 0.58).
- **Replication status:** Replicated across multiple independent meta-analyses but with important nuance — benefit is deficiency-dependent
- **Clinical significance threshold:** OR <0.80 considered clinically meaningful for infection prevention
- **Confidence in claim:** High for deficiency-specific benefit, Low for universal benefit
- **Key synthesis decision:** Changed strength from implied "Strong" to "Moderate" to reflect the nuanced finding. Updated specificClaim to note deficiency dependency. This is an important downgrade in the universal claim but upgrade in precision.

#### Benefit: Autoimmune Disease Prevention — "May modulate autoimmune processes, particularly in SLE"
- **Sources:** elkababi_2025_sle_meta (new), existing autoimmune citations
- **Effect sizes reported:**
  - El Kababi 2025 (21 studies, n=3,177 SLE patients): Significant improvement in SLEDAI scores, reduced inflammatory markers, improved complement levels
- **Pooled estimate:** Largest SLE-specific meta-analysis to date. Vitamin D supplementation shows consistent benefit in SLE patients across disease activity, inflammation, and complement measures.
- **Replication status:** Well-replicated in SLE specifically (21 studies); broader autoimmune claims still developing
- **Confidence in claim:** Moderate-High for SLE, Moderate for other autoimmune conditions

#### Benefit: Liver Health — "Supplementation improves hepatic function markers in chronic liver disease"
- **Sources:** martinekova_2025_liver_meta (new — this is a NEW benefit domain)
- **Effect sizes reported:**
  - Martinekova 2025 (46 RCTs, n=4,084): Significant improvement in AST, ALT, albumin, and fibrosis markers. Benefits observed across multiple liver pathologies (NAFLD, hepatitis, cirrhosis).
- **Pooled estimate:** First comprehensive meta-analysis of vitamin D in chronic liver disease. Large sample with consistent benefits across liver function markers.
- **Replication status:** Single meta-analysis but encompasses 46 individual RCTs — strong internal replication
- **Confidence in claim:** Moderate (single MA, but large and comprehensive)
- **Key synthesis decision:** Added as entirely new benefit domain. Liver health was not previously represented in the Vitamin D3 evidence profile.

#### Benefit: Glycemic Control — "Supplementation improves glycemic parameters in type 2 diabetes"
- **Sources:** brunamejias_2025_diabetes_meta (new — this is a NEW benefit domain)
- **Effect sizes reported:**
  - Bruna-Mejias 2025 (20 RCTs): Significant improvements in fasting blood glucose, HbA1c, and insulin resistance (HOMA-IR)
  - Zhang 2025 (40 RCTs, network MA): Dose-stratified analysis showing 2000-4000 IU/day optimal for metabolic benefit
- **Pooled estimate:** Two independent 2025 meta-analyses converge on significant glycemic benefit. Dose-response relationship established (Zhang 2025 network MA).
- **Replication status:** Replicated across two independent research groups with complementary methodologies
- **Confidence in claim:** Moderate-High (two MAs, dose-response established)
- **Key synthesis decision:** Added as entirely new benefit domain. Previous database entry did not include glycemic/metabolic benefits despite substantial evidence.

### Dosage Synthesis

#### Dosage: T2DM dose-response
- **Sources:** zhang_2025_t2dm_network_meta (new)
- **Optimal dosage determination:** Zhang 2025 network meta-analysis (40 RCTs) performed dose-stratified analysis comparing multiple vitamin D dosing regimens. Found 2000-4000 IU/day range optimal for metabolic benefit in T2DM. Higher doses (>4000 IU/day) did not show proportionally greater benefit.
- **Duration evidence:** Benefits observed in studies ranging 8-24 weeks. Most benefit apparent by 12 weeks.
- **Key synthesis decision:** Added as separate dosage entry specifically for T2DM/metabolic health, complementing existing general dosage guidance (1000-4000 IU). The network MA methodology provides stronger dosage evidence than individual RCTs.

### Cross-Study Patterns
- **Consistent findings across all studies:**
  - Vitamin D supplementation is safe at recommended doses (1000-4000 IU/day)
  - Benefits are generally stronger in vitamin D-deficient populations
  - Multiple organ systems respond to supplementation (bone, immune, liver, metabolic, autoimmune)
- **Contradictory findings:**
  - Immune function: Universal ARI protection no longer statistically significant (Jolliffe 2025) vs. earlier claims of broad protection (Martineau 2017). Resolution: Benefit is deficiency-specific, not universal.
- **Dose-response relationship:** Observed for metabolic outcomes (Zhang 2025); threshold effect for immune benefit (deficiency correction sufficient)
- **Population-specific effects:** Deficient populations (<25 nmol/L) show significantly larger benefits across all domains. Elderly, limited sun exposure, and populations at higher latitudes are priority supplementation targets.

### Limitations of This Synthesis
1. New papers are exclusively meta-analyses — no novel individual RCT data added in this update
2. Immune function downgrade is based on a single (albeit very large) updated IPD meta-analysis
3. Liver health and glycemic control domains each rest on 1-2 meta-analyses; more replication needed for Tier 1 confidence in these specific claims
4. Zhang 2025 network MA for dose-response is specific to T2DM — may not generalize to other conditions
5. No head-to-head comparison data between vitamin D3 and D2 in the new papers
