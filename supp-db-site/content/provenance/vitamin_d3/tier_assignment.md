**Decision:** Tier 1 because Vitamin D3 has multiple systematic reviews and meta-analyses across at least 8 distinct health domains (bone health, immune function, mood/depression, cardiovascular, autoimmune, liver health, glycemic control, muscle strength), with consistent findings replicated across independent research groups worldwide. The largest single meta-analysis (Jolliffe 2025) encompasses 46 RCTs with 64,573 participants. Previous Tier 2 classification was outdated — it reflected an earlier assessment when fewer meta-analyses existed.

### Research Maturity: Mature
**Justification:** Vitamin D3 is one of the most extensively studied supplements globally, with thousands of publications, hundreds of RCTs, and 20+ meta-analyses. Research spans 40+ years (1990-2025 in database). Active clinical trials continue to refine dosage and population-specific recommendations, but core efficacy and safety profiles are well-established.

### Evidence Gaps Identified
1. Long-term (>5 year) RCTs on cancer prevention outcomes still lacking definitive results
2. Optimal blood level thresholds may differ by ethnicity and latitude — more population-stratified data needed
3. Immune function benefit appears deficiency-dependent; universal supplementation benefit for non-deficient populations unproven (Jolliffe 2025)
4. Cognitive decline prevention evidence is moderate but lacks large dedicated RCTs
5. Dose-response for autoimmune disease prevention not yet established
6. Interaction with Vitamin K2 for cardiovascular outcomes needs more RCT data
