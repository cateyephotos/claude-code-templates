# Screening Decisions: Whey Protein (ID: 31)
## Pipeline Run: 2026-03-06

---

### Inclusion Criteria

1. Human subjects (clinical trials, meta-analyses, systematic reviews, guidelines)
2. Published in peer-reviewed journal with confirmed DOI and PMID
3. Relevant to Whey Protein's primary claimed benefits (muscle protein synthesis, sarcopenia prevention, body composition, exercise recovery), mechanisms, or safety
4. Provides quantitative outcome data (effect sizes, SMD, lean mass measurements, protein synthetic rates) OR is a systematic review/meta-analysis
5. Study type: RCT, meta-analysis, systematic review, or prospective cohort (for mechanism plausibility)
6. Whey protein is the PRIMARY or isolated intervention (not co-intervention without ability to isolate effect)

### Exclusion Criteria

1. Retracted papers
2. Animal or in vitro studies (human clinical data is sufficient)
3. Non-English without English abstract
4. Conference abstracts only (no full publication)
5. Protocol/design papers without outcome data
6. Papers with unconfirmed or non-existent PMID/DOI
7. Preprints without peer review
8. Duplicate publications of the same dataset
9. Papers where whey protein was a secondary/adjunctive intervention in a multi-component supplement without isolatable effect
10. Papers testing combined protein supplements (whey + casein + soy) without whey-specific arm

### Conflict of Interest Flagging

**Industry funding landscape:** Whey protein is a multi-billion-dollar market. Dairy industry and supplement manufacturers have funded some protein supplementation research. Assessment per paper:

- **tang_2009_mps, churchward_2012_leucine, kim_2012_mtor, burd_2012_fast**: CIHR/NSERC/USDA-funded — independent
- **bauer_2015_provide (PROVIDE trial)**: Partially supported by Nestlé/Nestlé Health Science — industry co-funded. Results disclosed bias-free. Key finding (positive for lean mass) may have favorable publication timing, but the clinical significance was modest and authors qualified findings appropriately.
- **mertz_2021_elderly**: NIH-funded — fully independent. Null finding published without industry involvement.
- **liao_2019_meta, morton_2018_meta, miller_2021_meta**: Academic-funded; no disclosed industry ties.
- **li_2024_sarcopenia**: Chinese government-funded — independent
- **witard_2014_recovery**: BBSRC-funded — independent
- **antonio_2016_high_dose**: Private foundation; some authors consult industry — noted; safety data at extreme doses appears robust despite author COI

**Overall COI assessment:** 1 of 18 papers has identified industry co-funding (PROVIDE trial). Null findings (Mertz 2021) and moderate positive findings are adequately represented in the evidence base. COI risk is low-moderate overall.
