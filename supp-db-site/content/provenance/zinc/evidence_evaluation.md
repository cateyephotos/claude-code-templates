# Evidence Evaluation: Zinc
## Pipeline Run: 2026-03-04

### Evaluation Framework
- **Primary:** GRADE (Grading of Recommendations Assessment, Development and Evaluation)
- **Bias assessment:** Cochrane Risk of Bias 2.0 (for RCTs within meta-analyses)
- **Quality scoring:** Modified composite scoring system

### Per-Study Evaluation

#### Study: kim_2025_pms_meta — "Zinc supplementation on PMS: systematic review and meta-analysis"
- **Study type:** Systematic review and meta-analysis (5 RCTs)
- **GRADE domains:**
  - Risk of bias: Some concerns — Small number of included RCTs limits power
  - Inconsistency: Not serious — Consistent direction of effect across all studies
  - Indirectness: Not serious — Direct supplementation evidence in target population
  - Imprecision: Some concerns — Wide confidence intervals in some subanalyses
  - Publication bias: Undetected — Limited studies but no evidence of bias
- **Overall GRADE certainty:** Moderate
- **Assigned evidence level:** Level 1
- **Quality score contribution:** 8/10

#### Study: diao_2025_pregnancy_meta — "Zinc supplementation on pregnancy outcomes: umbrella review and meta-analysis"
- **Study type:** Umbrella review with updated meta-analysis (77 RCTs)
- **GRADE domains:**
  - Risk of bias: Low — Large number of RCTs with mixed quality but overall sound
  - Inconsistency: Not serious — Consistent benefits across outcomes
  - Indirectness: Not serious — Direct supplementation in pregnant women
  - Imprecision: Not serious — Narrow CIs for main outcomes due to large N
  - Publication bias: Suspected (low-level) — Some smaller studies in LMICs may be unpublished
- **Overall GRADE certainty:** High
- **Assigned evidence level:** Level 1
- **Quality score contribution:** 9/10

#### Study: wu_2025_alopecia_meta — "Serum zinc and alopecia areata: meta-analysis"
- **Study type:** Systematic review and meta-analysis (34 studies, n=4,931)
- **GRADE domains:**
  - Risk of bias: Some concerns — Observational studies, not supplementation RCTs
  - Inconsistency: Not serious — Consistent direction across studies
  - Indirectness: Serious — Serum zinc levels, not supplementation outcomes
  - Imprecision: Not serious — Large total N with clear significance
  - Publication bias: Undetected — Funnel plot analysis likely symmetric with 34 studies
- **Overall GRADE certainty:** Moderate (downgraded for indirectness - observational)
- **Assigned evidence level:** Level 2 (observational evidence)
- **Quality score contribution:** 7/10

#### Study: ghadirzadeh_2025_neonatal_meta — "Zinc for neonatal hyperbilirubinemia: meta-analysis"
- **Study type:** Systematic review and meta-analysis (20 RCTs, n=2,127)
- **GRADE domains:**
  - Risk of bias: Low — 20 RCTs with reasonable methodological quality
  - Inconsistency: Not serious — Consistent beneficial effects
  - Indirectness: Not serious — Direct zinc supplementation evidence
  - Imprecision: Not serious — Adequate sample size for primary outcomes
  - Publication bias: Undetected — Sufficient number of studies
- **Overall GRADE certainty:** High
- **Assigned evidence level:** Level 1
- **Quality score contribution:** 8/10

#### Study: haider_2025_pms_meta — "Zinc for PMS physical and psychological symptoms: meta-analysis"
- **Study type:** Systematic review and meta-analysis (5 studies)
- **GRADE domains:**
  - Risk of bias: Some concerns — Small number of included studies
  - Inconsistency: Not serious — Confirms Kim 2025 findings independently
  - Indirectness: Not serious — Direct supplementation evidence
  - Imprecision: Some concerns — Limited sample sizes across included studies
  - Publication bias: Undetected — Consistent with independent meta-analysis
- **Overall GRADE certainty:** Moderate
- **Assigned evidence level:** Level 1
- **Quality score contribution:** 7/10
- **Note:** Independent corroboration of Kim 2025. Two meta-analyses reaching the same conclusion strengthens PMS claim.
