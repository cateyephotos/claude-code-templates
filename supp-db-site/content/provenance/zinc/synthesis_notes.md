# Research Synthesis: Zinc
## Pipeline Run: 2026-03-04

### Synthesis Methodology
- **Approach:** Narrative synthesis with quantitative effect size extraction from meta-analyses
- **Effect size metric:** Hedges's g, relative risk (RR), standardized mean difference (SMD) as reported in source meta-analyses
- **Confidence assessment:** GRADE certainty of evidence (applied per-study in evidence_evaluation.md)

### Benefit Synthesis

#### Benefit: PMS/Women's Health — "Zinc supplementation reduces premenstrual syndrome symptom severity"
- **Sources:** kim_2025_pms_meta (new), haider_2025_pms_meta (new)
- **Effect sizes reported:**
  - Kim 2025 (5 RCTs): Total PMS Hedges's g=-0.384, emotional g=-0.347, physical g=-0.512 (all significant)
  - Haider 2025 (5 studies): Confirms significant improvement in both physical and psychological PMS symptoms
- **Pooled estimate:** Two independent meta-analyses converge on significant PMS symptom reduction. Physical symptoms show larger effect (g=-0.512) than emotional symptoms (g=-0.347).
- **Replication status:** Independently replicated by two research groups in 2025
- **Clinical significance threshold:** g>0.3 considered clinically meaningful for symptom relief
- **Confidence in claim:** Moderate-High (two independent MAs, but each has only 5 studies)
- **Key synthesis decision:** Added as new benefit domain. PMS was not previously represented in the Zinc evidence profile. The independent replication strengthens confidence despite small number of primary studies.

#### Benefit: Maternal & Fetal Health — "Zinc supplementation improves pregnancy outcomes including fetal growth"
- **Sources:** diao_2025_pregnancy_meta (new), existing maternal health evidence
- **Effect sizes reported:**
  - Diao 2025 (77 RCTs): Fetal growth retardation RR=0.23 (significant), improved Apgar scores, increased birth length and head circumference
- **Pooled estimate:** The largest meta-analysis of zinc in pregnancy (77 RCTs) demonstrates clear benefits across multiple outcome measures. The fetal growth retardation risk reduction (RR=0.23) represents a substantial protective effect.
- **Replication status:** Well-replicated across 77 individual RCTs within the umbrella review
- **Clinical significance threshold:** RR<0.50 considered clinically significant for growth retardation prevention
- **Confidence in claim:** High (77 RCTs, multiple outcomes, umbrella review methodology)
- **Key synthesis decision:** Maternal/fetal health was partially represented (Sawada 2010 on maternal mental health). Diao 2025 establishes comprehensive pregnancy outcome benefits as a distinct, strong domain.

#### Benefit: Hair/Dermatological Health — "Zinc deficiency associated with alopecia areata"
- **Sources:** wu_2025_alopecia_meta (new)
- **Effect sizes reported:**
  - Wu 2025 (34 studies, n=4,931): Serum zinc significantly lower in AA patients vs controls (SMD=-0.69)
- **Pooled estimate:** Large meta-analysis establishes zinc deficiency as a risk factor for alopecia areata. The effect size (SMD=-0.69) is moderate-to-large.
- **Replication status:** Well-replicated across 34 studies
- **Confidence in claim:** Moderate (observational evidence — demonstrates association, not supplementation efficacy)
- **Key synthesis decision:** Added as dermatological benefit domain. Note this is serum level association, not supplementation evidence. Claim worded carefully as "associated with" rather than "treats."

#### Benefit: Neonatal Health — "Zinc supplementation reduces neonatal hyperbilirubinemia severity"
- **Sources:** ghadirzadeh_2025_neonatal_meta (new)
- **Effect sizes reported:**
  - Ghadirzadeh 2025 (20 RCTs, n=2,127): Significant reduction in peak bilirubin levels and phototherapy duration
- **Pooled estimate:** Consistent benefits across 20 RCTs supporting zinc's role in managing neonatal jaundice.
- **Replication status:** Well-replicated within 20 RCTs
- **Confidence in claim:** High (20 RCTs, direct supplementation evidence, adequate sample)
- **Key synthesis decision:** Added as new neonatal health benefit domain. Complements the Diao 2025 pregnancy findings — zinc benefits span from prenatal through neonatal period.

### Cross-Study Patterns
- **Consistent findings across all studies:**
  - Zinc benefits span reproductive, neonatal, dermatological, and women's health domains
  - Effects are generally moderate in size (g~0.3-0.5, RR~0.2-0.5)
  - Benefits most pronounced in deficient or at-risk populations
- **Contradictory findings:**
  - None identified among the 5 new papers
- **Dose-response relationship:** Not specifically addressed in the new papers (existing database has dose guidance)
- **Population-specific effects:**
  - Women of reproductive age: PMS benefits (Kim 2025, Haider 2025)
  - Pregnant women: Fetal growth and birth outcomes (Diao 2025)
  - Neonates: Bilirubin management (Ghadirzadeh 2025)
  - Alopecia areata patients: Zinc deficiency association (Wu 2025)

### Limitations of This Synthesis
1. PMS meta-analyses each contain only 5 primary studies — more RCTs needed for definitive conclusion
2. Alopecia areata evidence is observational (serum levels) not supplementation RCTs — weaker causal inference
3. No new immune function or cognitive meta-analyses found — existing evidence from pre-2025 remains basis for those claims
4. Neonatal hyperbilirubinemia is a specialized clinical context — may not generalize to general zinc benefit claims
5. No head-to-head comparisons between zinc forms (bisglycinate vs picolinate vs gluconate) in the new papers
