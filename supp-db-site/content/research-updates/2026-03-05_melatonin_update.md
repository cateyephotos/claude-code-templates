1. **Tang et al. 2025** (PMID: 40662882)
   - *Melatonin Use in the ICU: A Systematic Review and Meta-Analysis*
   - Critical Care Medicine, 53(9), e1714-e1724
   - DOI: [10.1097/CCM.0000000000006767](https://doi.org/10.1097/CCM.0000000000006767)
   - **Type:** GRADE-assessed SR + Meta-analysis
   - **Scale:** 32 RCTs, 3895 patients

2. **Terao & Kodama 2024** (PMID: 38461503)
   - *Comparative Efficacy, Tolerability, and Acceptability of Donanemab, Lecanemab, Aducanumab, Melatonin, and Aerobic Exercise for Cognitive Function in MCI and Mild AD*
   - Journal of Alzheimer's Disease, 98(3), 825-835
   - DOI: [10.3233/JAD-230911](https://doi.org/10.3233/JAD-230911)
   - **Type:** SR + Network Meta-analysis
   - **Scale:** 10 RCTs, 4599 patients
   - **Key findings:** Melatonin significantly more effective than all anti-amyloid antibodies and placebo. Sensitivity analysis excluding exercise confirmed superiority (no heterogeneity). Anti-amyloid agents significantly less tolerable.
   - **Impact:** First NMA directly comparing melatonin to FDA-approved AD drugs for cognitive outcomes

3. **Yousef et al. 2025** (PMID: 40588412)
   - *The Effectiveness of Melatonin for Sleep Disturbances in Parkinson's Disease: Systematic Review and Meta-Analysis*

   - **Type:** SR + Meta-analysis
   - **Scale:** 8 studies, 409 PD patients
   - **Key findings:** PSQI MD=-1.75 (p=0.004); ESS MD=-1.07 (p=0.009); SOL MD=-9.74 min (p=0.001); TST SMD=0.84 (p<0.00001) after heterogeneity adjustment. No effect on RBD. Improvements did not meet MCID threshold.
   - **Impact:** First PD-specific melatonin sleep MA; establishes disease-specific evidence

## Evidence Evaluation

- **GRADE Assessment:** LOW certainty for all outcomes
- **Evidence Level:** Level 1 (SR+MA with GRADE)
- **Risk of Bias:** Moderate — GRADE negatively affected by risk of bias and inconsistency
- **Precision:** Excellent — 32 RCTs, n=3895 (largest melatonin MA to date)
- **Consistency:** Moderate — significant heterogeneity for some outcomes
- **Directness:** High — directly studies melatonin in ICU populations
- **Quality Score:** 8/10

### Terao & Kodama 2024 — Alzheimer's Disease NMA
- **GRADE Assessment:** MODERATE quality
- **Evidence Level:** Level 1 (SR+NMA)
- **Risk of Bias:** Moderate — significant heterogeneity in primary analysis (resolved in sensitivity analysis)
- **Precision:** Good — 10 RCTs, n=4599 (though few melatonin-specific RCTs)
- **Consistency:** High in sensitivity analysis (excluding exercise), low in primary analysis
- **Directness:** High — directly compares melatonin to FDA-approved AD drugs
- **Quality Score:** 7/10

### Yousef et al. 2025 — Parkinson's Disease Sleep MA
- **GRADE Assessment:** MODERATE quality
- **Evidence Level:** Level 1 (SR+MA)
- **Risk of Bias:** Low to moderate — Cochrane RoB-2 assessment
- **Precision:** Moderate — 8 studies, n=409
- **Consistency:** Mixed — subjective measures improved, objective measures uncertain
- **Directness:** High — directly studies melatonin in PD sleep
- **Limitation:** Improvements did not meet MCID for subjective sleep quality
- **Quality Score:** 6/10

## Evidence Gaps Remaining

1. ICU outcomes have low certainty per GRADE — needs larger, lower-bias RCTs
2. Alzheimer's NMA based on limited melatonin-specific RCTs — needs dedicated AD melatonin MA
3. PD sleep improvements below MCID — clinical significance uncertain
4. No head-to-head immediate-release vs prolonged-release large MA
5. Antioxidant/neuroprotective clinical evidence lags behind preclinical data
6. Optimal dosing for non-sleep indications (delirium, cognition) not established
7. Pediatric neurodevelopmental evidence limited beyond basic sleep outcomes

## Clinical Implications

The three new studies collectively expand melatonin's evidence base from a focused sleep supplement to a multi-domain clinical intervention:

1. **Critical care**: Tang 2025 establishes melatonin as a potential adjunct for ICU delirium prevention (NNT ~10-15 based on RR=0.72). The low GRADE certainty means routine ICU use is premature, but the signal is clinically meaningful given melatonin's favorable safety profile.

2. **Neurodegeneration**: Terao 2024's finding that melatonin outperforms FDA-approved anti-amyloid antibodies for cognition in MCI/mild AD is provocative. While the small number of melatonin RCTs limits confidence, the favorable tolerability profile makes melatonin an attractive option for further investigation in cognitive decline prevention.

3. **Parkinson's disease**: Yousef 2025 confirms melatonin's sleep benefits extend to PD-specific populations, with significant improvements in subjective and objective sleep parameters. The sub-MCID issue for subjective measures warrants cautious interpretation but doesn't negate the statistically significant findings.

The convergence of evidence across sleep, ICU, neurodegeneration, and PD domains supports melatonin's Tier 1 status as one of the most evidence-supported supplements in the database.
