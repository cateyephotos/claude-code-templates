# Phase 2 Enhanced Citation System - Pilot Supplements

## 🎯 **Top 20 Priority Supplements for Enhanced Citation System**

Based on evidence quality (Tier 1 and top Tier 2), the following supplements have been selected for Phase 2 pilot implementation:

### **Tier 1 Supplements (Highest Priority)**
1. **Turmeric/Curcumin** - Multiple systematic reviews and meta-analyses
2. **Omega-3 Fatty Acids** - Extensive meta-analyses and systematic reviews  
3. **Melatonin** - Extensive meta-analyses for sleep disorders
4. **B-Complex Vitamins** - Multiple systematic reviews and strong RCT evidence
5. **Vitamin B12** - Extensive research for deficiency and cognitive function
6. **Folate** - Extensive research for cognitive function and mood
7. **Caffeine** - Extensive research showing strong evidence for cognitive enhancement
8. **Citicoline** - Multiple high-quality RCTs and meta-analyses

### **Top Tier 2 Supplements (High Priority)**
9. **Bacopa monnieri** - Multiple RCTs with moderate sample sizes
10. **Ashwagandha** - Multiple RCTs with good sample sizes
11. **Creatine** - Meta-analysis of memory studies available
12. **Magnesium** - Multiple RCTs for sleep and stress
13. **Vitamin D3** - Large observational studies and some RCTs
14. **L-Theanine** - Multiple small to moderate RCTs
15. **Rhodiola rosea** - Multiple RCTs with varying quality
16. **Phosphatidylserine** - Multiple RCTs showing cognitive benefits
17. **Acetyl-L-Carnitine** - Multiple RCTs for cognitive decline
18. **Ginkgo Biloba** - Extensive research with mixed but generally positive results
19. **Panax Ginseng** - Multiple RCTs showing cognitive and energy benefits
20. **Quercetin** - Strong evidence for senolytic effects and inflammation

## 🔬 **Implementation Strategy**

### **Phase 2A: Mechanism Citation Research**
For each pilot supplement, research and add:
- **Primary Mechanisms**: 2-4 mechanistic studies per pathway
- **Secondary Mechanisms**: 1-2 supporting studies per pathway
- **Mechanism Quality**: Focus on Level 1-3 evidence when available

### **Phase 2B: Benefit Citation Research** 
For each pilot supplement, research and add:
- **Primary Benefits**: 2-3 high-quality clinical trials per benefit
- **Secondary Benefits**: 1-2 supporting studies per benefit
- **Meta-Analysis Support**: Include systematic reviews where available

### **Phase 2C: Safety & Dosage Citations**
For each pilot supplement, research and add:
- **Safety Studies**: Comprehensive adverse event data
- **Drug Interactions**: Clinical interaction studies
- **Dosage Optimization**: Dose-response studies

## 📊 **Target Metrics for Pilot Implementation**

### **Citation Targets per Supplement**
- **Minimum Total Citations**: 8-12 studies per supplement
- **Mechanism Citations**: 3-6 studies (covering all major pathways)
- **Benefit Citations**: 4-6 studies (covering primary benefits)
- **Safety Citations**: 1-2 comprehensive safety studies
- **Dosage Citations**: 1-2 dose-response studies

### **Evidence Level Distribution Target**
- **Level 1-2 (Meta-analyses, Large RCTs)**: 60% of citations
- **Level 3 (Moderate RCTs)**: 30% of citations
- **Level 4-5 (Small studies, Preclinical)**: 10% of citations

### **Quality Standards**
- **DOI Verification**: 100% of citations must have DOI when available
- **PMID Inclusion**: Include PubMed IDs for all indexed studies
- **Full Methodology**: Include study design, sample size, duration
- **Effect Sizes**: Include quantitative results where available
- **Limitations**: Document study limitations and gaps

## 🔍 **Research Methodology**

### **Literature Search Strategy**
1. **Primary Sources**: PubMed, Cochrane Library, Web of Science
2. **Search Terms**: Supplement name + "randomized controlled trial", "meta-analysis", "systematic review"
3. **Date Range**: Focus on 2010-2025 for most recent evidence
4. **Quality Filters**: Minimum sample size n≥20 for clinical trials

### **Citation Quality Assessment**
1. **Jadad Scale**: For RCT quality assessment
2. **AMSTAR**: For systematic review quality
3. **Risk of Bias**: Cochrane risk of bias assessment
4. **Conflict of Interest**: Document funding sources and conflicts

This pilot implementation will establish the enhanced citation system foundation for eventual expansion to all 89 supplements in the database.
