Frequently Asked Questions

Common questions about SupplementDB, our methodology, and how to use the database

General

What is SupplementDB?

SupplementDB is a free, open-access research database that compiles peer-reviewed scientific evidence on dietary supplements for cognitive and mental health. It currently tracks 93 supplements across 13 categories, backed by 471+ research papers from journals indexed in PubMed and other major biomedical databases.

Is SupplementDB free to use?

Yes. SupplementDB is entirely free and open-access. There are no subscription fees, paywalls, or premium tiers. All supplement data, evidence ratings, citations, and research summaries are available to everyone at no cost.

Does SupplementDB sell supplements?

No. SupplementDB does not sell supplements, accept advertising from supplement manufacturers, or receive funding from the supplement industry. We are an independent research database. Commercial availability information is provided for reference only and does not constitute an endorsement of any product or brand.

Evidence & Research

How does SupplementDB rate supplement evidence?

We use a four-tier evidence rating system based on the quality, quantity, and consistency of available research:

  • Tier 1 — Strong Evidence: Multiple large RCTs and meta-analyses with consistent findings
  • Tier 2 — Moderate Evidence: Multiple RCTs with moderate sample sizes
  • Tier 3 — Preliminary Evidence: Limited RCTs, mostly observational data or small trials
  • Tier 4 — Emerging Evidence: Primarily preclinical or very early-phase human studies

Tiers reflect research quality and quantity, not supplement effectiveness. See our Evidence Rating System for full criteria.

What does the evidence tier badge color mean?

Evidence tier badges are color-coded for quick identification: Green (Tier 1, Strong Evidence) indicates robust research support from multiple large trials. Blue (Tier 2) reflects solid but less extensive research. Yellow (Tier 3) means limited clinical data. Gray (Tier 4) indicates primarily preclinical research.

How are citations verified?

Every citation undergoes multi-step verification: DOI validation to confirm it resolves to the correct paper, PMID cross-referencing against National Library of Medicine records, author and journal confirmation, study type classification, and sample size extraction. This ensures all referenced research is real, correctly attributed, and accurately categorized.

What study types does SupplementDB include?

We prioritize the highest quality evidence: systematic reviews, meta-analyses, network meta-analyses, and randomized controlled trials (RCTs). For supplements with limited clinical data, we also include cohort studies, observational research, and mechanistic/preclinical studies, with the evidence tier clearly reflecting the study quality.

How often is the database updated?

The database is updated on an ongoing basis as significant new research is published. Each supplement entry tracks its last evidence update date. Major updates typically follow the publication of new meta-analyses, large RCTs, or systematic reviews that meaningfully change the evidence landscape.

Supplement Safety

How should I interpret safety profiles?

Safety profiles are rated as Good (generally well-tolerated), Moderate (some adverse effects reported; monitoring recommended), or Caution (significant interactions or contraindications documented). Each profile includes common side effects, contraindications, and known drug interactions as reported in the research literature.

These ratings are based on studied populations and may not reflect your individual risk factors. Always consult a healthcare provider before use.

Are dosage ranges prescriptive?

No. Dosage ranges listed in SupplementDB reflect what has been used in published clinical studies, not personal recommendations. They include standardization requirements (e.g., "300mg daily standardized to 55% bacosides"), studied populations, and optimal study durations. Individual dosing should always be determined in consultation with a healthcare provider.

Is SupplementDB a substitute for medical advice?

No. SupplementDB is an educational resource that presents published research findings. It is not a healthcare provider and does not offer medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment recommendations. Always consult a qualified healthcare professional before starting, stopping, or changing any supplement regimen. See our full medical disclaimer.

Using the Database

How do I search and filter supplements?

Use the search bar on the main page to search by supplement name, scientific name, or common names. Filter by category (e.g., Nootropics, Adaptogens, Sleep Support) using the category dropdown, or filter by evidence tier to focus on supplements with stronger research support. You can also sort results by name, evidence tier, or category.

How do I read a supplement card?

Each supplement card displays the supplement name, scientific name, category, and evidence tier badge. Click or tap a card to view the full detail panel, which includes primary benefits, dosage information, mechanisms of action, safety profile, effect sizes, and all supporting citations with DOI links to the original research papers.

Can I suggest a supplement for inclusion?

Yes. Contact us at research@supplementdb.com with the supplement name and any relevant published research. For a supplement to be considered, it must have at least one published study in a peer-reviewed journal indexed in PubMed or equivalent biomedical database.

What should I do if I find an error?

Please report any errors to research@supplementdb.com with the supplement name, the specific data point in question, and the correct information with a supporting citation (DOI or PMID). We review all correction requests and update records as needed.
