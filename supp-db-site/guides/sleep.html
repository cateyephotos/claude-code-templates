Tier 1

Strong Evidence

Supplements supported by multiple meta-analyses or large randomized controlled trials with consistent replication across populations.

Melatonin

Tier 1

N-acetyl-5-methoxytryptamine

DOSAGE
0.5–3 mg · 30–60 min before bed
MT1/MT2 receptor activation Circadian rhythm regulation Antioxidant activity
Sleep Onset

Reduces sleep onset latency by 23.5 minutes in children and adolescents with consistent efficacy across age groups.

Ferracioli-Oda E, Qawasmi A, Bloch MH. PLoS One, 2013. Meta-analysis of 19 RCTs. PMID: 23691095

Sleep Quality

PSQI improvement WMD −1.24 (95% CI −1.77 to −0.71). Strongest effects in respiratory diseases (WMD −2.20).

Gholami F, Moradi L, Ziaei R, et al. Journal of Neurology, 2022. 23 RCTs. PMID: 33417003

Shift Work

Improved sleep quality in day sleep after night shift work (MD 0.17, 95% CI 0.09–0.25). Effective for circadian rhythm adjustment.

Liira J, Verbeek JH, Costa G, et al. Cochrane Database of Systematic Reviews, 2014. 9 studies, 526 participants. PMID: 25113164

Safety: Very Low Risk

Adverse events rate 12.2% vs 10.8% placebo. Most common: headache (2.7%), dizziness (2.2%), nausea (1.8%). No serious adverse events. No habituation or withdrawal effects.

Besag FMC, Vasey MJ, Lao KSJ, et al. CNS Drugs, 2019. PMID: 31722088

Magnesium

Tier 1

Various forms — glycinate preferred for sleep

DOSAGE
200–400 mg elemental · daily
GABA receptor modulation NMDA receptor antagonism Melatonin/cortisol regulation
Sleep Onset

Sleep onset latency reduced by 17.36 minutes vs placebo (p=0.0006). Total sleep time improved by 16.06 minutes.

Mah J, Pitre T. BMC Complementary Medicine and Therapies, 2021. Meta-analysis, 151 participants. PMID: 33865376 · DOI

Hormone Regulation

500 mg daily improved objective sleep measures: increased serum melatonin, decreased cortisol, and normalized renin concentrations in elderly subjects.

Abbasi B, Kimiagar M, Sadeghniiat K, et al. Journal of Research in Medical Sciences, 2012. Double-blind placebo-controlled RCT. PMID: 23853635

Mechanism

Potentiates GABA(A) receptor function at physiological concentrations (0.1–1 mM Mg²⁺), enhancing inhibitory neurotransmission relevant to sleep initiation.

Möykkynen T, Uusi-Oukari M, et al. NeuroReport, 2001. PMID: 11447329 · DOI

Safety: Low Risk

GI effects primarily with oxide form at high doses. Chelated forms (glycinate, malate) preferred for tolerability and bioavailability. Separate from antibiotics (tetracyclines, quinolones), bisphosphonates, and PPIs by 2–3 hours.

Gröber U, Schmidt J, Kisters K. Magnesium and Drugs. International Journal of Molecular Sciences, 2019. PMID: 31035385 · DOI

Tier 2

Moderate Evidence

Supplements supported by multiple smaller RCTs or a single meta-analysis with moderate confidence. Promising but requiring further replication.

L-Theanine

Tier 2

γ-Glutamylethylamide · found naturally in tea

DOSAGE
100–400 mg · 200 mg typical for sleep
GABA system modulation Alpha brain wave enhancement Glutamate receptor antagonism
Sleep Quality

Significant improvement in sleep quality scores and reduced time to fall asleep without next-day drowsiness across 19 articles and 897 participants.

Bulman SL, et al. Sleep Medicine Reviews, 2025. Systematic review and meta-analysis. PMID: 40056718

Stress Reduction

12.92–17.98% reduction in perceived stress scores across multiple validated scales in 11 studies across 6 countries (897 participants).

Hidese S, et al. Nutrients, 2019. PMID: 31623400

Safety: Very Low Risk

FDA GRAS status up to 250 mg per serving. No serious adverse events in 28-day studies at 400 mg/day. Safe and well-tolerated in children with ADHD at 400 mg daily for 6 weeks.

Lyon MR, et al. Alternative Medicine Review, 2011. PMID: 22214254

Passionflower

Tier 2

Passiflora incarnata

DOSAGE
250–800 mg extract · daily
GABA-A positive allosteric modulation MAO-A inhibition
Anxiety & Sleep

Anxiolytic effect comparable to oxazepam in generalized anxiety disorder patients, with moderate improvements in polysomnographic sleep quality measures. Particularly effective for anxiety-driven insomnia.

Traditional herbal remedy with moderate modern clinical evidence from multiple human RCTs.

Safety: Low Risk

Good traditional safety profile with centuries of use. Limited long-term clinical safety data. Often combined with other calming herbs (valerian, lemon balm).

Ashwagandha

Tier 2

Withania somnifera · KSM-66 or Sensoril extracts

DOSAGE
300–600 mg · standardized extract
HPA axis modulation Cortisol reduction GABAergic enhancement
Cortisol Reduction

Significant cortisol reduction MD = −2.58 (95% CI −4.99 to −0.16) compared to placebo across multiple RCTs with 491 participants.

Arumugam V, Vijayakumar V, et al. Explore (NY), 2024. Meta-analysis of 8 RCTs. PMID: 39348746 · DOI

Sleep

Systematic review and meta-analysis confirms sleep improvement in stressed adults, primarily through cortisol lowering and HPA axis normalization.

Cheah KL, et al. PLOS ONE, 2021. PMID: 34559859

Safety: Low Risk

Well-tolerated in clinical trials. Monitor thyroid function — ashwagandha may affect thyroid hormone levels. Best suited for stress-driven insomnia rather than primary sleep disorders.

Tier 3

Preliminary Evidence

Supplements with strong mechanistic rationale but limited human clinical trials. Promising areas requiring further controlled research.

5-HTP

Tier 3

5-hydroxytryptophan · serotonin precursor

DOSAGE
50–300 mg · daily
Serotonin precursor (bypasses TPH) Serotonin → melatonin conversion
Mechanism

Crosses the blood-brain barrier freely and bypasses the rate-limiting tryptophan hydroxylase enzyme, directly enhancing serotonin synthesis. Serotonin is then converted to melatonin via N-acetyltransferase.

Birdsall TC. Alternative Medicine Review, 1998. PMID: 9727088
Turner EH, Loftis JM, Blackwell AD. Pharmacology & Therapeutics, 2006. PMID: 16023217 · DOI

Safety: Caution Required

Do NOT combine with SSRIs, SNRIs, or MAOIs — risk of serotonin syndrome, a potentially life-threatening condition. GI side effects possible. Consult prescribing physician if on any serotonergic medication.

GABA

Tier 3

Gamma-aminobutyric acid · primary inhibitory neurotransmitter

DOSAGE
250–750 mg · daily
GABA receptor activation Cortisol reduction
Sleep Regulation

GABA is the brain's primary inhibitory neurotransmitter and essential for sleep-wake cycle regulation. Comprehensive mechanistic evidence establishes its role in sleep initiation and maintenance.

Gottesmann C. Neuroscience, 2002. PMID: 11983310 · DOI

Note: Active debate on whether oral GABA supplements effectively cross the blood-brain barrier. Biosynthetic (fermented) GABA may have improved absorption. Often more effective in combination with other sleep-supporting compounds.
Safety: Low Risk

Well-established safety profile. As the body's primary inhibitory neurotransmitter, GABA is endogenously produced. Limited evidence for adverse effects at supplemental doses.

Understanding Evidence Tiers

Tier 1

Strong Evidence

Multiple meta-analyses or large randomized controlled trials with consistent positive findings replicated across independent research groups and populations.

Tier 2

Moderate Evidence

Multiple smaller RCTs or a single meta-analysis with moderate confidence. Promising direction with some methodological limitations or need for replication.

Tier 3

Preliminary Evidence

Limited human clinical trials with strong mechanistic basis from in vitro or animal studies. Requires controlled clinical research to confirm benefits.

Safety Considerations

Always consult a healthcare provider before starting any supplement regimen, especially if you are taking prescription medications, pregnant, nursing, or managing a chronic condition.

Key Drug Interactions

Supplement Interacts With Risk Level Guidance
5-HTP SSRIs, SNRIs, MAOIs, triptans High Do not combine. Serotonin syndrome risk.
Magnesium Tetracycline/quinolone antibiotics, bisphosphonates, PPIs Moderate Separate doses by 2–3 hours.
Melatonin Blood thinners, immunosuppressants, diabetes medications Moderate Monitor with physician guidance.
Ashwagandha Thyroid medications, immunosuppressants, sedatives Moderate Monitor thyroid levels. May enhance sedation.
L-Theanine Blood pressure medications Low May enhance blood pressure lowering effects.

Pregnancy & Nursing

Insufficient safety data for most sleep supplements during pregnancy or breastfeeding. Melatonin is not recommended. Consult your OB/GYN.

Quality Assurance

Look for third-party tested products from independent organizations: USP, NSF International, or ConsumerLab. These verify potency and purity.
