Supplements That Interact With Blood Pressure Medications

Several supplements lower blood pressure. Combined with antihypertensive medications, they can cause blood pressure to drop too far — producing dizziness, lightheadedness, or fainting, especially on standing up.

Why this class matters

Mechanism: Additive hypotensive effect — supplements may cause vasodilation, natriuresis, or modulate renin-angiotensin signaling.

What to do: Monitor blood pressure at home when starting or stopping these supplements. Dose adjustments to antihypertensive medication may be needed. Rise slowly from sitting/lying positions.

Representative drugs:

  • Lisinopril (ACE inhibitor)
  • Losartan (ARB)
  • Amlodipine (CCB)
  • Metoprolol (beta blocker)
  • Hydrochlorothiazide
