Supplements That Interact With Immunosuppressants

Some supplements boost immune activity (cytokine production, T-cell or NK-cell function), which can counteract the intended action of immunosuppressant medications used for organ transplants, autoimmune disease, or certain cancers.

Why this class matters

Mechanism: Opposing pharmacodynamic effect — immune-stimulating botanicals may reduce the therapeutic effect of immunosuppression.

What to do: If you are on immunosuppressants for transplant, autoimmune disease, or active cancer treatment, avoid immune-modulating supplements unless explicitly approved by your specialist.

Representative drugs:

  • Cyclosporine
  • Tacrolimus
  • Methotrexate
  • Azathioprine
  • Mycophenolate
  • Biologics (e.g. adalimumab, infliximab)
