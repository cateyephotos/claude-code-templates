1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are stored on your device (computer, tablet, or mobile) when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently, provide a better user experience, and supply information to site operators.

Similar technologies include local storage, session storage, and pixel tags, which function in comparable ways.

In plain language: Cookies are tiny data files saved on your device by websites you visit. They help websites remember things about your visit.

2. How We Use Cookies

SupplementDB uses cookies and similar technologies primarily for analytics purposes to understand how visitors use our Website. We do not use cookies for:

  • Advertising or ad retargeting
  • User tracking across other websites
  • Storing personal information (names, emails, etc.)
  • Social media integration or tracking

5. Managing Your Cookie Preferences

You have several options for managing cookies:

5.1 Browser Settings

Most web browsers allow you to control cookies through their settings. You can typically:

  • View which cookies are stored on your device
  • Delete all or specific cookies
  • Block cookies from specific or all websites
  • Set your browser to notify you when a cookie is set

Common browser cookie settings:

  • Google Chrome
  • Mozilla Firefox
  • Apple Safari
  • Microsoft Edge

5.2 Do Not Track

Some browsers offer a "Do Not Track" (DNT) setting. While there is no universal standard for how websites should respond to DNT signals, we respect your privacy preferences and encourage you to use browser-based privacy tools.

5.3 Opt-Out of PostHog Analytics

You can opt out of PostHog analytics tracking by:

  • Using a browser extension that blocks analytics scripts (e.g., uBlock Origin, Privacy Badger)
  • Enabling your browser's built-in tracking protection
  • Clearing your browser's local storage and session storage for this site
Note: Blocking all cookies may affect your experience on the Website. Strictly necessary cookies (UI preferences) help the site function as expected.
