Privacy Policy

Last Updated: March 8, 2026

1. Introduction

SupplementDB ("we," "us," or "our") operates the website www.supplementdb.com ("the Website"). This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you visit our Website.

By using the Website, you consent to the data practices described in this Privacy Policy. If you do not agree with these practices, please do not use the Website.

In plain language: This document explains what data we collect when you visit SupplementDB and how we use it. By using our site, you agree to these practices.

2. Information We Collect

2.1 Automatically Collected Information

When you visit the Website, we may automatically collect certain information about your device and usage, including:

  • Device information — Browser type, operating system, device type, screen resolution
  • Usage data — Pages visited, time spent on pages, clicks, scrolls, and navigation patterns
  • Referral data — How you arrived at the Website (search engine, direct link, referral)
  • Location data — Approximate geographic location derived from your IP address (country/region level only)
  • Session data — Session duration, page load times, and interaction events

2.2 Information We Do Not Collect

SupplementDB does not:

  • Require user accounts or registration
  • Collect names, email addresses, or other personal identifiers through the Website itself
  • Process payment or financial information
  • Collect health or medical records
  • Knowingly collect information from children under 13 years of age

In plain language: We collect basic, anonymous usage data (what pages you visit, how long you stay) to understand how people use the site. We do not require accounts, collect personal details, or store health information.

3. Analytics and Tracking Technologies

We use PostHog, a product analytics platform, to understand how visitors interact with our Website. PostHog collects:

  • Page views and navigation paths
  • Click and interaction events (autocapture)
  • Page leave events
  • Session replay data (if enabled)
  • Device and browser characteristics

In plain language: We use PostHog to see which pages are popular, how people navigate the site, and where they click. This helps us improve the database. PostHog processes this data in the US.

4. Cookies and Similar Technologies

Our Website uses cookies and similar tracking technologies. For comprehensive information about the cookies we use, their purposes, and how to manage your preferences, please see our Cookie Policy.

5. How We Use Your Information

We use the automatically collected information to:

  • Improve our Website — Understand which supplements and guides are most visited to prioritize content updates
  • Analyze usage patterns — Identify how visitors navigate the database to improve user experience
  • Monitor performance — Track page load times and technical issues to maintain site reliability
  • Understand our audience — Aggregate demographic data (country/region level) to understand our user base

We do not use collected data for:

  • Targeted advertising or ad retargeting
  • Selling or renting personal information to third parties
  • Creating individual user profiles linked to personal identities
  • Making automated decisions that affect individuals

6. Data Sharing and Disclosure

We do not sell, trade, or rent your personal information. We may share anonymized, aggregated data in the following limited circumstances:

  • Service providers — With PostHog for analytics processing, subject to their privacy policy and data processing agreements
  • Legal requirements — When required by law, subpoena, court order, or governmental regulation
  • Protection of rights — When necessary to protect our rights, safety, or property, or that of our users or the public

7. Data Retention

Analytics data collected through PostHog is retained according to PostHog's standard data retention policies. We do not maintain long-term personal data storage beyond what is necessary for analytics purposes.

You may request deletion of any data associated with your browsing session by contacting us at the address provided below.

8. Your Privacy Rights

Depending on your location, you may have certain rights regarding your personal information:

8.1 For All Users

  • Opt-out of analytics — You can disable PostHog tracking by using browser privacy settings, enabling Do Not Track, or using browser extensions that block analytics scripts
  • Cookie management — You can manage or delete cookies through your browser settings (see our Cookie Policy)

8.2 For European Economic Area (EEA) Residents — GDPR

Under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR), you have the right to:

  • Access — Request a copy of the data we hold about you
  • Rectification — Request correction of inaccurate data
  • Erasure — Request deletion of your personal data ("right to be forgotten")
  • Restriction — Request that we limit processing of your data
  • Portability — Request your data in a portable format
  • Objection — Object to our processing of your data

Our legal basis for processing data is legitimate interest (improving our Website and understanding usage patterns).

8.3 For California Residents — CCPA

Under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA), you have the right to:

  • Know what personal information is collected about you
  • Know whether your personal information is sold or disclosed and to whom
  • Request deletion of your personal information
  • Not be discriminated against for exercising your privacy rights

We do not sell personal information. To exercise any of these rights, please contact us using the information below.

In plain language: You can opt out of our analytics tracking using your browser settings. If you're in the EU or California, you have additional legal rights over your data. We never sell your information to anyone.

9. Data Security

We implement reasonable technical and organizational measures to protect the information we collect. However, no method of electronic transmission or storage is 100% secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.

Our security measures include:

  • HTTPS encryption for all data in transit
  • Use of reputable third-party service providers with their own security certifications
  • Regular review of our data collection and processing practices

These third-party sites have their own privacy policies, and we are not responsible for their data practices. We encourage you to review the privacy policies of any third-party sites you visit through our links.

11. Children's Privacy

The Website is not intended for children under 13 years of age. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13. If we become aware that we have collected information from a child under 13, we will take steps to delete that information promptly.

12. International Data Transfers

By using the Website, you consent to the transfer of your information to the United States.
