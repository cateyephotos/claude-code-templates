Overview

Bacopa monnieri is classified as nootropics with moderate evidence (Tier 2) for its primary applications. Multiple RCTs with moderate sample sizes

Well-established nootropic herb with strong evidence for memory enhancement and cognitive function from multiple RCTs and meta-analyses. Bacosides act through antioxidant neuroprotection, acetylcholinesterase inhibition, serotonergic/cholinergic modulation, BDNF upregulation, and anti-inflammatory activity via microglial cytokine suppression. Meta-analyses confirm improvements in attention speed and memory recall at 300-600 mg/day standardized extract over 12+ weeks. Network meta-analysis of 29 RCTs (2,107 subjects) shows high-dose Bacopa (600+ mg/day) as top-ranked for working memory.

Key study populations: Healthy adults 18-65; Elderly with cognitive complaints

The extract also inhibited acetylcholinesterase activity in the brain.

Safety & Tolerability

Preclinical safety evaluation establishes no-observed adverse effect level (NOAEL) of 500 mg/kg in...

Good Safety Profile

(2007) —

Joshua Allan et al. evaluated BacoMind standardized extract safety across multiple toxicity studies. Single oral LD50 was 2,400 mg/kg. A 90-day subchronic study at 85, 210, and 500 mg/kg revealed no evidence of toxicity in clinical signs, neurological examination, hematological parameters, blood biochemistry, organ weights, or histopathology. NOAEL was established at 500 mg/kg body weight.

PubMed: 17560704

(2013) —

Stough et al. reviewed a decade of research at Swinburne University on the CDRI 08 extract of Bacopa monnieri. Studies indicated that CDRI 08 has several modes of action on the human brain with promising indications for cognitive improvement in the elderly and patients with neurodegenerative disorders. The extract demonstrated a consistent safety and tolerability profile across multiple trials.

Good Safety Profile

Systematic Overview of Bacopa monnieri (L.) Wettst. Dominant Poly-Herbal Formulas in Children and Adolescents.

Kean JD, Downey LA, Stough C (2017) — Medicines (Basel, Switzerland)

Kean et al. systematically reviewed clinical trials of Bacopa-dominant poly-herbal formulas in children and adolescents. Five studies reported sufficient data for effect size analysis with improvements in behavioral outcomes and cognitive abilities. However, only 44% of studies adequately reported safety and tolerability data, highlighting the need for better safety reporting in pediatric populations.
