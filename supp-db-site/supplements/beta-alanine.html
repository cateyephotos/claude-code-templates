Overview

Beta-Alanine is classified as performance enhancers with preliminary evidence (Tier 3) for its primary applications. Strong evidence for exercise endurance and muscle buffering capacity via carnosine synthesis

Performance supplement with strong evidence for exercise endurance and muscle buffering capacity

Key study populations: Athletes; Resistance trainees; Endurance athletes; Military personnel

Mechanisms of Action

1

Carnosine synthesis in skeletal muscle

2

Intracellular pH buffering during high-intensity exercise

3

Delayed onset of neuromuscular fatigue

4

Enhanced Type II muscle fiber carnosine content

Benefits

Cognitive Benefits

  • Sustained focus during exercise
  • Reduced mental fatigue during high-intensity efforts

Non-Cognitive Benefits

  • Exercise endurance
  • Muscle buffering
  • High-intensity performance
  • Carnosine elevation

Safety Profile

Overall Safety Rating: Good

Common Side Effects

  • Paraesthesia (tingling)
  • Skin flushing at high single doses

Contraindications

  • Pregnancy (limited data)
  • Taurine depletion concerns at very high doses

Drug Interactions

  • May interact with taurine metabolism
  • No major drug interactions reported
