Overview

Chlorella is classified as antioxidants with preliminary evidence (Tier 3) for its primary applications. Moderate evidence for detoxification and nutritional benefits; limited evidence for cognitive effects

Multiple meta-analyses of 19+ RCTs (n=797) demonstrate significant reductions in total cholesterol, LDL-C, blood pressure, and fasting glucose; immune-enhancing effects shown in RCT via NK cell activation; detoxification evidence limited to very small crossover trials; comprehensive mechanistic understanding of bioactive compounds including chlorophyll, Chlorella Growth Factor, and polysaccharides

Key study populations: Heavy metal exposure; Digestive issues; Immune support; Nutritional deficiency

Mechanisms of Action

Cardiovascular protection via anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and lipid-modulating pathways

Moderate

The effects of Chlorella vulgaris on cardiovascular risk factors: A comprehensive review on putative molecular mechanisms

Barghchi H, Dehnavi Z, Nattagh-Eshtivani E, Alwaily ER, Almulla AF, Kareem AK, Barati M, Ranjbar G, Mohammadzadeh A, Rahimi P, Pahlavani N (2023) — Biomedicine and Pharmacotherapy

Chlorella contains proteins, omega-3 fatty acids, polysaccharides, vitamins, and minerals,Anti-inflammatory activity via suppression of NF-kB and pro-inflammatory cytokines (TNF-alpha, IL-6),Antioxidant activity from chlorophyll, carotenoids (lutein, beta-carotene), and vitamin C/E,Lipid modulation through PPAR-alpha activation and cholesterol metabolism pathways,Insulin-sensitizing effects via AMPK pathway activation,Chlorella Growth Factor (CGF) — unique nucleotide-peptide complex with growth-promoting properties

PubMed: 37018990

Innate immune activation via NK cell stimulation and cytokine modulation

Moderate

Chlorella supplement health benefits in healthy Korean adults: randomized, double-blind, and placebo-controlled study

Kwak JH, Baek SH, Woo Y, Han JK, Kim BG, Kim OY, Shin JH (2012) — Nutrition Journal

NK cell activity significantly increased — demonstrates direct innate immune enhancement,IFN-gamma (Th1 cytokine) significantly increased — promotes antiviral and antitumor immunity,IL-1beta significantly increased — activates acute phase immune response,IL-12 and TNF-alpha trends toward increase,Polysaccharides (beta-glucans) and Chlorella Growth Factor likely responsible for immunostimulation

Clinical Benefits

Reduces total cholesterol, LDL-C, blood pressure, and fasting blood glucose across multiple RCTs

Moderate Well-replicated (19 RCTs pooled in meta-analysis)

Effect of Chlorella supplementation on cardiovascular risk factors: A meta-analysis of randomized controlled trials

Fallah AA, Sarmast E, Habibian Dehkordi S, Engardeh J, Mahmoodnia L, Khaledifar A, Jafari T (2017) — Clinical Nutrition

Total cholesterol reduced by -9.09 mg/dL (95% CI: -12.91 to -5.26, p<0.001),LDL-C reduced by -8.32 mg/dL (95% CI: -12.22 to -4.42, p<0.001),Systolic BP reduced by -4.51 mmHg (95% CI: -6.53 to -2.48, p<0.001),Diastolic BP reduced by -1.64 mmHg (95% CI: -3.28 to -0.01, p=0.049),Fasting blood glucose reduced by -4.23 mg/dL (95% CI: -8.29 to -0.17, p=0.041),No significant effects on TG, HDL-C, or BMI,Subgroup analysis: doses >4g/day and duration ≥8 weeks most effective

PubMed: 29037431

The effects of Chlorella supplementation on lipid profiles: A systematic review and dose-response meta-analysis of randomized controlled trials

Sherafati N, Bideshki MV, Ranjbar G, Saneei P (2022) — Complementary Therapies in Medicine

Significant reduction in total cholesterol (-7.47 mg/dL, p<0.05),Significant reduction in LDL-C (-7.71 mg/dL, p<0.05),No significant effect on triglycerides or HDL-C,Dose-response analysis: LDL-C reduction significant at 0-1500 mg/day range,Results largely consistent with Fallah 2017 meta-analysis

PubMed: 35331862

Reduces liver enzyme AST levels, especially in patients with non-alcoholic fatty liver disease

Moderate Replicated across 2 independent meta-analyses

Effect of Chlorella vulgaris on Liver Function Biomarkers: a Systematic Review and Meta-Analysis

Yarmohammadi H, Bagheri B, Saadatnia M, Esfahani MHN (2021) — Clinical Nutrition Research

Significant reduction in AST (-9.15 U/L) across all subjects,Larger AST reduction in NAFLD patients specifically (-16.42 U/L),No significant effect on ALT or ALP in overall analysis,Beneficial hepatoprotective effects especially in fatty liver disease

PubMed: 33564655

The effect of Chlorella vulgaris supplementation on health-related indices in patients with non-alcoholic fatty liver disease: A GRADE-assessed systematic review and dose-response meta-analysis

Jafari N, et al. (2026) — BMC Cardiovascular Disorders

GRADE-assessed evidence for Chlorella benefits in NAFLD,Dose-response meta-analysis of health-related indices,Supports hepatoprotective effects of Chlorella supplementation,Most recent comprehensive meta-analysis on Chlorella in liver disease

PubMed: 41652544

Enhances innate immune function by increasing NK cell activity and pro-inflammatory cytokine production

Moderate Single well-designed RCT; consistent with preclinical data

Beneficial immunostimulatory effect of short-term Chlorella supplementation: enhancement of natural killer cell activity and early inflammatory response (randomized, double-blinded, placebo-controlled trial)

Kwak JH, Baek SH, Woo Y, Han JK, Kim BG, Kim OY, Shin JH (2012) — Nutrition Journal

Significant increase in NK cell activity in Chlorella group (p<0.05),Significant increase in serum IFN-gamma levels (p<0.05),Significant increase in IL-1beta levels (p<0.001),Supplementation at 5g/day Chlorella vulgaris tablets,Demonstrates immune-stimulatory effects in healthy population

The effect of algae supplementation on anthropometric indices: A systematic review and meta-analysis of randomized controlled trials

Kazeminejad F, et al. (2025) — Nutrition Reviews

BMI reduction: -0.27 kg/m2 (statistically significant),Body weight reduction: -0.78 kg (statistically significant),Waist circumference reduction: -0.68 cm (statistically significant),Body fat reduction: -0.65 kg (statistically significant),GRADE certainty rated low to very low,Note: Includes both Spirulina and Chlorella studies; Chlorella-specific effects may differ

PubMed: 39461896

May facilitate detoxification of environmental pollutants (PAHs, heterocyclic amines) through epigenetic modulation and interference with absorption

Weak Two very small crossover trials (n=6-12); preliminary evidence only

Yang M, Youn JI, Kim SJ, Park JY (2015) — Environmental Toxicology and Pharmacology

Chlorella reduced global DNA hypermethylation status vs placebo (p=0.04),Marginally reduced urinary 1-hydroxypyrene (PAH metabolite, p<0.1),Positive association between methylation changes and PAH metabolite reduction (p<0.01),Suggests Chlorella aids PAH detoxification through epigenetic modulation

PubMed: 26432772

Detoxification of chlorella supplement on heterocyclic amines in Korean young adults

Marginally significant reduction in urinary MeIQx (heterocyclic amine metabolite, 0.05<p<0.1),No significant effect on urinary PhIP or IQx-8-COOH,Effects likely via interference with absorption or metabolism of carcinogenic HCAs,Very small sample size limits conclusions

PubMed: 25590673

Safety & Tolerability

Well tolerated across multiple clinical trials at doses up to 10g/day with no serious adverse events

Low

A review of recent clinical trials of the nutritional supplement Chlorella pyrenoidosa in the treatment of fibromyalgia, hypertension, and ulcerative colitis

Merchant RE, Andre CA (2001) — Alternative Therapies in Health and Medicine

10g/day Chlorella tablets + 100mL Chlorella extract well tolerated across all three conditions,Quality of life questionnaires indicated improved perception of health,No serious adverse events reported at high dose (10g/day),Potential benefits in fibromyalgia, hypertension, and ulcerative colitis (pilot data)

PubMed: 11347287

Nutritional supplementation with Chlorella pyrenoidosa for mild to moderate hypertension

Merchant RE, Andre CA, Sica DA (2002) — Journal of Medicinal Food

10g/day Chlorella tablets + 100mL Chlorella extract for 2 months,25% of subjects (6/24) achieved blood pressure goal (SiDBP <90 mmHg),Non-responders did not experience BP increase above baseline,Quality of life significantly improved as perceived by participants,No serious adverse events reported

PubMed: 12495586

Chlorella can bioaccumulate heavy metals from its growth environment, requiring quality-controlled sourcing

Low-Moderate

Chlorella vulgaris as a food substitute: Applications and benefits in the food industry

Wang CA, Onyeaka H, Miri T, Soltani F (2024) — Journal of Food Science

Chlorella can accumulate heavy metals from its growth environment,Stringent quality control measures required for commercial products,Sensory issues (taste, smell) may affect compliance,Clinical trials using quality-controlled products show good safety profile,Third-party testing recommended to verify absence of contaminants

PubMed: 39556490

Dosage Research

Meta-analytic subgroup analysis shows doses >4g/day for ≥8 weeks are most effective for cardiovascular outcomes; 5g/day is the most studied dose for immune benefits

Effect of Chlorella supplementation on cardiovascular risk factors: A meta-analysis of randomized controlled trials

Fallah AA, Sarmast E, Habibian Dehkordi S, Engardeh J, Mahmoodnia L, Khaledifar A, Jafari T (2017) — Clinical Nutrition

Subgroup analysis: doses >4g/day significantly reduced TC, LDL-C, SBP, and DBP,Duration ≥8 weeks showed significant effects across cardiovascular markers,Lower doses (<4g/day) showed less consistent effects,Individual RCTs used 3-10g/day; 5g/day is the most common dose in well-designed trials

PubMed: 29037431

Chlorella supplement health benefits in healthy Korean adults: randomized, double-blind, and placebo-controlled study

Kwak JH, Baek SH, Woo Y, Han JK, Kim BG, Kim OY, Shin JH (2012) — Nutrition Journal

5g/day Chlorella vulgaris tablets for 8 weeks — effective for immune outcomes,NK cell activity and IFN-gamma significantly improved at this dose,8-week duration sufficient for measurable immune enhancement

PubMed: 22849818
