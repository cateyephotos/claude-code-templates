Overview

Citrulline Malate is classified as performance enhancers with preliminary evidence (Tier 3) for its primary applications. Moderate evidence for exercise performance and nitric oxide enhancement with mixed results across studies

Performance supplement with moderate evidence for exercise performance and nitric oxide enhancement

Key study populations: Resistance trainees; Endurance athletes; Recreational exercisers

Mechanisms of Action

1

Arginine-nitric oxide pathway enhancement

2

Ammonia detoxification via urea cycle

3

Malate contribution to aerobic energy production

4

Vasodilation and improved blood flow

Benefits

Cognitive Benefits

  • Reduced mental fatigue during exercise
  • Enhanced blood flow to brain during exertion

Non-Cognitive Benefits

  • Nitric oxide production
  • Blood flow enhancement
  • Exercise endurance
  • Reduced muscle soreness

Safety Profile

Overall Safety Rating: Good

Common Side Effects

  • Mild GI discomfort at high doses
  • Possible nausea

Contraindications

  • Citrullinemia (rare genetic disorder)
  • Pregnancy (limited data)

Drug Interactions

  • PDE5 inhibitors (additive vasodilation)
  • Nitrates
  • Blood pressure medications
