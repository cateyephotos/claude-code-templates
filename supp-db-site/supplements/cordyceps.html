Overview

Cordyceps is classified as herbal extracts with moderate evidence (Tier 2) for its primary applications. Strong evidence for exercise performance and energy; moderate evidence for cognitive benefits

Rich mechanistic literature on cordycepin, adenosine, and polysaccharide bioactives; however, clinical RCTs show inconsistent results for exercise performance — positive in sedentary elderly but negative in trained cyclists, with many studies using multi-ingredient blends that confound attribution

Key study populations: Athletes; Chronic fatigue; Elderly; Respiratory conditions

Cordycepin and adenosine nucleoside bioactivity

Moderate

A review on Cordyceps (Caterpillar Fungus) neuroprotective bioactives and their therapeutic implications in neurodegenerative diseases

Sharma H, Upadhyay SK, Chand U, Sood S, Pathak D (2024) — Nutrients

Cordycepin (3'-deoxyadenosine) is the primary bioactive nucleoside in Cordyceps,Acts as adenosine receptor agonist affecting cellular energy metabolism,Key bioactives include adenosine, β-glucans, cordycepin, and ergosterol,Antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, and anti-apoptotic mechanisms documented,Potential neuroprotective effects via BDNF upregulation and neuroinflammation reduction

Research progress of cordycepin in the treatment of tumors

He R, Zhou W (2024) — Molecular Medicine Reports

Cordycepin inhibits MAPK/ERK and Hedgehog signaling pathways,Induces apoptosis and suppresses proliferation in tumor cell lines,Enhances sensitivity to radiotherapy in preclinical models,Demonstrates anti-inflammatory effects via NF-κB pathway modulation

Polysaccharide-mediated immunomodulation and antioxidant activity

Moderate

Bioactive compounds from Cordyceps and their therapeutic potential

Krishna KV, Ulhas RS, Li J, Yang B, Zheng M, Lu Y (2024) — Critical Reviews in Biotechnology

Over 200 bioactive metabolites identified including nucleosides, polysaccharides, proteins, and sterols,Polysaccharides (β-glucans) are primary immunomodulatory compounds,Anti-microbial, anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and immunomodulatory activities documented,Cordyceps polysaccharides enhance macrophage phagocytosis and NK cell activity,Ergosterol and fatty acids contribute to membrane-protective antioxidant effects

Clinical Benefits

Improves submaximal exercise thresholds (metabolic and ventilatory) in sedentary elderly adults

Moderate Single positive RCT; not replicated in trained populations

Effect of Cs-4 (Cordyceps sinensis) on exercise performance in healthy older subjects: a double-blind, placebo-controlled trial

Chen S, Li Z, Krochmal R, Abrazado M, Kim W, Cooper CB (2010) — Journal of Alternative and Complementary Medicine

Metabolic threshold increased 10.5% in Cs-4 group vs placebo (p<0.02),Ventilatory threshold increased 8.5% in Cs-4 group (p<0.04),No significant change in VO2max, peak work rate, or peak ventilation,Improvements were in submaximal exercise capacity, not maximal performance

PubMed: 20804368

Cordyceps militaris Improves Tolerance to High-Intensity Exercise After Acute and Chronic Supplementation

Hirsch KR, Smith-Ryan AE, Roelofs EJ, Trexler ET, Mock MG (2017) — Journal of Dietary Supplements

After 3 weeks: VO2max improved by 4.8 ml/kg/min (p=0.042) in mushroom blend group,Time to exhaustion increased by 69.8 seconds after 3 weeks (significant via 95% CI),After 1 week: time to exhaustion improved by 28.1 seconds (95% CI did not cross zero),Note: Used 4g/day mushroom blend containing Cordyceps militaris, not pure cordyceps

PubMed: 27408987

CordyMax Cs-4 supplementation does not improve endurance exercise performance

Parcell AC, Smith JM, Schulthies SS, Myrer JW, Fellingham G (2004) — International Journal of Sport Nutrition and Exercise Metabolism

No significant effect on VO2peak in trained cyclists (p>0.05),No significant effect on ventilatory threshold (p>0.05),No significant effect on time trial performance (p>0.05),Negative result in trained athletes contrasts positive finding in sedentary elderly (Chen 2010)

PubMed: 15118196

Low

Dosage Research

Clinical trials have used 1-3g/day Cs-4 extract or 4g/day mushroom powder, with 12 weeks minimum for exercise benefits in elderly

Effect of Cs-4 (Cordyceps sinensis) on exercise performance in healthy older subjects: a double-blind, placebo-controlled trial

Chen S, Li Z, Krochmal R, Abrazado M, Kim W, Cooper CB (2010) — Journal of Alternative and Complementary Medicine

Cs-4 (CordyMax) 333mg three times daily (approximately 1g/day total) for 12 weeks,Positive results at this dose for submaximal exercise thresholds in elderly,12-week duration was sufficient for measurable improvements

PubMed: 20804368

CordyMax Cs-4 supplementation does not improve endurance exercise performance

Parcell AC, Smith JM, Schulthies SS, Myrer JW, Fellingham G (2004) — International Journal of Sport Nutrition and Exercise Metabolism

CordyMax Cs-4 at 3g/day for 5 weeks showed no ergogenic effect in trained cyclists,Higher dose (3x the Chen 2010 dose) was ineffective in trained population,5-week duration may have been insufficient, or trained athletes may not respond
