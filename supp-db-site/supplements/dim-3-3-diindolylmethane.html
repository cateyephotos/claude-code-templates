Background

Overview

DIM (3,3'-Diindolylmethane) is classified as metabolic support with tier 3: preliminary evidence for its primary applications. Multiple small-to-moderate RCTs (n=12-551) demonstrate estrogen metabolism modulation (2-OHE1:16a-OHE1 ratio shift). Phase I/II cancer prevention trials show biomarker changes but no direct cancer outcome data. One systematic review of 22 human trials confirms safety but notes absence of efficacy evidence for treating established cancers. Cervical dysplasia trials show mixed results.

Key study populations: Premenopausal women; Postmenopausal women on HRT; Women with cervical dysplasia (CIN I-III); Women taking tamoxifen for breast cancer; Men with castrate-resistant prostate cancer; Patients with thyroid proliferative disease; Healthy adults

How It Works

Mechanisms of Action

1

Modulates estrogen metabolism by shifting 2-hydroxylation:16a-hydroxylation ratio via CYP1A1/CYP1A2 induction

2

Aryl hydrocarbon receptor (AhR) agonist/modulator affecting xenobiotic metabolism

3

NF-kB signaling inhibition reducing pro-inflammatory cytokine expression (TNF-a, IL-6, IL-1b)

4

Nrf2/ARE pathway activation upregulating detoxification enzymes (NQO1, GST, HO-1)

5

Androgen receptor downregulation and anti-androgenic activity

6

BRCA1 gene expression upregulation in estrogen-responsive tissues

7

Inhibition of Akt/mTOR and MAPK proliferative signaling pathways

8

Increased sex hormone-binding globulin (SHBG) production

Tolerability

Safety Profile

Overall Safety Rating: Good
Common Side Effects
  • Generally well-tolerated at doses up to 300mg/day
  • Mild GI upset (nausea) at higher doses
  • Dark-colored urine (harmless)
  • Headache at high doses (rare)
Contraindications
  • Hormone-sensitive cancers (use only under medical supervision)
  • Pregnancy and breastfeeding (insufficient safety data)
  • Concurrent use with tamoxifen (reduces tamoxifen metabolite levels)
  • Estrogen-dependent conditions without physician guidance
Drug Interactions
  • Reduces plasma tamoxifen, endoxifen, and 4-OH tamoxifen levels (Thomson 2017 RCT)
  • May alter estradiol exposure in women on transdermal estradiol patches
  • Potential CYP1A2 and CYP3A4 inducer affecting drug metabolism
  • May interact with hormonal contraceptives
  • Caution with anticoagulants (theoretical interaction)

Deep Dive

Enhanced Evidence Review

Detailed citation analysis organized by research domain.

Mechanisms of Action

A randomized, placebo-controlled trial of diindolylmethane for breast cancer biomarker modulation in patients taking tamoxifen

Strong Randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled trial 2017 n=98 completed (130 enrolled) 12 months

DIM 150mg BID significantly increased urinary 2-OHE1:16a-OHE1 ratio (+3.2 vs -0.7 placebo, P<0.001). SHBG also increased vs placebo. Demonstrates robust CYP1A1-mediated estrogen metabolism shift in humans.

PubMed: 28560655

3,3'-Diindolylmethane modulates estrogen metabolism in patients with thyroid proliferative disease: a pilot study

Strong Pilot clinical trial (Phase I) 2011 n=7 14 days

DIM 300mg/day was detectable in thyroid tissue, serum, and urine. Favorable shift in 2-OHE1:16a-OHE1 ratio consistent with antiestrogenic activity. Demonstrates tissue-level penetration and estrogen metabolism modulation.

PubMed: 21254914

Attenuation of multi-targeted proliferation-linked signaling by 3,3'-diindolylmethane (DIM): from bench to clinic

Strong Comprehensive review 2011

DIM acts as an AhR modulator, abrogating TCDD-induced recruitment of AhR to the COX-2 promoter. Represses COX-2 mRNA and protein expression. Naturally occurring AhR modulators like DIM overcome epigenetic activation of inflammatory pathways by environmental AhR agonists.

PubMed: 21703360

Unveiling the multifaceted pharmacological actions of indole-3-carbinol and diindolylmethane: a comprehensive review

Strong Comprehensive review 2025

DIM and AhR have strong binding affinity. DIM reduces oxidative stress through Nrf2/ARE/HO-1 pathway activation and mitigates inflammation by blocking TNF-a, ILs, TLR, RANKL, JAK/STAT, and NF-kB translocation. Disrupts AhR/NF-kB P65 interaction.

PubMed: 40094833

Attenuation of multi-targeted proliferation-linked signaling by 3,3'-diindolylmethane (DIM): from bench to clinic

Moderate Comprehensive review 2011

DIM inhibits NF-kB translocation and activation through multiple upstream mechanisms. Blocks TNF-a, interleukins, TLR, and RANKL signaling. Disrupts AhR/NF-kB P65 crosstalk to attenuate inflammatory amplification.

PubMed: 21703360

Unveiling the multifaceted pharmacological actions of indole-3-carbinol and diindolylmethane: a comprehensive review

Moderate Comprehensive review 2025

DIM stimulates Nrf2-regulated promoters including NQO1 (oxidoreductase), GSTa1 (glutathione S-transferase), and x-CT (cystine/glutamate transporter). Nrf2 release and nuclear translocation activates transcription of detoxification and antioxidant proteins. Upstream BRCA-1 and protein kinases involved.

PubMed: 40094833

A phase I dose-escalation study of oral BR-DIM (BioResponse 3,3'-Diindolylmethane) in castrate-resistant, non-metastatic prostate cancer

Moderate Phase I dose-escalation study 2010 n=12

BR-DIM modulates estrogen metabolism and acts as an anti-androgen downregulating the androgen receptor and PSA. One patient achieved 50% PSA decline at 225mg. MTD established at 300mg. Recommended phase II dose: 225mg BID.

PubMed: 20733950

Phase Ib placebo-controlled, tissue biomarker trial of diindolylmethane (BR-DIMNG) in patients with prostate cancer who are undergoing prostatectomy

Moderate Phase Ib placebo-controlled biomarker trial 2016 n=45 21-28 days pre-prostatectomy

DIM detected in only 7/28 prostate tissue specimens. Urinary estrone ratio showed meaningful differences between placebo and high-dose groups. Confirmed feasibility of preoperative supplementation window but suggested poor tissue penetration with this formulation.

PubMed: 26313229

Clinical Benefits

A randomized, placebo-controlled trial of diindolylmethane for breast cancer biomarker modulation in patients taking tamoxifen

Strong Randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled trial 2017 n=98 completed 12 months

DIM 150mg BID produced significant 2-OHE1:16a-OHE1 ratio increase (+3.2 vs -0.7 placebo, P<0.001). SHBG significantly increased. No change in breast density by mammography or MRI.

PubMed: 28560655

3,3'-Diindolylmethane modulates estrogen metabolism in patients with thyroid proliferative disease: a pilot study

Strong Pilot clinical trial 2011 n=7 14 days

DIM 300mg/day increased 2-OHE1:16a-OHE1 ratio in thyroid tissue, serum, and urine. DIM was sequestered in thyroid tissue confirming tissue-level activity.

PubMed: 21254914

Effectiveness of 3,3'-Diindolylmethane supplements on favoring the benign estrogen metabolism pathway and decreasing body fat in premenopausal women

Strong Randomized, double-blind controlled trial 2023 n=60 30 days treatment + 30 days follow-up

DIM 75mg/day (300mg DIM-BR) did not significantly increase estrogen metabolite ratio at 30 days but showed non-significant positive trend 30 days post-supplementation. Greater body fat reduction in DIM group vs placebo.

PubMed: 36111381

Double-blind randomized placebo-controlled multicenter clinical trial (phase IIa) on diindolylmethane's efficacy and safety in the treatment of CIN: implications for cervical cancer prevention

Moderate Phase IIa multicenter RCT 2015

Vaginal DIM suppositories at 100mg and 200mg/day showed significantly higher complete CIN regression rates vs placebo. Favorable safety profile with minimal adverse events.

PubMed: 26693258

Oral diindolylmethane (DIM): pilot evaluation of a nonsurgical treatment for cervical dysplasia

Moderate Double-blind randomized pilot trial 2010 n=64 (45 DIM, 19 placebo) 12 weeks

47% of DIM group showed improved dysplasia at first follow-up. 56% had improved colposcopic findings. Well-tolerated with no significant toxicity. However, no statistically significant difference between DIM and placebo groups.

PubMed: 19939441

Effect of diindolylmethane supplementation on low-grade cervical cytological abnormalities: double-blind, randomised, controlled trial

Moderate Double-blind, randomised, controlled trial 2012 n=551 6 months

CIN2+ rate: 9% DIM vs 12% placebo (RR 0.7). CIN3+ rate: 4.6% vs 5.1% (RR 0.9). Well-tolerated but unlikely to significantly affect cytology or HPV infection at 150mg/day. Largest DIM cervical trial to date.

PubMed: 22075942

Chemopreventive properties of 3,3'-diindolylmethane in breast cancer: evidence from experimental and human studies

Preliminary Narrative review 2016

Comprehensive evidence review supporting DIM chemopreventive properties through estrogen metabolism modulation. Epidemiological evidence supports cruciferous vegetable consumption and reduced breast cancer risk. However, no clinical trial directly demonstrates DIM efficacy for cancer treatment.

PubMed: 27261275

A randomized, placebo-controlled trial of diindolylmethane for breast cancer biomarker modulation in patients taking tamoxifen

Preliminary Randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled trial 2017 n=98 completed 12 months

Favorable estrogen metabolism shift but no change in breast density (a cancer risk biomarker). Tamoxifen metabolites significantly reduced, raising concern about co-administration. BRCA1 modulation pathway supported.

PubMed: 28560655

A phase I dose-escalation study of oral BR-DIM (BioResponse 3,3'-Diindolylmethane) in castrate-resistant, non-metastatic prostate cancer

Preliminary Phase I dose-escalation study 2010 n=12

Minimal toxicity at doses up to 300mg. MTD 300mg, RP2D 225mg BID. 1/12 patients had 50% PSA decline; 1/12 had PSA stabilization. Modest clinical signal requiring further investigation.

PubMed: 20733950

Phase Ib placebo-controlled, tissue biomarker trial of diindolylmethane (BR-DIMNG) in patients with prostate cancer who are undergoing prostatectomy

Preliminary Phase Ib placebo-controlled biomarker trial 2016 n=45 21-28 days

DIM detected in only 7/28 prostate tissue specimens, suggesting poor tissue penetration. AR staining reduced in 37.5% of DIM-treated specimens. Urinary estrone ratio showed dose-dependent changes.

PubMed: 26313229

Safety & Tolerability

Single-dose pharmacokinetics and tolerability of absorption-enhanced 3,3'-diindolylmethane in healthy subjects

Level 2 Phase I pharmacokinetic study 2008 n=healthy volunteers

No DIM-related adverse effects at doses up to 200mg. At 300mg, one subject reported vomiting probably related to study agent. Absorption-enhanced BR-DIM formulation achieved 50% higher plasma levels than crystalline DIM. Tmax approximately 2-3 hours.

PubMed: 18843002

Effect of diindolylmethane supplementation on low-grade cervical cytological abnormalities: double-blind, randomised, controlled trial

Level 2 Double-blind, randomised, controlled trial 2012 n=551 6 months

DIM 150mg/day well-tolerated over 6 months in the largest DIM clinical trial. No significant safety concerns reported in this population of women with low-grade cervical abnormalities.

PubMed: 22075942

A randomized, placebo-controlled trial of diindolylmethane for breast cancer biomarker modulation in patients taking tamoxifen

Level 2 Randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled trial 2017 n=98 12 months

Tamoxifen, endoxifen, 4-OH tamoxifen, and N-desmethyl-tamoxifen levels were significantly reduced in DIM arm. This is a clinically important safety finding as endoxifen is the primary active metabolite of tamoxifen. Authors concluded further research needed before recommending DIM with tamoxifen.

PubMed: 28560655

A phase I dose-escalation study of oral BR-DIM (BioResponse 3,3'-Diindolylmethane) in castrate-resistant, non-metastatic prostate cancer

Level 3 Phase I dose-escalation study 2010 n=12

Two patients experienced grade 3 asymptomatic hyponatremia at higher doses. Overall minimal toxicity observed across dose cohorts up to 300mg. Systematic safety surveillance across 4 dose cohorts.

PubMed: 20733950
