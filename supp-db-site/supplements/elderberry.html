Overview

Elderberry is classified as herbal extracts with moderate evidence (Tier 2) for its primary applications. Clinical trials showing immune support and antiviral effects

Three independent RCTs and one meta-analysis support elderberry extract for reducing duration and severity of upper respiratory infections, particularly influenza; strong antiviral mechanism evidence via neuraminidase inhibition and cytokine modulation; generally well-tolerated with theoretical cytokine storm concern unsubstantiated in clinical trials

Key study populations: Cold/flu season immune support; Upper respiratory infections; Immune compromised

Enhanced Evidence Review

Detailed citation analysis from 7 peer-reviewed sources, organized by research domain.

Mechanisms of Action

Cytokine modulation and immune cell activation

Moderate

The effect of Sambucol, a black elderberry-based, natural product, on the production of human cytokines: I. Inflammatory cytokines

Barak V, Halperin T, Kalickman I (2001) — European Cytokine Network

Sambucol increased production of inflammatory cytokines IL-1b, TNF-a, IL-6, and IL-8 by 1.3 to 6.2 fold,Effect was 2-45 fold greater than lipopolysaccharide (LPS, positive control) for some cytokines,Suggests elderberry activates the innate immune system via monocyte/macrophage stimulation,Cytokine activation may explain clinical antiviral and immune-boosting effects

PubMed: 12455180

Anthocyanin-mediated antiviral activity and neuraminidase inhibition

Moderate

Inhibition of several strains of influenza virus in vitro and reduction of symptoms by an elderberry extract (Sambucus nigra L.) during an outbreak of influenza B Panama

Zakay-Rones Z, Varsano N, Zlotnik M, Manor O, Regev L, Schlesinger M, Mumcuoglu M (1995) — Journal of Alternative and Complementary Medicine

Elderberry extract directly inhibited replication of human influenza A and B virus strains in vitro,Inhibition attributed to flavonoids binding to and preventing viral entry into host cells,Cyanidin-3-glucoside and cyanidin-3-sambubioside are primary active anthocyanins,Mechanism involves blocking viral hemagglutinin attachment to host cell receptors

PubMed: 9395631

Elderberry (Sambucus nigra L.) - Bioactive Compounds, Health Functions, and Applications

Liu D, He XQ, Wu DT, Li HB, Feng YB, Zou L, Gan RY (2022) — Journal of Agricultural and Food Chemistry

Elderberry rich in anthocyanins (cyanidin-3-glucoside, cyanidin-3-sambubioside), flavonols, and phenolic acids,Anthocyanins exert antiviral effects via neuraminidase inhibition (similar mechanism to oseltamivir/Tamiflu),Polyphenols show direct virucidal activity against enveloped viruses,Additional bioactivities include antioxidant, anti-diabetic, anti-depressant, and cardioprotective effects

PubMed: 35348337

Clinical Benefits

Reduces duration and severity of influenza and common cold symptoms

Moderate Replicated across 3 independent RCTs and confirmed by meta-analysis

Randomized study of the efficacy and safety of oral elderberry extract in the treatment of influenza A and B virus infections

Zakay-Rones Z, Thom E, Wollan T, Wadstein J (2004) — Journal of International Medical Research

Symptoms relieved on average 4 days earlier in elderberry group vs placebo (p<0.001),Significant improvement in influenza symptoms by day 3-4 in treatment group,Use of rescue medication (analgesics, nasal sprays) significantly lower in elderberry group,Standardized elderberry extract (Sambucol) 15ml syrup 4 times/day

PubMed: 15080016

Inhibition of several strains of influenza virus in vitro and reduction of symptoms by an elderberry extract (Sambucus nigra L.) during an outbreak of influenza B Panama

Zakay-Rones Z, Varsano N, Zlotnik M, Manor O, Regev L, Schlesinger M, Mumcuoglu M (1995) — Journal of Alternative and Complementary Medicine

93.3% of elderberry-treated patients showed significant improvement within 2 days,Placebo group: 91.7% showed improvement only after 6 days,Complete cure achieved in 2-3 days in elderberry group vs 6 days in placebo (p<0.001),In vitro: elderberry extract inhibited replication of influenza A and B strains,First RCT demonstrating clinical efficacy of elderberry for influenza

PubMed: 9395631

Elderberry Supplementation Reduces Cold Duration and Symptoms in Air-Travellers: A Randomized, Double-Blind Placebo-Controlled Clinical Trial

Tiralongo E, Wee SS, Lea RA (2016) — Nutrients

Total cold episode days significantly reduced: 57 in elderberry vs 117 in placebo (p=0.02),Cold duration significantly shorter in elderberry group (4.75 vs 6.88 days, p=0.02),Cold severity score significantly lower (mean difference 10.59, p=0.016),Largest elderberry RCT to date (n=312),600mg capsules elderberry extract daily (standardized to anthocyanins)

PubMed: 27023596

Black elderberries (Sambucus nigra) supplementation effectively treats upper respiratory symptoms: A meta-analysis of randomized, controlled clinical trials

Hawkins J, Baker C, Cherry L, Dunne E (2019) — Complementary Therapies in Medicine

Elderberry supplementation substantially reduced upper respiratory symptoms,Large overall effect size favoring elderberry over placebo,Greater effect observed for influenza-specific symptoms vs common cold,Results consistent across different elderberry preparations,Supports elderberry as a safe, cost-effective alternative for respiratory infections

PubMed: 30670267

Low

No serious adverse events reported during the 5-day treatment period,Elderberry syrup was well tolerated in both adults and children,No significant changes in laboratory parameters

Theoretical concern about cytokine overproduction during severe viral infections; not observed in clinical trials

Low

Elderberry (Sambucus nigra) for COVID-19: Pros and Cons

Asgary S, Rastkar A, Azimi P (2022) — Advanced Biomedical Research

Elderberry increases pro-inflammatory cytokines (IL-1b, TNF-a, IL-6, IL-8) which raised theoretical cytokine storm concerns during COVID-19,However, no clinical evidence of cytokine storm from elderberry supplementation in any human study,The immunomodulatory effects appear self-limiting at dietary supplement doses,Authors noted both potential benefits (antiviral, immunostimulant) and theoretical risks

PubMed: 36518861

Raw elderberries, bark, leaves, and seeds contain cyanogenic glycosides; only processed/cooked preparations are safe for consumption

Moderate

Ethnobotany, phytochemistry, pharmacology, and toxicology of the genus Sambucus L. (Viburnaceae)

Waswa EN, Kambura AK, Mwanzia VM, Kamau MM, Olango TM, Odago WO (2022) — Journal of Ethnopharmacology

Raw elderberries contain sambunigrin (cyanogenic glycoside) that releases cyanide on hydrolysis,Cooking, fermentation, or commercial processing effectively eliminates cyanogenic compounds,Over 425 bioactive constituents identified across Sambucus species,All commercial elderberry supplements use processed extracts and are safe at recommended doses,Leaves, bark, and unripe berries have higher cyanide potential and must not be consumed raw

PubMed: 35288288

Dosage Research

Clinical trials used 600mg/day capsules or 15ml syrup 4x/day (Sambucol), with treatment beginning within 48 hours of symptom onset for best results

Elderberry Supplementation Reduces Cold Duration and Symptoms in Air-Travellers: A Randomized, Double-Blind Placebo-Controlled Clinical Trial

Tiralongo E, Wee SS, Lea RA (2016) — Nutrients

600mg elderberry extract capsules daily (standardized to anthocyanins) was effective dose,Prophylactic use (before symptom onset) reduced cold episodes in travellers,Duration of supplementation: 10 days pre-travel through post-travel period

PubMed: 27023596

Randomized study of the efficacy and safety of oral elderberry extract in the treatment of influenza A and B virus infections

Zakay-Rones Z, Thom E, Wollan T, Wadstein J (2004) — Journal of International Medical Research

Sambucol elderberry syrup 15ml four times daily for 5 days,Treatment initiated within 48 hours of symptom onset,Acute treatment protocol (not prophylactic) — most common clinical use
