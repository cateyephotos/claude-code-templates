Overview

Forskolin is classified as herbal extracts with preliminary evidence (Tier 3) for its primary applications. Limited human studies but strong mechanistic evidence

Strong mechanistic understanding of adenylyl cyclase/cAMP activation with moderate clinical evidence from small RCTs showing favorable body composition changes and metabolic improvements

Key study populations: Metabolic enhancement; Weight management; Cognitive support

Enhanced Evidence Review

Detailed citation analysis from 8 peer-reviewed sources, organized by research domain.

Mechanisms of Action

Direct adenylyl cyclase activation and cAMP elevation

Strong

Plectranthus barbatus: a review of phytochemistry, ethnobotanical uses and pharmacology

Alasbahi RH, Melzig MF (2010) — Planta Medica

Forskolin uniquely and directly activates adenylyl cyclase independent of G-protein receptors,Increases intracellular cAMP in virtually all cell types tested,cAMP downstream effects include protein kinase A activation, lipolysis, and hormone synthesis,Clinical applications documented for cardiovascular, respiratory, and gastrointestinal disorders

PubMed: 20178070

Biotechnological interventions for production of forskolin from Coleus forskohlii

Roshni PT, Rekha PD (2024) — Physiology and Molecular Biology of Plants

Forskolin is the only known naturally occurring compound that directly activates adenylyl cyclase,cAMP elevation mediates effects relevant to glaucoma, asthma, obesity, allergies, cardiovascular diseases,Labdane diterpene structure enables unique membrane-permeable adenylyl cyclase interaction

PubMed: 38623169

Anti-inflammatory pathway modulation

Moderate

Coleus forskohlii extract modulates inflammation in murine model of allergic asthma

Ma C, Gu J, Sun D, Huang Q, Yu J, Zhong Y (2018) — Journal of Cellular Biochemistry

Reduced airway eosinophil infiltration in asthma model,Decreased pro-inflammatory cytokines IL-4, IL-5, IL-17,Increased anti-inflammatory cytokines IFN-γ and IL-10,Downregulated ICAM-1, p-p65 (NF-κB), and MMP-9 expression

PubMed: 30520122

Clinical Benefits

Reduces body fat percentage and fat mass while preserving lean body mass in overweight men

Moderate Partially replicated (2 independent RCTs)

Body composition and hormonal adaptations associated with forskolin consumption in overweight and obese men

Godard MP, Johnson BA, Richmond SR (2005) — Obesity Research

Significantly decreased body fat percentage and fat mass vs placebo (p≤0.05) by DXA,Significant increase in bone mass vs placebo (p≤0.05),Trend toward increased lean body mass (p=0.097),Serum free testosterone significantly increased vs placebo (p≤0.05)

PubMed: 16129715

Effects of coleus forskohlii supplementation on body composition and hematological profiles in mildly overweight women

Henderson S, Magu B, Raber C, Siljander S, Brorson A, Feliciano L (2005) — Journal of the International Society of Sports Nutrition

Coleus forskohlii tended to mitigate weight gain in overweight women,Reduced fatigue and hunger ratings,Did not promote statistically significant weight loss,No clinically significant adverse effects on metabolic markers or blood counts

PubMed: 18500958

Reduces metabolic syndrome risk factors including insulin resistance in overweight subjects

Moderate Single study

Coleus forskohlii Extract Supplementation in Conjunction with a Hypocaloric Diet Reduces the Risk Factors of Metabolic Syndrome in Overweight and Obese Subjects: A Randomized Controlled Trial

Loftus HL, Astell KJ, Mathai ML, Su XQ (2015) — Nutrients

Significant improvement in insulin concentration (p=0.001),Significant reduction in insulin resistance (p=0.01),Significant reductions in waist and hip circumference,Significant increase in HDL cholesterol

PubMed: 26593941

Increases serum free testosterone levels in overweight and obese men

Moderate Single study

Body composition and hormonal adaptations associated with forskolin consumption in overweight and obese men

Godard MP, Johnson BA, Richmond SR (2005) — Obesity Research

Serum free testosterone significantly increased in forskolin group vs placebo (p≤0.05),Total testosterone increased 16.77% in forskolin group vs 1.08% decrease in placebo,cAMP-mediated activation of steroidogenic pathways proposed as mechanism

PubMed: 16129715

Safety & Tolerability

Generally well tolerated at standard doses with no serious adverse events in clinical trials

Low

Effects of coleus forskohlii supplementation on body composition and hematological profiles in mildly overweight women

Henderson S, Magu B, Raber C, Siljander S, Brorson A, Feliciano L (2005) — Journal of the International Society of Sports Nutrition

No clinically significant adverse effects reported,No adverse changes in metabolic markers, hematological profiles, or vital signs,Well tolerated at 250mg 10% forskolin extract twice daily

PubMed: 18500958

Body composition and hormonal adaptations associated with forskolin consumption in overweight and obese men

Godard MP, Johnson BA, Richmond SR (2005) — Obesity Research

No serious adverse events reported over 12-week study period,Blood pressure changes not clinically significant,Standard clinical safety markers remained within normal ranges

PubMed: 16129715

cAMP-elevating effects warrant caution with blood pressure medications and anticoagulants

Moderate

Plectranthus barbatus: a review of phytochemistry, ethnobotanical uses and pharmacology

Alasbahi RH, Melzig MF (2010) — Planta Medica

cAMP elevation may potentiate effects of blood pressure medications,Vasodilatory and positive inotropic effects documented in pharmacological studies,Caution with anticoagulants due to potential antiplatelet activity,Caution in hypotensive patients and those on antihypertensive medications

PubMed: 20178070

Dosage Research

Standard dose used in clinical trials is 250mg of 10% Coleus forskohlii extract twice daily

Body composition and hormonal adaptations associated with forskolin consumption in overweight and obese men

Godard MP, Johnson BA, Richmond SR (2005) — Obesity Research

250mg of 10% forskolin extract (25mg forskolin) twice daily was the effective dose,12-week duration showed significant body composition changes,Same dosage protocol used consistently across clinical trials

PubMed: 16129715

Coleus forskohlii Extract Supplementation in Conjunction with a Hypocaloric Diet Reduces the Risk Factors of Metabolic Syndrome in Overweight and Obese Subjects: A Randomized Controlled Trial

Loftus HL, Astell KJ, Mathai ML, Su XQ (2015) — Nutrients

250mg Coleus forskohlii extract twice daily with hypocaloric diet,Effective for metabolic syndrome risk factor reduction at this dose,12-week duration adequate for insulin sensitivity improvements

PubMed: 26593941
