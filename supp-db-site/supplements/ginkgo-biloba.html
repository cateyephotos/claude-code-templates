Overview

Ginkgo Biloba is classified as herbal extracts with moderate evidence (Tier 2) for its primary applications. Cochrane review 2026 (82 RCTs, n=10613, GRADE) — most extensive herbal nootropic evidence base. Shows small-moderate benefits in dementia (low certainty) but probably no effect in MCI (moderate certainty). GB extracts ranked #1 for VCI cognition (d=0.83) in 173-trial MA (Masserini 2025). High-dose Bacopa outperformed GB for working memory in 29-RCT NMA (Tiemtad 2026). Overview of 16 SRs confirms favorable findings but poor SR quality. Tier 2: extensive RCT evidence but mixed certainty across populations.

Most extensively studied herbal nootropic. New Cochrane review 2026 (82 RCTs, n=10613) shows small-moderate benefits in dementia (low certainty) but probably no effect in MCI (moderate certainty). GB extracts ranked top for VCI cognition (d=0.83) in 173-trial MA. High-dose Bacopa outperformed Ginkgo for working memory in 29-RCT NMA.
