Glucosamine

Glucosamine sulfate

Also known as: Glucosamine sulfate, Glucosamine HCl, GS, GH

Tier 2: Large research base (30+ RCTs in meta-analyses, umbrella NMA n=5265) supporting modest joint space narrowing and symptom effects. Efficacy is contested by funding source — independent IPD meta-analysis (Runhaar 2017, n=1625, 5 trials) found null result vs placebo; industry-funded studies show SMD ~-0.47 vs independent SMD ~-0.19. OARSI 2014 classified as 'uncertain appropriateness'. Best evidence is for crystalline glucosamine sulfate 1500mg/day for structural outcomes (JSN reduction). Cannot reach Tier 1 due to fundamental inconsistency between independent and industry-funded evidence.

Overview

Glucosamine is classified as joint support with moderate evidence (Tier 2) for its primary applications. Tier 2: Large research base (30+ RCTs in meta-analyses, umbrella NMA n=5265) supporting modest joint space narrowing and symptom effects. Efficacy is contested by funding source — independent IPD meta-analysis (Runhaar 2017, n=1625, 5 trials) found null result vs placebo; industry-funded studies show SMD ~-0.47 vs independent SMD ~-0.19. OARSI 2014 classified as 'uncertain appropriateness'. Best evidence is for crystalline glucosamine sulfate 1500mg/day for structural outcomes (JSN reduction). Cannot reach Tier 1 due to fundamental inconsistency between independent and industry-funded evidence.

Well-established joint support supplement with strong mechanistic understanding and moderate evidence for cartilage health

Key study populations: Knee osteoarthritis patients; Hip osteoarthritis patients; Adults with joint pain

Mechanisms of Action

1

Cartilage matrix GAG and proteoglycan synthesis (in vitro evidence)

2

NF-κB pathway inhibition — anti-inflammatory (in vitro; not confirmed in human RCTs at clinical significance)

3

Joint space narrowing reduction (clinical evidence: modest but present for GS)

Benefits

Non-Cognitive Benefits

  • Joint pain reduction (contested)
  • Joint space narrowing reduction
  • Osteoarthritis symptom relief
  • Cartilage matrix support

Effect Sizes

Quantified effect sizes from clinical research, where available.

Domain Effect Size / Finding
Joint Pain Sub-threshold: VAS WMD -5.7 to -7.4mm (MCID=15mm; does not meet clinical significance in most independent meta-analyses)
Joint Space Narrowing Small but significant: SMD -0.42 (Gregori 2018, industry-conflicted); GS alone significant in Rabade 2024
Function WOMAC not significantly improved in independent meta-analyses

Safety Profile

Overall Safety Rating: Good

Common Side Effects

  • Mild GI upset
  • Nausea
  • Heartburn
  • Diarrhea (uncommon, <5%)

Contraindications

  • Shellfish allergies (most GS derived from shellfish)

Drug Interactions

  • Warfarin (monitor INR)
  • Diabetes medications (monitor blood glucose)
