Background

Overview

Glutathione is classified as antioxidant with tier 2: moderate evidence for its primary applications. Multiple randomized controlled trials demonstrate oral and liposomal glutathione can raise body GSH stores and improve immune markers (Richie 2015 RCT n=54; Sinha 2018 pilot n=12). However, one well-designed RCT (Allen & Bradley 2011 n=40) found no effect on oxidative stress biomarkers. Bioavailability remains debated; liposomal forms show stronger results than standard oral. Evidence is mixed and mostly from small trials, warranting Tier 2 until large multi-center replication.

Key study populations: Healthy adults; Healthy older adults; Type 2 diabetes patients; Individuals with metabolic syndrome; Healthy female volunteers (skin studies)

How It Works

Mechanisms of Action

1

Direct scavenging of reactive oxygen species (ROS) and reactive nitrogen species (RNS)

2

Glutathione peroxidase (GPx) cofactor for hydrogen peroxide and lipid peroxide detoxification

3

Phase II detoxification via glutathione S-transferase (GST) conjugation reactions

4

Maintenance of thiol redox balance (GSH/GSSG ratio) in cells

5

Regeneration of other antioxidants including vitamins C and E

6

Regulation of immune cell proliferation and NK cell cytotoxicity

7

Inhibition of tyrosinase activity reducing melanin synthesis in skin

8

Mitochondrial membrane integrity protection against oxidative damage

Tolerability

Safety Profile

Overall Safety Rating: Excellent
Common Side Effects
  • Mild flatulence
  • Loose stools
  • Occasional flushing
Contraindications
  • Pregnancy/breastfeeding (insufficient long-term data)
  • Organ transplant recipients on immunosuppressants (theoretical immune modulation concern)
Drug Interactions
  • May reduce efficacy of chemotherapy agents that rely on oxidative mechanisms
  • Potential interaction with nitroglycerin (enhanced hypotensive effect)
  • May affect acetaminophen metabolism via glutathione conjugation

Deep Dive

Enhanced Evidence Review

Detailed citation analysis organized by research domain.

Mechanisms of Action

Randomized controlled trial of oral glutathione supplementation on body stores of glutathione

Strong Randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled trial 2015 n=54 healthy non-smoking adults 6 months

Oral GSH at 1000 mg/day increased erythrocyte, plasma, and lymphocyte GSH levels by 30-35% and buccal cell GSH by 260% at 6 months. The 250 mg/day dose produced 17-29% increases. Oxidative stress markers (8-OHdG) decreased in both dose groups.

PubMed: 24791752

Application of Glutathione as Anti-Oxidative and Anti-Aging Drugs

Strong Review 2015

Comprehensive review establishing GSH as the primary intracellular thiol antioxidant. Decreased GSH levels are associated with aging and a wide range of pathological conditions including neurodegenerative disorders. GSH functions as cofactor for GPx enzymes and directly scavenges ROS.

PubMed: 26467067

Oral supplementation with liposomal glutathione elevates body stores of glutathione and markers of immune function

Moderate Pilot clinical study 2018 n=12 healthy adults 4 weeks

Liposomal GSH at 500 and 1000 mg/day elevated GSH within 1 week. Maximum increases at 2 weeks: 40% in whole blood, 25% in erythrocytes, 28% in plasma, 100% in PBMCs. Faster onset than standard oral GSH in Richie 2015 trial.

PubMed: 28853742

Effects of N-acetylcysteine, oral glutathione (GSH) and a novel sublingual form of GSH on oxidative stress markers: A comparative crossover study

Moderate Randomized crossover trial 2015 n=20 volunteers with metabolic syndrome 3 weeks per treatment arm

Sublingual GSH produced significantly higher plasma GSH levels and GSH/GSSG ratio (p=0.003) compared to standard oral GSH. Demonstrated significant superiority of sublingual delivery over oral form for both bioavailability and oxidative stress reduction.

PubMed: 26262996

Application of Glutathione as Anti-Oxidative and Anti-Aging Drugs

Strong Review 2015

GSH serves as essential co-substrate for glutathione S-transferase (GST) enzymes catalyzing Phase II conjugation of electrophilic xenobiotics, carcinogens, and drug metabolites. GSH depletion impairs detoxification capacity and increases susceptibility to toxic insult.

PubMed: 26467067

Glutathione and immune function

Moderate Review 2000

Intracellular GSH levels regulate T lymphocyte function and proliferation. Even moderate GSH depletion impairs immune cell activation. Cysteine supply is rate-limiting for immune cell GSH synthesis, particularly under conditions of oxidative stress or infection.

PubMed: 11115795

Oral supplementation with liposomal glutathione elevates body stores of glutathione and markers of immune function

Moderate Pilot clinical study 2018 n=12 healthy adults 4 weeks

Liposomal GSH elevated NK cell cytotoxicity by up to 400% at 2 weeks and lymphocyte proliferation by up to 60% at 2 weeks. Demonstrates functional immune enhancement from GSH supplementation.

PubMed: 28853742

Clinical Benefits

Randomized controlled trial of oral glutathione supplementation on body stores of glutathione

Moderate Randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled trial 2015 n=54 healthy non-smoking adults 6 months

1000 mg/day oral GSH increased erythrocyte, plasma, and lymphocyte GSH by 30-35% and buccal cell GSH by 260% at 6 months. 250 mg/day also effective (17-29% increases). Oxidative stress marker 8-OHdG decreased in both groups.

PubMed: 24791752

Effects of oral glutathione supplementation on systemic oxidative stress biomarkers in human volunteers

Moderate Randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled trial 2011 n=40 healthy adults 4 weeks

500 mg twice daily oral GSH for 4 weeks produced no significant changes in biomarkers of oxidative stress, lipid peroxidation, DNA adduct formation, or glutathione status in healthy adults. Suggests limited bioavailability of standard oral GSH in short-duration studies.

PubMed: 21875351

Randomized controlled trial of oral glutathione supplementation on body stores of glutathione

Moderate Randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled trial 2015 n=54 healthy non-smoking adults 6 months

NK cell cytotoxicity more than doubled in the 1000 mg/day group compared to placebo at 3 months. First RCT evidence linking oral GSH supplementation to functional immune enhancement.

PubMed: 24791752

Oral supplementation with liposomal glutathione elevates body stores of glutathione and markers of immune function

Moderate Pilot clinical study 2018 n=12 healthy adults 4 weeks

Liposomal GSH elevated NK cell cytotoxicity by up to 400% and lymphocyte proliferation by up to 60% within 2 weeks. More rapid immune enhancement than standard oral GSH observed in Richie 2015.

PubMed: 28853742

Randomized Clinical Trial of How Long-Term Glutathione Supplementation Offers Protection from Oxidative Damage and Improves HbA1c in Elderly Type 2 Diabetic Patients

Preliminary Randomized controlled trial 2022 n=104 elderly type 2 diabetic patients 6 months

6 months oral GSH supplementation (500 mg/day) significantly improved HbA1c, reduced oxidative damage markers (MDA, protein carbonyls), and increased blood GSH levels compared to placebo in elderly T2D patients.

PubMed: 35624890

Glutathione as an oral whitening agent: a randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled study

Preliminary Randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled trial 2012 n=60 medical students 4 weeks

500 mg/day oral GSH consistently decreased melanin indices at all six measured body sites. Statistically significant reductions at two sites compared to placebo. Authors noted long-term safety not yet established.

PubMed: 20524875

Deficient synthesis of glutathione underlies oxidative stress in aging and can be corrected by dietary cysteine and glycine supplementation

Moderate Clinical trial 2011 n=8 elderly + 8 young controls 14 days

Elderly subjects had markedly lower glycine and cysteine levels and reduced GSH synthesis. 2 weeks of cysteine and glycine supplementation increased GSH concentration by approximately 95% in elderly subjects, restoring levels to match young adults and significantly reducing oxidative stress markers.

PubMed: 21795440

Safety & Tolerability

Randomized controlled trial of oral glutathione supplementation on body stores of glutathione

Level 2 Randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled trial 2015 n=54 healthy adults 6 months

No serious adverse events reported at either 250 mg/day or 1000 mg/day over 6 months. Mild side effects included flatulence (n=5), loose stools, flushing (n=2), and weight gain (n=1). No clinically significant changes in hematology, hepatic, or renal function tests.

PubMed: 24791752

Effects of oral glutathione supplementation on systemic oxidative stress biomarkers in human volunteers

Level 2 Randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled trial 2011 n=40 healthy adults 4 weeks

1000 mg/day oral GSH for 4 weeks was well-tolerated with minimal side effects and >90% participant adherence. No serious adverse events reported.

PubMed: 21875351

Oral supplementation with liposomal glutathione elevates body stores of glutathione and markers of immune function

Level 3 Pilot clinical study 2018 n=12 healthy adults 4 weeks

Liposomal GSH at 500 mg/day and 1000 mg/day was safe and well-tolerated for 4 weeks. No adverse events reported in the study.

PubMed: 28853742

Effects of N-acetylcysteine, oral glutathione (GSH) and a novel sublingual form of GSH on oxidative stress markers: A comparative crossover study

Level 2 Randomized crossover trial 2015 n=20 volunteers with metabolic syndrome 3 weeks per arm

Sublingual GSH showed significantly higher plasma GSH levels and GSH/GSSG ratio (p=0.003) vs oral GSH. Establishes that delivery form substantially affects glutathione bioavailability and clinical outcomes.

PubMed: 26262996

Dosage Research

Randomized controlled trial of oral glutathione supplementation on body stores of glutathione

Level 2 Randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled trial 2015 n=54 6 months

Dose-response observed: 250 mg/day increased blood GSH by 17-29%, while 1000 mg/day increased by 30-35% in blood compartments and 260% in buccal cells. NK cell activity doubled at 1000 mg/day. Both doses effective but higher dose showed greater benefit.

PubMed: 24791752

Oral supplementation with liposomal glutathione elevates body stores of glutathione and markers of immune function

Level 3 Pilot clinical study 2018 n=12 4 weeks

Liposomal GSH at 500 and 1000 mg/day elevated whole blood GSH by 40%, plasma GSH by 28%, and PBMC GSH by 100% within 2 weeks. Maximum immune function enhancement (400% NK cell activity) also at 2 weeks.
