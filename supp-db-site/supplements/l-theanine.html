Overview

L-Theanine is classified as amino acids with moderate evidence (Tier 2) for its primary applications. Multiple small to moderate RCTs

Well-established amino acid from green tea with strong evidence for alpha brain wave enhancement, relaxation without sedation, stress/anxiety reduction at 200-400 mg/day, and synergistic cognitive benefits when combined with caffeine

Key study populations: Stressed adults; Students; Anxiety sufferers; Caffeine users; Psychiatric patients (adjunctive); Individuals with sleep difficulties

Enhanced Evidence Review

Detailed citation analysis from 17 peer-reviewed sources, organized by research domain.

Mechanisms of Action

Alpha brain wave enhancement promoting relaxed alertness

Strong Human EEG Study (Randomized, Placebo-controlled) 2008 n=35 healthy young adults

EEG study demonstrated that 50 mg L-theanine significantly increased alpha frequency band activity within 45-105 minutes of ingestion compared to placebo, indicating relaxation without drowsiness. Effect observed at realistic dietary levels found in tea consumption.

PubMed: 18296328

GABAergic neurotransmission enhancement and sleep/wake modulation

Strong Animal EEG Study 2019 n=ICR mice and SD rats

GABA/L-theanine combination decreased sleep latency by 14.9-20.7% and increased sleep duration by 26.8-87.3% compared to either alone. Increased GABA receptor and glutamate GluN1 receptor expression, demonstrating synergistic neuromodulatory properties through GABAergic pathways.

PubMed: 30707852

Neurotransmitter regulation through monoamine, GABA, BDNF, and HPA axis pathways

Strong Comprehensive Review 2024 n=Multiple animal, clinical, and epidemiological studies

Comprehensive review of tea component mechanisms for mental health. L-theanine regulates monoamine neurotransmitters (serotonin, dopamine), enhances GABA signaling, increases BDNF, suppresses oxidative stress and inflammation, and modulates the HPA axis and microbiota-gut-brain axis.

PubMed: 39593401

Glutamate receptor antagonism and GABA-A receptor neuroprotection

Strong Systematic Review 2022 n=Multiple preclinical and clinical studies

L-theanine acts as a low-affinity competitive glutamate receptor antagonist and glutamate reuptake inhibitor in the hippocampus. Also demonstrates neuroprotective effects through GABA-A receptor agonism, contributing to anxiolytic and cognitive-enhancing properties.

PubMed: 35111479

Clinical Benefits

Stress and anxiety reduction at 200-400 mg/day

Strong Evidence Systematic Review (PRISMA) 2020 n=9 RCTs Single dose to 8 weeks

PRISMA systematic review of 9 RCTs found L-theanine supplementation at 200-400 mg/day assists in stress and anxiety reduction in people exposed to stressful conditions, promoting relaxation without sedation or cognitive impairment.

PubMed: 31758301

Anxiolytic and anti-stress effects with clinical safety

Strong Evidence Integrative Narrative Review 2019 n=Multiple RCTs reviewed Up to 8 weeks

Review concluded L-theanine at 200-400 mg/day for up to 8 weeks is safe and induces anxiolytic and anti-stress effects in both acute and chronic conditions. Therapeutic potential extends to adjuvant use in schizophrenia management.

PubMed: 31412272

Sleep quality, cognitive function, and stress reduction in healthy adults

Strong Evidence Human RCT (Double-blind, Crossover) 2019 n=30 healthy adults (age 48 +/- 12 years) 4 weeks

200 mg/day L-theanine significantly decreased depression, anxiety, and sleep quality scores (PSQI). Improved sleep latency, sleep disturbance, and reduced sleep medication use. Enhanced verbal fluency and executive function compared to placebo.

PubMed: 31623400

Attention, working memory, and executive function improvement in older adults

Strong Evidence Human RCT (Double-blind, Placebo-controlled) 2021 n=Middle-aged and older adults (50-69 years) Single dose and 12 weeks

Single dose of L-theanine reduced Stroop test reaction time (attention) and increased correct answers while decreasing omission errors on working memory tasks. Demonstrates improvement in attention, working memory, and executive functions.

PubMed: 33751906

Synergistic cognitive performance improvement with caffeine

Strong Evidence Human RCT (Double-blind, Crossover) 2008 n=27 healthy volunteers Single dose (acute effects)

100 mg L-theanine + 50 mg caffeine improved attention-switching task speed and accuracy, and reduced susceptibility to distracting information in memory tasks at 60 and 90 minutes post-dose compared to caffeine alone or placebo.

PubMed: 18681988

Improved sustained attention and reduced tiredness with caffeine combination

Strong Evidence Human RCT (Double-blind, Crossover) 2010 n=44 young adults Single dose (acute effects at 20 and 70 min)

97 mg L-theanine + 40 mg caffeine significantly improved task-switching accuracy, subjective alertness, and reduced tiredness compared to placebo. Demonstrates L-theanine helps focus attention during cognitively demanding tasks.

PubMed: 21040626

Sleep quality improvement in ADHD children

Strong Evidence Human RCT (Double-blind, Placebo-controlled) 2011 n=98 boys with ADHD (ages 8-12) 6 weeks at 400 mg/day

400 mg/day L-theanine significantly improved sleep percentage and sleep efficiency measured by wrist actigraphy in boys with ADHD. Well tolerated with no significant adverse events. Represents potential safe adjunctive therapy for ADHD-associated sleep problems.

PubMed: 22214254

Improved sustained attention and total cognition in ADHD (fMRI evidence)

Moderate Evidence Human RCT (Crossover, fMRI) 2020 n=5 boys with ADHD (ages 8-15) Single dose (acute effects)

L-theanine improved total cognition composite on NIH Cognition Toolbox (p=0.040). L-theanine-caffeine combination improved total cognition (p=0.041) and sustained attention (d-prime on Go/NoGo task, p=0.033), with decreased default mode network reactivity on fMRI.

PubMed: 32753637

Safety & Tolerability

Good safety profile based on extensive toxicology and human data

Excellent Safety Profile Comprehensive Review 2025 n=Multiple studies and toxicology assessments

Comprehensive review of L-theanine chemistry, metabolism, and biological activity. Well absorbed from intestine, crosses blood-brain barrier. Good safety profile based on toxicology studies. L-theanine is naturally found in tea with centuries of safe consumption history.

PubMed: 39854799

Safe and well-tolerated at 400 mg/day in children with ADHD

Good Safety Profile Human RCT (Double-blind, Placebo-controlled) 2011 n=98 boys with ADHD (ages 8-12) 6 weeks

400 mg/day L-theanine (relatively high dose) was well tolerated in children with ADHD with no significant adverse events reported over 6 weeks. Supports safety across age groups including pediatric populations.

PubMed: 22214254

Safe in adults at 200-400 mg/day for up to 8 weeks

Good Safety Profile Integrative Review of Clinical Data 2019 n=Multiple RCTs reviewed

Review concluded L-theanine at 200-400 mg/day for up to 8 weeks is safe in acute and chronic conditions. Cases of hepatotoxicity associated with green tea extract (not isolated L-theanine) have been reported, warranting caution at very high extract doses.

PubMed: 31412272

Dosage Research

Effective dose: 200-400 mg/day for stress and anxiety reduction

Evidence-based Systematic Review 2020 n=9 RCTs

Systematic review identified 200-400 mg/day as the effective dose range for stress and anxiety reduction. Single doses of 200 mg show acute effects within 30-60 minutes. For chronic benefits, daily supplementation for 4-8 weeks is recommended.

PubMed: 31758301

Synergistic dose with caffeine: 100 mg L-theanine + 40-50 mg caffeine

Evidence-based Multiple RCTs 2008 n=27-44 healthy adults per study

Multiple RCTs demonstrate cognitive enhancement at approximately 2:1 ratio of L-theanine to caffeine. 97-100 mg L-theanine combined with 40-50 mg caffeine improves attention, task switching, and alertness. Ratio approximates natural levels in high-quality green tea.

PubMed: 18681988
