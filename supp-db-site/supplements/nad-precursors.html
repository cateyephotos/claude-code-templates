Overview

NAD+ Precursors is classified as metabolic support with moderate evidence (Tier 2) for its primary applications. Two published meta-analyses (Prokopidis 2025 JCSM; Chen 2024 Nutr Metab Cardiovasc Dis) plus 10+ independent RCTs including a Science landmark trial (Yoshino 2021) and Nat Commun cardiovascular RCT (Martens 2018); no single general-population RCT with n>200 prevents Tier 1

Key study populations: Elderly adults; Prediabetic adults; Athletes; Postmenopausal women

Mechanisms of Action

1

NAD+ biosynthesis via Preiss-Handler pathway (gut microbiota conversion)

2

Sirtuin activation (SIRT1-7)

3

Mitochondrial biogenesis and function

4

PARP1/CD38 competition for NAD+

5

DNA repair pathway support

Effect Sizes

Quantified effect sizes from clinical research, where available.

Domain Effect Size / Finding
Nad Blood Levels 150-300% increase (dose-dependent; Trammell 2016, Conze 2019)
Muscle Grip Strength SMD +0.31 (p=0.04; Prokopidis 2025 meta-analysis; n=236)
Systolic Blood Pressure -5.8 mmHg in hypertensive subgroup (Martens 2018; p<0.05)
Insulin Sensitivity Significant improvement in prediabetic women (Yoshino 2021 Science; p=0.04)
Glucose Hba1c Null effect in non-diabetic populations (Chen 2024 meta-analysis)
