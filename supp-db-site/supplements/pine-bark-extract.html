Overview

Pine Bark Extract is classified as antioxidants with moderate evidence (Tier 2) for its primary applications. Extensive research on Pycnogenol brand with cardiovascular and cognitive benefits

Pycnogenol (standardized pine bark extract) has extensive clinical trial data across multiple conditions including cardiometabolic health, chronic venous insufficiency, osteoarthritis, and asthma. Multiple meta-analyses demonstrate significant cardiometabolic effects (glucose, blood pressure, lipids), though a Cochrane Review of 27 RCTs concluded certainty is 'very low' for most outcomes due to small sample sizes, heterogeneity, and risk of bias

Key study populations: ADHD; Cardiovascular disease; Skin aging; Cognitive enhancement

Safety & Tolerability

Excellent safety profile with no treatment-related adverse effects at standard doses

Good Consistently safe across 27+ RCTs

Lima PB, Dias JAF, Esposito ACC, Miot LDB, Miot HA (2020) — Journal of the European Academy of Dermatology and Venereology

All participants completed the trial with no dropouts,No adverse effects related to oral Pycnogenol treatment at 150 mg/day,Pycnogenol described as 'well-tolerated' in the study conclusions,Dosage of 150 mg/day (75 mg twice daily) confirmed safe over 60 days

Pycnogenol for the treatment of chronic disorders (Cochrane Review safety assessment)

Robertson N, Schoonees A, Brand A, Volmink J (2020) — Cochrane Database of Systematic Reviews

No definitive safety concerns identified across 27 RCTs and 1,641 participants,Adverse events generally mild and comparable between Pycnogenol and placebo groups,Gastrointestinal discomfort reported occasionally but not significantly different from placebo,No serious adverse events attributed to Pycnogenol supplementation,Long-term safety data still limited — no studies exceeding 6 months

Dosage Research

Standard clinical dosing: 100-200 mg/day for most conditions; up to 360 mg/day in some studies

Evidence-based Consistent dosing range across multiple independent trials
