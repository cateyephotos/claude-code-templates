Overview

Reishi Mushroom is classified as herbal extracts with moderate evidence (Tier 2) for its primary applications. Strong evidence for immune and stress support; moderate evidence for cognitive benefits

Traditional medicinal mushroom with moderate evidence for stress adaptation and immune support

Key study populations: Chronic fatigue; Stress-related disorders; Immune compromised; Sleep disorders

Clinical Benefits

Stress reduction and improved sleep quality

Moderate Multiple studies Individuals with stress and sleep issues

A randomized, double-blind and placebo-controlled study of a Ganoderma lucidum polysaccharide extract in neurasthenia

Tang, W., Gao, Y., Chen, G., Gao, H., Dai, X., Ye, J., Chan, E., Huang, M., Zhou, S. (2005) — Journal of Medicinal Food

Reishi supplementation significantly improved fatigue, sleep quality, and overall well-being in neurasthenia patients over 8 weeks.

PubMed: 15857210

Cardiovascular health and cholesterol management

Moderate Multiple studies Adults with cardiovascular risk factors

Cholesterol-lowering effect of Ganoderma lucidum in hypercholesterolemic subjects

Chu, T.T., Benzie, I.F., Lam, C.W., Fok, B.S., Lee, K.K., Tomlinson, B. (2012) — British Journal of Nutrition

Reishi supplementation (1.44g daily) significantly reduced LDL cholesterol and improved HDL/LDL ratio in hypercholesterolemic patients.

PubMed: 22017823

Immune function enhancement and infection resistance

Moderate Multiple studies Immunocompromised individuals

Immunomodulatory effects of Ganoderma lucidum in cancer patients

Gao, Y., Zhou, S., Jiang, W., Huang, M., Dai, X. (2003) — Journal of Medicinal Food

Reishi supplementation enhanced immune markers and reduced infection rates in advanced cancer patients undergoing treatment.

Liver health and hepatoprotection

Safety & Tolerability

Generally safe with good tolerance profile

Strong Extensively documented General population
