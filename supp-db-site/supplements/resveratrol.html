Overview

Resveratrol is classified as polyphenols with moderate evidence (Tier 2) for its primary applications. Multiple meta-analyses (including 1 umbrella meta-analysis of 19 MAs, 81 RCTs, n=4088) confirm anti-inflammatory, body composition, and endothelial benefits. Consistent replication across independent research groups. Evidence tier upgraded from 3; SIRT1 mechanism reclassified to Theoretical after Mansouri 2025 null meta-analysis (11 RCTs).

Polyphenol with well-characterized SIRT1/AMPK/NF-kB mechanisms and extensive meta-analytic data showing modest blood pressure reduction at high doses (≥300 mg/day), small lipid profile improvements (TC and TG reduction), significant glucose/HbA1c benefits mainly in diabetic populations, limited cognitive effects in healthy humans, and excellent tolerability up to 5000 mg/day

Key study populations: Cardiovascular patients; Obese adults; Type 2 diabetes; Postmenopausal women; PCOS patients

Mechanisms of Action

1

NF-κB pathway inhibition (anti-inflammatory)

2

eNOS activation and nitric oxide production (endothelial)

3

SIRT1 activation (theoretical — null in human RCTs)

4

Antioxidant/ROS scavenging (indirect clinical evidence)

Safety Profile

Overall Safety Rating: Good

Common Side Effects

  • GI upset
  • Nausea
  • Headache

Contraindications

  • Blood clotting disorders
  • High-dose use (>1000mg/day) — elevated alkaline phosphatase risk

Drug Interactions

  • Blood thinners (anticoagulants)
  • NSAIDs
  • CYP2C9 substrates

Comprehensive pharmacological review of resveratrol. Active mechanisms include SIRT1 pathway activation, NF-kB-mediated anti-inflammatory signaling, antioxidant defense against reactive oxygen species, inhibition of platelet aggregation, anti-adipogenic effects through inhibiting fat accumulation while activating oxidative and lipolytic pathways, and neuroprotective properties. Review of resveratrol molecular mechanisms demonstrating activation of SIRT1 (silent information regulator 1), AMPK (AMP-activated protein kinase), and PI3K/Akt signaling pathways. Also modulates Nrf2 antioxidant defense, Wnt/beta-catenin signaling, and BMP-2/Smad pathways. These multi-pathway effects underlie its diverse biological activities.

Safety & Tolerability

Well tolerated with moderate, reversible adverse effects at doses ≥500 mg/day

Good Safety Profile

(2013) —

Review of resveratrol metabolism, biological effects, and toxicity in humans. Toxicological data confirm resveratrol is well tolerated. Adverse effects (mainly gastrointestinal including nausea, flatulence, abdominal discomfort, and diarrhea) occur at doses of ≥500 mg/day for long periods but remain moderate and reversible. Exhibits antioxidant, anti-inflammatory, glucose/lipid metabolism improvement, and cardiovascular parameter modulation.

PubMed: 23740855

Good Safety Profile

(2021) —

Comprehensive review concluding resveratrol is well tolerated and favorably affects cardiovascular, neurological, and diabetic disorders. Can be considered as nutritional additive and complementary drug. No serious adverse events reported in clinical studies. Caution advised with anticoagulant medications due to antiplatelet properties, and with CYP450-metabolized drugs due to potential interactions.

PubMed: 34649335

Safe pharmacokinetics up to 5000 mg oral dosing with no serious adverse events

Good Safety Profile

(2014) —

Human pharmacokinetic and safety study. Resveratrol derivatives were well tolerated up to oral dosing of 5000 mg with no serious adverse events. Plasma concentrations maintained over 24 hours with repeated daily dosing showing predictable pharmacokinetics consistent with theoretical accumulation curves. Supports safety of high-dose supplementation protocols.

PubMed: 24495149
