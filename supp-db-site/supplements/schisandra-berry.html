Overview

Schisandra Berry is classified as adaptogens with moderate evidence (Tier 2) for its primary applications. Well-documented liver protection and cognitive benefits

Schisandra chinensis demonstrates hepatoprotective and physical performance benefits in small RCTs with strong mechanistic support from lignans including schisandrins; significant CYP3A4 inhibition potential requires caution with co-administered medications

Key study populations: Athletes; Liver health support; Chronic fatigue

Mechanisms of Action

Schisandra lignans exert hepatoprotective, anti-inflammatory, antioxidant, and neuroprotective effects via multiple molecular pathways

Strong Well-established

Yang K, Qiu J, Huang Z, Yu Z, Wang W, Hu H, You Y (2021) — Journal of Ethnopharmacology

Over 200 lignans identified as primary bioactive compounds including schisandrins A, B, C and gomisins,Hepatoprotective effects mediated via Nrf2/ARE antioxidant pathway activation and NF-kB inhibition,CYP3A4 inhibition documented across multiple studies with clinical implications for drug interactions,Anti-inflammatory, antiviral, antitumor, and neuroprotective activities supported by preclinical evidence

PubMed: 34678416

A review on pharmacological activities of Schisandrin C: a potential bioactive lignan from Schisandra chinensis

Xu X, Guo J, Li C, Liu K, Li J (2023) — Chemistry & Biodiversity

Schisandrin C shows potent hepatoprotective activity via antioxidant and anti-inflammatory mechanisms,Demonstrates antitumor activity through apoptosis induction and cell cycle arrest,Anti-inflammatory effects mediated by suppression of NF-kB and MAPK signaling pathways,Neuroprotective potential through reduction of oxidative stress in neural tissue

PubMed: 37990607

Schisandrin B provides dual hepatoprotective and lipid-modulating effects relevant to NAFLD through mitochondrial glutathione regulation

Moderate Replicated in preclinical models

Schisandrin B: A Double-Edged Sword in Nonalcoholic Fatty Liver Disease

Leong PK, Ko KM (2016) — Oxidative Medicine and Cellular Longevity

Schisandrin B enhances mitochondrial glutathione antioxidant status in hepatocytes,Dual action: hepatoprotective at moderate doses but may increase hepatic lipogenesis at high doses,Activates Nrf2/ARE pathway increasing phase II detoxification enzyme expression,Relevant to NAFLD pathophysiology through effects on lipid metabolism and oxidative stress

Clinical Benefits

Schisandra chinensis extract significantly improves knee extensor muscle strength and physical performance in older adults

Strong Partially replicated

Effect of Schisandra chinensis extract supplementation on quadriceps muscle strength and fatigue in adult women: a randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled trial

Cho JH, Kim HK, Kim JY, Jung GW, Kim NS, Kim HJ (2021) — American Journal of Clinical Nutrition

Significant improvement in knee extensor muscle strength vs placebo (p<0.05),Reduced muscle fatigue during repeated contractions,Improved physical performance measures in older adults,1000 mg/day Schisandra chinensis extract well-tolerated with no serious adverse events

PubMed: 33710261

Effects of Schisandra chinensis extract supplementation on quadriceps muscle strength in middle-aged women: A randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled trial

Park JY, Kim JH, Kim JM, Lee BK, Lee JH, Jang HC (2020) — International Journal of Environmental Research and Public Health

Significant increase in quadriceps muscle strength compared to placebo,Improved exercise tolerance and reduced perception of fatigue,Benefits observed in sedentary middle-aged women population,Supports Schisandra as ergogenic supplement for age-related muscle decline

PubMed: 32260466

Schisandra chinensis extract reduces menopausal symptoms including hot flushes, sweating, and heart palpitations

A randomised, double-blind, placebo-controlled trial of Schisandra chinensis for menopausal symptoms

Safety & Tolerability

Schisandra chinensis and its lignans significantly modulate CYP3A4 and CYP1A2 activity, creating clinically relevant drug interaction potential

Strong Well-established

Effects of unprocessed versus vinegar-processed Schisandra chinensis on the activity and mRNA expression of CYP1A2, CYP2E1 and CYP3A4 enzymes in rats

Su T, Mao C, Yin F, Yu Z, Lin Y, Song Y, Lu T (2013) — Journal of Ethnopharmacology

Single or multiple doses of Schisandra extract induced CYP3A4 enzyme activity in rats,CYP1A2 enzyme activity was inhibited by Schisandra, more potent after vinegar processing,CYP2E1 induced after single dose but inhibited after multiple doses,mRNA expression results correlated with pharmacokinetic changes, confirming transcriptional regulation

PubMed: 23376044

Time- and NADPH-Dependent Inhibition on CYP3A by Gomisin A and the Pharmacokinetic Interactions between Gomisin A and Cyclophosphamide in Rats

Zhai J, Zhang F, Gao S, Chen L, Feng G, Yin J, Chen W (2017) — Molecules

Gomisin A is a potent competitive CYP3A inhibitor with Ki value of 1.10 uM,Time-dependent inhibition observed (KI=0.35 uM, kinact=1.96 per min),Bidirectional effect: early pretreatment decreases CYP3A metabolite, late pretreatment increases it due to CYP3A mRNA induction,Clinically relevant interaction potential with CYP3A substrates including immunosuppressants and chemotherapeutics

PubMed: 28786954

Effect of hepar-protecting Wuzhi capsule on pharmacokinetics and dose-effect character of tacrolimus in healthy volunteers

Teng F, Wang W, Zhang W, Qu J, Liu B, Chen J, Liu S, Li M, Chen W, Wei H (2022) — Biopharmaceutics & Drug Disposition

Wuzhi capsule (Schisandra sphenanthera extract) significantly increased tacrolimus Tmax in healthy volunteers,Apparent oral clearance of tacrolimus significantly decreased with co-administration,CYP3A5 genotype influenced the magnitude of the interaction,Confirms clinical relevance of Schisandra-CYP3A drug interactions in humans

PubMed: 35180322

Schisandra chinensis extract is generally well-tolerated at standard doses but requires monitoring for drug interactions

Moderate Consistent across trials

A comprehensive review on phytochemistry, pharmacology, and flavour of Schisandra chinensis and Schisandra sphenanthera

Yang K, Qiu J, Huang Z, Yu Z, Wang W, Hu H, You Y (2021) — Journal of Ethnopharmacology

Long history of safe use in traditional Chinese and Russian medicine,Most common side effects are mild GI discomfort at higher doses,No serious adverse events reported in clinical trials at standard doses,Primary safety concern is drug interaction potential via CYP3A4 and P-glycoprotein modulation

PubMed: 34678416

Dosage Research

1000 mg/day standardized Schisandra chinensis extract used in both strength RCTs,12-week supplementation duration showed significant muscle strength improvement,Dose well-tolerated with no dose-limiting adverse effects reported,Consistent with traditional adaptogenic dosing recommendations of 500-2000 mg/day
