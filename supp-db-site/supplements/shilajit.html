Background

Overview

Shilajit is classified as adaptogen with tier 3: preliminary evidence for its primary applications. Eight RCTs/clinical trials and one systematic review in humans, but most studies have small sample sizes (28-63), several come from the same research groups, and many use the same branded extract (PrimaVie). No large-scale independent replication for most endpoints.

Eight RCTs/clinical trials and one systematic review in humans, but most studies have small sample sizes (28-63), several come from the same research groups, and many use the same branded extract (PrimaVie). No large-scale independent replication for most endpoints.

Key study populations: Healthy male volunteers aged 45-55; Recreationally active men; Postmenopausal women with osteopenia; Patients with tibia fractures; Oligospermic men; Overweight/class I obese adults

Tolerability

Safety Profile

Overall Safety Rating: Good
Common Side Effects
  • No significant adverse effects reported across all trials
Contraindications
  • Pregnancy (insufficient data)
  • Breastfeeding (insufficient data)
  • Hemochromatosis or iron overload disorders
  • Gout or hyperuricemia (may contain purines)
Drug Interactions
  • May potentiate effects of antihypertensive medications
  • Theoretical interaction with anticoagulants due to platelet aggregation effects
  • Caution with hormone-sensitive conditions due to testosterone-increasing effects

Deep Dive

Enhanced Evidence Review

Detailed citation analysis organized by research domain.

Mechanisms of Action

Fulvic acid-mediated mitochondrial electron transport enhancement

Moderate Clinical trial 2016

8 weeks of 250 mg bid shilajit upregulated 17 ECM-related gene probe sets in skeletal muscle including tenascin XB, decorin, myoferlin, collagen, elastin, fibrillin 1, and fibronectin 1. Well tolerated with no changes in blood glucose, lipid profile, creatine kinase, or serum myoglobin.

PubMed: 27414521

RANKL/OPG ratio modulation for bone metabolism

Strong Randomized double-blind placebo-controlled trial 2022 n=60 48 weeks

Shilajit (250 mg or 500 mg) dose-dependently attenuated bone mineral density loss at lumbar spine and femoral neck vs placebo (p<0.001). Decreased CTX-1, BALP, RANKL; increased OPG. Significantly decreased MDA and hsCRP; increased GSH (all p<0.001).

PubMed: 35933897

Hypothalamic-pituitary-gonadal axis modulation for testosterone support

Moderate Randomized double-blind placebo-controlled trial 2016 90 days

250 mg bid shilajit significantly (p<0.05) increased total testosterone, free testosterone, and DHEAS compared to placebo in healthy men aged 45-55. Gonadotropic hormones (LH, FSH) were maintained.

PubMed: 26395129

Antioxidant activity via MDA reduction and GSH increase

Strong Randomized double-blind placebo-controlled trial 2022 n=60 48 weeks

Significantly decreased MDA and hsCRP; increased GSH (all p<0.001) with both 250 mg and 500 mg shilajit doses over 48 weeks.

PubMed: 35933897

Antioxidant activity via MDA reduction and GSH increase

Strong Clinical trial 2010 n=28 completers 90 days

Semen MDA decreased by 18.7% with 200 mg/d processed shilajit over 90 days, alongside significant increases in sperm count (+61.4%) and testosterone (+23.5%).

PubMed: 20078516

Clinical Benefits

Connective Tissue

Strong Randomized controlled trial 2024 n=35 8 weeks

Both 500 mg/d and 1000 mg/d shilajit significantly increased serum pro-c1alpha1 (collagen synthesis biomarker) vs placebo. Low dose d=1.2 (p=0.008); high dose d=1.3 (p=0.007). 75% of high-dose subjects exceeded MCID vs 30% placebo (p=0.03).

PubMed: 36546868

Bone Health

Strong Randomized double-blind placebo-controlled trial 2022 n=60 48 weeks

Dose-dependent attenuation of bone mineral density loss at lumbar spine and femoral neck vs placebo (p<0.001). Decreased CTX-1, BALP, RANKL; increased OPG.

PubMed: 35933897

Bone Health

Strong Randomized double-blind placebo-controlled trial 2020 n=160 28 days treatment, followed until union

Mean bone union time was 129 days (shilajit group) vs 153 days (placebo), approximately 16% faster healing (p<0.049). Dose: 1000 mg/d for 28 days. No significant difference in adverse effects.

PubMed: 32310691

Testosterone Support

Moderate Randomized double-blind placebo-controlled trial 2016 90 days

Significant increase (p<0.05) in total testosterone, free testosterone, and DHEAS vs placebo in healthy men aged 45-55. Gonadotropic hormones (LH, FSH) maintained.

PubMed: 26395129

Testosterone Support

Moderate Clinical trial 2010 n=28 completers 90 days

Serum testosterone increased by 23.5% (p<0.001) with 200 mg/d processed shilajit. Total sperm count +61.4%, motility +12.4-17.4%.

PubMed: 20078516

Testosterone Support

Moderate Systematic review 2024

Purified shilajit extract (PrimaVie) classified as 'possibly effective' for men with late-onset hypogonadism for increasing total testosterone. Most other testosterone boosters failed to increase testosterone.

PubMed: 37697053

Musculoskeletal Health

Moderate Randomized controlled trial 2019 n=63 8 weeks

High dose (500 mg/d): In upper 50th percentile strength group, post-fatigue MVIC decline was 8.9% vs 16.0% placebo (p=0.044). Baseline serum hydroxyproline significantly lower in high-dose group (1.5 vs 2.4 mcg/mL, p=0.024).

PubMed: 30728074

Male Fertility

Moderate Clinical trial (non-placebo-controlled) 2010 n=28 completers 90 days

Total sperm count +61.4%, motility +12.4-17.4%, normal sperm count +18.9% with 200 mg/d processed shilajit. Liver and renal profiles unaffected.

PubMed: 20078516

Skin Health

Low-Moderate Randomized controlled trial 2019 14 weeks

14 weeks of shilajit (125 or 250 mg bid) induced genes relevant to endothelial cell migration, blood vessel growth, and ECM in skin. Higher dose improved skin microperfusion. No adverse effects.

PubMed: 31161927

Safety & Tolerability

General tolerability across multiple clinical trials

Level 2 Randomized double-blind placebo-controlled trial 2022 n=60 48 weeks

No significant adverse events over 48 weeks at either 250 mg or 500 mg doses. Longest duration human safety data available.

PubMed: 35933897

General tolerability across multiple clinical trials

Level 2 Randomized double-blind placebo-controlled trial 2020 n=160 28 days

No significant difference in adverse effects between shilajit (1000 mg/d) and placebo groups in the largest shilajit RCT to date.

PubMed: 32310691

General tolerability across multiple clinical trials

Level 3 Clinical trial 2016 8 weeks

Well tolerated with no changes in blood glucose, lipid profile, creatine kinase, or serum myoglobin at 250 mg bid.

PubMed: 27414521

Dosage Research

200-1000 mg/day of standardized purified extract, most commonly 500 mg/day

Level 2 Randomized controlled trial 2024 n=35

Dose-response observed: 500 mg/d (d=1.2) and 1000 mg/d (d=1.3) both effective for collagen synthesis biomarker. 75% of high-dose subjects exceeded MCID.

PubMed: 36546868

200-1000 mg/day of standardized purified extract, most commonly 500 mg/day

Level 2 Randomized double-blind placebo-controlled trial 2022 n=60

Dose-dependent effects observed: 500 mg outperformed 250 mg for bone mineral density preservation over 48 weeks.

PubMed: 35933897
