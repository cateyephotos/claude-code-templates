Overview

Taurine is classified as amino acids with preliminary evidence (Tier 3) for its primary applications. Tier 3 assigned based on moderate meta-analytic evidence for endurance performance (Waldron 2018, Hedges' g=0.40, 10 studies) insufficient for Tier 2 due to moderate effect size and performance-only indication. Cardiovascular evidence derives from mechanistic and observational studies without consistent large-N RCT support. Well-established pharmacological role as the most abundant free amino acid in mammalian heart and muscle provides mechanism plausibility. GRAS-designated safety profile is strong. Further large-scale RCTs required for Tier 2 elevation.

Semi-essential amino acid with strong evidence for cardiovascular health, exercise performance, and neurological support

Key study populations: Athletes; Cardiovascular patients; Diabetics; General wellness
