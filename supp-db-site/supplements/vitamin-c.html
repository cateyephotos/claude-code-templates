Overview

Vitamin C is classified as essential nutrients with strong evidence (Tier 1) for its primary applications. Multiple meta-analyses across 5+ health domains (immune function, cardiovascular, cancer prevention, glycemic control, antioxidant), with umbrella review of 14 meta-analyses (Chai 2025) and large meta-analyses including Arshadi 2024 (69 studies) and Aragón-Vela 2026 (52 RCTs)

Well-characterized essential nutrient with extensive high-quality evidence for antioxidant and immune support

Key study populations: General population; High-stress individuals; Athletes; Elderly; Type 2 diabetes patients; Cancer prevention populations; COVID-19 patients

Mechanisms of Action

Effect Sizes

Quantified effect sizes from clinical research, where available.

Domain Effect Size / Finding
Antioxidant Status MDA significantly reduced, TAC and GPx significantly improved (Moabedi 2025, 17 RCTs, n=965)
Immune Function Cold duration reduced 8% adults, 14% children (Hemilä 2013); COVID-19 mortality OR=0.64 (Qin 2024, 22 studies)
Cancer Prevention Colorectal cancer RR=0.55, breast cancer RR=0.72, prostate cancer RR=0.88 (Arshadi 2024, 69 studies)
Cardiovascular Significant SBP reduction in T2DM (Aragón-Vela 2026, 52 RCTs, n=1,425)
Glycemic Control Significant FBG and HbA1c improvement — confirmed across 14 meta-analyses (Chai 2025 umbrella review)

Safety Profile

Overall Safety Rating: Excellent

Common Side Effects

  • GI upset (high doses)
  • Kidney stones (very high doses)

Contraindications

  • History of kidney stones
  • Hemochromatosis

Drug Interactions

  • Iron supplements (enhances absorption)
  • Some chemotherapy drugs

Carr, A.C., Maggini, S. (2017) — Nutrients

Clinical Benefits

Reduced duration and severity of common cold

Moderate Multiple studies General population

Vitamin C for preventing and treating the common cold

Hemilä, H., Chalker, E. (2013) — Cochrane Database of Systematic Reviews

Regular vitamin C supplementation reduces cold duration by 8% in adults and 14% in children, with greater effects in people under physical stress.

PubMed: 23440782

Cardiovascular health protection

Moderate Multiple studies Adults at cardiovascular risk

Vitamin C and cardiovascular disease: a systematic review

Ashor, A.W., Lara, J., Mathers, J.C., Siervo, M. (2014) — Journal of Nutritional Science

Vitamin C supplementation shows beneficial effects on endothelial function and may reduce cardiovascular disease risk through improved vascular health.

PubMed: 25191614

Enhanced iron absorption

Strong Extensively replicated Individuals with iron deficiency

Enhancers of iron absorption: ascorbic acid and other organic acids

Lynch, S.R., Cook, J.D. (1980) — American Journal of Clinical Nutrition

Vitamin C significantly enhances non-heme iron absorption by reducing ferric iron to ferrous iron and forming chelation complexes.

PubMed: 15743017

Skin health and wound healing

Strong Multiple studies General population

The role of vitamin C in skin health

Pullar, J.M., Carr, A.C., Vissers, M.C.M. (2017) — Nutrients

Vitamin C is essential for wound healing and skin health through its role in collagen synthesis and antioxidant protection.

PubMed: 28805671

Safety & Tolerability

Generally safe with excellent tolerance profile

Strong Extensively documented General population

Toward a new recommended dietary allowance for vitamin C based on antioxidant and health effects in humans

Levine, M., Rumsey, S.C., Daruwala, R., Park, J.B., Wang, Y. (1999) — American Journal of Clinical Nutrition

Vitamin C has an excellent safety profile with minimal adverse effects at doses up to 2000 mg/day. Higher doses may cause gastrointestinal upset.

PubMed: 10357726

Potential for kidney stone formation at very high doses

Moderate Multiple studies Individuals with history of kidney stones

Vitamin C intake and the risk of kidney stones

Taylor, E.N., Stampfer, M.J., Curhan, G.C. (2004) — Journal of the American Society of Nephrology

Very high vitamin C intake (>1000 mg/day) may increase kidney stone risk in susceptible individuals, particularly men with history of stones.

PubMed: 15213266
