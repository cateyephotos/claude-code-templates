Overview

Vitamin E is classified as essential nutrients with moderate evidence (Tier 2) for its primary applications. Multiple large RCTs (HOPE n=9,541; HOPE-TOO; PHS II n=14,641) and a meta-analysis (n=135,967) establish null CVD benefit and harm at ≥400 IU/day. Positive evidence exists for immune enhancement (Meydani 1997 RCT) and Alzheimer's functional preservation (Dysken 2014 RCT n=613). Tier 2 reflects mature, large-trial evidence base with complex clinical picture.

Well-characterized essential nutrient with strong antioxidant mechanisms but mixed clinical evidence

Key study populations: Elderly (immune function); Mild-to-moderate Alzheimer's patients; Cardiovascular risk populations

Mechanisms of Action

1

Lipid peroxidation inhibition (chain-breaking antioxidant in cell membranes and LDL)

2

T-cell and antibody response enhancement (immunomodulation)

3

Antioxidant neuroprotection — mitochondrial oxidative stress reduction in neurons

Benefits

Cognitive Benefits

  • Neuroprotection
  • Alzheimer's disease functional preservation

Non-Cognitive Benefits

  • Immune function enhancement (elderly)
  • Lipid peroxidation inhibition

Safety Profile

Overall Safety Rating: Caution

Common Side Effects

  • GI upset at high doses
  • Increased bleeding time

Contraindications

  • Blood clotting disorders
  • Pre-surgery (discontinue ≥2 weeks prior)

Drug Interactions

  • Anticoagulants (warfarin — potentiates effect)
  • Antiplatelet agents
  • Chemotherapy (may reduce efficacy)
